Cultural Disclaimer & Acknowledgment

Our commitment to respectful and authentic cultural representation

🌿 Acknowledgment | Mihi

Te Kete Ako acknowledges and respects the mana and taonga of mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge). We recognise that this platform was created with guidance from, and in collaboration with, those who hold cultural knowledge, and we are deeply grateful for their contributions.

We acknowledge Te Tiriti o Waitangi (The Treaty of Waitangi) and our responsibility as a platform operating in Aotearoa New Zealand to honour the partnership, participation, and protection principles that guide bicultural education.

He whakataukī | A proverb:

"Ko te manu e kai ana i te miro, nōna te ngahere. Ko te manu e kai ana i te mātauranga, nōna te ao."

The bird that partakes of the miro berry reigns in the forest. The bird that partakes of knowledge has access to the world.

📚 Our Approach to Mātauranga Māori

Respectful Integration

Our resources aim to integrate mātauranga Māori perspectives in ways that are:

  • Authentic – Grounded in genuine cultural knowledge and practices
  • Contextual – Presented with appropriate cultural context and framing
  • Educational – Designed to support learning for all students in Aotearoa
  • Respectful – Honouring the depth, complexity, and sacredness of Māori knowledge

Ongoing Learning

We acknowledge that we are on a continuous learning journey. While we strive for cultural accuracy and sensitivity, we recognise that:

  • Mātauranga Māori is diverse, with iwi-specific variations and practices
  • Some knowledge is sacred (tapu) and not intended for general sharing
  • Cultural protocols and perspectives continue to evolve
  • We may make mistakes despite our best intentions

⚠️ Important Considerations

Consultation and Guidance

Teachers and educators using Te Kete Ako resources are encouraged to:

  • Consult with local iwi, hapū, and Māori community members when planning lessons
  • Seek guidance from your school's Māori staff, whānau, or cultural advisors
  • Be aware of local tikanga (protocols) and cultural practices in your area
  • Adapt resources to reflect local contexts and iwi perspectives

Not a Substitute for Lived Experience

Te Kete Ako resources are educational tools and should not be viewed as comprehensive representations of mātauranga Māori or Te Ao Māori (the Māori world). They are designed to:

  • Introduce concepts and perspectives
  • Encourage further exploration and learning
  • Support curriculum integration
  • Foster cultural awareness and respect

We encourage students and teachers to seek deeper understanding through direct engagement with Māori communities, experts, and authentic sources.

🤝 Cultural Partnership

Te Kete Ako is committed to working in partnership with Māori educators, cultural advisors, and communities. We welcome:

  • Feedback on the accuracy and appropriateness of our content
  • Corrections where we have made errors or misrepresentations
  • Guidance on how we can improve our cultural responsiveness
  • Collaboration with Māori educators to develop new resources

Beta Platform Notice: During our beta phase, we are particularly focused on gathering feedback about the cultural appropriateness and effectiveness of our resources. Your input helps us improve!

🔍 Source Attribution

Where cultural knowledge, stories, or concepts are shared:

  • We strive to provide appropriate attribution and acknowledgment
  • We reference source materials where possible
  • We distinguish between general cultural knowledge and iwi-specific content
  • We avoid sharing sacred or restricted knowledge

📖 Te Reo Māori Usage

Te Kete Ako uses te reo Māori throughout the platform as part of our commitment to the revitalisation of te reo. We acknowledge:

  • Te reo Māori is a taonga (treasure) and an official language of Aotearoa
  • Our translations aim for accuracy, though we may sometimes use simplified forms for educational accessibility
  • Different dialects and regional variations exist
  • We welcome corrections to our te reo usage

🌱 Our Commitment Going Forward

Te Kete Ako commits to:

  • Continuous improvement of our cultural content and practices
  • Regular consultation with Māori educators and cultural advisors
  • Responsive action when issues are raised
  • Transparency about our processes and limitations
  • Investment in cultural capability development for our team

We believe that education is a powerful tool for building understanding, respect, and partnership between cultures. Our goal is to contribute positively to bicultural education in Aotearoa while always remaining humble about our role and limitations.
