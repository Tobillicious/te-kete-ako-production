Wāhanga 1: Oropuare

Vowel Sounds

This lesson helps ākonga strengthen their understanding of phoneme-grapheme relationships—the connection between sounds in spoken language and their written representations.

Whakatūwhera - Cultural Opening

Like the kōtukutuku that grows strong by the water, this workbook will help you grow your reading and writing skills.

Ngā Whāinga Ako - Learning Intentions

Students Will Learn

  • About vowel teams in English.
  • To identify vowel teams in words.

Students Will Demonstrate

  • By correctly identifying vowel teams in an interactive activity.

Ngā Mahi - Lesson Activities

1. Identify the Vowel Teams

In English, when two vowels join together they often make a single sound. We call these "vowel teams."

For example, in the word "rain," the vowel team "ai" makes the long A sound.
