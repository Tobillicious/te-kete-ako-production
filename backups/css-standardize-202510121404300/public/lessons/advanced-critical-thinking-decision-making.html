    üß† Advanced Critical Thinking: Decision-Making Frameworks

    Navigating Complex Choices with Cultural Wisdom and Analytical Rigor

    Students develop sophisticated decision-making skills by exploring multiple frameworks, including Te Ao MƒÅori approaches, while analyzing real-world scenarios that require careful consideration of multiple perspectives and long-term consequences.

    Whakat≈´whera - Cultural Opening

    In Te Ao MƒÅori, decision-making is not just about individual choice, but about considering the impact on the collective - past, present, and future generations. The whakataukƒ´ reminds us that people are at the heart of every decision. Today, we explore how to make thoughtful, ethical decisions that honor both analytical thinking and cultural wisdom, ensuring our choices benefit not just ourselves, but our communities and the world we leave for future generations.

    NgƒÅ WhƒÅinga Ako - Learning Intentions

    Students Will Learn

    • Multiple decision-making frameworks and when to use each
    • How cultural perspectives influence decision-making processes
    • Techniques for evaluating long-term consequences and ethical implications
    • Methods for incorporating diverse stakeholder perspectives

    Students Will Demonstrate

    • By applying different frameworks to complex scenarios
    • By creating decision trees that consider multiple outcomes
    • By presenting reasoned arguments that acknowledge cultural contexts
    • By evaluating the impact of decisions on different groups

    NgƒÅ Mahi - Lesson Activities (90 minutes)

    1. Decision Framework Exploration (30 mins)

    Setup: Students work in groups to explore different decision-making frameworks, then present their findings to the class.

    Te Ao MƒÅori Framework

    Key Questions:

    • How does this decision affect our relationships (whanaungatanga)?
    • What are the long-term consequences for future generations?
    • Does this honor our cultural values and traditions?
    • How does this impact our connection to the environment?

    When to Use: Community decisions, environmental choices, cultural matters

    Cost-Benefit Analysis

    Key Questions:

    • What are the immediate costs and benefits?
    • What are the long-term financial implications?
    • Who bears the costs and who receives the benefits?
    • Are there hidden costs we haven't considered?

    When to Use: Business decisions, resource allocation, policy choices

    Ethical Decision Matrix

    Key Questions:

    • What are the moral principles at stake?
    • Who might be harmed by this decision?
    • Is this decision fair to all affected parties?
    • Would I be comfortable if this decision were made public?

    When to Use: Personal choices, social issues, controversial topics

    Systems Thinking Approach

    Key Questions:

    • How does this decision affect the broader system?
    • What are the unintended consequences?
    • How do different parts of the system interact?
    • What feedback loops might be created?

    When to Use: Complex problems, organizational decisions, social change

    2. Cultural Decision-Making Deep Dive (25 mins)

    Activity: Students analyze how different cultural perspectives approach the same decision, focusing on values, priorities, and decision-making processes.

    Scenario: School Expansion Decision

    Context: A school needs to expand to accommodate growing enrollment. There are two options: build a new wing on existing land, or relocate to a larger site.

    Te Ao MƒÅori Perspective
    • Whakapapa: How does this affect our connection to this place?
    • Kaitiakitanga: What is our responsibility to care for this land?
    • Whanaungatanga: How does this impact our community relationships?
    • Manaakitanga: How does this affect our ability to care for students?
    Western Economic Perspective
    • Cost Efficiency: Which option provides the best value?
    • Growth Potential: Which supports future expansion?
    • Market Value: How does this affect property values?
    • Operational Costs: Which is more sustainable long-term?

    3. Real-World Case Study Analysis (25 mins)

    Activity: Students work in groups to analyze a complex real-world decision using multiple frameworks.

    Case Study: Renewable Energy Development

    Scenario: A community is considering building a wind farm on traditional MƒÅori land. The project would provide clean energy and economic benefits, but some community members are concerned about cultural and environmental impacts.

    Stakeholder Analysis
    • Local iwi and hap≈´
    • Environmental groups
    • Energy companies
    • Local government
    • Future generations
    Time Horizons
    • Immediate economic benefits
    • Medium-term cultural impact
    • Long-term environmental effects
    • Intergenerational consequences
    Ethical Considerations
    • Environmental justice
    • Cultural preservation
    • Economic equity
    • Informed consent

    4. Decision Tree Creation (10 mins)

    Activity: Students create a decision tree for a personal or community decision, incorporating multiple frameworks and considering various outcomes.

    • Assess ability to consider multiple perspectives

    Extension Activities

    • Research how other cultures approach complex decisions
