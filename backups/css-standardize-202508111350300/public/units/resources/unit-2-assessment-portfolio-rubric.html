    Unit 2: Decolonized History Assessment Portfolio

    Holistic Assessment Framework for Historical Understanding

    Assessment Philosophy - Whakat≈çhea Principles

    This assessment honors the principle that every student brings unique perspectives and strengths to understanding history. Rather than testing memory, we assess critical thinking, cultural understanding, and the ability to see historical patterns that connect to today's world.

    Portfolio Component Options (Choose 3-4)

    Students select components that best showcase their understanding of decolonized Aotearoa history, ensuring authentic assessment that honors diverse learning styles.

    1. Historical Agency Portfolio

    Document and analyze examples of MƒÅori agency, leadership, and resistance throughout Aotearoa's history.

    What Students Create:

    • Profile collection of 5-8 MƒÅori leaders across different time periods

    • Personal reflection on leadership qualities that resonate today

    Success Indicators:

    • Demonstrates understanding of MƒÅori agency in historical events
    • Analyzes diverse forms of leadership and resistance
    • Connects historical strategies to contemporary examples

    Challenge colonial historical myths by researching and presenting alternative perspectives on significant events.

    What Students Create:

    • Research project examining colonial vs MƒÅori perspectives on a chosen historical event

    • Presents well-researched alternative perspective
    • Uses appropriate historical methodology and evidence
    • Demonstrates understanding of historiography and power

    3. Contemporary Connections Analysis

    Link historical patterns and issues to contemporary Aotearoa, showing ongoing relevance of decolonized history.

    What Students Create:

    • Analysis of how historical events shaped modern institutions

    Analyze historical documents, oral histories, and artifacts from multiple perspectives, focusing on MƒÅori voices and experiences.

    What Students Create:

    • Critical analysis of each source's context, purpose, and limitations
    • Comparison of how MƒÅori and colonial sources present same events

    Success Indicators:

    Compare historical and contemporary MƒÅori activism strategies, analyzing their effectiveness and evolution over time.

    • Case studies of successful and challenging activism campaigns
    • Examination of how tactics have evolved with changing contexts
    • Personal reflection on qualities of effective activism

    Success Indicators:

    • Analyzes diverse forms of resistance and activism
    • Understands relationship between context and strategy
    • Evaluates effectiveness of different approaches
    • Connects historical activism to contemporary movements
