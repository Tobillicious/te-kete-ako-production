Year 8 Critical Thinking Unit

Te Whakaaro Māramatanga - Sharp Minds, Strong Decisions

Mangakotukutuku College | Term 3 | 8 Weeks

Develops critical thinking, logical reasoning, and decision-making through real-world NZ contexts

Lesson 1: He aha te Whakaaro Māramatanga?

Focus: Introduction to critical thinking in Te Ao Māori & Pākehā contexts

  • 📜 Whakataukī Analysis: Unpack Māori proverbs for logical reasoning
  • 💭 Google Jamboard: Brainstorm real-life examples of good/poor reasoning
  • 🎯 Cultural Focus: "He kōrero, he kōrero; he kai, he kai" - Words have consequences
  • 📝 Digital Task: Define critical thinking in English & Te Reo
Assessment: Exit ticket - Critical thinking definition in both languages

Lesson 2: Tika, Hē, me te Pono

Focus: Identifying bias, fake news, and credible sources

  • 📰 NZ Context: Analyze headlines about Matariki becoming public holiday
  • 🔍 Chromebook Task: Fact-checking challenge using Google Fact Check Explorer
  • 📖 Te Ao Māori Lens: Pūrākau (Māori narratives) as oral evidence
  • ⚖️ Compare: Written records vs. oral traditions
Assessment: Source reliability evaluation worksheet

Lesson 3: He Tātai Whakaaro

Focus: Recognising logical fallacies in arguments

  • 🎪 "Spot the Flaw": Analyze NZ political speeches/advertisements
  • 🎨 Digital Task: Create Canva infographics on common fallacies
  • 🔍 Focus: Ad hominem, straw man, false cause fallacies
  • 🌀 Māori Perspective: Western logic vs. holistic whakapapa connections
Assessment: Fallacy identification in media examples

Lesson 4: Te Ture me te Whakatau

Focus: Applying ethical frameworks to real dilemmas

  • 🌍 NZ Case Study: "Should NZ ban single-use plastics?"
  • ⚖️ Debate: Utilitarian vs. Kaitiakitanga perspectives
  • 📊 Chromebook Task: Mentimeter poll on ethical choices
  • 📱 Assessment: Ethical decision-making journal (Google Docs)
Assessment: Ethical framework comparison chart

Lesson 5: Te Pakirehua

Focus: Socratic questioning & problem-solving

  • 🦅 Mystery: "The Mystery of the Missing Moa" - Historical NZ mystery
  • 💬 Padlet Discussion: "What questions would you ask experts?"
  • 🗣️ Māori Integration: Explore Māori oral histories as evidence
  • 🔍 Evidence-Based Reasoning: Solve using archaeological data
Assessment: Question formulation rubric

Lesson 6: Te Arotake i ngā Kōrero

Focus: Argument mapping & persuasive techniques

  • 🏛️ NZ Context: Compare arguments for/against Māori wards in local councils
  • 🗺️ Chromebook Task: Use MindMup to map arguments visually
  • 📹 Assessment: Record Flipgrid video defending stance with evidence
  • 🎯 Focus: Identifying premises, conclusions, and evidence quality
Assessment: Argument map + video presentation

Lesson 7: Hei Whakatau Tikanga

Focus: Collaborative problem-solving

  • 💰 Scenario: Class must allocate $10,000 grant (environment, sports, or arts)
  • 👥 Māori Lens: Use whanaungatanga & kotahitanga in discussions
  • 📊 Digital Task: Google Forms survey for final decisions
  • 🤝 Process: Consensus-building and compromise strategies
Assessment: Group decision-making reflection

Lesson 8: Te Whakamātautau Whakaaro

Focus: Critical Thinking Challenge - Real-world application

  • 📊 Task: Critical analysis of current NZ issue (vaping bans, climate policy)
  • 🎙️ Option 1: Create podcast using Anchor.fm
  • 📱 Option 2: Google Slides presentation
  • 🗣️ Option 3: In-class debate with evidence
Assessment: Summative project + peer feedback via Google Sheets
