📜 About Whakapapa

Te Reo: Whakapapa is more than a family tree - it connects us to our tīpuna (ancestors), our whenua (land), and our place in the world. It shapes our identity and responsibilities.

English: Whakapapa means "to lie flat" or "to place in layers" - showing how generations build upon each other. Understanding your whakapapa helps you know where you come from and where you're going.

👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

Cultural Sensitivity: This activity requires care and cultural safety. Please note:

  • Some students may not know their whakapapa due to adoption, disconnection, or family circumstances - never pressure sharing
  • Non-Māori students should be encouraged to explore THEIR cultural heritage with equal respect
  • Whāngai (traditional Māori adoption) and chosen family are valid and honored
  • Consider inviting kaumātua or whānau to support students in learning about their whakapapa
  • Provide alternative prompts for students with complex family situations

How to Use:

  • Give students 1-2 weeks to complete (allows time to ask family questions)
  • Encourage creativity - photos, drawings, symbols, collage all welcome
  • Display completed posters (with student permission) to celebrate diversity
  • Can lead to mihi practice and oral presentations
  • Consider laminating finished posters as keepsakes
