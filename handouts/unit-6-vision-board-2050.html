👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

Purpose: This activity encourages students to think critically and creatively about what a self-determined Aotearoa could look like, grounded in rangatiratanga principles.

How to Use:

  • Can be individual OR collaborative (pairs/small groups)
  • Encourage visual thinking - drawings, symbols, diagrams welcome
  • Play Māori music or have cultural images visible for inspiration
  • Consider displaying finished vision boards around classroom
  • Can be basis for longer project or presentation

Extension Activities:

  • Gallery walk where students present their visions
  • Identify common themes across class visions
  • Create action plans for 1-2 specific changes needed
  • Interview kaumātua or community leaders about THEIR visions for 2050
  • Compare with historical visions (e.g., Te Tiriti promises)

NZ Curriculum Links:

  • Social Studies: Future focus, Social inquiry, Understanding how people view and shape the future
  • Key Competencies: Thinking (imagining possibilities), Relating to others (considering diverse perspectives), Participating and contributing (envisioning civic futures)
  • Values: Innovation, inquiry and curiosity, Ecological sustainability, Community and participation
