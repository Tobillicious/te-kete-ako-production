1. Traditional Environmental Assessment

Reflection Questions:
  1. What would healthy conditions look like according to traditional indicators (water clarity, species diversity)?
  2. How might microplastic pollution affect each of these natural signs?

2. Scientific Microplastic Analysis

Scenario: You are marine scientists studying microplastic contamination in the same area.

Data Analysis Questions:
  1. What patterns might you observe in microplastic distribution (e.g., more in some areas than others)?
  2. How do the scientific findings (e.g., plastic in fish) relate to the traditional indicators (e.g., unhealthy fish)?
  3. What human activities are the likely sources of this pollution?

3. Modern Kaitiakitanga Action Plan

Challenge: As modern kaitiaki, combine traditional and scientific knowledge to develop a response to microplastic pollution.
