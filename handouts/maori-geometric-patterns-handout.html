Art, Ancestors, and Algorithms

KÅwhaiwhai patterns, often seen on the rafters of a marae, are a perfect example of translation (sliding a pattern along a line) and reflection (flipping a pattern). The repeating patterns often represent the genealogy of the iwi, with each element flowing from the last.

The pÄtiki (flounder) pattern is a common design in tukutuku panels. It represents hospitality and abundance. Mathematically, it is a simple yet elegant example of rotational symmetry. The basic diamond shape is rotated around a central point to create the final design.
