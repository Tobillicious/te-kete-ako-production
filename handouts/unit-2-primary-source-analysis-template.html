📋 How to Use This Template

Primary sources are original documents from the time period being studied. When analyzing history - especially Aotearoa's colonial history - we must ask: Whose voice is this? What perspectives are missing? How might the story change if told by someone else?

👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

Purpose: This template teaches critical historical thinking with a decolonizing lens - centering multiple perspectives and identifying whose voices are privileged vs silenced in historical records.

Suggested Documents to Analyze:

  • Te Tiriti o Waitangi (Māori version vs English version comparison)
  • Governor Grey's proclamations during Land Wars
  • Rangatira letters/petitions to the Crown
  • Missionary journals about Māori communities
  • Newspaper coverage of battles/konfiskations
  • Waitangi Tribunal findings (modern perspective on historical injustices)
  • Personal accounts from both sides of the wars

Teaching Strategy:

  • Start with a clear, less controversial document to teach the process
  • Use "think-pair-share" for bias identification sections
  • Compare TWO documents about same event from different perspectives
  • Discuss: Why do certain perspectives dominate historical archives?
  • Emphasize: Centering Māori voices = justice, not "reverse bias"

Assessment Focus:

  • Can student identify author's bias and perspective?
  • Does student recognize missing voices/silenced perspectives?
  • Can student explain HOW perspective shapes historical narrative?
  • Does student use evidence from text to support analysis?
  • Does student demonstrate critical thinking about "official" histories?
