    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Learning Objectives

    By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

    • Develop essential skills for successful transition to secondary school
    • Understand key expectations and routines in high school
    • Apply time management and organization strategies
    • Navigate school spaces and resources confidently
    • Build positive relationships with kaiako (teachers) and peers
    • Incorporate tikanga Māori (Māori customs) into daily school life

    Te Ao Māori: Cultural Context

    Ngā Tikanga o te Kura Tuarua (Secondary School Customs)

    In Te Ao Māori, transitions are important wa (times) that require manaakitanga (care and support). Here are ways to honor tikanga in your high school journey:

    • Whakawhanaungatanga: Building relationships is key. Introduce yourself properly with your pepeha when meeting new kaiako.
    • Mana motuhake: Respect others' space and belongings in shared areas like the whare kai (dining area).
    • Kaitiakitanga: Care for school resources and environments as kaitiaki (guardians).
    • Whanaungatanga: Support your hoa (friends) who may be finding the transition challenging.

    Essential Skills for High School Success

    1. Time Management

    High school requires managing multiple subjects and deadlines. Try this:

    • Use your school diary every day
    • Break big tasks into smaller steps
    • Set reminders for important dates
    • Create a weekly study timetable

    Te reo tip: "He aha te wā?" means "What's the time?" - important for being punctual!

    2. Organization

    Stay on top of your materials with these strategies:

    • Color-code subjects in your folder
    • Pack your bag the night before
    • Keep a clean workspace at home
    • Use digital tools like Google Classroom

    Māori perspective: The concept of raupapa (order) helps maintain balance in life.

    3. Navigating School

    Practice these before school starts:

    • Walk your timetable route between classes
    • Locate key areas: library, whare paku (toilets), office
    • Learn teacher names and room numbers
    • Identify safe spaces and support people

    4. Building Relationships

    Complete this relationship-building challenge:

    Action Completed
    Learn one new classmate's name each day ☐
    Ask a teacher about their subject ☐
    Join a school club or group ☐
    Find a mentor or tuakana (older student) ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
