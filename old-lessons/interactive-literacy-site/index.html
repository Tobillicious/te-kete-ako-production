Nau mai, haere mai ki tēnei pukapuka mahi! Welcome to this workbook designed to help Year 8 ākonga strengthen their understanding of phoneme-grapheme relationships—the connection between sounds in spoken language and their written representations.

The name Mangakōtukutuku comes from the native fuchsia tree (kōtukutuku) that was commonly found near waterways in our local area. Like the kōtukutuku that grows strong by the water, this workbook will help you grow your reading and writing skills.

Whai Mana: Pursuing excellence through confidence

Ngā Rōpū Oropuare: Vowel Teams

In English, when two vowels join together they often make a single sound. We call these "vowel teams."

For example, in the word "rain," the vowel team "ai" makes the long A sound.
