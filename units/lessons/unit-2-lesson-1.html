Unit 2, Lesson 1: Pre-Colonial Innovation

Challenging the Myth of "Primitive" Technology

Lesson Overview

This lesson directly confronts the colonial myth that pre-contact Aotearoa was a "primitive" society. Students will explore the sophisticated technological, agricultural, and architectural achievements of M훮ori, reframing their understanding of what "technology" and "science" mean.

Learning Activities

1. Do Now: Defining "Technology" (10 mins)

In pairs, students list as many examples of "technology" as they can. After 2 minutes, ask them to circle any examples that do not require electricity. Discuss as a class how our modern view of technology is often limited to electronics.

2. Reading & Analysis (20 mins)

Distribute the Pre-Colonial M훮ori Innovation handout. Students read the handout and, in small groups, choose one of the "Domains of Innovation" to become experts on.

3. Expert Group Sharing (15 mins)

Each expert group shares back with the class, explaining why their chosen innovation was a significant scientific or engineering achievement.

4. Exit Ticket (5 mins)

"Name one way that pre-colonial M훮ori innovation demonstrates a deep understanding of science or engineering."

Lesson Details

Learning Intentions:

  • Identify examples of sophisticated pre-colonial M훮ori technology.
  • Explain how these innovations were adaptations to the environment of Aotearoa.
  • Challenge the colonial narrative of a "primitive" pre-contact society.
