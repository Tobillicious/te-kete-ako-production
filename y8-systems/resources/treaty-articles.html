Resource 4.1A: Simplified Treaty Articles

The Queen's governor can be in charge of all the people in New Zealand. (Kāwanatanga/Governance)
Māori chiefs keep their power and authority over their own people and their lands, villages, and treasures. (Tino Rangatiratanga)
The Queen's government must protect Māori and their way of life. (Protection)
Māori get all the same rights and privileges as British people. (Rights)
If Māori want to sell their land, they must offer it to the Queen's government first. (Pre-emption)
The Queen's government promises to make sure there is peace and order for everyone. (Peace and Order)
