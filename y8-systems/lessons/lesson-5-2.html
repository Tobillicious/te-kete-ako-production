Lesson 5.2: Sharing Indigenous Wisdom - Being Good Ancestors

Year 8 Social Studies: How Systems Shape Our Lives

Lesson Activities

1. Indigenous System Presentations (35 mins)

Groups present their decolonized systems as though addressing their community. Focus on how their system honors whakapapa, protects whenua, and serves collective wellbeing. Audience provides feedback using indigenous values: "How does this serve the next seven generations?"

2. Learning from Each Other (15 mins)

Groups reflect on feedback and discuss which indigenous principles they want to integrate into their own thinking about leadership and civic participation.

3. Commitment to Decolonization (15 mins)

Students write personal commitments: "As tangata whenua/tangata tiriti, how will I work toward decolonized systems in my lifetime?" Connect to being good ancestors for future generations.

4. Closing WhakatÅhea (10 mins)

Form a circle. Students share one word that represents their key learning about indigenous governance. Close with karakia to honor the learning journey and acknowledge ongoing responsibilities.

Learning Goal

I can explain how indigenous governance principles offer solutions for creating more just and sustainable systems for future generations.

Cultural Protocol

Honor the sacred nature of this learning. Remind students they are now carriers of indigenous wisdom and have responsibilities to use it respectfully.
