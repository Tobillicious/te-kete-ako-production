Native Bird Lifecycles

Explore the lifecycles of native birds and their importance to Aotearoa

🐦 Learning Objectives

Through studying native bird lifecycles, students will:

  • Understand the stages of bird lifecycles
  • Learn about unique adaptations of native birds
  • Recognize the cultural significance of birds in te ao Māori
  • Understand conservation challenges and solutions

🕊️ Bird 1: Kererū (NZ Wood Pigeon)

The kererū plays a vital role in forest ecosystems:

Lifecycle Stages:

🥚

Egg

1 egg per clutch
28-29 days incubation

🐣

Chick

Fed "crop milk"
30-40 days in nest

🕊️

Juvenile

Learning to fly
6-12 months

🦅

Adult

Breeding age
20+ years lifespan

Cultural Significance: Kererū are taonga species and vital seed dispersers for large-seeded native trees like tawa, miro, and taraire.
