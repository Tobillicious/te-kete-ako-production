Traditional Materials Science

Investigate the properties and uses of traditional Māori materials

🔬 Learning Objectives

Through this materials investigation, students will:

  • Understand properties of natural materials (strength, flexibility, water resistance)
  • Investigate how traditional Māori selected materials for specific purposes
  • Compare traditional and modern material properties
  • Apply scientific thinking to cultural practices

🌿 Material 1: Harakeke (NZ Flax)

Harakeke is one of the most versatile traditional materials:

Material Properties:

💪 Strength

High tensile strength when twisted into rope

🌊 Water Resistance

Natural oils repel water

🎨 Flexibility

Can be woven into intricate patterns

♻️ Sustainability

Renewable resource that regrows

🪵 Material 2: Tōtara Wood

Tōtara is highly valued for building and carving:

Key Property: Tōtara contains natural oils (totarol) that resist rot and insects - making it perfect for waka and whare construction!
