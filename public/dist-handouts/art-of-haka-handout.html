🎭 Elements of Kapa Haka | Ngā Āhuatanga o te Kapa Haka

While watching, identify these elements:

  • Whakaeke: Entrance - How does the group enter?
  • Waiata-ā-ringa: Action song - What story is being told through hand movements?
  • Haka: War dance/challenge - What emotions and power do you observe?
  • Poi: Swinging poi balls - Notice the coordination and rhythm
  • Mōteatea: Traditional chant - Listen for the vocal techniques
  • Whakawātea: Exit - How does the performance conclude?

💭 Reflection Questions | Ngā Pātai Whakaaro

  1. Mana & Power: How does Kapa Haka embody the concept of mana (prestige/power/authority)?
  2. Whakapapa & Connection: Why is it important that Kapa Haka connects past, present, and future generations?
  3. Collective vs Individual: What does Kapa Haka teach us about working together (kotahitanga)?
  4. Cultural Evolution: How has Kapa Haka adapted while staying true to its cultural roots?
  5. Respect & Protocol: What protocols (tikanga) should be followed when watching or participating in Kapa Haka?

🎨 Creative Response Activities

Performance Option:

  • Learn a simple waiata-ā-ringa or school haka with your class
  • Create your own haka representing your school values or class identity
  • Research and present on a famous haka (Ka Mate, Kapa O Pango, etc.)

Analysis Option:

  • Compare Kapa Haka to another cultural performance art form (e.g., Pasifika dance, Indian classical dance)
  • Write an essay: "How Kapa Haka Preserves and Revitalizes Māori Culture"
  • Create a visual guide explaining the different elements of a Kapa Haka performance

👩‍🏫 For Kaiako | Teacher Notes

Learning Objectives:

  • Students identify key elements of Kapa Haka performance
  • Students understand Kapa Haka as cultural expression and preservation
  • Students demonstrate cultural respect and awareness

Cultural Protocol: Before using this resource, consider consulting with your school's Māori department or local kapa haka group. If students will perform haka, ensure they understand the meaning and perform it with respect. Never use haka as entertainment without cultural context.
