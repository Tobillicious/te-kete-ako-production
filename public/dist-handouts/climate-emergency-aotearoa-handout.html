💭 Critical Analysis Questions

  1. Government Responsibility: What does it mean for a government to declare a "climate emergency"? What actions should follow?
  2. Youth Leadership: Why are young people leading the climate movement? What gives them the moral authority to demand action?
  3. Kaitiakitanga: How does the Māori concept of guardianship (kaitiakitanga) relate to climate action? How might Māori approaches offer solutions?
  4. Intergenerational Justice: How is climate change an issue of fairness between generations? What responsibilities do we have to future generations (mokopuna)?
  5. Local Action: What specific climate actions can Aotearoa take given our unique environment and resources?

🎯 Action Projects | Ngā Kaupapa Mahi

School Climate Action Plan:

  • Conduct a carbon audit of your school
  • Design a waste reduction or renewable energy initiative
  • Create a native planting restoration project (connecting to rongoā and biodiversity)
  • Organize a climate education event for your school community

Advocacy & Communication:

  • Write a letter to your local MP about climate action
  • Create social media campaign promoting kaitiakitanga-based climate solutions
  • Organize a student-led climate symposium
  • Interview local kaumātua about observed environmental changes
