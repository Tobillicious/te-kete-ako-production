Physics Of Traditional Games

Discover the science behind traditional Māori games and activities

🎯 Learning Objectives

Through exploring traditional games, students will:

  • Understand forces, motion, and energy in traditional activities
  • Apply physics concepts to analyze game mechanics
  • Connect scientific thinking with cultural knowledge
  • Appreciate the engineering behind traditional Māori games

⚡ Game 1: Ki-o-rahi (Ball Game Physics)

Ki-o-rahi involves throwing, catching, and running - full of physics!

Physics Concepts in Ki-o-rahi:

🎯 Projectile Motion

The ball follows a parabolic path when thrown

💨 Air Resistance

Slows the ball as it moves through the air

⚡ Force & Acceleration

Throwing force determines speed and distance

🔄 Angular Momentum

Spin affects ball stability in flight

🌀 Game 2: Poi (Circular Motion)

Poi demonstrates centripetal force and circular motion:

The Science: When you swing poi, centripetal force keeps it moving in a circle. The faster you swing, the greater the tension in the string!
