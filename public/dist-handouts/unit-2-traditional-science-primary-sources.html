The Unwritten Encyclopedia

Before written language was introduced to Aotearoa, MÄori held a vast encyclopedia of scientific knowledge in their minds. This knowledge, or mÄtauranga, was passed down through generations in kÅrero (stories), waiata (songs), and whakapapa (genealogies). When Europeans arrived, some recorded their observations of this knowledge in action. These records, while often biased, are valuable primary sources that give us a window into a sophisticated scientific tradition.
