Maramataka Time Mathematics

Explore the mathematics of the Māori lunar calendar

🌙 Learning Objectives

Through exploring the maramataka, students will:

  • Understand lunar cycles and their mathematical patterns
  • Calculate moon phases using the maramataka system
  • Compare different calendar systems mathematically
  • Apply patterns and sequences to track time

🌒 Part 1: The Lunar Cycle

The maramataka is based on the moon's 29.5-day cycle:

Key Fact: A lunar month is 29.53 days on average. Traditional Māori tracked each phase with specific names and purposes.

📅 Part 2: The 30 Nights of Maramataka

Each night has a name and specific characteristics:

Key Phases (Examples):

🌑 Whiro (Night 1)

New moon - time for rest and reflection

🌓 Ohua (Night 5-7)

Growing moon - energy building

🌕 Rakaunui (Night 14-15)

Full moon - peak activity time

🌘 Mutuwhenua (Night 28-29)

Waning moon - completion phase
