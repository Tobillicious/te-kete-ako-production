After Watching | Whai muri i te mātakitaki

💭 Critical Thinking Questions

  1. Translation Issues: Why do you think "Tino Rangatiratanga" (absolute chieftainship/sovereignty) was translated as "full exclusive possession"? What impact might this difference have had?
  2. Different Perspectives: How might Māori and Pākehā view the Treaty differently based on which version they read?
  3. Contemporary Relevance: Give three examples of how the Treaty affects life in Aotearoa today.
  4. Your Voice: As a young person living in Aotearoa, why is it important for YOU to understand Te Tiriti?

🎯 Extension Activities

Research Project:

  • Investigate a local Treaty settlement in your region

  • Create a timeline of key Treaty-related events from 1840 to today

👩‍🏫 For Kaiako | Teacher Notes

Learning Objectives:

  • Students understand the three articles of Te Tiriti o Waitangi
  • Students recognize translation differences and their significance
  • Students connect Treaty principles to contemporary Aotearoa

Differentiation:

  • Support: Pause video frequently, provide written article summaries, use visual organizers
  • Extension: Analyze Treaty Tribunal cases, debate sovereignty interpretations, connect to international indigenous rights

Cultural Safety: This is a sensitive topic. Ensure Māori student voices are centered. Be prepared for diverse perspectives and emotions. Consider inviting a local kaumātua or Treaty educator as a guest speaker.
