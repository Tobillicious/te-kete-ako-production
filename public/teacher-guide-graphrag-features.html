📚 Teacher Guide: GraphRAG Discovery Features

Unlock the power of AI-powered resource connections

What is GraphRAG?

GraphRAG (Graph-based Retrieval Augmented Generation) is Te Kete Ako's AI-powered knowledge graph that discovers connections between teaching resources across subjects, year levels, and cultural contexts.

💡 In Plain English: GraphRAG automatically finds resources that work well together, even if they're from different subjects or year levels. It's like having a super-smart librarian who knows every connection between every resource!

Three Discovery Features

🔗 1. Similar Resources

Where you'll find it: At the bottom of most lesson and handout pages

What it does: Shows 6 related resources that connect to the current page

Why it's useful: Helps you find extension activities, prerequisite lessons, or cross-curricular connections automatically

Example: On a Y8 Science "Cells & Whakapapa" lesson, you might see:
    Y7 Science: "Introduction to Living Things" (prerequisite)
  • Y9 Science: "Genetics & DNA" (extension)
  • English: "Whakapapa Narratives" (cross-curricular)

⚡ 2. Most Connected Resources

Where you'll find it: On subject hub pages (Science, Math, English, etc.)

What it does: Highlights the top 10 resources with the most connections to other resources

Why it's useful: These are your "cornerstone" resources - lessons that connect to many other topics and work well as anchor points for units

Connection Badges:
    ⚡ CHAMPION (50+ connections) - Highly versatile resources
  • 🌟 HIGHLY CONNECTED (20+ connections) - Great anchor lessons
  • 🔗 WELL CONNECTED (10+ connections) - Solid integration points

🌈 3. Quality Badges

Where you'll find them: On resource cards throughout the site

What they mean:

  • ⭐ 🌟🌟🌟🌟 High Quality+ (Gold) - Highest quality resources
  • 🌿 Cultural (Green) - Strong cultural integration
  • 🌟🌟🌟 Good+ (Blue) - High quality resources

Why it's useful: Quickly identify the best resources for your planning

How to Use These Features

📝 Scenario 1: Planning a Unit

  1. Go to your subject hub (e.g., Science Hub)
  2. Check the "Most Connected Resources" section
  3. Pick a CHAMPION or HIGHLY CONNECTED resource as your anchor lesson
  4. Open that lesson
  5. Scroll to "Similar Resources" at the bottom
  6. See suggested prerequisite, extension, and cross-curricular resources
  7. Build your unit sequence from these connections!

🔍 Scenario 2: Finding Extension Activities

  1. You've just taught a lesson (e.g., "Y7 Algebra Patterns")
  2. A student finishes early and needs extension work
  3. Scroll to "Similar Resources" on the lesson page
  4. Look for resources labeled "Build on this next →" or "Extends this concept"
  5. Send the student to that resource for deeper learning

🌏 Scenario 3: Cross-Curricular Planning

  1. You're planning an integrated unit with another teacher
  2. Open a lesson from your subject area
  3. Check "Similar Resources"
  4. Look for resources from other subjects (labeled "Cross-curricular connection")
  5. Share these connections with your co-planning teacher
  6. Build integrated lessons using GraphRAG's suggestions!

Understanding Relationship Labels

When you see Similar Resources, each one has a label explaining the connection:

Label What it Means
Build on this next → This is a natural next step after the current lesson
Follows naturally from this This connects logically to the current content
Related topic Similar subject matter or skills
Cross-curricular connection From a different subject but related concepts
Cultural perspective Adds mātauranga Māori or cultural context
Real-world application Shows how to apply the concepts practically

💡 Tips & Tricks

Smart Teaching Strategies

  • Start with CHAMPIONS: When planning new units, begin with resources that have 50+ connections - they're proven to integrate well with other content
  • Follow the cultural badges: Resources marked 🌿 Cultural have strong mātauranga Māori integration - great for culturally responsive practice
  • Mix quality levels: 🌟🌟🌟🌟 High Quality+ resources are polished and ready-to-use; 🌟🌟🌟 Good+ resources are also excellent but might need minor adaptation
  • Trust the AI: GraphRAG has analyzed 240,000+ relationships - its suggestions are based on actual content connections, not just keywords
  • Check regularly: The knowledge graph grows weekly as new resources are added and connections are discovered

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How does GraphRAG know which resources connect?

GraphRAG uses AI to analyze the content of every resource - reading the text, understanding the concepts, year levels, cultural contexts, and learning objectives. It then builds a knowledge graph showing how resources relate to each other.

Can I trust the AI's suggestions?

Yes! GraphRAG's suggestions are based on actual content analysis, not guesswork. However, you're the expert on your students - use these as starting points and adapt as needed for your context.

What if I don't see Similar Resources on a page?

Some older resources haven't been fully integrated into GraphRAG yet. We're adding more connections every week! If a resource is particularly important to you, let us know and we'll prioritize it.

Can I suggest connections the AI missed?

Absolutely! Use the feedback form at the bottom of any page to suggest connections. Your expertise helps improve the system for everyone!
