The official New Zealand Curriculum for Health & Physical Education, grounded in Te Whare Tapa Whā model of holistic wellbeing: taha tinana (physical), taha hinengaro (mental/emotional), taha whānau (social), taha wairua (spiritual).

🏠 Te Whare Tapa Whā

💪 Taha Tinana

Physical Wellbeing

Capacity for physical growth and development. The body's ability to function effectively.

🧠 Taha Hinengaro

Mental & Emotional Wellbeing

Capacity to think and feel. Express thoughts and emotions coherently.

🤝 Taha Whānau

Social Wellbeing

Capacity to belong, care, and share as part of family and community.

✨ Taha Wairua

Spiritual Wellbeing

Capacity for faith and wider connection. Values, identity, and purpose.

🎯 Achievement Objectives - Level 4 (Years 7-8)

💪 Personal Health & Physical Development

Y7 Y8

A1: Personal Growth & Development

  • Describe the characteristics of pubertal change and discuss positive adjustment strategies
  • Understand that personal growth and development are influenced by many factors

A2: Regular Physical Activity

  • Participate in creative and regular physical activities and identify factors that affect performance
  • Understand the benefits of regular physical activity for overall wellbeing

A3: Safety Management

  • Access and use information to make and action safe choices in a range of contexts
  • Demonstrate a range of interpersonal skills and responsible behaviors

🏃 Movement Concepts & Motor Skills

B1: Movement Skills

  • Develop movement skills in a range of environments and situations
  • Demonstrate controlled and accurate movements in variety of contexts

B2: Positive Attitudes

  • Demonstrate willingness to accept challenges and learn new skills
  • Participate positively in group activities

🤝 Relationships with Other People

C1: Relationships

  • Identify the effects of changing situations on relationships
  • Describe how to maintain relationships and manage changing relationships
  • Connect to whanaungatanga and manaakitanga values

C2: Identity, Sensitivity, and Respect

  • Identify factors that affect personal and group identity
  • Describe how to respect diversity and act with integrity
  • Demonstrate a range of interpersonal skills in different contexts

🌍 Healthy Communities & Environments

D1: Societal Attitudes & Values

  • Identify media influences on societal attitudes and values
  • Examine how these messages might impact on personal and group identities

D2: Community Resources

  • Identify community resources and strategies to support wellbeing
  • Take action to enhance people's wellbeing and environment
  • Connect to kaitiakitanga and community responsibility
