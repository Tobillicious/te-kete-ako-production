📊Achievement Objectives by Level

Level 3 (Years 5-6)

Y5-6

Number and Algebra

  • In a range of meaningful contexts, students will be engaged in thinking mathematically and statistically
  • Apply additive and simple multiplicative strategies flexibly to combine or partition whole numbers
  • Know fractions and percentages in everyday use

Geometry and Measurement

  • Classify plane shapes and prisms by their spatial features
  • Represent objects with drawings and models
  • Measure time, temperature, length, area, volume, capacity, weight, and angle using appropriate devices

Statistics

  • Conduct investigations using statistical enquiry cycle: gathering, sorting, displaying simple data, discussing results
  • Compare statements with evidence from investigations

Level 4 (Years 7-8)

Y7Y8

Number and Algebra

  • Apply multiplicative strategies flexibly to combine or partition whole numbers, decimal, and fractions
  • Express quantities in different ways (fractions, decimals, percentages)
  • Apply knowledge of rounding, place value, and partitioning to calculations
  • Use simple additive strategies with integers, ratios, and simple proportions

Geometry and Measurement

  • Use appropriate scales, devices, and metric units for length, area, volume, capacity, weight, temperature, angle, and time
  • Convert between metric units using whole numbers and commonly used decimals
  • Create and use appropriate units and devices to measure
  • Use side or edge length to find perimeters and areas of rectangles, parallelograms, and triangles

Statistics

  • Plan and conduct investigations using statistical enquiry cycle
  • Determine appropriate variables and data collection methods
  • Evaluate data collection methods
  • Use simple fractions and percentages to describe data
  • Make statements about implications for the wider population
