    Western Science MÄori Knowledge System
    1. Observation 1. Titiro (Observation) - Careful watching over time
    2. Question 2. PÄtai (Questioning) - Considering relationships
    3. Hypothesis 3. Whakapae (Proposition) - Based on ancestral knowledge
    4. Experiment 4. WhakamÄtautau (Testing) - Practical trials
    5. Analysis 5. TÄtari (Analysis) - Considering all factors
    6. Conclusion 6. Whakatau (Conclusion) - Consensus decision

    Interactive Activity: RongoÄ MÄori (Traditional Medicine) Investigation

    Scenario: MÄori traditionally used kawakawa leaves for medicinal purposes. Let's investigate their antibacterial properties using both scientific approaches.

    Western Scientific Approach:

    1. Observe that kawakawa seems to prevent infection when applied to wounds
    2. Question: Does kawakawa extract inhibit bacterial growth?
    3. Hypothesis: Kawakawa extract will prevent bacterial growth in petri dishes
    4. Experiment: Create agar plates with different concentrations of kawakawa extract
    5. Measure zones of inhibition after incubation

    MÄori Knowledge Approach:

    1. Titiro: Observe how ancestors used kawakawa for generations
    2. PÄtai: In what situations was it most effective?
    3. Whakapae: The plant is most effective when prepared in specific ways
    4. WhakamÄtautau: Test different preparation methods (fresh vs dried, different seasons)
    5. TÄtari: Consider environmental factors like time of harvest

    Lesson Sequence (2-3 sessions):

    1. Introduction (30 min):
      • Discuss what science is - broaden understanding beyond Western paradigm
      • Introduce MÄori scientific traditions through examples (navigation, medicine, agriculture)
      • Compare the two methodologies using the table provided
    2. Practical Investigation (60-90 min):
      • Divide class into groups - some use Western method, some use MÄori approach
      • Guide through investigation protocols for both methods
      • Emphasize respectful handling of MÄori knowledge
    3. Analysis and Reflection (60 min):
      • Groups share findings
      • Discuss strengths of each approach
