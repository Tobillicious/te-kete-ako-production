📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: "Before pharmacies and pills, how did people heal themselves? Māori used plants - rongoā! For 800+ years, tohunga (healers) passed down plant knowledge. Today we'll learn BOTH mātauranga Māori AND the science behind it!"

What is Rongoā?

Rongoā = traditional Māori healing using native plants, massage (mirimiri), spiritual practices (karakia). It's holistic - treats body, mind, and spirit together. Tohunga had deep knowledge of which plants heal which ailments.

2️⃣ Activity 1: Meet the Plants! (25 minutes)

Station rotation: 5 plant stations with samples (photos, dried leaves, or real plants if available).

The 5 Rongoā Plants:
1. KAWAKAWA

Uses: Stomach aches, cuts, bruises, infections
Part used: Leaves (chewed, made into tea, or poultice)
Science: Contains compounds with antibacterial properties!

2. HARAKEKE (Flax)

Uses: Burns, cuts, wounds (antibacterial gel inside leaves)
Part used: Gel from leaf base
Science: Gel has antiseptic properties (kills germs)

3. KŌWHAI

Uses: Bone/joint problems, sprains
Part used: Bark (made into wash or poultice)
Science: Anti-inflammatory compounds

4. MĀNUKA

Uses: Colds, flu, skin infections
Part used: Leaves (tea), honey from flowers
Science: Famous mānuka honey has powerful antibacterial properties (proven by science!)

5. HOROPITO (NZ Pepper Tree)

Uses: Skin conditions, infections, stomach issues
Part used: Leaves
Science: Contains compound that kills fungi and bacteria

Task: At each station, record: Plant name, traditional use, which part is used, one cool fact.

3️⃣ Activity 2: The Science Behind Rongoā (15 minutes)

Class discussion: How do plants make medicine?

Plant Chemistry:
  • Plants produce chemical compounds (molecules) to protect themselves from bugs, diseases, UV
  • MANY of these compounds also help HUMANS! (antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, pain relief)
  • Modern medicine often comes from plants (aspirin from willow bark, morphine from poppies)
  • Tohunga were plant chemists! They knew which plants worked through observation & experimentation over centuries

Discussion: "How is mātauranga Māori LIKE science? Both involve observation, testing, passing on knowledge!"

4️⃣ Create a Rongoā Card (10 minutes)

Individual task: Design an information card for ONE rongoā plant (like a Pokémon card!).

Include: Drawing/photo, Māori name, English name, traditional use, plant part used, one scientific fact.

⚠️ Important Cultural Note

  • DO NOT try rongoā at home without expert guidance! Plants can be powerful and some are poisonous if used incorrectly.
  • Rongoā is a living practice: Tohunga train for YEARS. We're learning about it, not practicing it.
  • Respect: This is sacred knowledge (tapu). We learn with humility and gratitude.
  • Modern integration: Today, some people use rongoā alongside Western medicine (with doctor's knowledge).
