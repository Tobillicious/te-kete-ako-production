YEAR 7 DIGITAL TECHNOLOGIES

Digital Citizenship & Kaitiakitanga

💻 Digital Technologies • ⏱️ 60 minutes • 👥 Year 7 • 🌿 Cultural: Digital guardianship

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Kaitiakitanga
Digital guardianship online
🤝 Respect
Treating others kindly online
💪 Integrity
Honest digital behavior

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Define digital citizenship and why it matters
  • Identify safe vs unsafe online behaviors
  • Apply kaitiakitanga principles to digital spaces
  • Create a personal digital citizenship plan

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can explain digital citizenship
  • I can identify 3 safe online practices
  • I can explain kaitiakitanga in digital context
  • I create 3 personal digital citizenship goals

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: "How many hours did you spend online yesterday? Raise hands: 0-1 hour? 1-3 hours? 3+ hours?"

Digital Citizenship =

How you behave, treat others, and protect yourself online. Just like being a good citizen in your community, you can be a good citizen online!

2️⃣ Activity 1: Digital Scenarios (20 minutes)

Group discussions: Read scenarios, decide: safe or unsafe? What would you do?

Scenario Examples:
  1. Someone you don't know friend-requests you. Accept? ❌ (Stranger danger online too!)
  2. Classmate posts mean comment about another student. Ignore? Report? ✅ (Report or speak up)
  3. You see cool game, wants your address. Share? ❌ (Never share personal info)
  4. Friend shares homework answers in group chat. Use them? ❌ (Academic dishonesty)
  5. You spot fake news about Māori. What do? ✅ (Check sources, report misinformation)

3️⃣ Activity 2: Digital Kaitiakitanga (15 minutes)

Cultural connection: Kaitiakitanga online = being a guardian of digital spaces.

Digital Kaitiakitanga Principles:
  • Manaakitanga: Care for others online (no cyberbullying, be kind)
  • Whanaungatanga: Build positive digital communities
  • Kaitiakitanga: Protect your digital whenua (data, privacy, reputation)
  • Tika (correctness): Share accurate info, fact-check
  • Pono (honesty): Be truthful, don't catfish or fake

Task: Create "Digital Kaitiakitanga" poster with 5 actions YOU will take to be a digital guardian.

4️⃣ Conclusion & Commitment (5 minutes)

Class pledge: Together read "Digital Kaitiakitanga Pledge" - commit to safe, respectful online behavior.

Exit ticket: "What is ONE thing you'll do differently online after today's lesson?"
