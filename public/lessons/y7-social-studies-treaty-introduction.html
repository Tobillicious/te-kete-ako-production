YEAR 7 SOCIAL STUDIES

Introduction to Te Tiriti o Waitangi

🏛️ Social Studies • ⏱️ 60 minutes • 👥 Year 7 • 🌿 Cultural: Te Tiriti foundation

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Respect
Honoring Treaty as founding document
💪 Integrity
Understanding historical truth
🎯 Excellence
Critical thinking about history

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Explain what Te Tiriti o Waitangi is and when it was signed
  • Identify the main parties involved (Crown and Māori rangatira)
  • Understand why Te Tiriti was created
  • Recognize Te Tiriti as NZ's founding document

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can state when Te Tiriti was signed (1840)
  • I can name 2 Māori leaders who signed
  • I can explain in simple terms what a treaty is
  • I can say why Te Tiriti matters to NZ today

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: Display a replica image of Te Tiriti. "What do you notice? What language can you see? Why might there be two languages on one document?"

What is a Treaty?

A treaty is a formal agreement between two or more groups (often countries or peoples). It's like a promise - both sides agree to certain things and have responsibilities. Treaties are VERY important in law and history.

Key question: "Why would Māori and the British make an agreement in 1840?"

2️⃣ Activity 1: Historical Context (15 minutes)

Teacher explains: What was happening in 1840 in Aotearoa?

1840 Context:
  • Māori: Had been living in Aotearoa for 500+ years, well-established iwi and hapū
  • British settlers: Arriving in increasing numbers (whalers, traders, missionaries)
  • Problems: Lawlessness, land disputes, no clear governance
  • British Crown: Wanted to establish law and order, protect settlers
  • Māori rangatira: Concerned about land sales and settler behavior, wanted protection
The Signing:
  • 📅 Date: February 6, 1840 (Waitangi Day!)
  • 📍 Place: Waitangi, Bay of Islands
  • ✍️ Signatories: About 540 Māori rangatira eventually signed
  • 👤 British rep: Captain William Hobson
  • 📜 Versions: English text AND Te Reo Māori text (different meanings!)

Group discussion: "Why do you think both Māori and British wanted this agreement?"

3️⃣ Activity 2: What Did Te Tiriti Say? (20 minutes)

Class activity: Learn the 3 main articles (simplified for Year 7).

The 3 Articles (Simplified):
Article 1: Governance

English version: Māori gave Britain sovereignty (total control)
Māori version: Māori gave Britain kāwanatanga (governorship - like management, not total control)

Article 2: Māori Rights

Māori keep tino rangatiratanga (authority/sovereignty) over their lands, villages, and treasures (taonga). British Crown has right to buy land if Māori want to sell.

Article 3: Equal Rights

Māori have the same rights as British citizens. The Crown protects Māori.

The BIG Problem:

The English and Māori versions say DIFFERENT things! This has caused problems and arguments ever since 1840. It's still being debated today.

Task (pairs): Discuss - "If you were a Māori rangatira in 1840, what would you want from this treaty? If you were British, what would you want?"

4️⃣ Activity 3: Why Does Te Tiriti Matter Today? (10 minutes)

Class discussion: Te Tiriti isn't just history - it affects NZ TODAY!

Modern Relevance:
  • Waitangi Tribunal: Court that hears Treaty breaches
  • Treaty settlements: Government giving land/money back to iwi for past wrongs
  • Partnership: Government and Māori work together on decisions
  • Your school: Te Tiriti principles in school values and curriculum
  • Waitangi Day: February 6 is a public holiday to remember Te Tiriti

Think-pair-share: "Can you think of one way Te Tiriti affects your life today?"

5️⃣ Conclusion & Reflection (5 minutes)

Class summary: Quick recap - "Who can tell me one thing they learned about Te Tiriti today?"

Exit ticket: "Draw a simple picture showing TWO groups (Māori and British) making an agreement. Label it 'Te Tiriti 1840'."

📊 Assessment

Success Indicators:
  • ✅ Students can state the year (1840)
  • ✅ Students understand a treaty is an agreement
  • ✅ Students know Te Tiriti involved Māori and British
  • ✅ Students recognize Te Tiriti still matters today

📦 Resources Needed

  • Image of Te Tiriti document (print or digital)
  • Whiteboard for timeline
  • Map of New Zealand showing Waitangi
  • Paper and pencils for exit ticket

💡 Teacher Notes

  • Cultural sensitivity: This is a foundational topic. Establish respectful discussion norms.
  • Simplification: Year 7 intro - details come in later years. Focus on "what" and "why," not deep analysis yet.
  • Extension: Advanced students can research specific rangatira who signed (e.g., Hōne Heke, Tāmati Wāka Nene).
  • Follow-up: Next lessons can explore the differences between versions, Treaty breaches, and modern settlements.
  • Local connection: Research if local iwi signed Te Tiriti - when and where?
