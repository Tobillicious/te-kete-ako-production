    A Forum for Justice: The Waitangi Tribunal

    ⚡ Quick Start: Teach This Tomorrow

    🕐 Prep Time: 15-20 minutes | 📄 Materials: Print handout + whiteboard

    3-Step Implementation:

    1. Tonight: Read lesson plan (10 min), print handout (5 min)
    2. Tomorrow Morning: Review whakataukī and cultural context (5 min)
    3. In Class: Follow lesson sequence, adapt as needed

    Unit: Walker - The Challenge to the Narrative | Lesson 1.4

    Year 10 | Social Studies, History, Civics, Law | 75 minutes

    ⚖️ School Value: Whaimana (Integrity)

    The Waitangi Tribunal demonstrates integrity by acknowledging historical wrongs and seeking to make them right, even when it's difficult or controversial.

    Overview: This lesson explores the establishment and evolution of the Waitangi Tribunal as a forum for addressing Treaty of Waitangi grievances. Students will understand how activism (Lesson 1.3) led to institutional change, examine how the Tribunal works, and analyze its significance in New Zealand's journey toward Treaty justice.

    🎯 WALT: We are learning to understand the role and significance of the Waitangi Tribunal in addressing historical injustices

    ✅ Success Criteria:

    • Explain why the Waitangi Tribunal was established in 1975
    • Describe how the Tribunal investigates Treaty breaches
    • Give two examples of significant Tribunal claims and outcomes
    • Evaluate the effectiveness of the Tribunal as a tool for justice
    • Connect to Whaimana (Integrity)

    📚 Key Vocabulary

    • Waitangi Tribunal: Permanent commission investigating alleged breaches of the Treaty
    • Grievance: Formal complaint about something wrong or unfair
    • Redress: Remedy or compensation for a wrong
    • Te Tiriti o Waitangi: The Treaty of Waitangi (Māori version)
    • Retrospective: Looking back on past events

    Timeline: 1975 (established) → 1985 (amended for historical claims back to 1840) → 1990s-present (major settlements)

    ⭐ WAGOLL Case Study Analysis

    Waikato-Tainui Raupatu Claim Example:

    1. Grievance: 1.2 million acres confiscated by Crown in 1863
    2. Evidence: Historical documents, oral histories, maps
    3. Findings: Confiscation was unjust and in breach of Treaty
    4. Redress: $170M compensation + apology (1995)

    Activity: Analyze your own case study using this framework, then create a one-page summary poster.
