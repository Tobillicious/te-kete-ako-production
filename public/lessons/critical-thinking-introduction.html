    Introduction to Critical Thinking

    Critical thinking is the ability to analyze information objectively, question assumptions, and make reasoned judgments. In Te Ao Māori, this aligns with the concept of whakaaro nui (deep thinking) and mātauranga (knowledge gained through careful observation and reflection).

    🎯 Lesson Activities

    Activity 1: What is Critical Thinking? (15 mins)

    Kaupapa: Students brainstorm what they think "critical thinking" means and explore its connection to mātauranga Māori.

    • Individual Reflection: Think of a time when you questioned something you were told. What made you skeptical? How did you find the truth?
    • Group Discussion: Share examples and identify common elements of critical thinking
    • Cultural Connection: How do kaumātua (elders) use critical thinking when sharing wisdom? What questions do they ask?

    Activity 2: Analyzing Claims (20 mins)

    Kaupapa: Practice evaluating statements using the "5 W's + H" framework.

    Sample Claim: "Social media is harmful to young people."

    1. Who says this? What is their perspective or bias?
    2. What evidence supports or contradicts this claim?
    3. When is this true? Are there contexts where it's not true?
    4. Where does this apply? Does it vary by location or culture?
    5. Why might someone make this claim? What's their motivation?
    6. How could we test whether this is true?

    Student Task: Choose a claim from current news or social media and analyze it using these questions.

    Activity 3: Te Ao Māori Critical Thinking (15 mins)

    Kaupapa: Explore how Māori oral traditions demonstrate critical thinking.

    • Pūrākau Analysis: In traditional stories, how do characters question assumptions?
    • Whakapapa Thinking: How does understanding connections help us think critically?
    • Kaitiakitanga: What questions do we ask when making decisions that affect future generations?

    Discussion Prompt: How is critical thinking in Te Ao Māori different from (or similar to) Western approaches? What can we learn from both?

    Activity 4: Reflection & Next Steps (10 mins)

    Exit Reflection:

    • What is one new thing you learned about critical thinking today?
    • How can you apply critical thinking in your daily life this week?
    • What questions do you still have?
