    Lesson 2: The Aotearoa Wars

    Re-framing the "Land Wars" as a conflict over sovereignty, analyzing the sophisticated strategies of Māori leaders.

    Lesson Objectives

    • Students will be able to explain the primary causes of the Aotearoa Wars from a Māori perspective.
    • Students will identify key Māori leaders and their diverse strategies of resistance.
    • Students will analyze the technological and tactical innovations developed by Māori during the wars.
    • Students will compare and contrast different perspectives on the same historical events.

    • Approach Māori content with respect and openness
    • Recognize students' diverse cultural backgrounds and experiences
    • Create space for Māori students to share their perspectives
    • Consult with whānau and local iwi for culturally sensitive topics

    Core Resources & Handouts

    This lesson explores the Aotearoa Wars through the lens of Māori leadership, strategy, and innovation.

    1. Leadership Profiles: Aotearoa Wars

    Introduces students to the diverse strategies and philosophies of key Māori resistance leaders like Te Kooti, Te Whiti, and Tītokowaru.

    2. Māori Military Innovation Study

    An analysis of Māori military technology and tactics, focusing on the revolutionary design of the gunfighter pā and guerrilla warfare.

    3. Fortification Engineering: Pā Design

    A technical deep-dive into the engineering principles of the gunfighter pā, comparing it to European fortifications of the era.

    4. Colonial vs. Māori Perspective Comparison

    A primary source analysis activity where students compare different accounts of the same event to identify bias and propaganda.

    5. Wars Strategy Analysis

    A high-level comparison of conventional and guerrilla warfare strategies, and the cultural and environmental factors that influenced them.

    Suggested Lesson Flow

    1. Introduction: Begin with the "Leadership Profiles" to introduce the key figures and their motivations.
    2. Technical Analysis: Use the "Military Innovation" and "Fortification Engineering" handouts to explore the 'how' of Māori resistance.
    3. Perspective Taking: The "Perspective Comparison" handout is a powerful mid-lesson activity for developing critical thinking.
    4. Synthesis & Strategy: Use the "Wars Strategy Analysis" to bring all the concepts together and facilitate a high-level discussion.
