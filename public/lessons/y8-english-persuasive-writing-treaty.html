YEAR 8 ENGLISH

Persuasive Writing: Treaty of Waitangi Perspectives

📝 English (Writing) • ⏱️ 75 minutes • 👥 Year 8 • 🌿 Cultural: Treaty analysis

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Respect
Understanding multiple perspectives
🎯 Excellence
Crafting powerful arguments
💪 Integrity
Historical accuracy & honesty

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Write persuasive arguments using TEXAS structure
  • Analyze Treaty of Waitangi from multiple perspectives
  • Use evidence to support claims
  • Practice respectful discussion of controversial topics

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can write a clear TOPIC sentence
  • I provide 3+ pieces of EVIDENCE
  • I ANALYZE why evidence matters
  • I write a strong SUMMARY sentence

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (15 minutes)

Hook: Display two historical quotes side by side:

"The Treaty gives Māori partnership with the Crown"

— Māori perspective

"The Treaty gives Britain sovereignty over New Zealand"

— Crown perspective

Discussion: "How can both statements be about the same document? What does this tell us about perspective?"

Treaty Context (Quick Recap):
  • Signed 1840 between Māori rangatira and British Crown
  • TWO versions: English & te reo Māori (different meanings!)
  • English version: Māori cede sovereignty
  • Māori version: Māori retain tino rangatiratanga (self-determination)

2️⃣ Activity 1: TEXAS Structure (15 minutes)

Teacher models: Introduce TEXAS paragraph structure for persuasive writing.

TEXAS Structure:
T — TOPIC sentence (your main claim)
E — EVIDENCE (facts, quotes, examples)
X — EXAMPLES (specific details)
A — ANALYSIS (explain WHY it matters)
S — SUMMARY sentence (restate claim)
Example TEXAS Paragraph:

[T] The Treaty of Waitangi was intended as a partnership between Māori and the Crown. [E] Article 2 of the Māori version guaranteed Māori "tino rangatiratanga" (absolute authority) over their lands and taonga. [X] For example, this meant iwi could continue making decisions about their whenua (land) and resources. [A] This is significant because it shows Māori did not intend to give up control - they expected shared governance. [S] Therefore, the Treaty was designed to protect Māori rights while establishing British law.

3️⃣ Activity 2: Perspective Analysis (25 minutes)

Jigsaw groups: Half the class researches Māori perspective, half researches Crown perspective.

Research Questions (15 min):
Group A: Māori Perspective
  • What did Māori believe they were agreeing to?
  • What was promised in the Māori text?
  • How was the Treaty breached?
  • What injustices occurred?
Group B: Crown Perspective
  • What did the British believe they were agreeing to?
  • What was stated in the English text?
  • Why did the Crown want the Treaty?
  • What were British intentions?

Expert exchange (10 min): Regroup so each new group has both Māori-experts and Crown-experts. Share findings.

4️⃣ Activity 3: Write Your TEXAS Paragraph (15 minutes)

Individual writing: Choose ONE perspective and write a persuasive TEXAS paragraph.

Prompt Options:
  1. "The Treaty of Waitangi was a partnership agreement" (Māori perspective)
  2. "The Treaty of Waitangi granted Britain governance" (Crown perspective)
  3. "The Treaty of Waitangi has been breached" (Critical perspective)
Requirements:
  • Clear TEXAS structure (label each part)
  • At least 3 pieces of evidence
  • Specific historical examples
  • Respectful tone (even when arguing)

5️⃣ Conclusion & Peer Review (5 minutes)

Pair-share: Exchange paragraphs with a partner. Check if all TEXAS elements are present. Give one compliment and one suggestion.

Exit ticket: "Why is it important to understand multiple perspectives on the Treaty?"

📦 Resources Needed

  • Treaty of Waitangi texts (English & Māori versions - print copies)
  • Research materials (textbooks, approved websites)
  • TEXAS structure handout/poster
  • Example paragraphs for modeling

💡 Teacher Notes

  • Cultural sensitivity: This is a complex, sometimes painful topic. Establish respectful ground rules before discussion.
  • Extension: Advanced students can write BOTH perspectives in two paragraphs, then a third paragraph synthesizing both views.
  • Differentiation: Provide sentence starters for struggling writers. Offer extra sources for advanced researchers.
  • Real-world connection: Discuss current Treaty settlements and ongoing Treaty debates in NZ politics.
  • Follow-up: Next lesson could focus on counter-arguments and rebuttals.
