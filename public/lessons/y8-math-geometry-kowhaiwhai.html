YEAR 8 MATHEMATICS

Geometry Through Kōwhaiwhai Patterns

📐 Mathematics (Geometry) • ⏱️ 65 minutes • 👥 Year 8 • 🌿 Cultural: Māori art & symmetry

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Respect
Honoring traditional Māori art forms
💫 Creativity
Designing original geometric patterns
🎯 Excellence
Precision in geometric thinking

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Identify types of symmetry (line, rotational)
  • Recognize geometric transformations (reflection, rotation, translation)
  • Analyze kōwhaiwhai patterns mathematically
  • Create symmetrical designs using geometric rules

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can identify lines of symmetry in patterns
  • I can describe rotational symmetry (order)
  • I can create a reflection of a shape
  • I design my own kōwhaiwhai with symmetry

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: Display images of kōwhaiwhai from wharenui (meeting houses). "What patterns do you see? What makes them beautiful?"

What is Kōwhaiwhai?

Kōwhaiwhai are painted scroll patterns found on the rafters (heke) of wharenui. Each design has a name and meaning. They use perfect symmetry and geometric precision - mathematics in action!

2️⃣ Activity 1: Symmetry Hunt (20 minutes)

Class exploration: Analyze kōwhaiwhai patterns for symmetry types.

Types of Symmetry:
Line Symmetry (Reflection)

If you fold the pattern along a line, both halves match exactly. Count the lines of symmetry!

Rotational Symmetry

If you rotate the pattern around a center point, it looks the same at certain angles. Order of rotation = how many times it matches in a full 360° turn.

Task (pairs): Given 4 kōwhaiwhai images, identify: (1) How many lines of symmetry? (2) What order of rotational symmetry?

3️⃣ Activity 2: Create Reflections (20 minutes)

Hands-on practice: Create mirror images using graph paper.

Reflection Activity:
  1. Draw a vertical line on graph paper (mirror line)
  2. On left side, draw simple kōwhaiwhai-inspired shape
  3. Reflect each point across the mirror line (count squares)
  4. Connect reflected points to create mirror image
  5. Color both halves symmetrically

Extension: Try reflection across horizontal line or diagonal line (45°)

4️⃣ Activity 3: Design Your Kōwhaiwhai (10 minutes)

Creative task: Design an original kōwhaiwhai pattern using symmetry rules.

Requirements:
  • Must have at least 1 line of symmetry
  • Use geometric shapes (circles, triangles, spirals, curves)
  • 2-3 colors maximum (traditional = red, black, white)
  • Pattern should repeat or flow

5️⃣ Conclusion & Gallery (5 minutes)

Gallery walk: Display all student kōwhaiwhai designs. Class votes on: most symmetrical, most creative, most traditional-looking.

Exit ticket: "What is one way geometry connects to Māori art?"

📊 Assessment

Success Indicators:
  • ✅ Identifies symmetry types correctly
  • ✅ Creates accurate reflections
  • ✅ Designs pattern with symmetry
  • ✅ Connects math to cultural art
