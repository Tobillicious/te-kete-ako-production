    Learning Objectives

    By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

    • Collect, organize and display multivariate data sets
    • Calculate measures of central tendency and spread for sports performance data
    • Interpret statistical information in the context of sports performance
    • Compare and contrast statistical measures between different sports
    • Understand the cultural significance of traditional Māori sports

    Cultural Context

    Traditional Māori Sports and Games

    Traditional Māori sports and games, known as tākaro, were not just for entertainment but also served important cultural purposes. They developed physical skills, mental acuity, and social cohesion. Some traditional games include:

    • Ki-o-rahi - A fast-paced ball game played on a circular field
    • Mū Tōrere - A strategic board game
    • Tītī tōrea - A stick game requiring coordination and rhythm
    • Poi - A performance art using weighted balls on strings

    When studying these traditional activities, it's important to approach them with respect for their cultural significance and origins.

    Teacher's Guide

    Implementation Steps

    1. Introduction (15 mins): Discuss the cultural significance of traditional Māori sports and introduce statistical concepts
    2. Data Collection (20 mins): Guide students through collecting performance data
    3. Statistical Analysis (25 mins): Demonstrate calculating measures of central tendency and spread
    4. Data Visualization (20 mins): Show how to create appropriate graphs and charts
    5. Interpretation (15 mins): Discuss what the statistics reveal about performance
    6. Assessment (10 mins): Review student work and provide feedback

    Cultural Safety Note:

    When discussing Māori culture and traditional games, ensure respectful language and acknowledge the cultural significance. Avoid oversimplifying or misrepresenting cultural practices.

    Assessment

    Success Criteria:

    • Accurately calculates statistical measures
    • Creates appropriate data visualizations
    • Interprets results in context
    • Demonstrates understanding of cultural context
    • Effectively compares different data sets

    Assessment Suggestions

    • Data collection worksheet (formative)
    • Statistical analysis report (summative)
    • Presentation of findings (oral assessment)
    • Peer assessment of visualizations

    Differentiation

    For Students Needing Support:

    • Provide pre-collected data sets
    • Use step-by-step calculation guides
    • Offer templates for data visualization
    • Pair students for collaborative work

    Extension Activities:

    • Research and analyze professional sports statistics
    • Create a predictive model for sports performance
    • Compare traditional Māori games with similar contemporary sports
    • Design a statistical study for a school sports team
