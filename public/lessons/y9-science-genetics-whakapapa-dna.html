Hook: "Why do you look like your parents? Why might you have your grandmother's eyes or grandfather's hair? The answer is in every cell: DNA!"

What is DNA?

DNA = DeoxyriboNucleic Acid. It's a molecule inside every cell that carries genetic instructions - like a recipe book for making YOU. Your DNA is unique (except identical twins) but also shares patterns with your parents, siblings, and ancestors going back thousands of years.

DNA & Whakapapa:

Whakapapa is genealogy - your connection to ancestors. DNA is the PHYSICAL whakapapa! When you recite your whakapapa, you're naming people whose DNA you carry. Science and culture tell the same story: you are connected to those who came before.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Activity 1: DNA Structure & Location (20 minutes)

Class exploration: Where is DNA? What does it look like?

DNA Basics:
  • Location: Inside the nucleus of every cell (in chromosomes)
  • Shape: Double helix (twisted ladder)
  • Building blocks: 4 bases (A, T, G, C) - like letters in a genetic alphabet
  • Genes: Sections of DNA that code for specific traits (eye color, height, etc.)
  • Chromosomes: Packages of DNA. Humans have 46 (23 from mum, 23 from dad)

Task: Draw a cell. Label: nucleus, chromosomes, DNA double helix. Annotate: "This is my whakapapa at the cellular level!"

3Ô∏è‚É£ Activity 2: Inherited Traits (20 minutes)

Class activity: What traits are inherited? Which are not?

Inherited (from DNA):
  • Eye color, hair color & texture
  • Height potential (genes + nutrition)
  • Skin tone
  • Ability to roll tongue
  • Attached vs free earlobes
  • Blood type
NOT Inherited (Acquired/Environmental):
  • Language you speak (you learn this!)
  • Scars, tattoos (body modifications)
  • Skills (playing sports, instruments)
  • Tan (environmental response)
  • Muscles (built through exercise)

Task (pairs): Survey your family - what traits did you inherit? Create a trait tree showing 3 generations.
