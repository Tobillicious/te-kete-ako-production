🎯 WALT

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can list income sources and calculate total
  • I can categorize expenses (fixed/variable)
  • I can calculate surplus/deficit
  • I create a balanced whānau budget

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (15 min)

Hook: "NZ's median household income is $107,000/year. But many whānau struggle. Why? Let's learn budgeting to help ourselves and our whānau!"

Budget = Plan for money. Income - Expenses = Savings (hopefully!)

2️⃣ Activity: Create Whānau Budget (35 min)

Scenario: Whānau of 4 (2 adults, 2 kids)

INCOME (Monthly):
  • Adult 1 salary: $4,200
  • Adult 2 salary: $3,800
  • Working for Families: $320
  • TOTAL INCOME: $8,320/month
EXPENSES (Monthly):
  • Rent/Mortgage: $2,400
  • Food: $1,200
  • Power/Water: $280
  • Internet/Phone: $150
  • Transport (petrol/bus): $400
  • Insurance: $180
  • School costs: $150
  • Whānau events (tangi, etc): $200
  • Calculate total!

Task: Calculate surplus/deficit. Discuss: Where could whānau save? What's essential?

3️⃣ Cultural Connection (15 min)

Whānau vs Individual Economics: Māori traditionally prioritize collective over individual. How does this affect budgeting?

  • Supporting extended whānau (not just nuclear family)
  • Contributing to tangi, birthdays, hui
  • Koha (gifts/donations) for community events
  • Balancing individual goals with collective responsibilities
