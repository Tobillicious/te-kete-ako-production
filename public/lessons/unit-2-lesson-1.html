    Lesson 1: Pre-Colonial Innovation

    Challenging the myth of "primitive" technology by exploring Māori achievements in engineering, agriculture, and navigation.

    Lesson Objectives

    • Students will be able to define "innovation" from a decolonized perspective.
    • Students will identify multiple examples of sophisticated Māori technology and knowledge systems.
    • Students will analyze primary source evidence to understand Māori scientific principles.
    • Students will compare and contrast Māori and modern approaches to problem-solving.

    🌿 Cultural Context | Horopaki Ahurea

    Whakataukī

    "Mā te mōhio ka ora, mā te ora ka mōhio"

    "Through knowledge comes wellbeing, through wellbeing comes knowledge"

    🏛️ Connection to Mangakōtukutuku College Values

    Whaimana: Integrity - Standing firm in truth and principle

    This learning connects to the value of whaiara by encouraging students to engage with content in ways that embody this value.

    🛡️ Cultural Safety Considerations

    • Approach Māori content with respect and openness
    • Recognize students' diverse cultural backgrounds and experiences
    • Create space for Māori students to share their perspectives
    • Consult with whānau and local iwi for culturally sensitive topics

    Core Resources & Handouts

    This lesson is built around a series of handouts that progressively deepen student understanding of Māori innovation.

    1. Pre-Colonial Innovation Deep Dive

    Introduces the concept of decolonized innovation and provides an overview of Māori technological excellence in multiple domains.

    2. Technology Definition Challenge

    A critical thinking activity that challenges students to broaden their definition of "technology" beyond a Eurocentric view.

    3. Traditional Science: Primary Sources

    Students analyze first-hand accounts from early European observers to find evidence of sophisticated Māori scientific practices.

    4. Innovation Domains Comparison

    A comparison chart activity that contrasts the principles of Māori and modern innovation across various sectors.

    5. Modern Applications Connection Guide

    Connects traditional Māori principles like kaitiakitanga to modern concepts like sustainability, biomimicry, and the circular economy.

    Suggested Lesson Flow

    1. Introduction: Start with the "Technology Definition Challenge" to open up discussion.
    2. Core Content: Use the "Pre-Colonial Innovation Deep Dive" as the main reading and reference text.
    3. Evidence: Students work with the "Traditional Science: Primary Sources" handout to act as historians.
    4. Comparison & Synthesis: Use the "Innovation Domains Comparison" chart to consolidate understanding.
    5. Application: Conclude with the "Modern Applications Connection Guide" to link historical knowledge to contemporary issues.

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
