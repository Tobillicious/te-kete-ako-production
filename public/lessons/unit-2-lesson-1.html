    Lesson 1: Pre-Colonial Innovation

    Challenging the myth of "primitive" technology by exploring MƒÅori achievements in engineering, agriculture, and navigation.

    Lesson Objectives

    • Students will be able to define "innovation" from a decolonized perspective.
    • Students will identify multiple examples of sophisticated MƒÅori technology and knowledge systems.
    • Students will analyze primary source evidence to understand MƒÅori scientific principles.
    • Students will compare and contrast MƒÅori and modern approaches to problem-solving.

    Core Resources & Handouts

    This lesson is built around a series of handouts that progressively deepen student understanding of MƒÅori innovation.

    1. Pre-Colonial Innovation Deep Dive

    Introduces the concept of decolonized innovation and provides an overview of MƒÅori technological excellence in multiple domains.

    2. Technology Definition Challenge

    A critical thinking activity that challenges students to broaden their definition of "technology" beyond a Eurocentric view.

    3. Traditional Science: Primary Sources

    Students analyze first-hand accounts from early European observers to find evidence of sophisticated MƒÅori scientific practices.

    4. Innovation Domains Comparison

    A comparison chart activity that contrasts the principles of MƒÅori and modern innovation across various sectors.

    5. Modern Applications Connection Guide

    Connects traditional MƒÅori principles like kaitiakitanga to modern concepts like sustainability, biomimicry, and the circular economy.

    Suggested Lesson Flow

    1. Introduction: Start with the "Technology Definition Challenge" to open up discussion.
    2. Core Content: Use the "Pre-Colonial Innovation Deep Dive" as the main reading and reference text.
    3. Evidence: Students work with the "Traditional Science: Primary Sources" handout to act as historians.
    4. Comparison & Synthesis: Use the "Innovation Domains Comparison" chart to consolidate understanding.
    5. Application: Conclude with the "Modern Applications Connection Guide" to link historical knowledge to contemporary issues.
