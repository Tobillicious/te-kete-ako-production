YEAR 8 SCIENCE

Cells & Whakapapa: Interconnected Systems

🔬 Science (Living World) • ⏱️ 60 minutes • 👥 Year 8 • 🌿 Cultural: Whakapapa as connection

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Whakapapa
Understanding interconnection
🎯 Excellence
Scientific observation skills
🤝 Community
Cells work together like whānau

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Identify main parts of a cell and their functions
  • Compare plant and animal cells
  • Understand cells as building blocks of life
  • Connect cellular organization to whakapapa concept

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can label 5 cell parts (nucleus, membrane, cytoplasm, mitochondria, cell wall)
  • I can explain what each part does
  • I can state 2 differences: plant vs animal cells
  • I can explain whakapapa as connection concept

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: "Your body has about 37 TRILLION cells! Each one is a tiny living factory. Just like how you're connected to ancestors through whakapapa, every cell in your body is connected and works together."

Whakapapa Concept:

Whakapapa means genealogy, but it's deeper - it's about CONNECTION. Your whakapapa connects you to ancestors, land, and community. Similarly, cells are connected: they work together, depend on each other, and form larger systems (tissues → organs → body).

2️⃣ Activity 1: Cell Structure (20 minutes)

Teacher models: Draw & label animal cell diagram.

Main Cell Parts:
  • Nucleus: Control center (like rangatira of cell) - contains DNA
  • Cell membrane: Outer boundary (like palisade fence) - controls what enters/leaves
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance (everything floats in this)
  • Mitochondria: Power plants (make energy from food)
  • Cell wall: (Plants only!) Rigid outer layer for support
  • Chloroplasts: (Plants only!) Make food from sunlight (photosynthesis)

Task: Students draw & label BOTH an animal cell and plant cell. Identify differences.

3️⃣ Activity 2: Whakapapa of Life (15 minutes)

Class discussion: How cells connect to form larger systems - like whakapapa!

Biological Whakapapa (Hierarchy):
Cells
(Smallest unit)
→
Tissues
(Groups of cells)
→
Organs
(Heart, lungs, etc.)
→
Systems
(Digestive, etc.)
→
Organism
(You!)

Connection to whakapapa: Just as you're connected to tīpuna (ancestors) who came before, cells are connected to tissues, which connect to organs, which form YOU. Everything has whakapapa!

4️⃣ Activity 3: Build a 3D Cell Model (10 minutes)

Creative task: Design (or sketch) a 3D cell model using everyday materials.

Model Ideas:
  • Clear plastic bag = cell membrane
  • Tennis ball = nucleus
  • Jelly/gelatin = cytoplasm
  • Grapes/berries = mitochondria
  • Green beans = chloroplasts (plant cells)

Task: Sketch your model design. Label all parts. If time, actually build it for homework!

5️⃣ Conclusion & Reflection (5 minutes)

Class summary: Quick quiz - "Point to the nucleus! What does it do?"

Exit ticket: "How is whakapapa like the organization of cells in your body?"
