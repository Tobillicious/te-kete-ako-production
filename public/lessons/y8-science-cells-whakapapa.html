1Ô∏è‚É£ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: "Your body has about 37 TRILLION cells! Each one is a tiny living factory. Just like how you're connected to ancestors through whakapapa, every cell in your body is connected and works together."

Whakapapa Concept:

Whakapapa means genealogy, but it's deeper - it's about CONNECTION. Your whakapapa connects you to ancestors, land, and community. Similarly, cells are connected: they work together, depend on each other, and form larger systems (tissues ‚Üí organs ‚Üí body).

2Ô∏è‚É£ Activity 1: Cell Structure (20 minutes)

Teacher models: Draw & label animal cell diagram.

Main Cell Parts:
  • Nucleus: Control center (like rangatira of cell) - contains DNA
  • Cell membrane: Outer boundary (like palisade fence) - controls what enters/leaves
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance (everything floats in this)
  • Mitochondria: Power plants (make energy from food)
  • Cell wall: (Plants only!) Rigid outer layer for support
  • Chloroplasts: (Plants only!) Make food from sunlight (photosynthesis)

Task: Students draw & label BOTH an animal cell and plant cell. Identify differences.
