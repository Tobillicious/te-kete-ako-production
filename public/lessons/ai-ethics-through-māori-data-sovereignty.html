    Cultural Context & Safety Notes

    Whakapapa: This lesson acknowledges that data carries the whakapapa (genealogy) of its origins and should be treated with the same respect as taonga (treasures).

    Tikanga: We follow the principle of kaitiakitanga (guardianship) when working with data, particularly Māori data.

    Cultural Safety: Students should understand that discussing Māori data sovereignty requires sensitivity and respect for indigenous knowledge systems.

    Language Protocol: Māori terms are introduced with English translations and used consistently throughout.

    Learning Objectives

    Students will be able to:

    • Explain the concept of Māori Data Sovereignty and its principles
    • Analyze how AI systems can impact indigenous data rights
    • Apply ethical frameworks to AI development scenarios
    • Design AI systems that respect data sovereignty principles
    • Evaluate existing AI technologies through a Māori data sovereignty lens

    NZC Alignment: Digital Technologies L6-8, Social Sciences L6-8, Tikanga-ā-Iwi

    Understanding Māori Data Sovereignty

    He Whakamārama | The Explanation

    Māori Data Sovereignty asserts that Māori have the right to control, access, and manage Māori data and information. This includes:

    • Rangatiratanga: Self-determination over data
    • Whakapapa: Understanding data lineage and connections
    • Kaitiakitanga: Guardianship and protection of data
    • Manaakitanga: Ethical and respectful data use

    Interactive Scenario: The AI Health App

    Imagine an AI health app being developed using health data from Aotearoa communities.

    Question 1: How might this app impact Māori data sovereignty?

    Consider: Who owns the data? How is consent obtained? Are Māori communities benefiting from this technology?

    Question 2: What principles should guide this AI development?

    Key principles: Free, prior and informed consent; Māori leadership in development; equitable benefit sharing; cultural safety in algorithms.

    Case Study Analysis

    Te Hiku Media's Speech Recognition Project

    Explore how Te Hiku Media developed te reo Māori speech recognition while maintaining data sovereignty:

    • Community-controlled data collection
    • Indigenous-led AI development
    • Benefits returning to Māori communities

    Practical Assessment

    Develop a consultation plan for AI development involving Māori communities.

    Teacher Implementation Guide

    Lesson Sequence (3-4 sessions)

    Session 1: Foundations

    Starter: "Data as Taonga" discussion (10 min)

    Main: Introduction to Māori Data Sovereignty principles (30 min)

    Activity: Interactive scenario analysis (20 min)

    Session 2: Application

    Starter: Case study review (15 min)

    Main: AI ethics framework development (40 min)

    Activity: Design challenge introduction (15 min)

    Session 3: Synthesis

    Starter: Peer feedback on designs (20 min)

    Main: Assessment completion (40 min)

    Plenary: Reflection on learning (10 min)
