📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (15 minutes)

Hook: "Article 2 of Te Tiriti promised Māori tino rangatiratanga - absolute authority over their lands and treasures. But what does that mean TODAY? Should Māori have sovereignty?"

What is Tino Rangatiratanga?

Tino rangatiratanga = absolute sovereignty, self-determination, authority to govern oneself. It's about Māori having the power to make decisions about their own lives, lands, resources, and future - not having decisions made FOR them.

2️⃣ Activity 1: Argumentative Structure (20 minutes)

Teacher models: The C.E.R. framework (Claim, Evidence, Reasoning)

Argument Structure:
CLAIM (Thesis)

Your main argument. Clear, specific, arguable.
Example: "Māori should have greater tino rangatiratanga over natural resources in Aotearoa."

EVIDENCE

Facts, statistics, quotes, examples from credible sources.
Example: "Article 2 of Te Tiriti guarantees tino rangatiratanga..." (cite source)

REASONING

Explain HOW your evidence supports your claim. Connect the dots!
Example: "This Treaty promise shows the Crown recognized Māori authority, which has never been fully honored..."

3️⃣ Activity 2: Research & Planning (25 minutes)

Individual task: Choose your stance, research, plan your argument.

Choose Your Position:
  • Option A: Māori should have greater tino rangatiratanga (more self-governance, resource control)
  • Option B: Current arrangements balance Treaty partnerships appropriately
  • Option C: A different approach to sovereignty (your own stance!)
Research Sources (Approved):
  • Te Tiriti o Waitangi text (both versions)
  • Waitangi Tribunal reports
  • News articles on Treaty settlements
  • Māori perspectives (iwi websites, leaders' speeches)
  • Government policy documents

Plan: Create outline - Claim + 3 pieces of evidence + reasoning for each + 1 counter-argument

4️⃣ Activity 3: Draft Introduction (10 minutes)

Write: Introduction paragraph with hook, context, and thesis statement.

Introduction Formula:
  1. Hook: Interesting fact, question, or statement to grab attention
  2. Context: Background info on tino rangatiratanga & Te Tiriti
  3. Thesis: Your clear claim (this is what you'll argue)

5️⃣ Peer Review & Conclusion (5 minutes)

Pair-share: Read intros to partner. Is thesis clear? Does it take a stance?

Homework: Complete full argumentative essay (5 paragraphs: intro, 3 body paragraphs with C.E.R., conclusion).

📊 Assessment Rubric

Criteria Excellent (4) Proficient (3)
Claim Clear, specific, arguable Clear but could be more specific
Evidence 3+ credible sources, well-cited 2-3 sources, mostly credible
Reasoning Clearly explains how evidence supports claim Some explanation, could be clearer
Counter-argument Addresses opposing view, refutes it Mentions opposing view briefly
