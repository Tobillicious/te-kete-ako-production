YEAR 9 SCIENCE

Climate Change & Local Action

🔬 Science (Planet Earth) • ⏱️ 70 minutes • 👥 Year 9 • 🌿 Cultural: Kaitiakitanga in action

🏫 School Values Connection

🌿 Kaitiakitanga
Environmental responsibility
💪 Courage
Taking action on difficult issues
🤝 Community
Collective climate action

🎯 WALT (We Are Learning To)

  • Explain the greenhouse effect and climate change
  • Identify local impacts of climate change in NZ
  • Calculate personal carbon footprints
  • Design realistic local climate action plans

✅ Success Criteria

  • I can explain how greenhouse gases trap heat
  • I can name 3 local NZ climate impacts
  • I can calculate my daily carbon emissions
  • I propose 3 actionable solutions

📚 Lesson Flow

1️⃣ Introduction (10 minutes)

Hook: Show graph of NZ temperatures rising over last 100 years. "What does this data tell us? Why should we care?"

NZ Climate Facts (2024):
  • NZ temperature: +1.1°C since 1900
  • Sea level rising ~2mm per year
  • More extreme weather events (floods, droughts, storms)
  • Glaciers retreating rapidly
  • Ocean acidification affecting marine life

2️⃣ Activity 1: Greenhouse Effect Model (15 minutes)

Teacher demonstration: Explain greenhouse effect using simple model.

How the Greenhouse Effect Works:
  1. ☀️ Sunlight enters Earth's atmosphere (short-wave radiation)
  2. 🌍 Earth absorbs energy and warms up
  3. 🔄 Earth emits heat back out (long-wave infrared radiation)
  4. 🌫️ Greenhouse gases trap some heat (like a blanket)
  5. 🔥 More greenhouse gases = more heat trapped (global warming)
Main Greenhouse Gases:
  • CO₂ (Carbon Dioxide) - From burning fossil fuels, deforestation
  • CH₄ (Methane) - From livestock (especially cows), landfills
  • N₂O (Nitrous Oxide) - From fertilizers, industrial processes
  • Water Vapor - Natural, but increases with warming

Task: Draw & label diagram of greenhouse effect. Show heat entering and trapped by gases.

Individual activity: Calculate your daily carbon emissions.

NZ Context:

Average NZ carbon footprint: ~20 kg CO₂ per person per day (7 tonnes/year). Global target to prevent catastrophic warming: ~6 kg/day (2 tonnes/year). We need to reduce by 70%!

Discussion: "What surprised you about your footprint? What's the biggest contributor?"

4️⃣ Activity 3: Local Climate Action Plan (20 minutes)

Group task: Design a climate action plan for our school or local community.

Action Plan Template:
  1. Choose a focus area: Transport, energy, waste, food, or water
  2. Identify the problem: What's the current carbon impact?
  3. Propose 3 solutions: Realistic actions we can take
  4. Calculate impact: How much CO₂ would we save?
  5. Implementation plan: Who, what, when, how?
Example Solutions:
  • 🚌 Transport: School bike racks + bike-to-school days
  • ♻️ Waste: Composting system for food scraps
  • 💡 Energy: LED bulbs + solar panels on school roof
  • 🌱 Food: Vegetarian Mondays in canteen
  • 💧 Water: Rainwater collection for gardens

5️⃣ Conclusion & Presentations (5 minutes)

Group presentations: Each group presents ONE solution (1 minute). Class votes on most impactful idea.

Exit ticket: "What is ONE action YOU will take this week to reduce your carbon footprint?"

📊 Assessment

Success Indicators:
  • ✅ Explains greenhouse effect accurately
  • ✅ Calculates carbon footprint correctly
  • ✅ Proposes realistic, actionable solutions
  • ✅ Connects actions to kaitiakitanga values

📦 Resources Needed

  • NZ climate data graphs (NIWA website)
  • Carbon footprint calculator worksheets
  • Calculators
  • Action plan templates

💡 Teacher Notes

  • Sensitive topic: Climate change can cause anxiety. Focus on empowerment and local action, not doom.
  • Extension: Actually implement top-voted solution! Work with school leadership.
  • Real-world connection: Invite local climate activist or iwi environmental manager to speak.
  • Follow-up: Track school's carbon reduction over term/year.
