🚀 Getting Started

Q: What is Te Kete Ako?
Te Kete Ako ("The Basket of Knowledge") is a comprehensive educational platform with 13,772 resources for Years 7-13, integrating mātauranga Māori with contemporary learning. All resources are free and aligned with the New Zealand Curriculum.
Q: How do I find resources for my subject and year level?
Three easy ways:
1. Subject Hubs: Click on Science, Mathematics, English, etc. in the navigation menu
2. Browse by Year: Use the Units menu to find year-specific resources
3. Search: Use the search bar on the homepage to find specific topics
Q: Do I need to create an account?
No! All resources are freely accessible without an account. However, creating a free account lets you save favorites, track progress, and get personalized recommendations.

👨‍🏫 For Teachers

Q: How do I print a lesson or handout?
Easy! Two methods:
1. Browser Print: Press Ctrl+P (Windows) or Cmd+P (Mac) - our print stylesheet automatically formats the page beautifully
2. Print Button: Look for the 🖨️ Print button at the top of most lessons and handouts
Q: Can I modify resources for my class?
Yes! All resources are open educational resources (OER). You can adapt them to your students' needs. We recommend using the lesson as-is first, then customizing based on your class context.
Q: How do I save my favorite resources?
Click the ⭐ Favorite button on any resource page (top right). You'll need a free teacher account to save favorites. Access all your saved resources from "My Kete" in the navigation menu.
Q: Are the cultural elements authentic?
Yes. 55% of our resources have been culturally integrated with input from Māori educators and community members. We continue to validate and improve cultural authenticity through ongoing consultation. If you notice any concerns, please use the "Report Issue" link.
Q: Can I use these resources in my LMS (Google Classroom, Moodle, etc.)?
Absolutely! Simply share the resource URL with your students. All pages work on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

📚 For Students

Q: How do I find my homework/assignment?
Your teacher will share a direct link to the resource. You can also browse by subject or search for the topic on our homepage.
Q: Do I need to create an account?
Not required! You can access all learning resources without an account. However, an account lets you track your progress and save resources you want to review later.
Q: Can I access this on my phone or tablet?
Yes! Te Kete Ako works on any device - phone, tablet, laptop, or desktop. All resources are mobile-optimized.
Q: What does "Te Kete Ako" mean?
"Te Kete Ako" means "The Basket of Knowledge" in Te Reo Māori. It comes from the whakataukī (proverb): "Whaowhia te kete mātauranga" - "Fill the basket of knowledge."

🔧 Technical Help

Q: The page isn't loading properly. What should I do?
Try these steps:
1. Refresh the page (F5 or Cmd+R)
2. Clear your browser cache
3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge all supported)
4. Check your internet connection
If problems persist, please contact us
Q: Can I use this offline?
Yes! Te Kete Ako is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Once you've visited a page, it will be available offline. On Chrome/Edge, you can also "Install" the app from your browser menu for offline access.
Q: Is my data safe and private?
Yes. We use industry-standard security (Supabase authentication) and follow New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 requirements. We never sell your data. Read our Privacy Policy for details.

🌿 Cultural Information

Q: I'm not Māori. Can I still use these resources?
Absolutely! Te Kete Ako is for ALL students and teachers in Aotearoa. The cultural elements enrich learning for everyone. We encourage all users to engage respectfully with mātauranga Māori as part of understanding our shared national identity.
Q: How do I pronounce Te Reo Māori words correctly?
Many resources include pronunciation guides. For general help, visit Te Aka Māori Dictionary which includes audio pronunciations.
Q: Who validates the cultural content?
Our resources are developed with input from Māori educators and community members. We maintain ongoing consultation processes to ensure cultural authenticity and appropriateness. If you have concerns about any content, please contact us.
