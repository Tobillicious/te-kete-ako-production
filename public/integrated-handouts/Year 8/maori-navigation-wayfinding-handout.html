    Cultural Sensitivity

    IMPORTANT: Traditional navigation knowledge is sacred and was passed down through specialized training. When teaching:

    • Acknowledge this is simplified introduction to complex knowledge system
    • Recognize that full navigation knowledge took years of apprenticeship
    • Invite local navigators or waka hourua crew to share if possible
    • Celebrate contemporary MÄori navigation revival (Te Aurere, Haunui, etc.)
    • Connect to local iwi maritime traditions

    Cross-Curricular Opportunities

    Science: Astronomy, oceanography, meteorology, ecology

    Mathematics: Geometry, measurement, estimation, mental calculation

    Social Studies: Pacific migration, cultural knowledge systems, innovation

    Technology: Traditional vs modern navigation systems

    Differentiation

    Support: Focus on 2-3 navigation techniques, provide visual aids, use simpler calculations

    Extension: Research specific navigation stars, calculate voyage times, study modern waka hourua expeditions, interview navigators

    Practical Activities

    • Nighttime star observation (with supervision)
    • Wave tank demonstrations
    • Cloud watching and pattern recognition
    • Mental mapping exercises
    • Visit waka hourua if available locally
