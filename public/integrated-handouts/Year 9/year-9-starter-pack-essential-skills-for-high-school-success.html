    Learning Objectives

    By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

    • Apply effective time management and organization strategies
    • Navigate school systems and expectations with confidence
    • Demonstrate understanding of key tikanga Māori in school settings
    • Use appropriate study techniques for different learning situations
    • Access support systems within the school community

    Te Ao Māori Context: Transitioning with Mana

    In Māori tradition, transitions (whakawhiti) are important times that require special care and attention. As you move from intermediate to high school, remember these key concepts:

    • Manaakitanga: Show care and respect for your new teachers, classmates, and school environment
    • Whanaungatanga: Build positive relationships with your new school whānau
    • Kaitiakitanga: Take responsibility for your learning space and resources
    • Tūrangawaewae: Establish your place in the school community

    He kākano ahau i ruia mai i Rangiātea - I am a seed sown in Rangiātea (you carry great potential within you)

    2. Navigating School Systems

    Complete this scavenger hunt in your first week to learn about your school:

    1. Find the Māori department and learn one new kupu (word)
    2. Locate three places you can go for help (dean, counselor, whānau room)
    3. Identify where to find your timetable online
    4. Discover two extracurricular activities you might join
    5. Find out where lost property is kept

    Teacher Implementation Notes

    Timing: Best used in the first 2-3 weeks of Term 1. Allow 3-4 lessons to complete core activities.

    Differentiation:

    • For students needing more support: Pair with buddy, focus on checklist completion
    • For extension: Have students create digital versions of resources to share

    Cultural Considerations:

    • Invite Māori student leaders to share their transition experiences
    • Connect activities to local iwi knowledge where possible
    • Validate different cultural perspectives on education

    Assessment: Use checklist completion as formative assessment. Pepeha or Survival Guide can be summative tasks.
