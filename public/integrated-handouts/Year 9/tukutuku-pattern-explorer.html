    🎨 Tukutuku Pattern Explorer

    Interactive Mathematics Through Traditional Māori Art

    "Discover the mathematical genius embedded in traditional tukutuku patterns - where culture and mathematics unite in perfect harmony."

    🎯 Learning Objectives

    Through this interactive exploration, you will:

    🔄 Master Transformations

    Understand translation, rotation, reflection, and scaling through hands-on pattern creation

    🎨 Appreciate Cultural Mathematics

    Discover how traditional Māori artisans used advanced geometric principles in their designs

    📏 Apply Coordinate Geometry

    Use coordinate systems to describe and analyze pattern transformations

    🔗 Connect Knowledge Systems

    Bridge traditional knowledge with modern mathematical understanding

    📐 Mathematical Concepts in Action

    As you explore the patterns, you're discovering these important mathematical ideas:

    Translation (Nekehanga)

    Definition: Moving every point of a shape the same distance in the same direction

    In Tukutuku: How patterns repeat across wharenui walls in regular intervals

    Equation: (x, y) → (x + a, y + b)

    Rotation (Tawhiri)

    Definition: Turning a shape around a fixed point by a specific angle

    In Tukutuku: Four winds pattern showing 90° rotations around center

    Angles: Common rotations are 90°, 180°, 270°

    Reflection (Whakaata)

    Definition: Flipping a shape across a mirror line

    In Tukutuku: Symmetrical patterns representing balance and harmony

    Lines: Can reflect across horizontal, vertical, or diagonal lines

    Scaling (Whakanui)

    Definition: Making a shape larger or smaller while keeping the same proportions

    In Tukutuku: How patterns adapt to different panel sizes

    Factor: Scale factor > 1 enlarges, < 1 reduces

    🎯 Learning Activities

    Use these activities to deepen your understanding:

    🔍 Pattern Detective

    Challenge: Choose the Pātikitiki (diamond) pattern and use translation. Count how many complete patterns you can see. Calculate the area covered by the patterns using the grid.

    Extension: How would the area change if you doubled the grid size?

    🌪️ Rotation Investigation

    Challenge: Select the Tawhiri (cross) pattern and use rotation. Animate it and observe. What angle separates each rotation? How many rotations create a full turn?

    Extension: Try to predict what the pattern will look like after a 45° rotation.

    🪞 Symmetry Explorer

    Challenge: Use the Roimata Toroa (chevron) pattern with reflection. How many lines of symmetry can you identify? Draw them on paper.

    Extension: Create your own pattern with exactly 4 lines of symmetry.

    📏 Coordinate Master

    Challenge: Click different points on the canvas and record their coordinates. Try to predict where a point will be after translation by (50, 30).

    Extension: Use coordinates to describe the exact position of pattern centers.

    🎨 Cultural Designer

    Challenge: Choose a pattern that represents your own story or values. Use transformations to create a design that tells your narrative.

    Extension: Write a paragraph explaining the mathematical and cultural significance of your design.
