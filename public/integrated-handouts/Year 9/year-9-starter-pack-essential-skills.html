    Learning Objectives

    By engaging with this resource, students will:

    • Develop effective organizational strategies for high school
    • Understand key tikanga (protocols) for school environments
    • Apply time management techniques to balance academic and personal commitments
    • Build positive relationships with peers and teachers
    • Navigate digital learning platforms confidently
    • Develop strategies for seeking help when needed

    NZ Curriculum Alignment: Key Competencies - Managing Self, Relating to Others, Participating and Contributing

    Te Ao Māori Context

    Whakataukī (Proverb): "Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini" - My success is not mine alone, but the success of many.

    Tikanga for School Success

    • Manaakitanga: Show respect and care for others in your learning community
    • Whanaungatanga: Build positive relationships with teachers and classmates
    • Ako: Embrace being both a learner and teacher in different situations
    • Kaitiakitanga: Take responsibility for your learning environment and resources

    Reflection Activity: Pepeha

    Create your personal pepeha (introduction) to share with your class:

    1. Your mountain (maunga)
    2. Your river/lake (awa/moana)
    3. Your family/whānau
    4. Your name and something special about you

    Tip: This helps teachers and classmates understand who you are and where you come from.

    Essential Skills for High School

    1. Organization Systems

    Create systems that work for you:

    • Use a physical planner or digital calendar
    • Color-code subjects for easy identification
    • Set up subject folders (digital and physical)
    • Pack your bag the night before

    2. Digital Literacy

    Master these essential digital skills:

    • Logging into school systems (email, LMS)
    • File management (naming, organizing)
    • Basic troubleshooting (restart, check connections)
    • Digital citizenship (appropriate online behavior)

    Tech Scavenger Hunt

    Find and document these school tech resources:

    1. Where is the IT help desk?
    2. What's the school's WiFi network name?
    3. How do you access the library catalog?
    4. Where can you print documents?

    Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Developing (1-2) Achieving (3-4) Excelling (5-6)
    Organization Basic systems in place Effective systems used regularly Sophisticated systems supporting learning
    Time Management Occasionally meets deadlines Consistently meets deadlines Plans ahead and balances commitments
    Participation Minimal engagement Active in class discussions Leads discussions and supports peers
    Seeking Help Rarely asks for assistance Seeks help when needed Proactively identifies support needs

    Extension Activities

    1. Leadership Challenge

    Identify one area where you can demonstrate leadership:

    • Peer mentoring for new students
    • Organizing a study group
    • Starting a club or initiative

    2. Cultural Connections

    Research and present on one of these topics:

    • The history of your school's local area (rohe)
    • Traditional Māori learning methods (ako)
    • Significant Māori leaders in education

    Teacher Implementation Guide

    Suggested Timeline

    • Week 1: Introduce resource, complete pepeha and organization activities
    • Week 2: Focus on digital literacy and time management
    • Week 3: Relationship building and support systems
    • Ongoing: Revisit sections as needed throughout term

    Differentiation Strategies

    • Provide templates for students needing additional support
    • Pair students for peer mentoring activities
    • Offer digital and paper-based options for activities

    Assessment Suggestions

    • Student self-assessment using the rubric
    • Teacher observation of organizational systems
    • Peer feedback on participation
