    Biotechnology Ethics Through Māori Worldview

    Exploring ethical frameworks through indigenous perspectives

    Science Years 11-13 Essential Cultural Integration
    🌿

    He Whakamārama | Cultural Context

    Māori worldview emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living things and the responsibility of humans as kaitiaki (guardians) of the natural world. This perspective offers unique insights into biotechnology ethics that balance innovation with stewardship.

    Key Concepts

    Mauri

    Life Force

    The essential life force in all things, living and non-living. In biotechnology, considerations of mauri help us understand the ethical implications of modifying living systems.

    Kaitiakitanga

    Guardianship

    The responsibility and practice of caring for the natural world. This principle guides sustainable biotechnology practices that protect ecosystems.

    Whakapapa

    Genealogy

    The layers of relationships connecting all things. In biotechnology, whakapapa considerations help us understand the broader impacts of genetic modifications.

    Ethical Frameworks Comparison

    Western Ethical Frameworks

    Utilitarian Approach

    Focuses on maximizing overall benefit and minimizing harm

    Deontological Approach

    Emphasizes moral duties and universal principles

    Rights-Based Approach

    Prioritizes individual rights and autonomy

    Māori Ethical Frameworks

    Relational Ethics

    Emphasizes relationships and responsibilities to all living things

    Collective Wellbeing

    Prioritizes community and ecosystem health over individual gain

    Interconnectedness

    Recognizes the spiritual and physical connections between all elements

    Case Studies

    Genetic Modification of Native Species

    Environmental Ethics

    Exploring the ethical implications of using genetic modification to enhance the resilience of native species in response to climate change.

    Ethical Questions:

    • What are our responsibilities to protect native species?
    • How do we balance intervention with natural evolution?
    • Who decides which modifications are acceptable?

    Biotechnology and Food Sovereignty

    Social Ethics

    Examining how biotechnology impacts Māori food sovereignty and traditional food systems.

    Ethical Questions:

    • How does biotechnology affect traditional food practices?
    • Who benefits from biotechnological food innovations?
    • How do we protect indigenous food knowledge?
