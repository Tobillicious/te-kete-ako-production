    🎯 Learning Objectives (He Whāinga Ako)

    • Understand the difference between fixed and growth mindsets
    • Recognize how "not yet" reframes failure as learning opportunity
    • Connect growth mindset to Māori concepts of perseverance
    • Apply growth mindset strategies to own learning
    • Develop resilience and positive self-talk

    🧠 Fixed vs Growth Mindset

    ❌ Fixed Mindset

    • "I can't do this"
    • "I'm not good at math/writing/etc."
    • "This is too hard"
    • "I give up"
    • "I'll never understand this"

    Result: Avoids challenges, gives up easily, sees failure as permanent

    ✅ Growth Mindset

    • "I can't do this yet"
    • "I'm learning how to..."
    • "This is challenging, but I'll keep trying"
    • "I'll try a different strategy"
    • "I'm getting better with practice"

    Result: Embraces challenges, persists through difficulty, sees failure as feedback

    🌿 Māori Concepts of Learning & Perseverance

    Ako: Reciprocal Learning

    Ako means both teaching and learning - recognizing that everyone is both teacher and learner. This aligns with growth mindset: we're all continuously learning.

    Whakataukī About Perseverance

    • "Kia kaha" - Be strong (encouragement to persist)
    • "Kia māia" - Be brave (courage to try difficult things)
    • "Kia manawanui" - Be steadfast (patience and determination)
    • "He kai kei aku ringa" - There is food at the end of my hands (through effort, success comes)

    Tū Rangatira: Standing Tall

    The concept of tū rangatira (standing with dignity and pride) connects to growth mindset - maintaining mana while learning from mistakes and challenges.

    💬 Key Discussion Points

    Understanding Mindsets

    1. What is the difference between a "fixed mindset" and a "growth mindset"?
    2. How can the phrase "not yet" change our approach to challenges?
    3. Can you think of a time when you had a fixed mindset? What happened?

    Cultural Connections

    1. How does "kia kaha, kia māia, kia manawanui" relate to growth mindset?
    2. What does "ako" (teaching and learning together) teach us about mindset?
    3. How can we maintain tū rangatira (dignity) while making mistakes?

    Personal Application

    1. What are some ways you can apply the "power of yet" in your own learning?
    2. What subject or skill do you currently struggle with? How can you reframe it with "yet"?
    3. How can you support classmates in developing growth mindset?

    Reflection & Growth

    1. Think of something you can do now that you couldn't do a year ago. How did you learn it?
    2. What role did mistakes and practice play in your learning?
    3. How will you use "yet" this week when facing challenges?

    💪 Growth Mindset Strategies

    1. Reframe Your Language

    "I can't do this" → "I can't do this yet"

    "I'm bad at this" → "I'm learning this"

    2. Embrace Challenges

    Choose difficult tasks that stretch your abilities

    See challenges as opportunities to grow

    3. Learn from Mistakes

    Mistakes are feedback, not failure

    Ask: "What can I learn from this?"

    4. Celebrate Effort

    Praise yourself for trying, not just succeeding

    Recognize progress, however small

    5. Seek Feedback

    Ask for help and guidance

    Use feedback to improve

    6. Practice Persistence

    Keep going when things get hard

    Remember: "Kia manawanui" (be steadfast)

    🌟 Extension Activities

    Growth Mindset Journal

    Keep a week-long journal. Each day, record: 1) A challenge you faced, 2) Your initial reaction, 3) How you reframed it with "yet", 4) What you learned.

    Mindset Posters

    Create bilingual posters (English/te reo Māori) with growth mindset phrases. Include whakataukī about perseverance. Display in classroom.

    Interview Project

    Interview someone who has mastered a difficult skill (athlete, musician, kaumātua). Ask about their learning journey, mistakes, and persistence.

    Personal Goal Setting

    Set a learning goal for something you "can't do yet." Create an action plan with milestones. Track progress over term.

    👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

    Teaching Growth Mindset

    Model it yourself: Share your own learning challenges and how you persist

    Praise effort: "I can see you worked hard on this" vs "You're so smart"

    Normalize struggle: "This is supposed to be challenging - that's how we grow"

    Celebrate mistakes: "Great mistake! What did we learn?"

    Cultural Integration

    Connect growth mindset to Māori learning concepts:

    • Ako: Everyone is both teacher and learner
    • Whanaungatanga: Learning together, supporting each other
    • Manaakitanga: Caring for each other through learning challenges
    • Tū rangatira: Maintaining dignity while learning from mistakes

    Classroom Environment

    Create a growth mindset culture:

    • Display growth mindset language (bilingual)
    • Celebrate effort and progress, not just achievement
    • Share stories of famous people's failures and persistence
    • Make mistakes safe and valuable
    • Use "yet" language consistently

    Differentiation

    Support: Provide sentence starters, mindset examples, regular check-ins

    Extension: Research neuroplasticity, analyze mindset in different cultures, create mindset intervention plans
