    Understanding Media Messages

    Critical Thinking Unit - Lesson 1 of 22 | Year 9-10 | Social Studies

    🌿 Cultural Context | Horopaki Ahurea

    Whakataukī

    "Mā te mōhio ka ora, mā te ora ka mōhio"

    "Through knowledge comes wellbeing, through wellbeing comes knowledge"

    In this lesson, we seek the truth behind media messages, questioning what we see and hear to find genuine understanding.

    🏛️ Connection to Mangakōtukutuku College Values

    Whaiara: Rising Up - Taking action to create positive change

    whaiara by empowering students to rise up as critical thinkers who question media narratives, challenge misinformation, and advocate for accurate representation of Māori perspectives in media.

    • Approach Māori content with respect and openness
    • Recognize students' diverse cultural backgrounds and experiences
    • Create space for Māori students to share their perspectives on media representation
    • Acknowledge historical and ongoing misrepresentation of Māori in mainstream media
    • Consult with whānau and local iwi for culturally sensitive topics

    🎯 Ngā Whāinga Ako - Learning Intentions

    Students Will Learn

    • 📚 Different types of media messages (inform, persuade, entertain, educate) and their purposes
    • 🧠 How media uses techniques to influence our thinking, emotions, and decisions
    • 🔍 A five-question critical thinking framework for analyzing any media message
    • 🌿 How te ao Māori perspectives are represented (or misrepresented) in media

    Students Will Demonstrate

    • ✅ By sorting media examples into categories and explaining their choices
    • ✅ By applying the critical questions framework to analyze a media example
    • ✅ By identifying techniques used to attract attention and convey messages
    • ✅ By reflecting on how media representation affects understanding of Māori culture

    📋 NZ Curriculum Alignment

    Social Studies Level 5: Understand how people's perspectives are influenced by media messages (Achievement Objective 5.4)

    Key Competencies: Thinking, Using language symbols and texts, Relating to others

    Te Marautanga o Aotearoa: Te whakamahi i te reo Māori hei kōrero, hei whakaaro, hei tuhinga

    🔍 Engage: Media Around Us (10 minutes)

    Starter Activity: Let's start by exploring different media examples. Click each button to see how different media types present information:

    Think-Pair-Share: Which example caught your attention most? Why do you think that is? Discuss with a partner for 2 minutes.

    📖 Explore: Types of Media Messages (25 minutes)

    Teacher Input (5 mins): Media messages come in many forms, each with different purposes:

    📰 Inform

    Purpose: To provide facts and information

    Examples: News reports, documentaries, educational content

    Techniques: Objective language, citations, expert sources

    🎯 Persuade

    Purpose: To convince you to believe or do something

    Examples: Advertisements, opinion pieces, political campaigns

    Techniques: Emotional appeals, celebrity endorsements, urgency

    🎪 Entertain

    Purpose: To amuse and engage audiences

    Examples: TV shows, movies, memes, games

    Techniques: Humor, drama, suspense, relatability

    🎓 Educate

    Purpose: To teach skills or knowledge

    Examples: Tutorials, how-to guides, educational videos

    Techniques: Step-by-step instructions, demonstrations, practice

    🤝 Activity: Media Sort (15 mins)

    Setup: Provide each pair with a selection of printed media examples (news articles, ads, social media posts, educational posters) OR allow access to devices for online examples.

    Task: Working in pairs, collect 5 different media examples. For each one:

    1. Identify which category it belongs to (inform, persuade, entertain, educate)
    2. List 2-3 techniques used to convey the message
    3. Note any Māori representation (positive, negative, or absent)

    Share (5 mins): Each pair shares one example with the class and explains their categorization.

    Differentiation: Advanced learners can identify examples that fit multiple categories and explain why.

    🌺 Te Ao Māori Perspective (5 minutes)

    Teacher Prompt: In Māori culture, storytelling (pūrākau) has always been central to sharing knowledge and values. Today's media continues this tradition, but we must ask critical questions:

    Critical Questions for Māori Media Representation:

    • 🗣️ Representation: How are Māori stories and perspectives represented? Are they accurate and respectful?
    • 👥 Authority: Who gets to tell these stories? Are Māori voices centered?
    • 🔍 Gaps: What perspectives might be missing? Whose voices are silenced?
    • 💭 Impact: How does this representation affect our understanding of Māori culture and history?

    Class Discussion: Can anyone share an example of Māori representation in media they've seen recently? Was it positive, negative, or stereotypical?

    🧠 Explain: Critical Questions Framework (15 minutes)

    Teacher Input (5 mins): When analyzing any media message, always ask these five critical questions:

    1. WHO? Who created this message and why? What are their motivations?
    2. WHAT? What techniques are used to attract attention? (colors, music, language, images)
    3. HOW? How might different people understand this differently? (age, culture, background)
    4. VALUES? What values or points of view are represented? What's being promoted?
    5. MISSING? What information might be missing? Whose perspectives are excluded?

    🔬 Practice Together (10 mins)

    Teacher: Display a chosen media example (e.g., a recent news article about Māori issues, a popular advertisement, or a viral social media post).

    Guided Analysis: Work through each of the five questions as a class, recording student responses on the board.

    Example Media Suggestions:

    • A news article about Māori Language Week
    • An advertisement featuring Māori culture or imagery
    • A social media post about a local environmental issue

    Key Teaching Point: Model how to look beyond the surface message to uncover deeper meanings, biases, and techniques.

    📝 Apply: Independent Assessment Task (20 minutes)

    Individual Analysis Task: Choose one media example from the selection provided and complete a detailed analysis using the Critical Questions Framework.

    Your Response Should Include:

    1. ✅ Identification: Media type and primary purpose (inform/persuade/entertain/educate)
    2. 🔍 Techniques: Specific techniques used to attract attention and convey the message
    3. 🌐 Cultural Representation: How Māori perspectives are represented (or absent)
    4. 💭 Critical Evaluation: Your personal response using the five critical questions
    5. 🎯 Impact: How this message might influence different audiences

    Success Criteria:

    • ✓ All five critical questions addressed with specific evidence
    • ✓ Clear understanding of media purpose and techniques demonstrated
    • ✓ Cultural awareness shown, particularly regarding Māori representation
    • ✓ Personal critical thinking evident (not just description)
    • ✓ Written clearly with proper paragraphs and examples

    Teacher Note: Provide 3-4 diverse media examples for students to choose from, ensuring at least one features Māori content or perspectives.

    🤔 Reflect & Connect (5 minutes)

    Individual Reflection (3 minutes):

    • How has today's lesson changed how you view media?
    • What's one question you still have about media messages?
    • How might you apply these critical thinking skills outside the classroom?

    Class Share (2 minutes):

    Share one insight with the class before we conclude.

    📊 Aromatawai - Assessment & Next Steps

    Formative Assessment

    Teachers should observe and assess:

    • ✓ Participation in media sort activity - Are students correctly categorizing media types?
    • ✓ Quality of class discussion contributions - Are students asking critical questions?
    • ✓ Written analysis task - Does it demonstrate understanding of the framework?
    • ✓ Cultural awareness - Are students considering Māori representation?

    Homework & Extension

    Required Homework:

    Find one media example from your daily life (social media, news, advertisement) and apply our five critical questions. Bring it to next lesson for discussion.

    Extension (Optional):

    • Research a historical example of media misrepresentation of Māori
    • Create your own media message that promotes positive Māori representation
    • Interview a whānau member about how media representation has changed over time
