    ğŸ“š Primary, Secondary & Tertiary Sources (15 minutes)

    Teacher Input (10 mins): Understanding the difference between source types helps you choose the right evidence for your research.

    Primary Sources

    Original, first-hand evidence

    Examples:

    • Treaty of Waitangi document
    • Diary entries, letters
    • Photographs, artwork
    • Interview transcripts
    • KaumÄtua oral histories
    • Research data, surveys

    Use when: You need direct evidence or want to analyze original material

    Secondary Sources

    Analysis/interpretation of primary sources

    Examples:

    • History textbooks
    • Academic journal articles
    • Documentaries
    • News analysis
    • Biographies
    • Literature reviews

    Use when: You need expert analysis or want to understand different perspectives

    Tertiary Sources

    Summaries/compilations of primary & secondary sources

    Examples:

    • Encyclopedias
    • Wikipedia
    • Dictionaries
    • Fact books
    • Bibliographies

    Use when: You need background info or want to find primary/secondary sources
