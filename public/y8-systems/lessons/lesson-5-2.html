NgƒÅ Mahi - Lesson Activities (75 minutes)

1. Group Formation & Indigenous Protocols (15 mins)

Setup: Distribute the collaboration framework. Explain the importance of group protocols based on indigenous values.

Activity: Groups establish protocols based on shared decision-making, collective responsibility, and cultural respect. Assign rotating roles (facilitator, recorder, timekeeper, cultural advisor).

Prompt: "How can we ensure our group embodies whanaungatanga (relationships) and manaakitanga (care) in our work together?"

Resource: Collaboration Framework

2. Research Existing Societies (25 mins)

Setup: Provide access to research materials on indigenous, contemporary, and historical societies. Guide groups to use systems thinking lens.

Activity: Groups research 2-3 existing societies to identify systemic strengths, challenges, and governance approaches. Focus on how societies address collective wellbeing versus individual gain.

Prompt: "What systems make this society function well? What systemic problems exist? How do indigenous societies approach these differently?"

Resource: Indigenous Systems Examples

3. Vision Development with Cultural Principles (25 mins)

Setup: Display key indigenous principles: whakapapa (relationships), kaitiakitanga (guardianship), mana (dignity), collective decision-making. Provide vision template.

Activity: Using research insights, groups develop their society's foundational vision statement integrating indigenous principles. Must identify 2-3 real-world challenges to address.

Prompt: "How can your society be grounded in relationships and collective responsibility? What injustices will you address through your design?"

Resource: Decolonized Design Template

4. Week 2 System Design Planning (10 mins)

Setup: Review Week 2 focus: designing governance, economy, rights, culture, and environment systems.

Activity: Groups identify specific research needs for system design and begin using the interactive design tool to organize ideas. Schedule first out-of-class meeting.

Prompt: "Which system will your group design first? What indigenous examples will you research this week?"

Resource: Interactive Design Tool
