    Lesson 5.2: Guided Inquiry Project - Week 1 Research Framework

    Research & Vision Development for Society Design

    📚 Week 1: Research & Vision Phase

    Today's Focus: Launch Group Research

    Groups begin their 5-week journey by establishing collaboration protocols, researching existing societies, and developing their initial vision using indigenous principles.

    1. Group Formation & Protocols (15 mins)

    Use the collaboration framework to establish group protocols based on indigenous values: shared decision-making, collective responsibility, and cultural respect. Assign rotating roles and create group agreements.

    2. Research Existing Societies (25 mins)

    Groups research 2-3 existing societies (indigenous, contemporary, historical) to identify strengths, challenges, and governance approaches. Focus on how different societies address collective wellbeing.

    3. Vision Development (25 mins)

    Using research insights, groups begin developing their society's foundational vision. Must incorporate: whakapapa (relationships), kaitiakitanga (guardianship), collective decision-making, and cultural respect.

    4. Week 2 Planning (10 mins)

    Groups plan their system design approach for next week and identify specific research needs. Begin using the interactive design tool to organize their ideas.

    🛠️ Continue with Interactive Design Tool

    Groups should continue their work using the comprehensive digital society design tool throughout the week.

    Launch Design Tool

    Learning Goal

    I can explain how indigenous governance principles offer solutions for creating more just and sustainable systems for future generations.

    Cultural Protocol

    Honor the sacred nature of this learning. Remind students they are now carriers of indigenous wisdom and have responsibilities to use it respectfully.
