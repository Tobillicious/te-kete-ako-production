The Challenge: Design Your Ideal Society

Working in groups of 4-5, you will research, plan, and design a completely new society that addresses the challenges we've studied throughout this unit. Your society must integrate the indigenous governance principles and decolonized thinking we've explored.

This is a 5-week guided inquiry project where you'll work collaboratively to create a comprehensive society design that serves the collective good and honors cultural wisdom.

ğŸ“… Project Timeline (5 Weeks)

Week 1: Research & Vision

Groups research existing societies, identify challenges, and develop their vision using cultural principles. Establish collaboration protocols.

Week 2-3: System Design

Design governance, economy, rights, culture, and environment systems. Apply indigenous principles and address real-world challenges.

Week 4: Refinement

Test systems for consistency, gather feedback, address potential issues, and prepare presentation materials.

Week 5: Presentation

Present society designs to community, defend design choices, and reflect on learning about systems and governance.

NgÄ Mahi - Lesson Activities (75 minutes)

1. Project Introduction & Group Formation (15 mins)

Setup: Present the Design Your Society project challenge. Show examples from the interactive design tool.

Activity: Students form groups of 4-5, ensuring diverse perspectives. Each group establishes initial collaboration protocols using the framework.

Prompt: "What makes a group function well? How will you ensure everyone's voice is heard?"

Resource: Group Collaboration Framework

2. Exploring Indigenous Governance Principles (20 mins)

Setup: Review indigenous governance principles learned in Unit 2. Display key concepts: whakapapa, kaitiakitanga, collective decision-making.

Activity: Groups discuss how to meaningfully integrate these principles into their society design (not tokenistic inclusion).

Prompt: "How can your society be grounded in relationships and collective wellbeing rather than individual gain?"

Resource: Indigenous Governance Principles

3. Society Design Tool Walkthrough (15 mins)

Setup: Demonstrate the interactive Society Design Tool on screen. Show how it guides through governance, economy, rights, culture, and environment systems.

Activity: Groups explore the tool on devices, familiarizing themselves with the design process and submission requirements.

Prompt: "What systems seem most challenging to design? Which are you most excited about?"

Resource: Interactive Design Tool

4. Initial Vision Development (20 mins)

Setup: Provide groups with vision development template. Guide them to think about values, principles, and core challenges their society will address.

Activity: Groups begin drafting their society's foundational vision statement and identify 2-3 real-world challenges they want to address.

Prompt: "What kind of world do you want to create? What injustices or challenges will your society address?"

Resource: Decolonized Design Template

5. Project Plan & Next Steps (5 mins)

Setup: Review the 5-week timeline and assessment rubric. Clarify expectations and deadlines.

Activity: Groups record their initial tasks for Week 1 research and schedule their first out-of-class meeting.

Prompt: "What research do you need to do this week? Who will be responsible for what?"

Resource: Assessment Rubric
