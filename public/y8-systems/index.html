    What is the most important thing in the world? It is people, it is people, it is people!

    This unit explores systems of power and governance through both Western and Māori perspectives, emphasizing tikanga-based decision making and the importance of collective wellbeing.

    • Understand how power operates in different systems
    • Compare Māori and Pākehā governance approaches
    • Analyze social change movements and their impact
    • Develop skills in civic participation and advocacy
    • Apply tikanga principles to contemporary issues

    • System design project (individual)
    • Power analysis presentation (group)
    • Social change campaign proposal
    • Reflective portfolio on civic engagement
    • Peer assessment of collaboration skills

    Introduction to different types of power and how they shape our daily lives.

    Examining how power operates in schools, families, and communities.

    Understanding how societies organize themselves through various systems.

    Exploring traditional Māori decision-making and leadership structures.

    Comparing different forms of government and democratic processes.

    Learning how young people can participate in democratic processes.

    Examining historical and contemporary movements for social change.

    Developing skills in effective advocacy and campaign planning.

    Beginning the culminating project to design an ideal system.

    Sharing and reflecting on system design solutions.
