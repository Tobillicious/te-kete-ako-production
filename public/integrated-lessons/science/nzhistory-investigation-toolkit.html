SECONDARY Sources

Created AFTER the time period, analyzing/interpreting events

  • History textbooks
  • Documentary films
  • Historical analysis articles
  • Museum exhibitions
  • Waitangi Tribunal findings
  • Academic research papers

üî¨ The Historical Investigation Process

Step 1: Ask a Historical Question

Good questions are specific, investigatable, and meaningful

Example: "How did the 1975 Land March change New Zealand politics?"

Step 2: Find Primary & Secondary Sources

Use archives, libraries, oral histories, official records, newspapers

Step 3: Evaluate Source Reliability

Consider: Who created this? When? Why? What biases might they have?

Step 4: Analyze & Cross-Reference

Compare multiple sources. Look for agreements and contradictions.

Step 5: Construct Your Answer

Use evidence to build an argument. Acknowledge limitations and multiple perspectives.
