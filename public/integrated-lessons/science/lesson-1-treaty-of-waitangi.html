    ğŸ“‹ Lesson Structure

    ğŸ¤” Pre-Listening (10 minutes)

    Activating Prior Knowledge

    Teacher Action: Distribute the student worksheet. Begin with a class discussion.

    Think-Pair-Share: "What is a Treaty?"

    • Think (2 min): Students individually write down what they know about Te Tiriti o Waitangi on their worksheet.
    • Pair (3 min): In pairs, students share their ideas and add to their notes.
    • Share (5 min): Pairs contribute key ideas to a class mind map. Focus on words like 'agreement', 'partnership', 'conflict', 'translation'.

    ğŸ§ During-Listening (25 minutes)

    Active Listening & Note-Taking

    Teacher Action: Play the podcast episode. Students will complete Part 2 of their worksheet as they listen.

    Worksheet Task

    Students will listen for and record key information on their worksheet, focusing on:

    • Key People: Who were the main figures involved?
    • Key Differences: What were the major differences between the two versions of the treaty?
    • Key Consequences: What happened immediately after the signing?
    Teacher Note: You may need to pause the podcast at key moments (e.g., after the explanation of the different versions) to allow students time to write.

    ğŸ—£ï¸ Post-Listening (20 minutes)

    Synthesizing & Discussing

    Teacher Action: Facilitate a group and then a class discussion based on the podcast and worksheet notes.

    Group Discussion (10 minutes)

    In groups of 3-4, students discuss the questions in Part 3 of their worksheet, focusing on the impact and ongoing relevance of the treaty.

    Class Debrief (10 minutes)

    Each group shares one key insight from their discussion. The teacher can guide the conversation with questions like:

    • "Why is it important to understand both versions of the Treaty?"
    • "How did the podcast change or confirm your understanding of this event?"
    • "Why is Te Tiriti o Waitangi still so important in Aotearoa today?"
