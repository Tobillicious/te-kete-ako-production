    Lesson 1.3: Years of Anger - The Protest Movements

    Unit: Walker - The Challenge to the Narrative
    Year Level: Year 10
    Subject: Social Studies, History, Civics
    Duration: 75 minutes

    📖 Lesson Overview

    This lesson examines the Māori protest movements of the 1970s, focusing on Ngā Tamatoa (The Young Warriors) and other activist groups. Students will explore the causes of Māori anger and activism, analyze the strategies and goals of these movements, and understand Dr. Ranginui Walker's role as both participant and chronicler of this pivotal period in New Zealand history.

    📚 Key Vocabulary

    Activism

    Vigorous campaigning to bring about political or social change.

    Protest

    A statement or action expressing disapproval of or objection to something.

    Ngā Tamatoa

    "The Young Warriors" - a Māori activist group founded in 1970.

    Civil Disobedience

    The active refusal to obey certain laws as a form of peaceful protest.

    Tino Rangatiratanga

    Self-determination, sovereignty, autonomy.

    🎯 Learning Intention (WALT)

    We are learning to understand the causes and consequences of the Māori protest movements of the 1970s.

    ✅ Success Criteria

    • I can identify at least three reasons why Māori activists protested in the 1970s.
    • I can describe the strategies and goals of Ngā Tamatoa and other protest groups.
    • I can explain Dr. Ranginui Walker's role in and perspective on these movements.
    • I can analyze the effectiveness of protest as a tool for social change.
    • I can connect this topic to the school value of Whaiara (Rising Up).
