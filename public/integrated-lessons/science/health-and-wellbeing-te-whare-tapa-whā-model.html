    Te Whare Tapa Whā: A Holistic Health Model

    Learning Objectives

    • Understand the four dimensions of Te Whare Tapa Whā and how they contribute to wellbeing.
    • Reflect on personal wellbeing using this model.
    • Apply the model to real-life scenarios.
    • Respect and engage with Māori perspectives on health.

    Cultural Context

    Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori health model developed by Sir Mason Durie. It compares wellbeing to a wharenui (meeting house), where each wall represents a different aspect of health. If one wall is weak, the whole house is unstable.

    Cultural Safety Note: Ensure respectful discussion of Māori concepts. Encourage students to engage with the model in a way that honours its origins.

    The Four Dimensions

    Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

    Your body's health – exercise, nutrition, sleep, and physical safety.

    Taha Hinengaro (Mental & Emotional Wellbeing)

    Your thoughts, feelings, and mental resilience.

    Taha Whānau (Social Wellbeing)

    Relationships with family, friends, and community.

    Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

    Your sense of purpose, values, and connection to the world.

    Activity: Personal Wellbeing Reflection

    Task: Draw your own "whare" and rate each wall (1-5) based on your current wellbeing. Write one action to strengthen each dimension.

    Discussion: Share in small groups (optional) – what patterns do you notice?

    Assessment Suggestions

    • Self-assessment: Reflection journal on personal wellbeing.
    • Group task: Create a poster explaining Te Whare Tapa Whā.
    • Scenario analysis: Apply the model to a case study.

    Extension Activities

    • Research another indigenous wellbeing model (e.g., Fonofale) and compare.
    • Interview a kaumātua about traditional Māori health practices.
    • Design a wellbeing program for your school using Te Whare Tapa Whā.

    Adaptations for Diverse Learners

    • Visual learners: Use diagrams and colour-coding.
    • Kinesthetic learners: Build a 3D model of the whare.
    • ESOL students: Provide vocabulary lists and sentence starters.
    • Simplified version: Focus on one dimension at a time.
