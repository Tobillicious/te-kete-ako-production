    He Mihi / Introduction

    This resource explores the physics of sound through the lens of traditional MÄori instruments (ngÄ taonga pÅ«oro). Students will discover how these culturally significant objects demonstrate fundamental principles of sound waves, vibration, and resonance.

    Cultural Context

    NgÄ taonga pÅ«oro (MÄori musical instruments) are more than just sound-making devices. They are considered treasures (taonga) that connect people to the natural world, ancestors, and spiritual realms. Each instrument has specific cultural protocols, stories, and appropriate contexts for use.

    Traditional knowledge holders (tohunga) understood the physics of these instruments through observation and practice, even without modern scientific terminology. This resource respectfully bridges traditional MÄori knowledge with contemporary physics education.

    Cultural Safety & Respectful Practice

    • Always handle instruments with care and respect
    • Some instruments have specific cultural protocols - research before use
    • When possible, invite local knowledge holders to share expertise
    • Acknowledge the iwi/tribal origins of specific instruments
    • Use te reo MÄori terms correctly and consistently

    • Connect physics concepts to cultural practices and knowledge systems
