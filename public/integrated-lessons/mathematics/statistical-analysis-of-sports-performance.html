    Statistical Analysis of Sports Performance

    Exploring Data in Modern and Traditional Māori Sports

    Learning Objectives (NZC Level 5-6 Mathematics & PE)

    • Collect, organise, and interpret statistical data from sports.
    • Calculate measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and spread (range, IQR).
    • Compare performance data between modern sports and traditional Māori games.
    • Understand the cultural significance of Māori sports like ki-o-rahi and tī rākau.
    • Use digital tools (Google Sheets, online graphs) to analyse data.

    Engagement Activity: "Which Sport is More Consistent?"

    Watch short clips of ki-o-rahi (traditional Māori ball game) and basketball. In groups, brainstorm:

    • What performance metrics could we track? (e.g., scoring frequency, pass accuracy)
    • How might traditional games differ statistically from modern sports?

    Data Collection & Analysis

    Students will gather data from two sources:

    1. Modern Sport (e.g., Basketball): Track points scored per player in a game.
    2. Traditional Māori Game (e.g., Tī Rākau): Measure successful stick catches per minute.
    Sport Data to Collect Statistical Measures
    Basketball Points per player, assists, rebounds Mean, median, range
    Tī Rākau (stick games) Catches per minute, reaction time Mode, interquartile range

    Digital Task: Create a Comparative Graph

    Using Google Sheets or similar, input your data and generate:

    • A box-and-whisker plot comparing scoring distributions.
    • A bar graph showing average performance differences.

    Cultural Context & Reflection

    Why study Māori sports statistically?

    • Traditional games like ki-o-rahi were used for training, strategy, and community bonding.
    • Statistical analysis helps preserve and appreciate Indigenous knowledge systems.

    Discuss: How might data analysis improve training in both modern and traditional sports?
