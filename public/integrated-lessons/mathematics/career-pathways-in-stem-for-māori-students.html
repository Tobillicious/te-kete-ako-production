Career Pathways in STEM for Māori Students

Ngā Ara Mahi Pūtaiao, Pūkaha, Hangarau me te Pāngarau mā ngā Ākonga Māori

Exploring culturally connected STEM opportunities for Years 11-13

Introduction

This resource explores STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) career pathways with specific connections to Te Ao Māori. We'll examine how traditional Māori knowledge and modern STEM fields intersect, and explore career opportunities that allow you to contribute to your communities while pursuing your interests in STEM.

Cultural Safety Note

This resource acknowledges the importance of cultural identity in career choices. We recognize that for many Māori students, career decisions are influenced by whānau, hapū, and iwi connections. All examples and case studies have been developed in consultation with Māori STEM professionals.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Identify at least five STEM career pathways with connections to Te Ao Māori
  • Explain how mātauranga Māori can inform and enhance STEM practices
  • Research and present information about a Māori STEM professional
  • Develop a personal career action plan that incorporates cultural values
  • Identify relevant tertiary education pathways for chosen STEM careers

Māori Perspectives in STEM

Traditional Māori knowledge systems contain sophisticated understandings of the natural world that align with modern STEM principles. Let's explore some connections:

Rongoā Māori & Science

Traditional healing practices demonstrate sophisticated understanding of plant properties and biochemistry.

Whakairo & Engineering

Traditional carving requires precise mathematical understanding of symmetry, proportion, and structural integrity.

Navigation & Astronomy

Traditional wayfinding demonstrates advanced knowledge of astronomy, ocean currents, and weather patterns.

Environmental Science

Kaitiakitanga principles align with modern sustainability and conservation science.

Explore STEM Career Pathways

Select a career area to learn more about opportunities and Māori professionals in these fields:

Environmental Science

Combine traditional knowledge of kaitiakitanga with modern environmental science to protect and restore Aotearoa's unique ecosystems.

Career Opportunities:

  • Environmental Consultant
  • Conservation Biologist
  • Marine Scientist
  • Climate Change Analyst

Māori Professional Spotlight: Dr. Dan Hikuroa

Earth scientist and senior lecturer who integrates mātauranga Māori with Western science in environmental management.

Educational Pathways:

Bachelor of Science (Environmental Science), Bachelor of Environmental Studies

Health Sciences

Improve health outcomes for Māori communities by combining rongoā Māori with modern medical science.

Career Opportunities:

  • Medical Researcher
  • Public Health Specialist
  • Biomedical Scientist
  • Genetic Counselor

Māori Professional Spotlight: Dr. Matire Harwood

Medical researcher and GP focusing on improving Māori health outcomes through culturally responsive care.

Educational Pathways:

Bachelor of Health Sciences, Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery, Bachelor of Biomedical Science

Technology

Develop innovative digital solutions that preserve and share Māori language, culture, and knowledge.

Career Opportunities:

  • Software Developer
  • UX/UI Designer
  • Data Analyst
  • Cybersecurity Specialist

Māori Professional Spotlight: Dr. Te Taka Keegan

Computer scientist who has worked on Māori language technology including Microsoft's Māori language interface.

Educational Pathways:

Bachelor of Computer Science, Bachelor of Information Sciences, Bachelor of Software Engineering

Engineering

Design and build sustainable infrastructure that respects whenua and incorporates Māori design principles.

Career Opportunities:

  • Civil Engineer
  • Environmental Engineer
  • Renewable Energy Engineer
  • Structural Engineer

Māori Professional Spotlight: Dr. Kepa Morgan

Civil and environmental engineer who developed the Mauri Model decision-making framework.

Educational Pathways:

Bachelor of Engineering (various specializations)

Data Science

Use data to tell Māori stories, inform iwi decision-making, and address social and economic challenges.

Career Opportunities:

  • Data Scientist
  • Statistical Analyst
  • Business Intelligence Developer
  • Research Scientist

Māori Professional Spotlight: Dr. Tahu Kukutai

Professor of Demography who specializes in Māori and Indigenous demographic research.

Educational Pathways:

Bachelor of Science (Statistics), Bachelor of Data Science, Bachelor of Information Sciences
