    📊 Mathematics and Statistics Learning Area

    "In the mathematics and statistics learning area, students learn about and appreciate the power of symbolic representation, reasoning, and abstraction. They learn to investigate, interpret, and explain patterns and relationships in quantity, space, time, data, and uncertainty."

    NZ Curriculum Mathematics Strands:

    📈 Number and Algebra

    Patterns, relationships, equations

    📏 Geometry and Measurement

    Shape, space, measurement

    📊 Statistics

    Data collection, analysis, probability

    Years 1-2: Foundation Mathematics

    Number and Algebra

    • Counting Systems: Māori Counting Systems - Learning takahi, rua, toru through cultural contexts
    • Basic Operations: Family Tree Mathematics - Counting generations and relationships
    • Patterns: Tukutuku Pattern Mathematics - Geometric patterns in weaving

    Geometry and Measurement

    • Shape Recognition: Marae Architecture Shapes - Identifying shapes in cultural buildings
    • Traditional Measurement: Traditional Body Measurements - Using anana, ringa measurements

    Statistics

    • Data Collection: Family Information Gathering - Simple surveys about whānau
    • Simple Graphs: Seasonal Calendar Graphs - Recording traditional seasonal indicators

    Years 3-4: Developing Mathematical Thinking

    Number and Algebra

    • Place Value: Kumara Storage Mathematics - Understanding hundreds, tens, ones through traditional storage
    • Fractions: Hangi Sharing Mathematics - Fair sharing of food using fractions
    • Time: Maramataka Calendar Mathematics - Traditional time concepts

    Geometry and Measurement

    • Area and Perimeter: Traditional Garden Mathematics - Measuring māra kai plots
    • 3D Shapes: Waka Construction Geometry - 3D shapes in boat building

    Statistics

    • Probability: Traditional Weather Prediction - Likelihood of weather patterns
    • Data Display: Iwi Population Statistics - Creating bar graphs and pictographs

    Years 5-6: Mathematical Reasoning & Problem Solving

    Number and Algebra

    • Decimal Understanding: Navigation Mathematics - Precise measurements for ocean journeys
    • Proportional Reasoning: Traditional Recipe Scaling - Scaling hangi recipes for different group sizes
    • Basic Algebra: Resource Allocation Problems - Using variables in traditional resource management

    Geometry and Measurement

    • Coordinate Geometry: Star Navigation Coordinates - Using coordinate systems for navigation
    • Scale and Ratio: Marae Blueprint Mathematics - Working with architectural scales

    Statistics

    • Survey Design: Community Needs Analysis - Designing and conducting meaningful surveys
    • Data Interpretation: Māori Population Trends - Analyzing demographic data critically

    Years 7-8: Advanced Mathematical Concepts

    Number and Algebra

    • Linear Equations: Sustainable Fishing Mathematics - Modeling fish population recovery
    • Financial Mathematics: Iwi Economic Development - Interest, investment, and economic planning
    • Exponential Growth: Te Reo Revitalization Statistics - Modeling language learning growth

    Geometry and Measurement

    • Trigonometry Basics: Traditional Navigation Trigonometry - Using angles and distances
    • Circle Geometry: Ceremonial Space Geometry - Mathematics of circular gathering spaces

    Statistics

    • Statistical Inference: Treaty Settlement Analysis - Making inferences from complex data
    • Bivariate Data: Health and Education Correlations - Analyzing relationships in social data

    🔗 Cross-Curricular Mathematics Connections

    📖 Mathematics + English

    Data storytelling, mathematical explanations, reading graphs and statistics in media

    🔬 Mathematics + Science

    Measurement in experiments, graphing scientific data, modeling natural phenomena

    🌍 Mathematics + Social Sciences

    Population statistics, economic modeling, geographic data analysis, historical timelines

    🎨 Mathematics + The Arts

    Geometric patterns in art, music mathematics, symmetry in design, proportions in visual arts

    🏆 Assessment Strategies for Mathematics

    Problem-Based Assessment

    Real-world mathematical problems drawn from cultural contexts, requiring multiple solution strategies and explanations.

    Mathematical Communication

    Students explain their thinking, justify solutions, and present mathematical findings to authentic audiences.

    Portfolio Development

    Collections of mathematical work showing growth in understanding, with reflections on learning and cultural connections.
