    Cultural Context: Tikanga MƒÅori

    Traditional MƒÅori navigation is part of a rich knowledge system called mƒÅtauranga MƒÅori. When studying this topic:

    • Recognize that this knowledge was developed over centuries of observation and experience
    • Understand that wayfinding was (and is) both a practical skill and spiritual practice
    • Respect that some knowledge may be tapu (sacred) to certain iwi (tribes)
    • Pronounce MƒÅori terms correctly - practice with students (e.g., whakatere = fah-kah-teh-reh)
    • Acknowledge the iwi whose rohe (territory) you're in when discussing local navigation knowledge

    Key concept: Kaitiakitanga - the guardianship of knowledge and environment that guided traditional navigation.

    Modern GPS Technology

    Global Positioning System (GPS) uses satellites to determine precise locations:

    • 24+ satellites orbiting Earth
    • Triangulation from multiple satellites
    • Accuracy within meters (sometimes centimeters)
    • Works globally in all weather conditions

    Extension Activities

    1. Navigation Challenge

    Plan a journey from your school to a nearby landmark using:

    • Traditional methods only (create a star chart, note environmental signs)
    • Modern methods only (GPS coordinates, digital maps)
    • A combination of both

    Present your plans and compare their strengths/weaknesses.

    2. Star Observation Journal

    Over two weeks, observe and record:

    • Positions of 3 bright stars at the same time each night
    • Moon phases and position
    • Any visible planets
    • Weather patterns and their effect on visibility

    3. Technology Debate

    Prepare arguments for and against: "Traditional navigation methods are obsolete in the age of GPS."

    Teacher Implementation Notes

    Timing: This unit can be taught over 4-6 lessons depending on depth and extensions.

    Preparation:

    • Consult with local iwi or MƒÅori knowledge holders if possible
    • Test any digital tools beforehand (Google Earth, star map apps)
    • Prepare printed star charts for cloudy nights
    • Identify safe outdoor observation areas

    Differentiation:

    • Support: Provide partially completed comparison tables
    • Extension: Research specific iwi navigation traditions
    • Practical: Create simple quadrant instruments for angle measurement

    Safety: For night observations, ensure proper supervision and parent notifications.
