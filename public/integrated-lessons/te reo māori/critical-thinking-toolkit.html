Bloom's Taxonomy

Levels of Thinking (Low to High):

  1. Remember: Recall facts and basic concepts
  2. Understand: Explain ideas or concepts
  3. Apply: Use information in new situations
  4. Analyze: Draw connections among ideas
  5. Evaluate: Justify decisions or courses of action
  6. Create: Produce new or original work

Aim for the higher levels - that's where deep learning happens!

Socratic Questioning

Types of Powerful Questions:

  • Clarification: "What do you mean by...?"
  • Assumptions: "What are we assuming here?"
  • Evidence: "How do we know this is true?"
  • Perspectives: "What's an alternative viewpoint?"
  • Implications: "What would happen if...?"
  • Questions about questions: "Why is this question important?"
