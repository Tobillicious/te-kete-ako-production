🌿 What is Pūrākau?

Pūrākau are traditional Māori narratives that encode knowledge, values, and history. They include:

  • Kōrero tuku iho: Historical accounts passed down through generations
  • Whakapapa: Genealogical connections
  • Tikanga: Cultural practices and protocols
  • Mātauranga: Traditional knowledge systems

✨ Your Digital Pūrākau Project

Project Steps:

  1. Choose a story: Whānau history, local history, cultural practice, or traditional pūrākau
  2. Research: Interview kaumātua, research archives, gather photos/artifacts
  3. Plan your narrative: Beginning, middle, end with cultural context
  4. Select digital format: Video, podcast, website, or social media series
  5. Create and edit: Use tools like iMovie, Canva, Audacity
  6. Share respectfully: Consider cultural protocols before publishing

🛡️ Cultural Safety Considerations

  • Always get permission before sharing others' stories
  • Respect tapu (sacred) content - not all stories are for public sharing
  • Consult with whānau/iwi about appropriate representation
  • Give proper attribution and credit
  • Use te reo Māori correctly with audio pronunciation when possible
