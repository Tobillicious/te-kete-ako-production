    Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

    This lesson is grounded in the principles of MÄori Data Sovereignty, which asserts that MÄori have inherent rights to control MÄori data, including:

    • Whakapapa - Understanding the relationships and connections in data
    • Kaitiakitanga - Guardianship and protection of data
    • Manaakitanga - Ethical use of data that respects people
    • Tapu - Recognizing the sacredness of certain information

    When working with MÄori data or developing systems that might impact MÄori communities, we must consider:

    1. Who has the right to collect, access, and use this data?
    2. How can we ensure the data benefits the communities it comes from?
    3. What protocols should be in place to protect cultural knowledge?

    Developing Ethical AI Systems

    When creating AI systems that might use or affect MÄori data, consider this framework:

    Principle AI Development Consideration Practical Application
    Tino Rangatiratanga (Self-determination) MÄori control over MÄori data Include MÄori in governance structures for data projects
    Manaakitanga (Respect) Design systems that respect cultural values Implement cultural review processes for algorithms
    Kaitiakitanga (Guardianship) Protect data for future generations Establish data protection protocols aligned with MÄori values
    Whanaungatanga (Relationships) Build systems that strengthen community Design for community benefit, not just individual use

    Activity: Ethical AI Design Challenge

    In small groups, design an AI system concept that respects MÄori Data Sovereignty principles:

    1. Identify a problem your AI system will address
    2. Describe how your system will collect and use data
    3. Explain how you will ensure MÄori control over MÄori data
    4. Outline your consultation process with relevant communities
    5. Present your concept to the class

    Research Project

    Investigate a real-world case where MÄori Data Sovereignty principles were either upheld or violated in technology development. Present your findings to the class, including:

    • Description of the case
    • Analysis of how data sovereignty principles applied
    • Recommendations for how the situation could have been handled better

    Community Engagement Plan

    Develop a detailed plan for engaging with MÄori communities when developing an AI system that might use MÄori data. Include:

    • Who you would consult with
    • What consultation methods you would use
    • How you would incorporate feedback
    • How you would maintain ongoing relationships

    Teacher Implementation Notes

    Prior Knowledge: Students should have basic understanding of AI systems and data collection practices.

    Duration: 3-5 lessons (depending on depth of activities)

    Differentiation:

    • For less experienced students: Focus on understanding basic principles through discussion
    • For more advanced students: Extend with deeper research and system design

    Cultural Safety:

    • Consult with local MÄori experts if possible
    • Be aware that some students may have personal connections to data sovereignty issues
    • Create a respectful environment for discussing cultural concepts

    Assessment Options:

    • Written report on AI ethics case study
    • Presentation of ethical AI design
    • Reflective journal on learning
