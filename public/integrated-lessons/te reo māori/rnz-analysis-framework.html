Media Analysis: Understanding News with Critical Eyes

What is Media Literacy?

The ability to:

  • Access and find reliable media
  • Analyze media messages critically
  • Evaluate credibility and bias
  • Create responsible media content

Analyzing News Articles

Key Questions:

ElementQuestions to Ask
Headline Is it clickbait? Does it accurately reflect the story?
Author Who wrote it? What's their expertise/bias?
Sources Who is quoted? Are multiple perspectives included?
Language Is it objective or emotional? Any loaded words?
Purpose To inform, persuade, entertain, or sell?

Bias Recognition

Types of Bias:

  • Selection bias: Choosing which stories to cover
  • Omission bias: Leaving out important information
  • Language bias: Using loaded or emotional words
  • Source bias: Only quoting one perspective
  • Visual bias: Photos chosen to influence emotion

Cultural Representation in Media

Analyzing Māori Representation:

Critical questions:

  • Are Māori portrayed stereotypically or authentically?
  • Are Māori voices centered in stories about Māori?
  • Is te reo Māori used respectfully and accurately?
  • Are diverse Māori perspectives shown?
  • Is historical/cultural context provided?

Practice Activity: Compare Coverage

Find the same story on 3 different news sites:

  1. RNZ (public broadcaster)
  2. NZ Herald (privately owned)
  3. The Spinoff (independent)

Compare:

  • Headlines - any differences?
  • Sources quoted - who gets voice?
  • Language used - objective or biased?
  • Information included/excluded

Creating Responsible Media

If you create media content:

  • ✅ Fact-check everything
  • ✅ Include multiple perspectives
  • ✅ Use respectful language
  • ✅ Credit sources properly
  • ✅ Consider cultural sensitivity
  • ✅ Think about impact on audience

Assessment

  • Analyze one news article using the framework above
  • Compare coverage of the same story across 3 outlets
  • Write your own balanced news article on a school event
  • Create a media literacy guide for Year 7 students
