Whakakite me te Kōrero - Show and Tell in Te Reo

Activity Purpose

Show and Tell in te reo helps students:

  • Practice public speaking in te reo Māori
  • Build confidence using language in context
  • Learn vocabulary from each other
  • Share cultural connections to objects

Sentence Structures to Use

Basic Templates:

Ko [name] tēnei - This is [object]
He [color] tēnei - This is [color]
Nō [place/person] tēnei - This is from [place/person]
He taonga tēnei - This is a treasure
I whiwhi au i tēnei... - I got this...

Example Show and Tell:

Kia ora tātou.
Ko tēnei tāku pounamu.
He taonga tēnei nō tōku kuia.
He kākariki te tae.
He pai ki ahau tēnei taonga.
Kia ora.

"Hello everyone. This is my greenstone. This treasure is from my grandmother. The color is green. I like this treasure. Thank you."

Object Ideas

What to bring:

  • Family taonga (treasure)
  • Cultural item (koru necklace, woven bracelet)
  • Special book or toy
  • Photo of special place
  • Something you made
  • Gift from someone special

Preparation Steps

  1. Choose your object (something meaningful)
  2. Write your script (5-6 sentences in te reo)
  3. Practice pronunciation (use online dictionary)
  4. Get feedback (teacher/peer check)
  5. Practice delivery (speak clearly, make eye contact)
  6. Present with confidence!

Useful Vocabulary

Te ReoEnglish
taongatreasure
kohagift
āhuaappearance
hangato make/create
tawhitoold/ancient
hounew

Assessment Criteria

  • ✅ Uses at least 5 complete te reo sentences
  • ✅ Accurate pronunciation and grammar
  • ✅ Speaks clearly and confidently
  • ✅ Makes cultural connections if applicable
  • ✅ Engages audience (eye contact, volume)

Audience Participation

After each presentation, classmates can:

  • Ask questions in te reo: "He aha tēnā?" (What is that?)
  • Give compliments: "Kāpai!" "Ka pai to kōrero!"
  • Learn new vocabulary from presentations

Ka rawe! This is a fun way to practice te reo in a supportive environment!
