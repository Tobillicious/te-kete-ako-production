The Engineering Design Process

6 Steps:

  1. Ask: What's the problem? What are the requirements?
  2. Research: What solutions exist? What materials are available?
  3. Imagine: Brainstorm multiple solutions
  4. Plan: Choose best idea, create detailed plans
  5. Create: Build a prototype
  6. Improve: Test, evaluate, iterate

Design Challenge: Bridge Building

üåâ The Challenge:

Problem: Design and build a bridge that can span 50cm and hold 500g

Constraints:

  • Materials: 50 popsicle sticks, glue, string
  • Time: 60 minutes to design and build
  • Must be freestanding (not attached to tables)

Success Criteria:

  • Functionality: Holds required weight
  • Efficiency: Minimal material use
  • Aesthetics: Pleasing design
  • Cultural integration: Incorporates koru or other MƒÅori design elements

Apply the Design Process

Step-by-Step:

1. Ask (5 min):

What makes a strong bridge? What loads does it need to support?

2. Research (10 min):

Study bridge types: beam, arch, truss, suspension. Which is strongest?

3. Imagine (10 min):

Sketch 3 different design ideas. Label parts and materials.

4. Plan (10 min):

Choose your best design. Create detailed building plan with measurements.

5. Create (30 min):

Build your prototype. Work carefully and test stability as you go.

6. Improve (15 min):

Test your bridge. What broke? What can be reinforced? Iterate if time permits.
