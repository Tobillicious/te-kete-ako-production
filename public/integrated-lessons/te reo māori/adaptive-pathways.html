Adaptive Learning Pathways: Personalized Education

What are Adaptive Pathways?

Adaptive learning recognizes that every student is unique, with different:

  • Learning styles: Visual, auditory, kinesthetic, reading/writing
  • Prior knowledge: Different starting points
  • Pace: Some need more time, others move faster
  • Interests: Connections to cultural identity and passions
  • Strengths: Areas of natural talent

Multiple Pathways to Success

Example: Learning About Photosynthesis

📖 Pathway A

Read textbook, take notes, complete quiz

🎨 Pathway B

Create visual diagram, present to class

🔬 Pathway C

Conduct experiment, record results

🎬 Pathway D

Create explainer video with animations

All pathways achieve the same learning objective, just through different methods!

Cultural Pathways

Connecting Learning to Identity:

Many topics can be approached through cultural lenses:

  • Mathematics: Learn through traditional Māori games, navigation, or pattern-making
  • Science: Explore through rongoā (traditional medicine) or environmental kaitiakitanga
  • English: Study through pūrākau (traditional stories) or contemporary Māori writing

Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

Three Principles:

  1. Multiple means of engagement: Offer choices in topics and activities
  2. Multiple means of representation: Present information in various formats
  3. Multiple means of expression: Allow different ways to show learning

Choose Your Own Adventure

Assessment Options for This Unit:

Pick ONE way to demonstrate your understanding:

  1. 📝 Written essay (800 words)
  2. 🎤 Oral presentation (5 minutes)
  3. 🎨 Visual poster/infographic
  4. 🎬 Video explanation (3-5 minutes)
  5. 🎭 Dramatic performance/skit
  6. 💻 Interactive digital resource
  7. 🎵 Song or waiata
  8. 🏗️ Physical model or demonstration

Self-Directed Learning

Taking Ownership of Your Learning:

  • Set your own goals
  • Choose topics that interest you
  • Manage your time and pace
  • Seek help when needed
  • Reflect on what works for you

Your Path is Unique

Honor your learning style, cultural identity, and personal strengths!
