    Unit 1 Lesson 3: Mātauranga Māori Inquiry Cards

    Cut out these cards and use them for group inquiry activities. Each card represents a different area of Māori knowledge systems. Students can research, discuss, and present their findings.

    🌟
    Whakapapa
    How do genealogical connections shape Māori identity and relationships?
    🌿
    Kaitiakitanga
    What does it mean to be guardians of the environment?
    🏔️
    Whakatōhea
    How do mountains, rivers, and landmarks hold spiritual significance?
    🎭
    Kapa Haka
    How do performing arts preserve and share cultural knowledge?
    🏛️
    Tikanga
    What customs and protocols guide Māori social interactions?
    📚
    Kōrero
    How do oral traditions and stories pass down knowledge?
    🎨
    Toi Māori
    What meanings and messages are carried in traditional arts?
    🌊
    Whakatōhea Navigation
    How did Māori navigate vast ocean distances using natural signs?
    🌱
    Rongoā
    What traditional healing knowledge exists in Māori medicine?
    🏡
    Whare Construction
    How do traditional building methods reflect cultural values?
    ⭐
    Astronomy
    How did Māori use stars for navigation and seasonal planning?
    🥬
    Māhinga Kai
    What traditional food systems sustained Māori communities?

    🔍 Inquiry Process Suggestions

    Research Questions

    • What is the traditional knowledge?
    • How is it practiced today?
    • Why is it important?
    • How can we respect and learn from it?

    Presentation Ideas

    • Create a visual poster
    • Prepare a short presentation
    • Write a reflection piece
    • Design an infographic
