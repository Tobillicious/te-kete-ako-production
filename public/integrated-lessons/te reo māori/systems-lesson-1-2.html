🔄 Introduction to Systems Thinking

📚 Lesson 1: What is a System?

A system is a group of interconnected parts that work together to achieve a purpose. Systems have inputs, processes, outputs, and feedback loops.

Examples of Systems:

  • 🌍 Ecosystems: Plants, animals, water, soil interact
  • 🏫 School system: Students, teachers, resources, curriculum
  • 💧 Water cycle: Evaporation → condensation → precipitation → collection
  • 🚗 Traffic system: Roads, vehicles, signals, drivers

📚 Lesson 2: Interconnections & Feedback Loops

🔗 Interconnections

In systems, everything is connected. Changing one part affects the whole system. This is whakapapa thinking - recognizing relationships!

🔄 Feedback Loops

Positive feedback: Change amplifies (snowball effect)
Negative feedback: Change stabilizes (thermostat effect)

Example:

🌳 Deforestation → less rain → drier soil → fewer trees (positive feedback loop making problem worse)

🎯 Activity: Map a System

Choose one system and draw a diagram showing:

  1. All the parts (components)
  2. How they connect (arrows showing relationships)
  3. At least one feedback loop
