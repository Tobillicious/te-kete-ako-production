🗻 Pepeha: Introducing Yourself with Place

Connecting Identity to Whenua, Awa, Maunga

"Ko tōku reo, ko tōku ohooho. Ko tōku reo, ko tōku māpihi maurea"

My language is my awakening. My language is the window to my soul

🌿 What is Pepeha?

Pepeha is a traditional Māori way of introducing yourself by connecting your identity to the land - your mountain, river, sea, ancestral canoe, iwi, and hapū. It's not just "where you're from" - it's a statement of your whakapapa (genealogy) and tūrangawaewae (place to stand).

Example Pepeha:

Ko Taranaki te maunga

Ko Whanganui te awa

Ko Aotea te waka

Ko Ngāti Apa te iwi

Ko Ngāti Parewahawaha te hapū

Ko Tāmati ahau

Translation: Taranaki is my mountain, Whanganui is my river, Aotea is my waka, Ngāti Apa is my iwi, Ngāti Parewahawaha is my hapū, I am Tāmati

✍️ Create Your Own Pepeha

For ALL Students:

Everyone can create a pepeha! If you don't have Māori whakapapa, you can identify the mountain, river, and places that are significant to YOU and your whānau. This connects you to the land you live on.

Research Your Places:

  1. What mountain can you see from your home/school?
  2. What river or stream is nearest to you?
  3. If applicable: What waka did your ancestors arrive on?
  4. If applicable: What iwi/hapū do you belong to?
  5. If not Māori: What places are significant to your family?
