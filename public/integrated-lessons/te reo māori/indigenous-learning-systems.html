Traditional Māori Learning

How Mātauranga Was Passed Down:

  • Wānanga: Extended study sessions with experts
  • Ako: Both teaching and learning (reciprocal)
  • Tuakana-teina: Older siblings teaching younger
  • Whakapapa: Organizing knowledge through genealogy
  • Pūrākau: Stories encoding complex concepts
  • Waiata: Songs as memory aids
  • Karakia: Incantations for specific purposes

Tuakana-Teina Model

Peer Learning Partnership:

Tuakana (older/more experienced): Guides and supports teina

Teina (younger/less experienced): Learns and asks questions

Benefits of This Model:

  • Tuakana reinforces their knowledge by teaching
  • Teina gets personalized support
  • Builds whanaungatanga (relationships)
  • Everyone has a role and responsibility

Learning in Context

Traditional vs Modern Education:

Best practice: Integrate both approaches!

Ako - Reciprocal Learning

In te ao Māori, ako means both "to teach" AND "to learn"

This reflects the belief that:

  • Teachers learn from students too
  • Everyone has knowledge to share
  • Learning is a collaborative process
  • The expert/novice roles can shift

Practical Application

Implement Tuakana-Teina in Your Learning:

  1. Pair up with someone at a different skill level
  2. Tuakana teaches a concept to teina
  3. Teina asks questions and practices
  4. Roles can switch for different topics!

Reflection: How does this feel different from traditional classroom learning?

Assessment

  • Compare traditional Māori and modern learning systems
  • Participate in tuakana-teina partnership for 2 weeks
  • Reflect on which learning styles work best for you
  • Design a lesson using Māori pedagogy principles
