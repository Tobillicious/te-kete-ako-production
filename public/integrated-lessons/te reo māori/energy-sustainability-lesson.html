Energy & Sustainability: Living as Kaitiaki

"Mō tātou, ā, mō kā uri ā muri ake nei"

"For us and our children after us"

Learning Objectives

  • Understand different energy sources (renewable vs non-renewable)
  • Calculate energy use and carbon footprint
  • Apply kaitiakitanga to energy decisions
  • Design sustainable energy solutions

Energy Sources

☀️ Renewable

  • Solar (te rā)
  • Wind (te hau)
  • Hydro (te wai)
  • Geothermal (ngā waiariki)

⛽ Non-Renewable

  • Coal (waro)
  • Oil (hinu)
  • Natural gas
  • Nuclear

Māori Energy Innovations

Iwi-Led Projects:

  • Ngāti Awa: Geothermal power plants in Bay of Plenty
  • Ngāti Whātua: Solar farm development
  • Ngāi Tahu: Wind farm investments
  • Tūaropaki Trust: Multiple renewable sources

These projects demonstrate kaitiakitanga - caring for Papatūānuku through clean energy!

Energy Audit Activity

Calculate Your Home's Energy Use:

  1. List all electrical appliances in your home
  2. Estimate daily usage hours for each
  3. Research wattage for each appliance
  4. Calculate: kWh = (Watts × Hours) ÷ 1000
  5. Total your household monthly energy use

Reflection: Where could you reduce energy use? How does this relate to kaitiakitanga?

Sustainable Living Challenge

2-Week Kaitiakitanga Challenge:

  • ✅ Turn off lights when leaving rooms
  • ✅ Unplug devices when not in use
  • ✅ Use natural light when possible
  • ✅ Shorter showers (save hot water)
  • ✅ Walk/bike instead of car for short trips
  • ✅ Line-dry clothes instead of dryer

Track your actions and estimate energy saved!

Assessment

  • Complete home energy audit with recommendations
  • Research one Māori energy initiative and present findings
  • Create an energy awareness campaign for school
  • Design a renewable energy solution for a local need
