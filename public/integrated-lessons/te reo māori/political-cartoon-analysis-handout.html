Analyzing Political Cartoons

What are Political Cartoons?

Political cartoons use visual imagery, humor, and satire to:

  • Comment on current events
  • Criticize politicians or policies
  • Make complex issues accessible
  • Influence public opinion

Cartoon Analysis Framework

Key Elements to Identify:

ElementQuestions
Subject What issue/event is being addressed?
Characters Who is depicted? How are they portrayed?
Symbols What objects represent bigger ideas?
Exaggeration What's exaggerated for effect?
Labels/Captions What text clarifies the message?
Message What is the cartoonist's opinion?

Common Visual Techniques

  • Caricature: Exaggerated features of people
  • Analogy: Comparing situation to something else
  • Irony: Saying opposite of what's meant
  • Juxtaposition: Placing contrasting elements together
  • Symbolism: Objects representing abstract ideas (e.g., scales = justice)

NZ Political Cartoon Context

Common NZ Symbols:

  • Kiwi bird: Represents New Zealand/New Zealanders
  • Silver fern: National identity
  • Beehive: Parliament building/government
  • Treaty document: Te Tiriti o Waitangi
  • Taniwha: Often represents Māori interests/rights

Practice Activity

Analyze a Political Cartoon:

  1. Find a political cartoon from NZ Herald, Stuff, or The Spinoff
  2. Identify all visual elements (characters, symbols, text)
  3. Determine the issue being addressed
  4. Explain the cartoonist's message/opinion
  5. Evaluate: Is it effective? Why/why not?
  6. Consider: How are Māori represented (if applicable)?

Create Your Own

Design a Political Cartoon:

Choose a current NZ issue:

  • Climate change action
  • Housing affordability
  • Education funding
  • Treaty settlements

Include:

  • Clear visual message
  • Appropriate symbols
  • Caption/labels if needed
  • Respectful portrayal (avoid stereotypes)
