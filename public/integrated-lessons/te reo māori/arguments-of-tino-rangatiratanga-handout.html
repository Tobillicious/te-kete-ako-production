Arguments for Tino Rangatiratanga

Understanding Māori Self-Determination

"Tino rangatiratanga"

"Absolute chieftainship" - Self-determination and sovereignty

What is Tino Rangatiratanga?

Tino rangatiratanga means Māori self-determination - the right to:

  • Control their own affairs
  • Make decisions about their resources
  • Govern themselves according to tikanga
  • Protect and develop their culture
  • Exercise authority over taonga

Treaty Basis

Article 2 of Te Tiriti:

"Ko te Kuini o Ingarani ka wakarite ka wakaae ki ngā Rangatira ki ngā hapū – ki ngā tangata katoa o Nu Tirani te tino rangatiratanga o ō rātou whenua o rātou kāinga me ō rātou taonga katoa."

"The Queen of England agrees and consents to the chiefs, the hapū, and all the people of New Zealand, the full chieftainship of their lands, their villages, and all their treasures."

Key Arguments

Arguments Supporting Tino Rangatiratanga:

1. Treaty Guarantee

Te Tiriti explicitly guaranteed Māori would retain tino rangatiratanga. This has never been voluntarily given up.

2. Indigenous Rights

UN Declaration on Rights of Indigenous Peoples (2007) affirms right to self-determination. NZ signed this.

3. Better Outcomes

When Māori control their own resources and programs (e.g., iwi-led initiatives), outcomes improve for Māori communities.

4. Cultural Survival

Self-determination enables Māori to protect and develop their culture, language, and tikanga on their own terms.

5. Justice & Reconciliation

After 180+ years of Crown breaches of Te Tiriti, honoring tino rangatiratanga is a path to justice.

Contemporary Examples

Tino Rangatiratanga in Practice:

  • Iwi Treaty settlements: Returning assets and control
  • Mātaitai reserves: Māori-managed fisheries
  • Kura kaupapa Māori: Māori-medium education
  • Whānau Ora: Self-determined health services
  • Māori wards: Representation in local government

Counter-Arguments to Consider

Critical thinking requires examining all perspectives:

  • Concerns about "one law for all"
  • Questions about defining who has tino rangatiratanga
  • Balancing self-determination with national unity
  • Implementation challenges

Note: Understanding opposing views strengthens your ability to argue your position.

Discussion Activity

Research arguments for both sides, then debate respectfully with evidence.

Assessment

  • Use at least 3 pieces of evidence from different sources
