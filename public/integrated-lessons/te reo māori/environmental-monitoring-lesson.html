üî¨ Scientific Water Quality Tests

1. pH Test (Acidity/Alkalinity)

Healthy freshwater: pH 6.5-8.5

Method: pH strips or digital meter. Too acidic (<6.5) can harm fish; too alkaline (>8.5) suggests pollution.

2. Temperature

Healthy: 10-18¬∞C (depends on season/location)

Method: Thermometer. Higher temps = less dissolved oxygen, stressing aquatic life.

3. Dissolved Oxygen

Healthy: >6 mg/L

Method: DO meter or test kit. Low oxygen = fish can't survive.

4. Turbidity (Cloudiness)

Healthy: <10 NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Units)

Method: Turbidity tube or meter. High turbidity = erosion/sediment.

  2. Traditional assessment: Observe tohu (fish, plants, water clarity, k≈çura)
