Sustainable Technology: Digital Kaitiakitanga

"Mō tātou, ā, mō kā uri ā muri ake nei"

"For us and our children after us"

Learning Objectives

  • Understand environmental impacts of technology
  • Apply kaitiakitanga principles to technology use
  • Identify sustainable technology practices
  • Design solutions for digital sustainability

The Hidden Cost of Technology

E-Waste Crisis

  • 50 million tonnes of e-waste generated globally each year
  • Only 20% is properly recycled
  • Smartphones contain rare earth minerals from mining
  • Data centers consume massive amounts of energy

Question: How does this align (or conflict) with kaitiakitanga?

Digital Kaitiakitanga Principles

Being Guardians of Digital Resources

  1. Reduce: Only buy technology you truly need
  2. Reuse: Repair and extend device lifespan
  3. Recycle: Properly dispose of old electronics
  4. Respect: Understand the true cost of devices
  5. Responsibility: Make conscious technology choices

Sustainable Tech Practices

Practical Actions

  • ✅ Use devices for longer (5+ years instead of 2)
  • ✅ Buy refurbished/second-hand when possible
  • ✅ Turn off devices when not in use
  • ✅ Delete unnecessary emails and files (reduces server energy)
  • ✅ Choose eco-certified products
  • ✅ Support Right to Repair movements

Māori Innovation in Sustainability

Examples of Māori-Led Solutions

  • Ngāti Whātua solar farm - renewable energy for iwi
  • Traditional resource management - rahui and seasonal harvesting
  • Circular economy practices - nothing wasted in traditional systems

Design Challenge

Create a Sustainable Tech Solution

Task: Design an initiative to reduce technology's environmental impact at your school

Consider:

  • What technology waste exists at your school?
  • How could kaitiakitanga principles guide a solution?
  • Who would benefit from your initiative?
  • How would you implement it?

Present your solution as a poster, video, or written proposal

Assessment

  1. Calculate your personal technology carbon footprint
  2. Create a 2-week sustainable technology action plan
  3. Research one Māori sustainability initiative and present findings
  4. Write a 300-word reflection on digital kaitiakitanga

Extension

  • Interview kaumātua about traditional resource management
  • Organize an e-waste collection drive at school
  • Research the Right to Repair movement
  • Create educational content for younger students
