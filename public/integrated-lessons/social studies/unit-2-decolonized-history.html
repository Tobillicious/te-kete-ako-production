üìö What is Decolonized History?

Definition:

Decolonized history means telling the story of the past while centering Indigenous voices, perspectives, and sources. It challenges colonial narratives that frame Indigenous peoples as passive recipients of "civilization" and instead recognizes them as active agents with sophisticated knowledge systems, governance, and cultures.

Key Principles of Decolonized History:

1. Center Indigenous Voices

When studying Indigenous history, Indigenous sources (oral histories, whakapapa, taonga) are PRIMARY sources, not supplementary

2. Question Colonial Framing

Challenge terms like "discovery," "settlement," and "civilization" that assume European superiority and erase Indigenous presence

3. Acknowledge Indigenous Agency

Recognize Indigenous peoples as active decision-makers, innovators, and resistors, not passive victims

4. Name Colonization's Impacts

Be explicit about land confiscation, language suppression, cultural destruction - don't soften or euphemize
