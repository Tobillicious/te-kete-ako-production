    At a school hangi, your MÄori friend's whÄnau has prepared traditional kai. They invite everyone to share the meal together. You notice there are certain ways people are serving and eating the food, and your friend explains this is about manaakitanga (hospitality).

    • How does sharing kai strengthen community connections?
    • What can you contribute to manaakitanga, even if you didn't cook?
    • How might this tikanga apply in other situations beyond food?

    Scenario 4: Caring for the Environment

    During a beach cleanup, a local kaumÄtua (elder) explains the concept of kaitiakitanga - being guardians of the environment. They share how their iwi has cared for this coast for hundreds of years and invite students to think about their role as kaitiaki.

    • How does kaitiakitanga differ from other approaches to environmental care?
