    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Nau Mai, Haere Mai

    Welcome!

    Nau mai, haere mai ki tēnei pukapuka mahi! Welcome to this workbook designed to help Year 8 ākonga strengthen their understanding of phoneme-grapheme relationships—the connection between sounds in spoken language and their written representations.

    Whai Mana

    Whaimana is the process of gaining power, authority or prestige, empowering oneself to achieve goals and aspirations.

    Whai Ora

    Whaiora refers to pursuing optimal health and well-being, both physically and mentally, in a balanced and harmonious way.

    Whai Ara

    Whaiara refers to the journey of life and the path one follows to achieve their goals and aspirations.

    The name Mangakōtukutuku comes from the native fuchsia tree (kōtukutuku) that was commonly found near waterways in our local area. Like the kōtukutuku that grows strong by the water, this workbook will help you grow your reading and writing skills.

    Wāhanga 1: Oropuare (Vowel Sounds)

    Whai Mana: Pursuing excellence through confidence

    Ngā Rōpū Oropuare: Vowel Teams

    In English, when two vowels join together they often make a single sound. We call these "vowel teams."

    For example, in the word "rain," the vowel team "ai" makes the long A sound.

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
