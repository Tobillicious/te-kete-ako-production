    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Learning Objectives

    By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

    • Understand the concept of digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
    • Configure and optimize Chromebooks for effective learning
    • Apply tikanga Māori principles to digital spaces
    • Identify accessibility features and use them appropriately
    • Create digital content that considers diverse user needs

    Cultural Context: Te Ao Māori and Digital Equity

    In Māori worldview, the concept of manaakitanga (hospitality, care for others) extends to digital spaces. When we consider digital equity, we must think about:

    • Whanaungatanga - Building relationships and connections through technology
    • Kaitiakitanga - Responsible guardianship of digital resources
    • Wairuatanga - Considering the spiritual and cultural wellbeing in digital interactions

    Whakaaro: Reflection Activity

    How might digital exclusion affect whānau, hapū, and iwi communities differently?

    • Access to te reo Māori digital resources
    • Connection with whānau in rural areas
    • Participation in cultural events through digital platforms
    • Access to education and employment opportunities

    Chromebook Optimization Guide

    Essential Settings

    1. Accessibility Features:
      • Enable ChromeVox (screen reader)
      • Configure high contrast mode
      • Set up dictation tools
    2. Productivity Tools:
      • Organize Google Drive with color coding
      • Set up offline access for key apps
      • Create multiple user profiles for different tasks
    3. Cultural Considerations:
      • Install te reo Māori keyboard
      • Bookmark Māori language resources
      • Set up culturally appropriate backgrounds

    Hands-on Task: Digital Manaakitanga

    Create a guide for younger students showing how to set up their Chromebook with accessibility and cultural features. Include:

    • Screenshots with annotations
    • Simple step-by-step instructions
    • Explanation of why each feature matters

    Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Emerging (1-2) Developing (3-4) Proficient (5-6)

    Digital Equity Understanding Can define digital equity Explains local impacts of digital divide Proposes solutions for equity challenges

    Extension Activities

    • Research and compare internet access statistics for different regions of Aotearoa
    • Interview kaumātua about technology changes they've experienced
    • Create a bilingual (English/te reo Māori) tech support video
    • Design an app concept that addresses a digital equity challenge

    Teacher Implementation Notes

    Prior Knowledge

    Students should have basic Chromebook navigation skills. Pre-assess familiarity with accessibility features.

    Differentiation

    • Support: Provide step-by-step visual guides for Chromebook setup
    • Extension: Challenge students to research indigenous digital equity initiatives globally

    Cultural Safety

    Ensure students understand proper protocols for representing tikanga Māori in digital spaces. Consult with your school's kaitiaki Māori if unsure.

    Timing

    This unit works well as a 4-6 week module. The hands-on task can be completed in 2-3 lessons.

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
