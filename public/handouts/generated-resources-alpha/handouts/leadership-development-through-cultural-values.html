🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

  • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
  • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
  • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
  • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

Understanding Māori Leadership Principles

Traditional Māori leadership is based on service to the people (manaakitanga), collective responsibility (kotahitanga), and wisdom (mātauranga). These principles remain highly relevant for modern leadership.

Key Māori Leadership Values:

  • Manaakitanga - Hospitality, kindness, and care for others
  • Whanaungatanga - Building and maintaining relationships
  • Kaitiakitanga - Guardianship and stewardship
  • Rangatiratanga - Self-determination and leadership
  • Wairuatanga - Spiritual dimension of leadership

Reflection Activity: Your Leadership Values

Rank these values from most to least important to your personal leadership style. Add any other values you consider important.

Discuss with a partner: How might these values influence decision-making in a school setting?

Case Study: Modern Māori Leaders

Research one of these contemporary Māori leaders and identify how they demonstrate traditional leadership values:

  • Dame Whina Cooper (Māori land rights)
  • Sir Tipene O'Regan (Ngāi Tahu leader)
  • Dr Lance O'Sullivan (health innovator)
  • Waimihi Hotere (arts and education)

Group Activity: Leadership Scenario

Your school is planning a cultural festival. In small groups, discuss:

  1. How would you apply manaakitanga in planning this event?
  2. What aspects of kaitiakitanga might be relevant?
  3. How would you ensure kotahitanga (unity) among participants?

Prepare a 2-minute presentation of your ideas to the class.

Developing Your Leadership Practice

Consider these questions for personal reflection:

  • Who are the leaders you admire? What qualities do they demonstrate?
  • How can you serve your community as a leader?
  • What knowledge and skills do you need to develop further?
  • How can you balance individual achievement with collective wellbeing?

Action Plan

Identify one leadership opportunity in your school or community. Create a plan showing:

  1. Your goal
  2. Which Māori leadership values you will apply
  3. Who you will work with
  4. How you will measure success
