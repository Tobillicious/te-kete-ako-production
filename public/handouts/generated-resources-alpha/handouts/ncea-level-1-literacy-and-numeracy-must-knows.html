    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    🛡️ Cultural Safety Considerations

    • Approach Māori content with respect and openness
    • Recognize students' diverse cultural backgrounds and experiences
    • Create space for Māori students to share their perspectives
    • Consult with whānau and local iwi for culturally sensitive topics

    Learning Objectives

    By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

    • Understand the core requirements for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards
    • Develop strategies for culturally responsive assessment preparation
    • Apply tikanga Māori (Māori customs) in learning and assessment contexts
    • Identify personal strengths and areas for development in essential skills
    • Create an individualised study plan for success

    Cultural Context: Te Ao Māori Perspectives

    In preparing for assessments, we acknowledge Te Tiriti o Waitangi principles by:

    • Valuing mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) alongside other knowledge systems
    • Creating inclusive learning environments (whanaungatanga)
    • Recognising diverse ways of demonstrating understanding (ako)
    • Maintaining mana (dignity) throughout the assessment process

    Literacy Essentials

    Key Skills Checklist

    • Read and understand a range of texts (minimum 2000 words)
    • Write clear, structured responses (minimum 500 words)
    • Use appropriate vocabulary and terminology
    • Demonstrate critical thinking in responses
    • Apply proofreading strategies

    Common Literacy Standards

    Standard Description Credits
    US 32405 Write formal texts for academic purposes 5
    US 32406 Read written texts to understand ideas and information 5

    Numeracy Essentials

    Key Skills Checklist

    • Solve problems using number operations
    • Interpret statistical information
    • Apply measurement concepts
    • Use geometric reasoning
    • Demonstrate algebraic thinking

    Common Numeracy Standards

    Standard Description Credits
    US 32401 Apply numeric reasoning in solving problems 5
    US 32402 Interpret statistical information for a purpose 5

    Extension Activities

    1. Pūrākau (Storytelling) Challenge

    Create a modern pūrākau that explains a mathematical concept or literacy skill using Māori cultural references.

    2. Bilingual Glossary

    Develop a glossary of 10 key terms in English and te reo Māori with definitions and examples.

    3. Peer Teaching

    Prepare and deliver a 5-minute lesson teaching one literacy or numeracy concept to a classmate using the tuakana-teina (mentoring) approach.

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
