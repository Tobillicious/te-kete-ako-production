    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    He Whakamōhiotanga | Introduction

    This resource explores STEM career pathways that align with Māori worldviews and values. We'll examine how traditional knowledge systems intersect with modern STEM fields and highlight successful Māori professionals in these areas.

    Starter Activity: Whakataukī Reflection

    "Ko te pae tawhiti, whāia kia tata. Ko te pae tata, whakamaua kia tīna."
    "Seek out distant horizons and cherish those you attain."

    In pairs, discuss what this whakataukī might mean in relation to career planning and STEM education.

    Te Horopaki Ahurea | Cultural Context

    Māori have always been innovators, scientists and technologists - from celestial navigation to sustainable resource management. Today, Māori professionals are leading in STEM fields while maintaining cultural connections.

    Dr. Ocean Mercier

    Field: Physics and Māori Science

    Iwi: Ngāti Porou

    Associate Professor at Victoria University who bridges indigenous knowledge and Western science, particularly in environmental management.

    Dr. Rangi Matamua

    Field: Astronomy and Māori Star Knowledge

    Iwi: Tūhoe

    Leading expert in Māori astronomy who has revitalized traditional star knowledge while working with modern astronomical techniques.

    Ngā Ara Mahi | Career Pathways

    Interactive Career Wheel

    Explore how different STEM fields connect with Māori values and knowledge systems:

    • Environmental Science: Kaitiakitanga (guardianship) of land and water
    • Data Science: Pattern recognition in whakapapa and traditional knowledge
    • Engineering: Traditional Māori technologies and modern infrastructure
    • Health Sciences: Rongoā Māori and Western medicine integration
    • Computer Science: Digital platforms for te reo revitalization

    Case Study: Māori Innovation in STEM

    Research one of these Māori-led STEM initiatives:

    • Māori Marsden-funded science research projects
    • Te Hiku Media's language technology projects
    • Māori-owned tech companies like AskYourTeam
    • Iwi-based environmental monitoring programs

    Te Whakamahere Ara | Pathway Planning

    Activity: Create Your STEM Whakapapa

    Develop a visual representation of your potential STEM career pathway that:

    1. Identifies your strengths and interests
    2. Shows how these connect to STEM fields
    3. Includes cultural dimensions important to you
    4. Outlines potential education/training steps
    5. Identifies role models or mentors

    This could be a flowchart, mindmap, or digital presentation.

    ``` ## Learning Objectives (NZC Aligned) 1. **Understand** how Māori knowledge systems intersect with STEM disciplines (NZC Science: Understanding about science) 2. **Explore** diverse STEM career pathways that incorporate Māori perspectives (NZC Careers: Career management) 3. **Analyze** case studies of successful Māori STEM professionals (NZC Social Sciences: Identity, culture and organisation) 4. **Develop** a personal career pathway plan that integrates STEM interests with cultural values (NZC Health: Personal identity and self-worth) 5. **Evaluate** the role of STEM in addressing challenges facing Māori communities (NZC Technology: Nature of technology) ## Cultural Context & Safety Notes - This lesson centers Māori worldviews in STEM education, validating indigenous knowledge systems - Ensure pronunciation of te reo Māori is correct (provide audio support if needed) - Be aware that some students may have limited exposure to STEM careers - maintain an inclusive approach - Highlight diverse pathways (university, apprenticeships, iwi initiatives) to avoid privileging any single route - Invite local Māori STEM professionals to share experiences where possible - Create space for students to explore how their own cultural identity connects with STEM ## Teacher Implementation Guide ### Preparation (1-2 days before) 1. Familiarize yourself with the Māori STEM professionals mentioned 2. Prepare printed copies of career profiles for students who prefer hardcopy 3. Test all digital resources and links 4. Identify local Māori STEM role models who could potentially visit ### Lesson Flow (Suggested 2-3 sessions) **Session 1: Introduction & Cultural Context** 1. Begin with karakia (optional) and whakataukī discussion (15 mins) 2. Present overview of Māori in STEM (20 mins) 3. Small group exploration of profile cards (25 mins) 4. Reflection: "What surprised you about these careers?" (10 mins) **Session 2: Career Exploration** 1. Interactive career wheel activity (20 mins) 2. Case study research in pairs (30 mins) 3. Group sharing of findings (20 mins) **Session 3: Pathway Planning** 1. Introduce STEM whakapapa activity (10 mins) 2. Individual work time with teacher support (40 mins) 3. Optional sharing of pathways (20 mins) 4. Closing reflection and next steps (10 mins) ## Assessment Suggestions ### Formative Assessment: - Participation in discussions and activities - Quality of contributions to case study research - Depth of reflection in whakataukī discussion ### Summative Assessment (Rubric): | Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) | |----------|--------------|----------------|----------------|---------------| | Cultural Connection | Clearly integrates Māori perspectives throughout | Shows good awareness of cultural dimensions | Some cultural references present | Limited cultural connection | | STEM Understanding | Demonstrates deep understanding of STEM fields | Shows good grasp of STEM concepts | Basic understanding evident | Limited STEM knowledge | | Pathway Development | Detailed, realistic plan with multiple steps | Clear plan with logical progression | Some planning evident | Minimal planning | | Presentation Quality | Highly engaging, creative presentation | Clear, well-organized presentation | Basic presentation | Incomplete presentation | ## Extension Activities 1. **Māori STEM Innovators Research Project** - Research a Māori innovator in STEM (historical or contemporary) - Create a multimedia presentation highlighting their contributions - Analyze how they've blended traditional and modern knowledge 2. **STEM Solution for Local Challenge** - Identify a challenge facing your local Māori community - Design a STEM-based solution that incorporates mātauranga Māori - Present as a proposal to relevant stakeholders 3. **Virtual Mentorship Program** - Connect with Māori STEM professionals via video conference - Prepare interview questions about their career journey - Create a summary report of insights gained ## Adaptations for Diverse Learners ### For Students Needing Additional Support: - Provide pre-selected, simplified case studies - Offer template for career pathway planning - Allow verbal rather than written responses where appropriate - Pair with peer mentors during activities ### For Advanced Learners: - Challenge to research tertiary pathways for specific careers - Develop a comparative analysis of Western and Māori approaches to a STEM field - Create a resource promoting STEM to younger Māori students ### For Distance Learners: - Convert interactive activities to digital formats (e.g., online mindmaps) - Set up virtual small group discussions - Provide video recordings of profile information ### For Students with Limited Digital Access: - Print all digital resources in advance - Offer offline activity alternatives - Provide paper-based templates for all tasks
