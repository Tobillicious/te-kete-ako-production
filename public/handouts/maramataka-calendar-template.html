Māramataka Calendar Template

Track moon phases and their traditional associations. Record observations or data over 4 weeks.

How to Use

  1. Fill in dates for the current lunar month (start with new moon if possible).
  2. Observe the moon each night; sketch the phase in the calendar square.
  3. Record the traditional māramataka name (use online resource or teacher guide).
  4. Note observations: weather, fishing/planting outcomes, personal energy, tides, etc.
  5. After 4 weeks, analyse: Do you see patterns? Do traditional predictions match your observations?

Statistical Thinking Connection

Māramataka practitioners were doing statistics: collecting observations, identifying patterns, making predictions, testing accuracy. This is the PPDAC cycle (Problem, Plan, Data, Analysis, Conclusion) centuries before it was formalized in textbooks!
