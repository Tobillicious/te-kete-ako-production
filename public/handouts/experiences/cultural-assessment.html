    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    🌿 Cultural Assessment Framework

    Authentic, culturally responsive assessment approaches for Te Ao Māori integration

    Mātauranga Māori Assessment Principles

    🌱 Tino Rangatiratanga

    Self-determination in learning - students have agency in demonstrating their knowledge

    🔗 Whakapapa

    Assessment recognizes relationships between concepts, cultural knowledge, and personal connections

    🤝 Manaakitanga

    Assessment processes show care, respect, and hospitality toward learners

    🌿 Kaitiakitanga

    Guardianship of cultural knowledge - ensuring respectful use and representation

    🛡️ Cultural Safety Considerations

    • Approach Māori content with respect and openness
    • Recognize students' diverse cultural backgrounds and experiences
    • Create space for Māori students to share their perspectives
    • Consult with whānau and local iwi for culturally sensitive topics

    Culturally Responsive Assessment Methods

    Oral Traditions & Storytelling

    • Student-led presentations incorporating pūrākau (stories)
    • Oral history projects connecting with whānau and community
    • Performance assessments through whakataukī interpretation

    Creative Expression

    • Artistic representations of cultural concepts
    • Multimedia projects combining traditional and digital media
    • Collaborative works reflecting collective knowledge creation

    Community Connection

    • Projects that engage with local iwi and communities
    • Assessment by cultural experts and kaumātua
    • Real-world applications benefiting the community

    Cultural Assessment Rubric

    Cultural Authenticity

    Demonstrates deep understanding with authentic cultural integration
    Shows good cultural awareness with appropriate application
    Basic cultural understanding with some integration
    Limited cultural awareness or inappropriate application

    📊 Assessment Tools | Ngā Taputapu Aromatawai

    ✅ Formative Assessment (During Learning)

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    Quick Check: Thumbs up/sideways/down for understanding

    🎯 Self-Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Not Yet Getting There Got It!
    I understand the key concepts ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can explain it to others ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can apply it to new situations ☐ ☐ ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
