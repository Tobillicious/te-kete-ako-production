RÄhui: Traditional conservation practice

RÄhui = temporary ban on harvesting from an area. This allows populations to recover. Still used today by iwi!

Example: If kaimoana (seafood) numbers drop in an area, iwi places rÄhui for 1-2 years. No fishing allowed. Population recovers. RÄhui lifted. Sustainable forever!
Maramataka: Lunar calendar for best harvest times

MÄori used moon phases to know WHEN to fish, plant, harvest. Certain days = abundant fish. Other days = rest. Science confirms this works!
