🌿 Activity 2: Māori Sustainable Harvesting (20 minutes)

Rāhui: Traditional conservation practice

Rāhui = temporary ban on harvesting from an area. This allows populations to recover. Still used today by iwi!

Example: If kaimoana (seafood) numbers drop in an area, iwi places rāhui for 1-2 years. No fishing allowed. Population recovers. Rāhui lifted. Sustainable forever!
Maramataka: Lunar calendar for best harvest times

Māori used moon phases to know WHEN to fish, plant, harvest. Certain days = abundant fish. Other days = rest. Science confirms this works!

🎣 Activity 3: Kaitiakitanga in Action (20 minutes)

Scenario: You're a Kaitiaki (Guardian) of a river

Your iwi has fished this river for 400 years. Recently, you notice:

  • Tuna (eel) numbers down 60%
  • Smaller fish than usual
  • Some kaimoana species disappeared
  • Water quality declining (dairy farm upstream)
Your Task: Create a Kaitiakitanga Plan

Answer these questions to develop your management plan:

1. Should you place a rāhui? Why or why not?

2. If you use rāhui, how long should it last? (Explain using food chain science)

3. How would you address the upstream dairy farm issue?

💭 Reflection Questions

  1. How does understanding food chains help with conservation?
  2. Why is kaitiakitanga (guardianship) important for sustainability?
  3. Can modern science and mātauranga Māori work together? How?

🌟 Extension Challenge

Research a REAL rāhui placed by a NZ iwi in the last 5 years. What was the issue? How long was the rāhui? Did it work? Present findings to class (2 minutes).

📋 Teacher Notes

  • Duration: 60 minutes (can extend with research)
  • Materials: Colored pencils, NZ species reference sheets, internet access for research
  • Assessment: Check food chain accuracy (4 levels, correct energy flow, NZ species)
  • Cultural note: Rāhui is still actively used - respect as living practice, not just history
  • Extension: Visit local marae/iwi to hear about kaitiakitanga in action
