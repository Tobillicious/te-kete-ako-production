    He Whakamārama | Cultural Context

    Māori games (tākaro) were traditionally used to develop physical skills, mental agility, and social cohesion. They were often connected to stories, values, and the natural world. When engaging with these activities, we acknowledge the cultural heritage and wisdom embedded within them.

    Ngā Tākaro | The Games

    Mu Tōrere

    ⭐⭐⭐

    A strategic board game played on an 8-pointed star. Players move their pieces (kēmu) to block opponents.

    Algebraic Focus:

    • Pattern recognition
    • Strategic planning
    • Predicting opponent moves

    Whai (String Games)

    ⭐⭐

    Creating patterns with string that tell stories and represent natural forms.

    Algebraic Focus:

    • Geometric patterns
    • Sequences
    • Transformations

    Tī Rākau (Stick Games)

    ⭐⭐⭐

    Rhythmic passing of sticks while maintaining patterns and sequences.

    Algebraic Focus:

    • Sequences
    • Timing patterns
    • Coordination patterns

    Te Mahi Tuatahi | Activity 1: Mu Tōrere Strategy Analysis

    Game Setup

    Mu Tōrere is played on an 8-pointed star with a center point. Each player has 4 pieces placed on opposite sides of the star.

    Strategic Thinking Challenge

    If Player A has a winning strategy that involves moving pieces in a specific sequence, and Player B responds with counter-moves:

    Let x = number of moves until Player A can force a win

    Let y = number of defensive moves Player B can make

    If x = 2y - 3, and Player B has 5 defensive moves available, how many moves until Player A wins?

    Algebraic Representation

    Create algebraic expressions to represent:

    • The total number of possible moves from any position
    • The relationship between pieces on the board and available moves
    • Predicting the outcome based on current piece positions

    Te Mahi Tuarua | Activity 2: Whai Pattern Sequences

    Pattern Analysis

    Whai (string games) create geometric patterns through specific sequences of moves.

    Pattern Name Number of Moves Mathematical Relationship
    Whai (Simple Diamond) 4 M = 2n (where n = number of diamonds)
    Matariki (Seven Sisters) 7 M = n + (n-1) (where n = number of stars)
    Koru (Spiral) 6 M = 3n - 3 (where n = spiral rotations)

    Te Mahi Tuatoru | Activity 3: Tī Rākau Sequences

    Rhythm and Timing Patterns

    Tī Rākau involves creating and maintaining complex sequences of stick movements.

    Example Sequence Analysis

    Consider a basic passing pattern: Right-Left-Catch-Repeat

    If we assign values: R=1, L=2, C=3, the sequence becomes: 1, 2, 3, 1, 2, 3...

    This creates a repeating pattern with a period of 3.

    Sequence Challenge

    Create a sequence with the following properties:

    • Contains at least 5 different movements
    • Repeats every 8 movements
    • Can be represented by an algebraic expression
