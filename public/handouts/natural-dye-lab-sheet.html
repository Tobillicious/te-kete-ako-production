Natural Dye Lab Investigation

Explore the chemistry of plant-based dyes and pH colour changes.

Aim

To extract natural dyes from plant materials and investigate how pH affects colour.

Materials

  • Plant material: onion skins (yellow), red cabbage, or tƒÅnekaha bark (with permission)
  • White cotton fabric strips (pre-washed)
  • Water (500 mL per dye bath)
  • Beakers, hot plate or kettle, stirring rod
  • pH solutions: vinegar (acid), baking soda solution (base), plain water (control)
  • pH paper

Method

  1. Karakia: Recite short karakia acknowledging the plant's mauri and our responsibility as kaitiaki.
  2. Prepare dye bath: Simmer plant material in 500 mL water for 20-30 min until water is deeply coloured. Strain out solids.
  3. Divide dye: Pour into 3 beakers (150 mL each).
  4. Adjust pH:
    • Beaker A: Add 20 mL vinegar (acidic).
    • Beaker B: Keep as control (neutral).
    • Beaker C: Add 1 tsp baking soda (basic).
  5. Test pH: Use pH paper; record values.
  6. Dye fabric: Place one fabric strip in each beaker. Soak 10 min, stirring occasionally.
  7. Rinse & dry: Remove fabric, rinse in cold water, hang to dry.
  8. Compare: Observe colour differences. Take photos.

Analysis Questions

  1. How did pH affect the dye colour? Explain using the term "anthocyanin" (plant pigment that changes with pH).
  2. Why do traditional dyers sometimes add wood ash (alkaline) or iron-rich mud (acidic mordant) to dye baths?

MƒÅtauranga Connection

Traditional dyers observed that different water sources, woods used for fires, and seasonal plant harvests produced colour variations. They were manipulating pH, mineral content, and pigment concentration through deep empirical knowledge. Modern chemistry provides the molecular explanation, but the practice was already sophisticated science.

Extension: Mordant Experiment

Repeat the experiment using iron mordant (rusty nails in vinegar solution) or alum (potassium aluminium sulfate). Compare colour fastness after 1 week in sunlight. Research why metal ions bond pigments to fibres.
