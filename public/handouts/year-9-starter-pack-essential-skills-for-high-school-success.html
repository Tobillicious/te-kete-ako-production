Te Ao MƒÅori: Cultural Context

NgƒÅ Tikanga o te Kura Tuarua (Secondary School Customs)

In Te Ao MƒÅori, transitions are important wa (times) that require manaakitanga (care and support). Here are ways to honor tikanga in your high school journey:

  • Whakawhanaungatanga: Building relationships is key. Introduce yourself properly with your pepeha when meeting new kaiako.
  • Mana motuhake: Respect others' space and belongings in shared areas like the whare kai (dining area).
  • Kaitiakitanga: Care for school resources and environments as kaitiaki (guardians).
  • Whanaungatanga: Support your hoa (friends) who may be finding the transition challenging.

Essential Skills for High School Success

1. Time Management

High school requires managing multiple subjects and deadlines. Try this:

  • Use your school diary every day
  • Break big tasks into smaller steps
  • Set reminders for important dates
  • Create a weekly study timetable

Te reo tip: "He aha te wƒÅ?" means "What's the time?" - important for being punctual!

2. Organization

Stay on top of your materials with these strategies:

  • Color-code subjects in your folder
  • Pack your bag the night before
  • Keep a clean workspace at home
  • Use digital tools like Google Classroom

MƒÅori perspective: The concept of raupapa (order) helps maintain balance in life.

3. Navigating School

Practice these before school starts:

  • Walk your timetable route between classes
  • Locate key areas: library, whare paku (toilets), office
  • Learn teacher names and room numbers
  • Identify safe spaces and support people
