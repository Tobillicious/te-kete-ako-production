    Cultural Context

    This activity examines historical Treaty of Waitangi settlement data through statistical analysis. We acknowledge the complex history and ongoing impacts of Treaty breaches. Approach this topic with respect for the mana of iwi and the significance of these settlements in addressing historical injustices.

    Learning Objectives

    • Analyze and interpret statistical data relating to Treaty settlements (AS91264, AS91581)
    • Apply appropriate statistical methods to real-world data (AS91267, AS91584)
    • Understand the historical context of Treaty settlements in Aotearoa
    • Communicate findings about statistical relationships in context

    Treaty Settlement Data Set

    The following table shows selected Treaty settlement data from 1994-2020:

    Iwi Year Settled Financial Redress ($m) Cultural Redress Items Area (ha)
    Ngāi Tahu 1998 170 36 35,000
    Waikato-Tainui 1995 170 25 16,000
    Ngāti Awa 2003 42.3 19 4,500
    Ngāti Tūwharetoa 2018 35 14 3,000
    Ngāti Manawa 2012 11.5 8 1,200
    Ngāti Pāhauwera 2011 20 12 2,800

    Interactive Analysis

    Use this data to complete the following statistical investigations:

    1. Calculate measures of center (mean, median) for financial redress amounts
    2. Create a scatter plot showing relationship between settlement year and financial redress
    3. Calculate correlation coefficients for different variable pairs
    4. Identify any outliers in the data set and suggest possible reasons

    Historical Context

    The Treaty settlement process attempts to address historical breaches of Te Tiriti o Waitangi. Settlements typically include:

    • Financial redress (monetary compensation)
    • Cultural redress (return of significant sites, place name changes)
    • Relationship agreements (ongoing partnerships)

    Statistical Investigation

    Part A: Descriptive Statistics

    Calculate the following for financial redress amounts:

    • Mean, median, mode
    • Range, interquartile range
    • Standard deviation

    Part B: Bivariate Analysis

    Investigate relationships between:

    1. Settlement year and financial redress amount
    2. Area and financial redress amount
    3. Cultural redress items and financial redress amount

    For each relationship:

    • Create appropriate graphs
    • Calculate correlation coefficients
    • Write statements about the strength and direction of relationships

    Assessment Suggestions

    Students could be assessed on:

    Criteria Achieved Merit Excellence
    Statistical calculations Correct calculations Appropriate method selection Insightful interpretation
    Graphical representations Basic graphs created Appropriate graph choices Sophisticated visual analysis
    Contextual understanding Basic facts included Connects stats to context Insightful cultural analysis

    Extension Activities

    Advanced Analysis:

    1. Research and add 3 more iwi settlements to the dataset
    2. Create a time series analysis of settlement amounts over time
    3. Compare per-capita settlement amounts using iwi population data
    4. Investigate inflation-adjusted values of settlements

    Critical Thinking:

    • What limitations does this quantitative data have in telling the full story of Treaty settlements?
    • How might non-quantifiable aspects of settlements (e.g., spiritual connections) be represented?
    • Compare the statistical approach with Māori data sovereignty principles

    Adaptations for Diverse Learners

    Learner Need Adaptation
    Struggling with calculations Provide partially completed calculations or spreadsheet templates
    Visual learners Include graph templates and color-coding
    Extension students Add additional variables like iwi population or regional GDP
    Te reo Māori learners Provide bilingual glossary of statistical terms

    Teacher Implementation Guide

    Before the lesson:

    1. Familiarize yourself with Treaty settlement history (see Te Ara Encyclopedia)
    2. Prepare digital copies of the data for students to analyze
    3. Set up spreadsheet software or statistical packages

    Lesson sequence:

    1. Begin with karakia (optional) and discussion of Treaty context (20 mins)
    2. Introduce the dataset and statistical concepts (15 mins)
    3. Guided practice with sample calculations (20 mins)
    4. Independent/group investigation (40 mins)
    5. Discussion of findings and cultural context (25 mins)

    Cultural safety notes:

    • Emphasize that settlements represent partial redress, not full compensation
    • Be aware that some students may have personal connections to settlements
    • Use respectful language when discussing historical injustices
    • Consider inviting a local kaumātua or Treaty educator to speak
