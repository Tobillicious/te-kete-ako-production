    ğŸ“š Pattern Information

    Poutama (Stairway to Heaven)

    Represents the ascent of TÄwhaki to the heavens and the pursuit of knowledge. Mathematically demonstrates geometric progression and stepwise growth.

    Mathematical Properties:
    • Geometric progression
    • Stepwise growth pattern
    • Triangular number sequence

    Roimata Toroa (Tears of the Albatross)

    Symbolizes sadness, loss, and remembrance. Mathematically shows alternating patterns and reflective symmetry.

    Mathematical Properties:
    • Alternating patterns
    • Reflective symmetry
    • Binary sequences

    Patiki (Flounder)

    Represents abundance and provision. Mathematically demonstrates diamond shapes and rotational symmetry.

    Mathematical Properties:
    • Diamond geometry
    • Rotational symmetry
    • Tessellation patterns

    ğŸ¯ Student Activities

    Activity 1: Pattern Recognition

    Generate different patterns and identify the mathematical rules governing each design. Record your findings in a table.

    Learning Outcomes:
    • Identify mathematical patterns
    • Describe pattern rules
    • Predict pattern continuation

    Activity 2: Transformation Exploration

    Create a pattern and apply different transformations (reflection, rotation, translation). Analyze how the mathematical properties change.

    Learning Outcomes:
    • Apply geometric transformations
    • Analyze transformation effects
    • Understand symmetry properties

    Activity 3: Pattern Creation

    Design your own tukutuku pattern using mathematical principles. Write a mathematical description of your pattern.

    Learning Outcomes:
    • Create original patterns
    • Apply mathematical thinking
    • Communicate mathematical ideas
