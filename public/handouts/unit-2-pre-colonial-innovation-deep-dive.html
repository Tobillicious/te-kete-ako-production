    When we hear "innovation," what comes to mind? Silicon Valley? The Industrial Revolution? These colonial definitions of innovation ignore thousands of years of sophisticated MÄori technological development that was sustainable, culturally integrated, and remarkably effective. This deep dive challenges you to expand your understanding of what innovation really means.

    âœ… Historical Reality

    MÄori didn't need wheels because they developed superior technologies for their environment:

    • Rollers and sleds were used for moving heavy objects like waka and whare

    • Sustainable materials meant tools could always be replaced
    • Tool-making was integrated with spiritual and cultural practices
