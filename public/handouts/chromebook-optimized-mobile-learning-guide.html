    Teacher Implementation Guide

    Preparing for the Unit

    Familiarize yourself with Chromebook accessibility features. Survey students about their home internet access and digital device availability to understand potential equity issues.

    Week 1: Introduction to Digital Equity

    Begin with a class discussion about what "digital equity" means. Use local examples and statistics about internet access in Aotearoa. Introduce key MÄori concepts like whanaungatanga (relationships) in digital spaces.

    Week 2: Chromebook Accessibility Features

    Guide students through exploring Chromebook accessibility settings. Have them experiment with features like screen readers, magnification, high contrast mode, and speech-to-text.

    Week 3: Creating Accessible Content

    Teach students how to create digital content that is accessible to all. Focus on document structure, alt-text for images, captioning videos, and color contrast considerations.

    Week 4: Digital Equity Project

    Students work in groups to identify a digital equity issue in their community and propose a solution using Chromebook tools. Encourage them to consider cultural perspectives in their solutions.

    Adaptations for Different Learning Needs

    Visual Impairments

    Provide audio descriptions, use high contrast materials, and ensure all digital content is compatible with screen readers.

    Hearing Impairments

    Use captions for all videos, provide written instructions, and utilize visual aids.

    Motor Difficulties

    Offer alternative input methods like voice commands, and ensure clickable elements are large enough.

    Limited English Proficiency

    Use translation tools, provide bilingual resources, and incorporate visual supports.

    Limited Digital Access

    Provide printed alternatives, offline resources, and extended time for digital tasks.

    Neurodiversity

    Offer flexible pacing, minimize distractions, and provide clear, structured instructions.
