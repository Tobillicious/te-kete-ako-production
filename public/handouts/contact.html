    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

      Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

    🧺 How do I save resources to My Kete?

    Create a free account, then look for the bookmark (🔖) icons throughout the site. Click to save any resource to your personal collection for easy access later.

    📋 Are resources aligned to NZ Curriculum?

    Yes! Every resource includes clear Achievement Objective links and curriculum level indicators. Look for the "Curriculum Links" section on each resource page.

    💰 Is Te Kete Ako free to use?

    Yes! All educational resources are completely free for educators in New Zealand. Our mission is to support quality education for all akonga (students).

    🌿 How do you ensure cultural accuracy?

    We work with cultural advisors and follow established cultural protocols. All Te Ao Māori content is reviewed by qualified cultural consultants before publication.

    🔍 How does the AI search work?

    Our GraphRAG system intelligently connects resources based on topics, skills, and curriculum objectives. Just describe what you're looking for and it will find relevant materials across our entire collection.

    ⏰ Response Times

    We aim to respond to all inquiries within 24-48 hours during school term time. Technical issues are prioritized and typically resolved within 24 hours.
