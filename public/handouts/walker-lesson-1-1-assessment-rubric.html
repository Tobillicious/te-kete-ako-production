    Lesson 1.1: Who was Ranginui Walker?

    Year 10 Social Studies | NZ Curriculum Level 5

    Assessment Task: Reflection on Whaimana (Integrity)

    Criteria Beginning (1) Developing (2) Proficient (3) Advanced (4)
    Understanding of Walker's Life Identifies 1 role or event from Walker's life with minimal detail Identifies 2 roles or events with some detail and accuracy Identifies 3+ roles with detailed, accurate information from the text Provides comprehensive understanding of Walker's multiple roles with nuanced connections between them
    Connection to Whaimana (Integrity) Makes a basic statement about integrity but connection is unclear Makes clear connection between one example and integrity Makes strong connections between 2+ examples and demonstrates understanding of how they show integrity Makes sophisticated connections, explains WHY these actions required integrity, discusses broader implications
    Use of Evidence Little to no specific evidence from the text Uses 1 specific example from the text Uses 2+ specific, relevant examples with clear explanations Uses 3+ detailed examples, explains their significance, and connects them to broader themes
    Vocabulary & Language Basic vocabulary, some errors in key terms Uses some key vocabulary correctly (activist, narrative) Uses key vocabulary accurately including Māori terms (Whaimana, tino rangatiratanga) Uses sophisticated vocabulary fluently, integrates Māori and English terms naturally, shows deep understanding
    Writing Quality Unclear organization, multiple errors that impede understanding Basic paragraph structure, some errors but meaning is clear Well-organized paragraph with clear topic sentence and supporting details, minor errors Sophisticated, engaging writing with excellent organization, varied sentence structure, virtually error-free

    🎯 Marking Guide

    • 5 criteria × 4 points each = 20 points total
    • Grading Scale:
      • 18-20 points = Excellence
      • 15-17 points = Merit
      • 10-14 points = Achieved
      • Below 10 = Not Yet Achieved (provide feedback and opportunity to resubmit)

    💡 Feedback Sentence Starters for Teachers

    For students needing support:

    • "You've made a good start by mentioning... Try adding more specific examples from the text about..."
    • "To strengthen your connection to Whaimana, explain WHY this action showed integrity..."
    • "Consider including the Māori vocabulary we learned: tino rangatiratanga, Ngā Tamatoa..."

    For proficient students:

    • "Excellent use of evidence! To reach advanced, try explaining the broader significance of..."
    • "Strong connection to Whaimana. Consider also discussing how his integrity inspired others..."
