📝 Persuasive Letter Structure | Te Hanganga

Your name, school address, today's date. Then recipient's name, title, organization, address.

"Dear Minister Shaw," or "Tēnā koe [Name]," - always formal and respectful.

Hook: Grab attention with powerful fact, question, or quote.

"NZ glaciers have shrunk by 34% since 1977. As a Year 9 student who will inherit this crisis, I write to urge immediate action."

Use PEEL structure for each paragraph:

    Point - State your argument
  • Evidence - Use scientific data or expert quotes
  • Explanation - Explain why this matters
  • Link - Connect to kaitiakitanga or next point

Specific, realistic request. "I urge you to commit $X to renewable energy" or "Please implement school-wide composting by Term 3."

Reinforce urgency and hope. Connect to whakataukī or future vision. End strong!

"Sincerely," "Ngā mihi," or "With hope for the future," + your name

💡 Persuasive Techniques Toolkit

Technique What It Does Climate Example
Ethos (Credibility) Build trust with audience "As students who've studied NZ ecosystems for 8 weeks..."
Pathos (Emotion) Connect to feelings "Imagine your mokopuna asking, 'Why didn't you act?'"
Logos (Logic) Use facts and reasoning "NZ emissions have risen 24% since 1990 (MfE data)..."
Rhetorical Questions Make reader reflect "Can we call ourselves kaitiaki if we ignore this crisis?"
Rule of Three Emphasize through repetition "For our land. For our oceans. For our future."
Inclusive Language Build connection ("we") "Together, we can transition to 100% renewable energy..."

🔬 Evidence Bank | Ngā Taunakitanga

Use these verified NZ climate facts in your letter:

  • NZ's greenhouse gas emissions increased 24% between 1990-2019 (MfE)
  • Agriculture accounts for 48% of NZ's emissions (highest in OECD)
  • Sea level rise threatens 72,000 NZ homes by 2100 (NIWA)
  • Native species like kea, kākāpō face extinction if warming exceeds 1.5°C

  • Lake Onslow battery project could store 100% renewable energy
  • NZ has 85% renewable electricity already (can reach 100%!)
  • Native forest restoration sequesters 8-12 tonnes CO2/hectare/year
  • Māori-led geothermal projects (Ngāti Awa, Tūaropaki) show sustainable pathways

  • Traditional Māori worldview: Humans are part of nature, not separate
  • Kaitiakitanga = guardianship responsibility for Papatūānuku (Earth Mother)
  • Intergenerational thinking: What world do we leave our mokopuna (grandchildren)?
  • Māori climate solutions honor traditional knowledge + modern science

✍️ Writing Scaffold | Te Tūāpapa Tuhituhi

Sentence starters:

  • "As a young kaitiaki of Aotearoa, I write to you about..."
  • "Climate change is no longer a distant threat—it's affecting NZ now, and..."
  • "I am writing to urge immediate action on climate change because..."

Structure:

  • Point: "NZ's emissions are rising when they should be falling."
  • Evidence: "Ministry data shows 24% increase since 1990..."
  • Explanation: "This threatens our unique ecosystems and coastal communities because..."
  • Link: "The solution lies in renewable energy transformation."

Structure:

  • Point: "NZ can lead the world in renewable energy."
  • Evidence: "We already have 85% renewable electricity..."
  • Explanation: "Māori-led projects like Ngāti Awa geothermal show how..."
  • Link: "This aligns with kaitiakitanga values and economic benefits."

Be specific! Examples:

  • "I urge you to commit $500M to the Lake Onslow battery project in the 2026 budget."
  • "Please mandate solar panels on all new school buildings by 2027."
  • "Support Māori-led renewable energy projects with $50M funding."

Ending options:

  • "Future generations are counting on leaders like you to act with courage."
  • "As the whakataukī says: 'Kia whakatōmuri te haere whakamua' - learn from the past to walk wisely into the future."
  • "Our whenua (land), our moana (ocean), our people - all depend on the decisions you make today."
