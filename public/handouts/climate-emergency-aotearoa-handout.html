🌿 Te Ao Māori Perspective: Kaitiakitanga

Kaitiakitanga (guardianship) is a central concept in Māori culture - the responsibility to care for and protect the environment for future generations.

Traditional Environmental Stewardship

Māori have practiced sustainable environmental management for over 700 years in Aotearoa. Key principles include:

  • Whakapapa - Genealogical connections to land and sea
  • Mauri - Life force of natural systems
  • Tapu and noa - Sacred protection of resources
  • Rāhui - Temporary restrictions to allow regeneration

💬 Key Discussion Points

Understanding Climate Emergency

  1. What does it mean for a government to declare a "climate emergency"?
  2. What specific actions should follow such a declaration?
  3. How does this compare to other national emergencies?

Youth Perspectives

  1. What are the main concerns expressed by young people in the video?
  2. Why do you think youth are particularly vocal about climate action?
  3. What emotions do the speakers convey? Why?

Kaitiakitanga & Climate Action

  1. How does kaitiakitanga relate to modern climate action?
  2. What traditional Māori practices support environmental sustainability?
  3. How can we apply these principles to contemporary climate challenges?

Critical Thinking & Solutions

  1. What actions do the speakers believe are necessary?
  2. Evaluate: Are these actions realistic? Why or why not?
  3. What role can young people play in climate action in Aotearoa?

🌟 Extension Activities

Research Project

Research a traditional Māori environmental practice (e.g., rāhui, seasonal harvesting). Present how it could be applied to modern climate challenges.

Action Plan

Create a climate action plan for your school incorporating both modern science and mātauranga Māori principles.

Debate

Debate: "Traditional knowledge systems are as important as modern science in addressing climate change." Use evidence from both perspectives.

👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

Cultural Sensitivity

When discussing climate change and kaitiakitanga, be mindful that:

  • Different iwi may have different perspectives and practices
  • Invite local kaumātua or cultural advisors to share their knowledge
  • Acknowledge that Māori communities are disproportionately affected by climate change
  • Celebrate Māori leadership in environmental protection

Differentiation

Support: Provide sentence starters, vocabulary lists, graphic organizers

Extension: Research local iwi environmental initiatives, interview community leaders
