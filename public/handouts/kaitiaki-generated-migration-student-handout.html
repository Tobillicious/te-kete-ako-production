🗣️ Kupu Hou - New Vocabulary

Waka - /wah-kah/ - Traditional ocean-going canoe

Hawaiki - /hah-why-kee/ - Spiritual homeland

Whakapapa - /fah-kah-pah-pah/ - Genealogy, connections

Tangata whenua - People of the land

Iwi - /ee-wee/ - Tribe, extended kinship group

Kōrero - /koh-reh-roh/ - Story, talk, discussion

Tohunga - /toh-hung-ah/ - Expert, spiritual leader

Mauri - /mah-oo-ree/ - Life force, essence

Matariki - /mah-tah-ree-kee/ - Seven Sisters star cluster

Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa - The Pacific Ocean

🌙 Cultural Wisdom

Navigation wasn't just about getting from A to B. It was about:

  • Spiritual connection to Tangaroa (god of the sea)
  • Reading the mauri (life force) of the ocean
  • Understanding whakapapa between stars, sea, and people
  • Carrying cultural knowledge safely to new lands

Star Navigation Challenge:

  1. If Matariki rises in the east, which direction should you sail to reach Aotearoa from Hawaiki?
    2. Circle: NORTH / SOUTH / EAST / WEST / SOUTHEAST
  2. What other natural signs might help navigators? (Think: birds, waves, clouds)

🎭 Activity 3: Create Your Migration Pūrākau

Write a short story (150-200 words) from the perspective of someone on the great migration. Choose your role:

🧭 Navigator

You read the stars and guide the waka

Focus on: skills, responsibility, connection to nature

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Member

You're leaving everything familiar behind

Focus on: emotions, hopes, fears, memories

Remember: This is creative writing based on historical understanding. Real iwi stories belong to specific whānau and should be told by them.

My Migration Pūrākau:

Write your story here... Start with describing who you are and why you're on this journey.

🏠 Extension: Connect to Your Whānau

Family Migration Questions to Ask at Home:

  1. What is our iwi connection? (If Māori)
  2. When did our family come to New Zealand?
  3. What stories do we have about our ancestors' journeys?
  4. What cultural practices did our family bring with them?
  5. How do our family stories compare to the waka traditions?

Important: Every family has migration stories - whether from Pacific Islands, Europe, Asia, or within Aotearoa. All stories matter and deserve respect.
