    Name: _________________ Date: __________

    🏛️ Cultural Protocol Note

    The words in this vocabulary sheet are part of sacred navigational knowledge passed down through generations of Polynesian and Māori navigators. Please use these terms with respect and understanding of their cultural significance.

    Learning te reo Māori: Take time to practice pronunciation. Listen to audio resources when available and ask for help from te reo speakers.

    🌏 General Navigation Terms (Kupu Whakatere)
    Te Reo Māori Pronunciation English Translation Cultural Context
    Whakatere fah-kah-teh-reh Navigation, to navigate The art and science of guiding a vessel across the ocean
    Waka wah-kah Canoe, vessel Traditional watercraft, from small canoes to large voyaging vessels
    Waka hourua wah-kah hoh-roo-ah Double-hulled voyaging canoe Large ocean-going vessel used for long-distance voyaging
    Kaihautū kai-how-too Navigator, helmsman The skilled person responsible for navigation and direction
    Haerenga hi-eh-reh-ngah Journey, voyage The act of traveling, especially by sea
    Moana moh-ah-nah Ocean, sea The vast waters connecting Pacific peoples
    ⭐ Celestial Navigation (Whakatere Whetū)
    Te Reo Māori Pronunciation English Translation Cultural Context
    Whetū feh-too Star Primary navigation reference points in the night sky
    Matariki mah-tah-ree-kee Pleiades star cluster Important navigation star cluster, marks Māori New Year
    Te Waka o Tamarereti teh wah-kah oh tah-mah-reh-reh-tee The Milky Way (lit. Tamarereti's Canoe) Major celestial reference, rich with cultural stories
    Te Māngai o te Atua teh mah-ngai oh teh ah-too-ah Southern Cross constellation Key navigation constellation pointing to south
    Rā rah Sun Daytime navigation reference
    Marama mah-rah-mah Moon, month Used for timekeeping and night navigation
    🧭 Directions (Ngā Ahunga)
    Te Reo Māori Pronunciation English Translation Cultural Context
    Raki rah-kee North Direction toward the tropics from Aotearoa
    Tonga toh-ngah South Direction toward the southern ocean and Antarctica
    Rāwhiti rah-fee-tee East Direction of the rising sun
    Uru oo-roo West Direction of the setting sun
    Ahunga ah-hoo-ngah Direction, bearing The orientation or course being followed
    🌊 Natural Navigation Signs (Ngā Tohu Taiao)
    Te Reo Māori Pronunciation English Translation Cultural Context
    Ngaru ngah-roo Wave Ocean waves provided direction and distance information
    Tai tai Tide, sea Understanding tides was essential for safe navigation
    Hau how Wind Wind patterns guided speed and direction
    Manu mah-noo Bird Seabirds indicated proximity to land
    Kapua kah-poo-ah Cloud Cloud formations showed land masses and weather
    Taiao tai-ah-oh Environment, natural world All natural signs read together as a navigation system
    🔢 Mathematical Navigation Terms (Kupu Pāngarau)
    Te Reo Māori Pronunciation English Translation Cultural Context
    Tawhā tah-fah Measure, measurement Traditional measurement systems for distance
    Tāwhiri tah-fee-ree Speed, velocity The rate of travel through water
    Tawhiti tah-fee-tee Distance, far Measurement of how far one must travel
    Wā wah Time, season Temporal measurement for journey planning
    Koki koh-kee Angle, corner Angular measurements between navigation points

    • Further Reading: Research books about traditional Polynesian navigation and Māori seafaring traditions

    👩‍🏫 Teacher Notes

    Teaching Recommendations:

    • Pronunciation Practice: Model correct pronunciation multiple times. Use audio resources if available.
    • Cultural Respect: Emphasize that these are not just words to memorize, but important cultural knowledge
    • Community Consultation: If possible, invite a te reo Māori speaker or cultural advisor to visit the class
    • Regular Review: Incorporate these terms throughout the navigation unit, not just in this lesson

    Assessment Suggestions:

    • Oral pronunciation assessment (with cultural advisor if possible)
    • Written vocabulary quiz with cultural context questions
    • Project where students use vocabulary in authentic contexts
    • Peer teaching activities where students teach each other new terms

    Activity Answers:

    Activity 1: 1-Canoe, 2-Star, 3-Ocean, 4-North, 5-Wave

    Activity 2: 1-kaihautū, 2-whetū, 3-waka hourua, 4-Tamarereti, 5-Matariki

    Differentiation:

    • Support: Focus on 10-15 essential terms first, provide pronunciation guides, pair with confident speakers
    • Extension: Research additional navigation terms, create flashcards for whole class, teach terms to younger students
    • EAL: Provide translations in students' first languages, use more visual aids, allow extra time for pronunciation practice
