    📏 Activity 2: Distance Calculations (15 minutes)

    Navigation Planning

    A traditional navigator is planning a voyage from Rarotonga to Aotearoa (New Zealand). The distance is approximately 3,500 kilometers.

    Speed and Time Calculations:

    If the canoe travels at an average speed of 15 km/h, how many hours will the journey take?

    Time = Distance ÷ Speed

    Time = km ÷ km/h = hours

    Convert hours to days (24 hours = 1 day):

    Journey time = hours ÷ 24 = days

    🌊 Activity 3: Wave Pattern Mathematics (15 minutes)

    Wave Frequency Calculation

    Ocean swells travel in predictable patterns. If the main swell has a wavelength of 150 meters and travels at 15 meters per second, calculate the wave frequency.

    Wave frequency formula:

    Wave frequency (Hz) = Wave speed ÷ Wavelength

    Wave frequency = m/s ÷ m = Hz

    This means: waves pass a point every second

    Land Detection Challenge:

    If an island is 50 km away, and waves travel at 15 m/s, how long does it take for wave patterns to reach the canoe?

    Distance to island = km = meters

    Wave speed = m/s

    Time for waves to reach canoe = m ÷ m/s = seconds

    Convert to minutes: seconds ÷ 60 = minutes

    🤏 Activity 4: Traditional Measurement Practice (15 minutes)

    Hand Measurements

    Traditional navigators used their bodies to measure angles:

    • 1 fist = 10°
    • 1 palm = 5°
    • 1 finger = 2°
    Measurement Practice:

    Measure the angle from one corner of the room to the opposite corner using your fist.

    Record: fists = degrees

    Measure the width of a window using finger widths.

    Record: fingers = degrees

    Estimate the angle from floor to ceiling where you're sitting: degrees

    Navigation Calculation:

    If a navigator measures a key star at 3 fists + 1 palm above the horizon, what is the total angle?

    3 fists = 3 × 10° = °

    1 palm = °

    Total angle = ° + ° = °
