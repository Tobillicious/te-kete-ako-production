Question 1 - Sample Answer:

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (biotic factors) interacting with each other and their physical environment (abiotic factors) in a specific area. It includes plants, animals, microorganisms, plus non-living elements like water, soil, air, and sunlight.

Question 2 - Answers:

  • Kerer≈´: BIOTIC (living bird)
  • Sunlight: ABIOTIC (non-living energy source)
  • Rimu tree: BIOTIC (living plant)
  • Temperature: ABIOTIC (non-living physical factor)
  • Fungi: BIOTIC (living organism)
  • Water/Rainfall: ABIOTIC (non-living resource)
  • Tuatara: BIOTIC (living reptile)
  • Soil nutrients: ABIOTIC (non-living chemicals)

Question 4 - Sample Endemic Species:

  • Kiwi (bird), Tuatara (reptile), KƒÅkƒÅp≈ç (bird)
  • Kerer≈´ (bird), WƒìtƒÅ (insect), Powelliphanta snails
  • Kauri (tree), Harakeke/Flax (plant), Ponga/Silver fern (plant)

Why endemic? 80 million years of isolation meant species evolved uniquely without predators, developing flightlessness (kiwi, kƒÅkƒÅp≈ç) and other special adaptations.

Question 7 - Sample Threats & Actions:

  • Threat: Introduced predators (rats, stoats) | Action: Predator-free trapping programs with community involvement
  • Threat: Habitat loss from development | Action: Replanting native species, creating wildlife corridors
  • Threat: Climate change | Action: Monitor ecosystems, protect migration routes, reduce carbon footprint
