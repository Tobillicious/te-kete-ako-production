🏛️ Whakataukī (Cultural Opening)

Māori: "Ko te reo te tuakiri, ko te reo te mauri"

English: "Language is identity, language is life force"

Relevance: The sounds of taonga pūoro (traditional instruments) carry the voice and mauri (life force) of our ancestors. Understanding the physics of these sounds deepens our appreciation of their cultural significance.

📚 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the physics of sound waves (frequency, amplitude, wavelength)
  • Explain how traditional Māori instruments produce sound
  • Apply physics concepts to analyze taonga pūoro
  • Appreciate the sophistication of traditional instrument design

🪈 Part 2: Taonga Pūoro (Traditional Instruments)

🌿 About Taonga Pūoro

Taonga pūoro (tah-oh-ngah poo-oh-roh) means "musical treasures." These traditional instruments were used for communication, ceremony, and storytelling. Each instrument has specific cultural protocols and significance.

🎺 Pūtōrino (Flute/Trumpet)

  • Made from wood or whale bone
  • Can be played as flute (gentle) or trumpet (powerful)
  • Different playing angles produce different sounds
  • Used for communication and ceremony

  • Struck instruments made from wood
  • Sound produced by vibration of the material

🌬️ Pūmoana (Shell Trumpet)

  • Made from large shells
  • Used for ceremonial calls and long-distance communication
  • Natural spiral shape creates unique acoustics
  • Can be heard for kilometers

👩‍🏫 Teacher Answer Key & Teaching Notes

Question 1 - Answers:

  • a) Hertz (Hz)
  • b) Higher
  • c) Volume/loudness
  • d) One point on a wave and the same point on the next wave (peak to peak or trough to trough)

Question 2 - Calculations:

  • a) λ = 343 ÷ 440 = 0.78 m (or 78 cm)
  • b) λ = 343 ÷ 100 = 3.43 m (much longer wavelength for low frequency)

Question 3 - Physics Explanations:

  • a) Shorter air column = higher frequency = higher pitch. Covering holes makes column longer = lower pitch
  • b) Blowing harder increases amplitude (volume) but doesn't significantly change the length/frequency relationship that determines pitch

Question 4 - Percussion Physics:

  • a) Larger object vibrates more slowly = lower frequency = lower pitch (inverse relationship between size and frequency)
  • b) Density, hardness, elasticity all affect tone quality. Harder/denser materials produce clearer, sharper sounds

Question 5 - Shell Trumpet:

  • a) Large amplitude (loud sound), smooth transmission through air, resonance in the shell amplifies sound
  • b) Spiral acts as acoustic chamber that amplifies and projects sound, creates resonance that sustains the note

Question 9 - Calculation:

f = 343 ÷ (4 × 0.25) = 343 ÷ 1 = 343 Hz (approximately the note F)

Cultural Protocol IMPORTANT:

  • Do NOT have students make actual taonga pūoro without cultural supervision
  • DO consult with local iwi or taonga pūoro practitioners before teaching
  • DO use educational models or recordings with proper attribution
  • DO emphasize these are sacred cultural items, not just physics demonstrations
  • Consider: Inviting a taonga pūoro player to demonstrate (with appropriate koha/payment)

Assessment Rubric:

  • Excellence: Sophisticated physics understanding, demonstrates cultural sensitivity, makes meaningful connections between science and culture
  • Merit: Good physics understanding, shows cultural awareness, makes some connections
  • Achieved: Basic physics concepts understood, demonstrates respect for cultural content

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide physics formula sheet, use simpler calculations, allow group work
  • Extension: Research harmonic series, investigate resonance in different materials, compare to Western instruments
  • EAL: Provide bilingual physics terminology, visual diagrams, allow first language explanations
