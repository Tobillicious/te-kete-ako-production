    Traditional Māori Navigation Mathematics - Lesson 2

    Name: _________________ Date: __________ Year: 8

    🏛️ Whakataukī (Cultural Opening)

    Māori: "Mā te huruhuru ka rere te manu"

    English: "With feathers, a bird can fly"

    Relevance: Just as a bird needs all its feathers to fly, a navigator needs all their mathematical skills to complete a successful voyage!

    📚 Learning Objectives

    • Use the formula: Distance = Speed × Time
    • Calculate journey times for traditional voyages
    • Solve real-world navigation problems
    • Understand the mathematical planning behind Pacific voyages

    Key Formulas to Remember:

    Distance = Speed × Time

    Speed = Distance ÷ Time

    Time = Distance ÷ Speed

    🚣 Part 1: Basic Speed and Distance

    Question 1: Simple Journey

    A traditional waka (canoe) travels at a steady speed of 10 km/h. How far will it travel in 6 hours?

    Formula: Distance = Speed × Time

    Show your working:

    Answer: _______________ km

    Question 2: Calculate Speed

    A waka travels 120 km in 8 hours. What is its average speed?

    Formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time

    Show your working:

    Answer: _______________ km/h

    Question 3: Calculate Time

    If a waka needs to travel 200 km at an average speed of 12.5 km/h, how long will the journey take?

    Formula: Time = Distance ÷ Speed

    Show your working:

    Answer: _______________ hours

    🌏 Part 2: Real Pacific Voyages

    Historical records and modern reconstructions show that traditional waka hourua (double-hulled voyaging canoes) could travel at speeds of 10-20 km/h depending on wind conditions.

    Question 4: Rarotonga to Aotearoa

    The distance from Rarotonga (Cook Islands) to Aotearoa (New Zealand) is approximately 3,500 km. If a waka travels at an average speed of 15 km/h, how long would the journey take?

    Show your working:

    Answer in hours: _______________ hours

    Convert to days (hint: divide by 24): _______________ days

    Question 5: Journey Planning

    A traditional navigator plans a voyage that will take 12 days. The waka can travel at 14 km/h. How far can they travel?

    Hint: First convert 12 days to hours (12 × 24 = ? hours)

    Show your working:

    Answer: _______________ km

    • Day 1: Travel 180 km at 15 km/h

    • Day 3: Travel 120 km at 10 km/h (light winds)

    Calculate:

    1. Time spent traveling each day
    2. Total distance traveled
    3. Average speed for the whole journey

    👩‍🏫 Teacher Answer Key

    Question 1:

    Answer: 10 × 6 = 60 km

    Question 2:

    Answer: 120 ÷ 8 = 15 km/h

    Question 3:

    Answer: 200 ÷ 12.5 = 16 hours

    Question 4:

    Answer: 3,500 ÷ 15 = 233.33 hours = 9.72 days (approximately 10 days)

    Question 5:

    Answer: 12 days × 24 hours = 288 hours; 288 × 14 = 4,032 km

    Question 6:

    Answer:

    • a) Day 1: 12h, Day 2: 8h, Day 3: 12h = 32h total
    • b) Total distance: 180 + 160 + 120 = 460 km
    • c) Average speed: 460 ÷ 32 = 14.375 km/h

    Question 7 - Table Answers:

    • Day 1: Time = 15 hours
    • Day 2: Distance = 252 km
    • Day 3: Speed = 15 km/h
    • Day 4: Time = 15 hours

    Question 8:

    Answer: 20 people × 3 liters × 15 days = 900 liters

    Question 9:

    Answer: Route A: 140 hours; Route B: 160 hours. Route A is 20 hours (almost 1 day) faster.

    Question 10:

    Answer: Sailing time = 240 - (4 × 10) = 200 hours. Speed needed = 3,000 ÷ 200 = 15 km/h

    Assessment Rubric:

    • Excellent (4): All calculations correct, formulas used appropriately, clear working shown
    • Good (3): Most calculations correct, formulas mostly correct, working shown
    • Satisfactory (2): Some calculations correct, basic understanding of formulas
    • Needs Improvement (1): Few correct answers, formula understanding limited

    Differentiation Notes:

    • Support: Provide formula cards, calculators, worked examples, simplified numbers
    • Extension: Research actual historical voyage times, calculate with varying wind conditions, investigate ocean currents
    • EAL: Provide bilingual formula sheets, visual aids, worked examples
