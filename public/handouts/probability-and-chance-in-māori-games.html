    He Whakamārama ā-Iwi | Cultural Context

    Traditional Māori games (tākaro) were not just for entertainment but also taught important skills, strategy, and mathematical concepts. This lesson explores probability through three traditional games: Pūtōrino (a flute game), Poi Rākau (stick games), and Whai (string patterns).

    Cultural Safety Note: When teaching Māori content, ensure you pronounce Māori words correctly (practice with students or use online resources). Acknowledge the iwi associated with these games where possible. Always approach Māori knowledge with respect.

    Ngā Whāinga Ako | Learning Objectives

    • Calculate probabilities of simple and compound events (NZC Level 4-5 Mathematics)
    • Compare theoretical and experimental probabilities
    • Understand how probability concepts were applied in traditional Māori games
    • Analyze game strategies using probability concepts

    Activity 2: Poi Rākau Stick Game Analysis

    In this traditional stick game, players throw sticks between each other in patterns.

    Number of Sticks Possible Catch Combinations Probability of Successful Catch
    1 stick 2 (left or right hand) 1/2 = 50%
    2 sticks 4 (LL, LR, RL, RR) 1/4 = 25%
    3 sticks 8 combinations 1/8 = 12.5%

    Practical Task: In pairs, simulate the stick game using pencils. Record 20 attempts each with 1, 2, then 3 sticks. Compare your experimental probabilities with the theoretical probabilities above.

    Activity 3: Whai (String Game) Patterns

    Whai involves creating patterns with a loop of string. Some patterns are more likely to appear than others based on the sequence of moves.

    Basic moves:

    • Mauī (left) - 50% probability
    • Matau (right) - 50% probability

    Create a probability tree for a 3-move sequence. What's the probability of getting:

    • All left moves?
    • Two left and one right in any order?

    He Tohutohu mā te Kaiako | Teacher Implementation Guide

    1. Preparation: Learn correct pronunciation of Māori terms (use Māori Dictionary)
    2. Introduction (10 min): Discuss the purpose of traditional Māori games and introduce probability concepts
    3. Activity 1 (20 min): Pūtōrino probability calculations and interactive component
    4. Activity 2 (25 min): Poi Rākau practical experiment with data recording
    5. Activity 3 (20 min): Whai probability tree analysis
    6. Conclusion (15 min): Compare theoretical vs experimental results, discuss cultural significance

    Resources Needed: Pencils (for stick game simulation), string for whai, printed worksheets
