    "Ko te whenua te waiū mō ngā uri whakatipu"
    The land is the milk for future generations

    Traditional Māori technologies demonstrate sustainable practices

    Learning Objectives

    • Understand how traditional Māori knowledge (mātauranga Māori) informs sustainable energy solutions
    • Compare traditional and modern approaches to energy use and conservation
    • Analyze the scientific principles behind traditional energy technologies
    • Develop proposals for integrating traditional knowledge with modern energy solutions
    • Demonstrate respect for tikanga Māori (Māori protocols) when discussing traditional knowledge

    Cultural Context

    Traditional Māori knowledge systems developed over centuries of observation and adaptation to Aotearoa's unique environment. This mātauranga (knowledge) includes sophisticated understanding of energy flows, thermal dynamics, and sustainable resource use.

    Cultural Safety Considerations

    • Always acknowledge the source when sharing traditional knowledge
    • Recognize that some knowledge may be tapu (sacred/restricted)
    • Invite local kaumātua (elders) or experts to contribute where possible
    • Present information as contemporary applications of traditional principles rather than "ancient" knowledge
    • Use te reo Māori terms appropriately and consistently

    Traditional Energy Technologies

    1. Hāngī - Earth Oven Technology

    The traditional hāngī demonstrates efficient thermal energy transfer:

    • Uses volcanic rocks as heat reservoirs
    • Earth insulation maintains steady temperatures
    • Steam cooking preserves nutrients

    Scientific Analysis Activity

    Calculate the energy efficiency of a hāngī compared to modern ovens:

    1. Research typical energy inputs (wood, electricity)
    2. Measure food output (mass cooked per energy unit)
    3. Compare heat retention properties of materials
    4. Consider complete energy cycles (fuel sourcing to waste)

    2. Passive Solar Design in Whare

    Traditional Māori architecture incorporated passive solar principles:

    Feature Scientific Principle Modern Application
    Orientation to sun Maximizes winter solar gain Passive house design
    Eaves design Blocks summer sun while admitting winter sun Solar shading calculations
    Ventilation gaps Stack effect for natural cooling Natural ventilation systems

    Modern Applications

    Contemporary projects integrating traditional knowledge:

    • Geothermal power generation informed by traditional hot spring uses
    • Biomass energy systems based on traditional fuel knowledge
    • Micro-hydro systems inspired by traditional water management

    Design Challenge

    Create a sustainable energy solution for your school that combines traditional knowledge with modern technology:

    1. Identify an energy need (heating, lighting, etc.)
    2. Research traditional solutions
    3. Investigate modern technologies
    4. Design a hybrid solution
    5. Present your proposal with scientific justification

    Assessment

    Criteria Achieved Merit Excellence
    Understanding of traditional knowledge Describes basic concepts Explains with accurate details Analyzes connections to modern science
    Scientific analysis Identifies some scientific principles Explains multiple principles clearly Evaluates effectiveness scientifically
    Cultural respect Uses some te reo appropriately Demonstrates good understanding of protocols Shows deep engagement with cultural context
    Innovation Proposes simple combination Develops thoughtful integration Creates novel, well-justified solution

    Extension Activities

    Advanced Research Project

    Investigate one traditional energy technology in depth:

    • Conduct energy efficiency experiments
    • Interview knowledge holders (with proper protocols)
    • Develop mathematical models of the system
    • Propose improvements using modern materials

    Comparative Study

    Compare Māori energy knowledge with that of another indigenous culture:

    • Identify common principles
    • Analyze adaptations to different environments
    • Evaluate potential for global applications

    Adaptations for Diverse Learners

    Learner Need Adaptation
    Visual learners Provide diagrams of energy flows, create infographics
    Kinesthetic learners Build models of traditional technologies, conduct hands-on experiments
    ESOL students Provide bilingual vocabulary lists, use visual supports
    Advanced students Assign independent research projects, connect with university researchers

    Implementation Guide

    Lesson Sequence (5 sessions):

    1. Introduction: Explore traditional Māori perspectives on energy and sustainability
    2. Case Studies: Examine specific traditional technologies and their scientific principles
    3. Field Work: Visit local sites demonstrating traditional or hybrid energy systems
    4. Design Phase: Students develop their own hybrid solutions
    5. Presentations: Share and evaluate proposals

    Resources Needed:

    • Access to local kaumātua or experts
    • Materials for model building (recycled materials encouraged)
    • Thermometers and other basic measurement tools
    • Digital devices for research and presentations

    ## Teacher Implementation Guide ### 1. Preparation - Consult with local Māori advisors to ensure cultural appropriateness - Gather materials for hands-on activities (thermometers, modeling clay, etc.) - Prepare digital resources for student research - Identify potential field trip locations (marae with traditional architecture, geothermal sites) ### 2. Lesson Sequence **Session 1: Introduction to Traditional Energy Knowledge** - Begin with karakia (prayer) if appropriate for your school context - Explore Māori worldview concepts like kaitiakitanga (guardianship) - Compare traditional and modern energy use patterns **Session 2: Case Study Analysis** - Small groups investigate different traditional technologies - Create diagrams explaining energy flows - Present findings to class **Session 3: Field Work/Expert Visit** - Visit relevant site or invite knowledgeable guest - Record observations and measurements - Discuss connections to modern science **Session 4: Design Challenge** - Students work in pairs/groups on hybrid solutions - Provide guidance on both technical and cultural aspects - Prepare presentation materials **Session 5: Presentations & Reflection** - Students present their solutions - Peer feedback using assessment criteria - Reflective discussion on learning ### 3. Assessment Options - Design portfolio documenting process - Scientific report analyzing traditional technology - Presentation of hybrid solution - Reflection on cultural learning ### 4.
