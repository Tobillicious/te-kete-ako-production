Learning Outcomes

  • Describe chemical properties of natural fibres (cellulose, lignin, pectin in harakeke).
  • Explain acid-base reactions in dye mordanting and pH-based colour changes.
  • Investigate mineral composition of uku (clay) and its uses as pigment.
  • Apply chemistry concepts to replicate traditional dye extraction safely.
  • Evaluate sustainable material practices through a chemistry lens.

Assessment

  • Practical: Dye extraction lab report (method, observations, chemical explanations)
  • Research: Material science of one traditional taonga (e.g., waka caulking, k≈çk≈çwai pigment)
  • Summative: Design a sustainable product using traditional + modern chemistry knowledge
