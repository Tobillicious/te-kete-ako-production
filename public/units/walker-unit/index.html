Dr. Ranginui Walker - Scholar, Activist, Truth-Teller

Dr. Ranginui Walker (1932-2016) was a pioneering Māori scholar, educator, and activist who challenged Pākehā-dominated narratives of New Zealand history. His landmark book "Ka Whawhai Tonu Matou: Struggle Without End" (1990) presented Māori history from a Māori perspective, fundamentally changing how Aotearoa understands its past.

Key Themes in This Unit:

  • Narrative Power: Who gets to tell the story of history? Whose voices are centered?
  • Urban Migration: The Great Migration of Māori from rural to urban areas (1940s-1970s)
  • Activism & Resistance: Ngā Tamatoa, land marches, and the fight for Treaty justice
  • Waitangi Tribunal: Legal pathways to redress historical injustices
  • Māori Renaissance: Cultural revitalization and reclaiming identity
