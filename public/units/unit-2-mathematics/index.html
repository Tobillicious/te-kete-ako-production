Unit 2: Decolonized Narratives

Pre-Colonial Innovation & Mathematical Thinking

"Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini"

My strength is not mine alone, but that of the collective

Unit Overview

This unit challenges the myth of "primitive" pre-colonial societies by exploring sophisticated Indigenous mathematics, navigation systems, and architectural innovations. Students analyze historical evidence through mathematical frameworks and discover the complexity of traditional knowledge systems.

1+ Lessons
3-4 Hours
2 Resources
1 Assessment

Learning Objectives

Mathematics (NZ Curriculum Level 5-6)

  • Measurement: Analyze precision in traditional navigation and architecture
  • Geometry: Study sophisticated geometric principles in Indigenous design
  • Systems Thinking: Understand interconnected mathematical systems
  • Data Analysis: Evaluate historical and archaeological evidence mathematically

Cultural & Critical Thinking

  • Challenge colonial narratives about Indigenous capabilities
  • Recognize mathematical sophistication in traditional practices
  • Develop critical analysis of historical bias
  • Apply decolonization principles to knowledge systems
