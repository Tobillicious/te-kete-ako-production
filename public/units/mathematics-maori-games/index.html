Mathematics & Māori Games

Explore algebraic thinking, probability, geometry, statistics, and mathematical modeling through the lens of traditional Māori games and cultural practices.

📅 Duration: 4-5 weeks
🎯 Year Level: 7-10
📚 5 Lessons
🌿 Culturally Integrated

🌿 Cultural Context & Protocols

Traditional Māori games (ngā tākaro) are not just entertainment—they are sophisticated systems for teaching mathematical concepts, strategic thinking, and cultural values. Through games like mū tōrere, poi rākau, and ki-o-rahi, our tūpuna developed and practiced complex mathematical thinking including:

  • Algebraic patterns in game strategy and piece movement
  • Probability in chance-based games and decision-making
  • Geometry in playing field design and spatial reasoning
  • Statistics in performance tracking and skill development
  • Mathematical modeling of ecological systems through seasonal games

🙏 Cultural Protocol: Before teaching any traditional game, research its origins and proper protocols. Where possible, invite local kaumātua or cultural advisors to share the game in its full cultural context. Some games may have regional variations or specific tikanga around when and how they are played.
