Lesson 2.2: The Legacy of Raupatu

Understanding the Waikato War and land confiscations that shaped Te Puea's mission

🌅 DO NOW (0-5 min)

"Imagine your family lost their home and all their land. How would this affect your life? Write 2-3 sentences about what you would lose (not just physical things, but opportunities, connections, identity)."

📖 The Waikato War (1863-1864)

What Happened:

  • 1863: Governor Grey invaded Waikato despite no provocation
  • Reason: Kīngitanga seen as challenge to Crown; wanted land for Pākehā settlement
  • Outcome: Māori defeated, 1.2 million acres confiscated
  • Impact: Tainui people displaced, impoverished, separated from ancestral lands

👥 Main Activity: Cause & Effect Web (15-40 min)

In small groups (3-4), create a cause-and-effect web showing:

CENTER: Raupatu (Land Confiscation)

📍 Physical Effects:

  • Homelessness
  • Poverty
  • Loss of food sources

💰 Economic Effects:

  • Unable to farm
  • Lost economic base
  • Dependence on Crown

🌿 Cultural Effects:

  • Separation from ancestral lands
  • Marae destroyed
  • Whakapapa disrupted

✨ Spiritual Effects:

  • Disconnection from whakapapa
  • Mana diminished
  • Loss of tūrangawaewae

✍️ Independent Writing (40-60 min)

🌿 Connecting to Whaimana (Integrity):

Prompt: "The raupatu was a profound injustice. How does seeking truth and redress for this injustice demonstrate Whaimana (Integrity)? How did Te Puea show integrity in facing this legacy?"

💡 Consider:

  • Acknowledging historical wrongs
  • Seeking justice (not revenge)
  • Long-term commitment to truth
  • Restoration vs. bitterness

🎯 Plenary (60-75 min)

Discussion: King Tāwhiao said "I shall remain in my own boundaries." What did he mean? Why was land so important to Waikato Māori?

Exit Ticket: "What is one question you have about the raupatu that you'd like to explore further?"
