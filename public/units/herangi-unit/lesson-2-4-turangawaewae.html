🏛️ Building Tūrangawaewae

The Challenge:

After the raupatu (Lesson 2.2) and WWI resistance (Lesson 2.3), Waikato Māori had no central marae. Te Puea envisioned building Tūrangawaewae at Ngāruawāhia, the traditional capital of the Kīngitanga.

📅 Timeline of Construction:

  • 1920s: Land acquisition begins (swampy, difficult land)
  • 1921-1925: Drainage and land preparation (backbreaking work)
  • 1925-1929: Building wharenui (meeting house) and other structures
  • 1929: Official opening - Tūrangawaewae established

👥 Group Activity: How It Was Built

💪 Physical Work:

  • Draining swampland
  • Hauling materials
  • Building structures
  • Planting gardens

💰 Fundraising:

  • Concert parties
  • Selling produce
  • Community donations
  • Advocacy for support

🤝 Community:

  • Collective labor (mahi tahi)
  • Young and old working together
  • Rebuilding social bonds
  • Shared purpose and hope

🌿 Cultural Significance:

  • Restored mana of Kīngitanga
  • Created spiritual center
  • Reconnected people to whenua
  • Symbol of resilience

✍️ Writing Task

Prompt: "How did building Tūrangawaewae restore Whaiora (wellbeing) for Waikato people? Consider physical, cultural, and spiritual dimensions."

💡 Think About:

  • Physical: Shelter, gathering place, food production
  • Cultural: Marae protocols restored, whakapapa reconnected
  • Spiritual: Sacred space, connection to ancestors
  • Social: Community rebuilt, belonging restored

🎯 Plenary

Discussion: "Tūrangawaewae means 'a place to stand.' What does this metaphor mean beyond the physical marae?"

Exit Ticket: "What's your tūrangawaewae (place where you belong)?"
