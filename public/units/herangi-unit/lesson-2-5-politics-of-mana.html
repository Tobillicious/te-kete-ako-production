Lesson 2.5: The Politics of Mana

Te Puea's political leadership and enduring legacy

🎓 CULMINATING LESSON: Synthesizes all themes from Lessons 2.1-2.4

🌅 DO NOW

"Who is a leader you admire? What makes them great?"

Recall Lesson 2.1's DO NOW - has your answer changed after learning about Te Puea?

👑 Te Puea's Political Achievements

🏛️ Restored Kīngitanga Prestige

  • Rebuilt marae as political center
  • Hosted royal visitors (1920s-1940s)
  • Strengthened Kīngitanga's national profile

🤝 Crown Relations

  • Advocated for land returns
  • Negotiated with government officials
  • Balanced resistance with diplomacy

🌱 Mentored Next Generation

  • Trained future leaders
  • Passed on cultural knowledge
  • Ensured Kīngitanga continuity

👥 Group Activity: Leadership Map

Groups create a "Leadership Map" showing Te Puea's achievements across three dimensions:

🏗️ Physical

Marae, housing, farming, infrastructure

🏛️ Political

Crown relations, Kīngitanga strength, advocacy

🌿 Cultural

Revival of traditions, te reo, tikanga, whakapapa

✍️ Unit Synthesis Writing

Culminating Task: Write a paragraph connecting Te Puea to ALL THREE school values:

  • Whaimana (Integrity): Standing by King Tāwhiao's injunction, refusing conscription
  • Whaiora (Wellbeing): Building Tūrangawaewae, caring for orphans, creating community
  • Whaiara (Rising Up): Rising from raupatu devastation to rebuild her people's future

💡 WAGOLL paragraph will be shown before students begin

🎯 Why "Mother of the Nation"?

Te Puea is called "The Mother of the Nation" because she:

  • Nurtured her people through crisis (influenza, poverty)
  • Built a home for the homeless (Tūrangawaewae)
  • Restored hope and dignity after generations of dispossession
  • Created a foundation for future generations
  • Demonstrated that one person's determination can change history

🎯 Plenary & Final Reflection

Class Discussion: "What does Te Puea's life teach us about leadership, resilience, and service?"

Exit Ticket: "What is one way you can show Whaimana, Whaiora, or Whaiara in your own life?"

Design a memorial for Te Puea explaining her achievements (visual + written component)

Investigate how Tūrangawaewae operates today and present on her lasting impact (10-15 min presentation)
