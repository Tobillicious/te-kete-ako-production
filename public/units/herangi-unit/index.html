Hērangi Unit

The Heart of the Kīngitanga

Te Puea Hērangi - Builder, Leader, Guardian of Her People

"Ko te aroha te mea nui"

Love and compassion are the greatest treasures — Te Puea's leadership was rooted in deep care for her people

📖 Unit Overview

This unit explores the life and leadership of Princess Te Puea Hērangi, one of the most influential Māori leaders of the 20th century. Students will examine her pivotal role in revitalizing the Kīngitanga, building Tūrangawaewae marae, and her unwavering commitment to the wellbeing of her people despite devastating obstacles.

🏛️ School Values Connection:

Whaimana (Integrity)

Te Puea's principled stand against conscription during WWI upheld the integrity of the Kīngitanga's stance

Whaiora (Wellbeing)

Central theme: community wellbeing through care for orphans and creating Tūrangawaewae

Whaiara (Rising Up)

Te Puea enabled Tainui to rise from devastation and build a new future

📚 Key Topics:

  • The History and Significance of the Kīngitanga
  • The Waikato War and Land Confiscations (Raupatu)
  • Te Puea's stand against Conscription (WWI)
  • The 1918 Influenza Pandemic and its aftermath
  • The building of Tūrangawaewae Marae
  • Negotiating with the Crown: The path to settlement
