Unit 1: Ko Wai Au?

Personal Identity Through Narrative & Cultural Storytelling

"Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au"

I am the river, and the river is me

Unit Overview

This unit explores personal and cultural identity through narrative writing, using pūrākau (Māori storytelling traditions) as a framework. Students develop their voice while learning foundational writing craft: engaging hooks, showing not telling, and narrative structure.

4Lessons
12-16Hours
6Resources
1Assessment

Learning Objectives

Writing Craft (NZ Curriculum Level 5-6)

  • Narrative Structure: Craft compelling personal narratives using pūrākau framework
  • Writing Techniques: Master hooks, showing vs telling, descriptive language
  • Voice & Style: Develop authentic personal voice in writing
  • Creative Expression: Use creative writing inspired by whakataukī

Cultural Integration

  • Understand pūrākau as narrative framework
  • Explore whakataukī as creative inspiration
  • Connect personal identity to cultural identity
  • Value storytelling in Te Ao Māori

Lessons

Lesson 1

Engaging Hooks - Capturing Reader Attention

Learn to craft compelling openings that draw readers into your narrative. Essential foundation for all storytelling.

3-4 hours Writing Craft
Lesson 2

Show, Don't Tell - Descriptive Writing

Master the fundamental technique of showing through sensory details rather than simply telling.

3-4 hours Writing Craft
Lesson 3

Narrative Writing Using Māori Story Structures

Apply pūrākau storytelling framework to personal narratives, connecting cultural tradition with contemporary writing.

3-4 hours Cultural Storytelling
Lesson 4

Creative Writing Inspired by Whakataukī

Use whakataukī (Māori proverbs) as creative inspiration for personal reflection and storytelling.

3-4 hours Creative Expression

Assessment

Personal Narrative Portfolio

Students create a collection of personal narratives that demonstrate:

  • Engaging hooks that capture reader attention
  • Showing vs telling through sensory details
  • Use of pūrākau storytelling structure
  • Personal voice and authentic expression
  • Connection between personal and cultural identity

Differentiation

  • Extension: Multi-genre narrative combining traditional and contemporary forms
  • Support: Scaffolded templates for narrative structure
  • Cultural Adaptation: Choice of narrative focus (personal, whānau, community)
