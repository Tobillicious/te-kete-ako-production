📖 Unit Overview

This unit examines the legislative work of Koro Wētere (1935-2018), who served as Minister of Māori Affairs during the transformative 1980s-90s. As a cabinet minister, Wētere championed the Māori Language Act 1987 (making te reo an official language) and shaped the Treaty settlement process that returned billions in assets to iwi. Students will explore how working within government systems can achieve structural change - complementing the protest movements studied in previous units.

🏛️ School Values Connection:

Whaimana (Integrity)

Maintained integrity to Māori interests while navigating complex political compromises

Whaiora (Wellbeing)

Treaty settlements returned resources essential for long-term iwi wellbeing

Whaiara (Rising Up)

Māori Language Act elevated te reo from stigmatized language to official national treasure
