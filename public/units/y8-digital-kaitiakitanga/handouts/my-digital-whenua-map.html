    Tōku Mahere Whenua Tāhiko | My Digital Whenua Map

    In the space below, draw a map of the digital places you visit. Think of it like a map of an island. What are the big cities (apps you use a lot)? What are the small villages (sites you visit sometimes)? What are the roads that connect them?

    Mahere Kī | Map Key

    Use symbols or colours to show what each place is for. Here are some ideas:

    Talking with friends & whānau (e.g., Messenger, Discord)
    Playing games (e.g., Roblox, Minecraft)
    Watching videos & entertainment (e.g., YouTube, TikTok)

    Ngā Pātai Whakaaro | Reflection Questions

    1. Which digital space on your map feels the most like your 'home'? Why?

    2. Which space feels the most like a 'public park' where anyone can be? Why?

    3. Are there any places on your map where you feel you have to act differently than you do in real life? Why?

    4. Look at the connections between your spaces. What information might be travelling along those roads?

    📊 Assessment Tools | Ngā Taputapu Aromatawai

    ✅ Formative Assessment (During Learning)

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    Quick Check: Thumbs up/sideways/down for understanding

    🎯 Self-Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Not Yet Getting There Got It!
    I understand the key concepts ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can explain it to others ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can apply it to new situations ☐ ☐ ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?

    📝 Summative Assessment Options

    Written Test

    Multiple choice, short answer, extended response

    Project

    Research, create, present findings

    Presentation

    Oral, visual, or multimedia

    Practical Demo

    Skills demonstration, performance task
