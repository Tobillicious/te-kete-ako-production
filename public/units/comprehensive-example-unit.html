    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others

    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    🏛️ Cultural Foundation

    Whakataukī: "Ko te pae tawhiti whāia kia tata, ko te pae tata whakamaua kia tīna"

    Translation: "Seek the distant horizons, hold fast to those close at hand"

    Cultural Context: Emphasizes both ambitious goals (distant horizons) and practical skills (close at hand) - perfect for navigation mathematics.

    📚 Lesson 1: Introduction to Traditional Navigation (60 minutes)

    🎯 Learning Objectives

    Knowledge

    • Traditional navigators divided horizon into 32 directions
    • Mathematical accuracy was essential for survival
    • Navigation combines astronomy, mathematics, and environmental observation

    Skills

    • Calculate angles between star directions
    • Use traditional measurement methods
    • Apply mathematical concepts to real-world problems

    Values

    • Appreciate sophistication of traditional knowledge
    • Respect cultural protocols and tikanga
    • Understand mathematics has cultural contexts

    📊 Complete Assessment Framework

    🎯 Summative Assessment: Traditional Navigation Challenge

    Project Description: Students plan and mathematically justify a traditional Polynesian voyage from Rarotonga to Aotearoa.

    Assessment Components:

    • Route planning with mathematical calculations
    • Cultural context and protocols explanation
    • Presentation to class with visual aids
    • Reflection on learning journey

    Mathematical Accuracy (40%)

    • Excellent: All calculations correct, clear working shown
    • Good: Most calculations correct, minor errors
    • Satisfactory: Some calculations correct, basic understanding
    • Needs Improvement: Many calculation errors, unclear working

    Cultural Understanding (30%)

    • Excellent: Deep understanding, respectful integration
    • Good: Good understanding, appropriate use
    • Satisfactory: Basic understanding, some cultural awareness
    • Needs Improvement: Limited understanding, cultural insensitivity

    Presentation Quality (20%)

    • Excellent: Clear, engaging, well-organized
    • Good: Clear presentation, minor organization issues
    • Satisfactory: Adequate presentation, some clarity issues
    • Needs Improvement: Unclear presentation, poor organization

    Problem-Solving Approach (10%)

    • Excellent: Creative solutions, logical reasoning
    • Good: Sound approach, minor reasoning gaps
    • Satisfactory: Basic problem-solving, limited creativity
    • Needs Improvement: Poor reasoning, no creative thinking
