Kaitiakitanga - Environmental Guardianship

This unit weaves together scientific inquiry with the MƒÅori principle of kaitiakitanga - the responsibility to act as guardians and protectors of our natural world. Students investigate ecosystems not just as observers, but as active participants with a duty to care for and sustain ecological balance.

Key Cultural Connections:

  • Whakapapa: Understanding interconnectedness and genealogical links in nature
  • Mauri: Life force present in all living things and ecosystems
  • Taonga: Native species as treasures to be protected
  • Kaitiakitanga: Active guardianship and sustainable practices

üí° Big Ideas

  • Ecosystems are networks of interdependent relationships between living things and their environment
  • Energy flows through ecosystems via food chains and food webs
  • Kaitiakitanga guides responsible, relational care for local environments
  • Scientific investigation and mƒÅtauranga MƒÅori both inform wise action

üéØ Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  • Describe components and interactions in a local ecosystem
  • Construct and interpret food chains and food webs
  • Explain human impacts on ecosystems
  • Propose kaitiakitanga-informed conservation actions
  • Collect, organize, and present field data ethically
