Kaitiakitanga - Environmental Guardianship

This unit weaves together scientific inquiry with the MÄori principle of kaitiakitanga - the responsibility to act as guardians and protectors of our natural world. Students investigate ecosystems not just as observers, but as active participants with a duty to care for and sustain ecological balance.

Key Cultural Connections:

  • Whakapapa: Understanding interconnectedness and genealogical links in nature
  • Mauri: Life force present in all living things and ecosystems
  • Taonga: Native species as treasures to be protected
  • Kaitiakitanga: Active guardianship and sustainable practices
