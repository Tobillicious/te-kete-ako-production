Kaitiakitanga - Environmental Guardianship

This unit weaves together scientific inquiry with the Māori principle of kaitiakitanga - the responsibility to act as guardians and protectors of our natural world. Students investigate ecosystems not just as observers, but as active participants with a duty to care for and sustain ecological balance.

Key Cultural Connections:

  • Whakapapa: Understanding interconnectedness and genealogical links in nature
  • Mauri: Life force present in all living things and ecosystems
  • Taonga: Native species as treasures to be protected
  • Kaitiakitanga: Active guardianship and sustainable practices

💡 Big Ideas

  • Ecosystems are networks of interdependent relationships between living things and their environment
  • Energy flows through ecosystems via food chains and food webs
  • Kaitiakitanga guides responsible, relational care for local environments
  • Scientific investigation and mātauranga Māori both inform wise action

🎯 Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  • Describe components and interactions in a local ecosystem
  • Construct and interpret food chains and food webs
  • Explain human impacts on ecosystems
  • Propose kaitiakitanga-informed conservation actions
  • Collect, organize, and present field data ethically

Unit Lessons

1

What is an Ecosystem?

Explore ecosystem components (biotic/abiotic) and introduce the Māori concept of kaitiakitanga as environmental guardianship. Map local ecosystems with iwi place names.

🌍 Ecosystem Basics 🌿 Kaitiakitanga 🗺️ Field Work
2

Food Webs & Energy Flow

Model energy flow and interdependence through food chains and food webs. Consider mahinga kai (traditional food-gathering) species and their ecological roles.

🔗 Food Chains ⚡ Energy Flow 🐟 Mahinga Kai
3

Human Impact & Conservation

Investigate local environmental pressures and threats. Draft culturally grounded conservation action plans based on kaitiakitanga principles and scientific evidence.

🌍 Human Impact 🛡️ Conservation 📋 Action Plan
4

Ecosystem Balance

Explore how ecosystems maintain balance and respond to disturbances. Investigate predator-prey relationships and the concept of mauri (life force) in ecological systems.

⚖️ Balance 🔄 Resilience 💫 Mauri
5

Culminating Project

Students research a local ecosystem challenge and present a kaitiakitanga-informed action plan, integrating scientific evidence with mātauranga Māori perspectives.

🔬 Research 📊 Presentation ✅ Assessment
