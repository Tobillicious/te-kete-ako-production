🏛️ Cultural Context

Whakataukī

"Mā te mōhio, mā te mārama, ka ora te iwi"

"Through knowledge and understanding, the people will thrive"

🏛️ Connection to Mangakōtukutuku College Values

Whaiara: Rising Up - Taking action to create positive change

This learning connects to the value of whaiara by encouraging students to use mathematics as a tool for understanding and improving their world, from environmental modeling to financial decision-making.

🛡️ Cultural Safety Considerations

  • Approach mathematical concepts with respect for traditional knowledge systems
  • Recognize that mathematical thinking exists in all cultures
  • Create space for students to connect mathematics to their cultural contexts
  • Value both Western and indigenous mathematical traditions equally

👨‍🏫 Teacher Implementation Guide

📋 Lesson Structure

  • 5-10 min: Cultural connection & whakataukī
  • 15-20 min: Concept introduction with real examples
  • 30-40 min: Guided practice & cultural application
  • 10-15 min: Independent practice & reflection

🎯 Assessment Approach

  • Formative: Daily exit tickets & peer feedback
  • Summative: Unit projects with cultural contexts
  • Self-Assessment: Learning reflection journals
  • Portfolio: Mathematical thinking evidence

🌿 Cultural Integration

  • Whakataukī: Mathematical wisdom from proverbs
  • Traditional Examples: Māori patterns & navigation
  • Contemporary Issues: Climate, economics, technology
  • Community Connection: Local iwi consultation
