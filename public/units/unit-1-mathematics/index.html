Unit 1: Ko Wai Au?

Personal Whakapapa & Identity through Mathematical Thinking

"Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au"

I am the river, and the river is me

Unit Overview

This unit explores personal and cultural identity through the lens of mathematics, using whakapapa (genealogy) as a framework for understanding mathematical patterns, exponential growth, and networks. Students investigate their own whakapapa while developing core mathematical concepts.

2 Lessons
6-8 Hours
3 Resources
1 Assessment

Learning Objectives

Mathematics (NZ Curriculum Level 5-6)

  • Number: Understand and apply exponential growth in whakapapa contexts (2n ancestors)
  • Patterns: Identify and describe mathematical patterns in genealogical trees
  • Networks: Represent relationships using graph theory and network diagrams
  • Data Analysis: Collect, organize, and analyze whakapapa data

Cultural Integration

  • Understand whakapapa as a foundational concept in Te Ao Māori
  • Explore connections between mathematical thinking and mātauranga Māori
  • Recognize the value of personal and cultural identity in learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori principles when handling whakapapa information

Lessons

Lesson 1

Ko Wai Au? - Personal Whakapapa Exploration

Students explore their own whakapapa and discover exponential growth through genealogical mathematics. Introduction to 2n patterns and network visualization.

3-4 hours Investigation & Creation
Lesson 2

Mātauranga Māori - Traditional Knowledge Systems

Deep dive into how mathematical thinking is embedded in mātauranga Māori, exploring traditional navigation, celestial patterns, and systematic knowledge organization.

3-4 hours Cultural Mathematics

Assessment

Unit Project: My Mathematical Whakapapa

Students create a comprehensive whakapapa visualization that demonstrates:

  • Mathematical understanding of exponential growth
  • Network theory and relationship mapping
  • Cultural respect and tikanga in presenting whakapapa
  • Data organization and visualization skills

Differentiation

  • Extension: Explore probability and genetics in whakapapa inheritance
  • Support: Simplified 3-generation whakapapa with clear exponential patterns
  • Cultural Adaptation: Students may use chosen whānau models if appropriate
