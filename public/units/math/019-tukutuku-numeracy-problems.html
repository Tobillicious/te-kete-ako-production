    Tukutuku Numeracy Problems

    Mathematical calculations and problem-solving using traditional Māori weaving patterns

    Mathematics Year 9 3-4 lessons
    1

    Pattern Counting & Multiplication

    Scenario: A tukutuku panel has a repeating pattern of 3 diamond shapes followed by 2 triangle shapes.

    Question: If the panel is 40 shapes long, how many complete patterns are there? How many diamonds and triangles in total?

    Show your work:

    Extension: If the pattern repeats 8 times across the panel, what's the total number of shapes? Write this as an algebraic expression.

    2

    Area & Perimeter Calculations

    Scenario: Each diamond shape in a tukutuku pattern has a base of 6cm and height of 8cm. Each triangle has a base of 4cm and height of 5cm.

    Question: Calculate the total area of one complete pattern (3 diamonds + 2 triangles).

    Formulas:

    Diamond area = (base × height) ÷ 2

    Triangle area = (base × height) ÷ 2

    Extension: If the panel has 8 complete patterns, what's the total area? Convert to square meters.

    3

    Ratio & Proportional Reasoning

    Scenario: In a traditional tukutuku design, the ratio of black to white weaving strips is 3:2.

    Question: If a panel uses 50 black strips, how many white strips are needed? What's the total number of strips?

    Coordinate Geometry & Transformations

    Scenario: A diamond shape in a tukutuku pattern has vertices at (2,4), (6,4), (8,8), and (4,8).

    Question: Find the area and perimeter of this diamond. What happens to the coordinates if you translate it 3 units right and 2 units up?

    5

    Statistical Analysis & Data Interpretation

    Scenario: A class measured the angles in 20 different tukutuku patterns. The results (in degrees) were: 45, 60, 45, 90, 60, 45, 90, 60, 45, 90, 60, 45, 90, 60, 45, 90, 60, 45, 90, 60.

    Question: Calculate the mean, median, mode, and range of these angles. Create a frequency table.

    Data analysis:

    Extension: If one more pattern with a 75° angle is added, how does this affect the mean and median?

    6

    Financial Mathematics & Budgeting

    Scenario: A weaver is creating a tukutuku panel that costs $2.50 per black strip and $1.80 per white strip. The design requires 30 black strips and 20 white strips per pattern.

    Question: What's the total material cost for one pattern? If the weaver makes a profit of 40% on the labor, what's the selling price?

    Extension: If the weaver wants to make a 25% profit instead of 40%, what's the new selling price? How much less profit is made per panel?

    📝 Answer Key

    Problem 1:

    8 complete patterns. 24 diamonds, 16 triangles. Algebraic: 8(3D + 2T) = 40 shapes

    Problem 2:

    Diamond: 24cm², Triangle: 10cm². Total: 92cm² per pattern. 736cm² total = 0.0736m²

    Problem 3:

    33.33 white strips. Total: 83.33 strips. 160 black strips needs 106.67 white strips (27% increase)

    Problem 4:

    Area: 16 square units. Perimeter: 16 units. New coordinates: (5,6), (9,6), (11,10), (7,10)

    Problem 5:

    Mean: 62.5°, Median: 60°, Mode: 45° and 60°, Range: 45°. New mean: 63.75°, median unchanged

    Problem 6:

    Material cost: $111. Labor profit: $44.40. Selling price: $155.40. 25% profit: $138.75, $16.65 less profit
