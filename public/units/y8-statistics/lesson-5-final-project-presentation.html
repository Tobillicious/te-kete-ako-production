    ğŸ” Lesson 5.2: Data Analysis & Statistical Investigation

    Focus: Applying all statistical skills learned in the unit to analyze project data

    Data Analysis Workshop (35 minutes)

    Self-Directed Learning: Groups work on their projects with teacher support

    Data Organization Station
    • Clean and organize collected data
    • Identify data types (categorical/numerical)
    • Check for missing or unusual values
    • Create summary statistics
    Statistical Analysis Station
    • Calculate means, medians, and modes
    • Identify patterns and trends
    • Create appropriate graphs and charts
    • Look for correlations and relationships
    Probability & Prediction Station
    • Make predictions based on trends
    • Calculate probabilities where relevant
    • Consider confidence in predictions
    • Identify limitations of data
    ğŸ¯ Key Questions to Guide Analysis
    1. Pattern Recognition: What trends do you see in your data?
    2. Significance Testing: Are the patterns meaningful or could they be random?
    3. Cultural Context: How do your findings relate to cultural or social factors?
    4. Action Items: What recommendations emerge from your analysis?
    5. Limitations: What don't your data tell you? What are the gaps?

    Peer Consultation & Feedback (15 minutes)

    Statistical Peer Review: Groups share preliminary findings with another group for feedback

    Peer Review Protocol:
    1. Data Presentation (3 min): Show your main findings and one key graph
    2. Statistical Questions (2 min): Peers ask about methods and calculations
    3. Feedback & Suggestions (2 min): What could be clearer or more convincing?
    4. Role Swap: Repeat process with groups switching roles

    ğŸ¨ Lesson 5.3: Presentation Creation & Practice

    Focus: Creating compelling presentations that communicate statistical findings effectively

    Presentation Design Workshop (25 minutes)

    ğŸ¤ Effective Statistical Communication
    Content Structure
    1. Hook (1 min): Start with surprising statistic or compelling question
    2. Context (1 min): Why does this issue matter to our community?
    3. Methods (1 min): How did you collect and analyze data?
    4. Key Findings (3 min): What are your main discoveries?
    5. Recommendations (2 min): What should be done based on your findings?
    6. Questions (2 min): Engage audience in discussion
    Visual Design Principles
    • Clear Charts: Large, readable graphs with clear labels
    • Key Statistics: Highlight 2-3 most important numbers
    • Cultural Imagery: Include relevant and respectful cultural elements
    • Call to Action: End with specific, achievable recommendations
    • Backup Data: Have detailed analysis ready for questions

    Tools Available: Google Slides, Canva, poster materials, or interactive digital displays

    Support: Teacher circulates to provide feedback on statistical accuracy and presentation clarity

    Practice Presentations & Peer Coaching (25 minutes)

    Rehearsal Structure: Groups practice with constructive peer feedback

    Practice Session Format (8 minutes per group):
    1. Full Presentation (5 min): Present to practice audience of 2-3 other groups
    2. Feedback Round (2 min): Audience provides specific suggestions for improvement
    3. Quick Q&A (1 min): Practice handling questions about statistical methods
    Feedback Focus Areas:
    • Statistical Clarity: Are the numbers and methods clear?
    • Cultural Sensitivity: Is cultural content respectful and meaningful?
    • Audience Engagement: Does the presentation hold attention?
    • Actionable Outcomes: Are recommendations specific and realistic?
