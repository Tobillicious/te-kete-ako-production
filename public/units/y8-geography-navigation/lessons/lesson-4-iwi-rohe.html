Learning Objectives

  • Define rohe (tribal territory) and explain how it differs from colonial land divisions.
  • Identify local iwi/hap≈´ and their rohe using maps and oral histories.
  • Analyse the relationship between place names (toponymy) and geographic features/stories.
  • Understand kaitiakitanga as spatial responsibility.

Activity 1: Mapping Rohe (20 min)

  1. Display a map of Aotearoa showing iwi boundaries (e.g., Te Ara Encyclopedia or tribal rohe map).
  2. Compare to modern regional council boundaries. What overlaps? What conflicts?
  3. Students trace their local iwi rohe and research:
    • Iwi name & meaning
    • Founding waka or ancestor
    • Key geographic features (maunga, awa, moana)
    • Current kaitiaki initiatives
