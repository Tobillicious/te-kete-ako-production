Traditional Māori Navigation Mathematics Unit

Year 8 - Complete Assessment Framework

🎯 About This Rubric

This master rubric provides comprehensive assessment criteria for all aspects of the Traditional Navigation Mathematics unit. It integrates:

  • Mathematical Skills: Calculations, problem-solving, geometric reasoning
  • Cultural Understanding: Respect for mātauranga Māori and traditional knowledge
  • NZ Curriculum Alignment: Level 4 Mathematics achievement objectives
  • Holistic Assessment: Knowledge, skills, values, and cultural competency

  • Mathematical Skills & Application: 35% of total grade

  • Communication & Presentation: 20% of total grade
  • Critical Thinking & Reflection: 15% of total grade

👩‍🏫 Implementation Notes for Teachers

Using This Rubric:

  • Share rubric with students BEFORE beginning assessment tasks
  • Use rubric for both formative feedback and summative assessment
  • Consider self-assessment and peer-assessment using appropriate sections
  • Adapt criteria weightings based on specific learning objectives of each task

Cultural Assessment Considerations:

  • Consult with cultural advisors about cultural understanding criteria
  • Consider inviting kaumātua to participate in cultural assessment
  • Balance Western assessment approaches with Māori assessment values (whanaungatanga, manaakitanga, kotahitanga)
  • Recognize that cultural understanding develops over time - allow for growth

Moderation Guidelines:

  • Use exemplars to calibrate scoring across teachers
  • Conduct regular moderation meetings to ensure consistency
  • Document reasoning for borderline decisions
  • Seek second opinions on complex or borderline assessments

Providing Feedback:

  • Give specific, actionable feedback for improvement
  • Highlight strengths as well as areas for growth
  • Include culturally responsive feedback (acknowledge cultural growth)
  • Provide opportunities for reassessment where appropriate
