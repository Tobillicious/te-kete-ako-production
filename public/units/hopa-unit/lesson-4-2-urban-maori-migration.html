Urban Māori Migration: The Great Shift

Unit: Hopa - The Scholar and the People | Lesson 4.2

Year 10 | Social Studies | 75 minutes

🎯 WALT: Understand the urban Māori migration of 1950s-70s and its impact on communities

📊 The Numbers: Rural to Urban Shift

  • 1945: 75% of Māori lived in rural areas
  • 1976: 75% of Māori lived in cities (complete reversal!)
  • Migration driven by: jobs in factories, better wages, education opportunities
  • Challenges: Loss of cultural connection, language decline, urban poverty, discrimination

🎨 Activity: Migration Stories

Interview someone in your whānau or community who experienced urban migration. Ask: What did you gain? What did you lose? How did you maintain cultural identity in the city?
