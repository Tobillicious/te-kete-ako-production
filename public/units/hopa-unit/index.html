Hopa Unit

The Scholar and the People

Dr. Ngāpare Hopa - Academic Pioneer, Urban Māori Champion, Trailblazing Wahine

"Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini"

My strength is not that of an individual, but that of the collective — Hopa's research honored the collective wisdom of urban Māori communities

📖 Unit Overview

This unit explores the groundbreaking life of Dr. Ngāpare Hopa (1940-), the first Māori woman to earn a doctorate from Oxford University. A sociologist and researcher, Hopa dedicated her career to understanding and advocating for urban Māori communities during the rapid urbanization of the 1960s-80s. Students will examine her academic excellence, community-centered research, and role in shaping policies that improved Māori wellbeing in cities.

🏛️ School Values Connection:

Whaimana (Integrity)

Hopa's research maintained integrity to Māori communities by centering their voices and experiences

Whaiora (Wellbeing)

Her work directly addressed urban Māori wellbeing challenges - housing, health, education, identity

Whaiara (Rising Up)

As a wahine Māori achieving Oxford doctorate, Hopa demonstrated rising up through academic excellence
