Research WITH Communities, Not ON Them

Unit: Hopa - The Scholar and the People | Lesson 4.3

Year 10 | Social Studies | 75 minutes

🎯 WALT: Understand community-centered research methodology and why it matters for Māori research

🔬 Two Approaches to Research

❌ Research ON People (Traditional Western)

Researcher extracts data, community gets nothing back, treated as subjects not partners

✅ Research WITH People (Hopa's Approach)

Community involved in design, benefits shared, research serves community needs, honors community knowledge

📝 Activity: Design Community Research

If you were researching an issue in YOUR community (e.g., youth engagement, cultural identity, mental health), how would you do it Hopa's way? Plan: Who would you involve? What questions would you ask? How would you share results?
