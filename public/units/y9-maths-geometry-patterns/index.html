Tukutuku - Mathematical Art & Cultural Storytelling

Tukutuku are ornamental lattice panels traditionally used to decorate the interior walls of wharenui (meeting houses). Each pattern tells a story, conveys meaning, and demonstrates sophisticated mathematical principles including symmetry, tessellation, and geometric transformation.

Mathematical Concepts in Tukutuku:

  • Symmetry: Line symmetry (reflection) and rotational symmetry in traditional patterns
  • Tessellation: Repeating patterns that cover surfaces without gaps
  • Transformation: Translation, rotation, and reflection to create intricate designs
  • Proportion & Balance: Mathematical ratios creating visual harmony

💡 Big Ideas

  • Geometric transformations (rotation, reflection, translation) create patterns and designs
  • Symmetry is a fundamental principle in both mathematics and traditional Māori art
  • Tessellations combine transformations to fill space without gaps or overlaps
  • Mathematics is embedded in cultural art forms and storytelling

🎯 Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  • Identify and create reflective, rotational, and translational symmetry
  • Perform geometric transformations on 2D shapes
  • Analyze and replicate traditional tukutuku patterns mathematically
  • Create original tessellating designs using multiple transformations
  • Explain the cultural significance of geometric patterns in Māori art

Unit Lessons

1

Symmetry in Nature

Explore line symmetry and rotational symmetry in natural forms and Māori art. Identify symmetry in pūhoro (tattoo patterns) and koru (spiral forms).

🔄 Symmetry 🌿 Nature
2

Tessellations & Tukutuku

Investigate tessellations (patterns that fill space) through analysis of traditional tukutuku panels. Learn how shapes combine to create seamless designs.

🎨 Tukutuku 🔲 Tessellation
3

Rotational Symmetry

Perform rotations around a center point using angles. Explore how rotational symmetry appears in whakairo (carving) and taonga pūoro (musical instruments).

🔄 Rotation 🎨 Whakairo
4

Reflection Transformations

Perform reflections across horizontal, vertical, and diagonal lines. Analyze mirror symmetry in poutama (stairway to heaven) and niho taniwha (teeth of the taniwha) patterns.

🪞 Reflection 🪜 Poutama
5

Translation Patterns

Perform translations (slides) on coordinate grids. Create repeating border patterns found in traditional tāniko (finger-woven bands) and kowhaiwhai (rafter patterns).

➡️ Translation 🎨 Tāniko
6

Combining Transformations

Apply sequences of transformations (rotation + reflection, translation + rotation) to create complex tukutuku patterns. Understand transformation composition.

🔀 Composition 🧩 Complex Patterns
7

Tukutuku Transformations

Analyze authentic tukutuku designs mathematically. Replicate traditional patterns using precise transformations and understand their cultural narratives.

🏛️ Traditional 📐 Precision
8

Final Tukutuku Project

Design and create an original tukutuku-inspired pattern using all transformation types. Present the mathematical analysis and cultural narrative behind the design.

🎨 Creative Project 📊 Presentation ✅ Assessment
