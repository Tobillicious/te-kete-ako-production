Tukutuku - Mathematical Art & Cultural Storytelling

Tukutuku are ornamental lattice panels traditionally used to decorate the interior walls of wharenui (meeting houses). Each pattern tells a story, conveys meaning, and demonstrates sophisticated mathematical principles including symmetry, tessellation, and geometric transformation.

Mathematical Concepts in Tukutuku:

  • Symmetry: Line symmetry (reflection) and rotational symmetry in traditional patterns
  • Tessellation: Repeating patterns that cover surfaces without gaps
  • Transformation: Translation, rotation, and reflection to create intricate designs
  • Proportion & Balance: Mathematical ratios creating visual harmony

üí° Big Ideas

  • Geometric transformations (rotation, reflection, translation) create patterns and designs
  • Symmetry is a fundamental principle in both mathematics and traditional MƒÅori art
  • Tessellations combine transformations to fill space without gaps or overlaps
  • Mathematics is embedded in cultural art forms and storytelling

üéØ Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  • Perform geometric transformations on 2D shapes
  • Analyze and replicate traditional tukutuku patterns mathematically
  • Create original tessellating designs using multiple transformations
  • Explain the cultural significance of geometric patterns in MƒÅori art
