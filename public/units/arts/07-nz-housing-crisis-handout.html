    Te Urupare ki te Mate Whare: Understanding NZ's Housing Crisis

    Economic, Social, and Cultural Perspectives on Housing in Aotearoa

    📺 Video Resource⏱️ 60 minutes🎓 Year 10-13🏛️ Social Studies / Economics

    📺 Watch: Inside New Zealand's Housing Crisis

    ▶️ Watch Video on YouTube

    Investigating the causes, impacts, and potential solutions to New Zealand's housing affordability crisis

    🎯 Learning Objectives (He Whāinga Ako)

    • Understand the economic factors contributing to housing unaffordability
    • Analyze the social impacts of housing crisis on different communities
    • Recognize disproportionate impacts on Māori and Pacific peoples
    • Evaluate proposed solutions from multiple perspectives
    • Connect housing issues to concepts of home, belonging, and wellbeing

    🏠 The Housing Crisis: Key Factors

    📈 Supply & Demand

    Issue: Not enough houses being built to meet population growth

    Impact: Prices rise when demand exceeds supply

    💰 Investment Property

    Issue: Houses treated as investments rather than homes

    Impact: First-home buyers priced out of market

    🏗️ Construction Costs

    Issue: Building materials and labor expensive

    Impact: New houses cost more, fewer built

    🏦 Lending Practices

    Issue: Bank lending fuels price increases

    Impact: Debt levels rise, risk increases

    📍 Urban Planning

    Issue: Zoning restrictions limit density

    Impact: Urban sprawl, infrastructure challenges

    🌍 Global Factors

    Issue: International investment, migration patterns

    Impact: Local market affected by global forces

    🌿 Māori Housing: Specific Challenges

    Disproportionate Impact

    Māori and Pacific peoples are disproportionately affected by the housing crisis:

    • Lower home ownership rates: Māori 30% vs Pākehā 60%+
    • Overcrowding: Higher rates in Māori households
    • Housing quality: More likely to live in substandard housing
    • Health impacts: Cold, damp housing affects whānau wellbeing

    Historical Context

    Land Loss: Māori lost 95% of land through colonization, confiscation, and questionable purchases - affecting intergenerational wealth and housing security.

    Urban Migration: 1950s-70s Māori moved to cities for work but faced discrimination in housing, leading to concentrated poverty in certain areas.

    Papakāinga Housing

    Papakāinga (Māori housing on ancestral land) offers alternative model:

    • Housing on multiply-owned Māori land
    • Keeps whānau connected to whenua (land)
    • Challenges: Financing, infrastructure, legal complexity
    • Potential: Culturally-appropriate, affordable housing solution

    💬 Key Discussion Points

    Understanding the Crisis

    1. What are the main factors contributing to the housing crisis in New Zealand?
    2. How does supply and demand affect housing prices?
    3. Why has treating houses as investments created problems?

    Social Impacts

    1. How does the housing crisis affect young people and families?
    2. What is the connection between housing and health/wellbeing?
    3. Why are Māori and Pacific peoples disproportionately affected?

    Historical & Cultural Context

    1. How did historical land loss affect Māori housing security?
    2. What is papakāinga housing? What are its benefits and challenges?
    3. How does the concept of "home" differ between cultures?

    Solutions & Policy

    1. What are some potential solutions discussed in the video?
    2. Evaluate: Which solutions would be most effective? Why?
    3. What role should government play in housing?
    4. How can housing policy address historical inequities?

    🌟 Extension Activities

    Data Analysis

    Research housing statistics for your region. Create graphs showing: median house prices over time, home ownership rates by ethnicity, rental vs ownership percentages.

    Policy Proposal

    Design a housing policy that addresses both affordability AND historical inequities. Consider: supply, demand, investment, cultural needs, sustainability.

    Case Study

    Research a successful papakāinga development. What challenges did they overcome? What lessons can be applied elsewhere?

    Community Investigation

    Interview whānau members about their housing experiences across generations. How has housing changed? What challenges have they faced?

    👨‍🏫 Teacher Notes

    Sensitive Topic

    Be aware: Students may have personal/whānau experiences with housing insecurity. Create safe, respectful discussion environment.

    Cultural Context

    When discussing Māori housing:

    • Acknowledge historical land loss and its ongoing impacts
    • Recognize housing as connected to identity, belonging, and wellbeing
    • Celebrate innovative solutions like papakāinga
    • Avoid deficit narratives - focus on systemic issues, not individual choices

    Cross-Curricular Opportunities

    Economics: Supply/demand, market forces, government intervention

    Mathematics: Data analysis, graphing, statistical trends

    Geography: Urban planning, infrastructure, sustainability

    Social Studies: Inequality, policy, historical impacts

    Differentiation

    Support: Provide economic vocabulary lists, simplified graphs, structured discussion frameworks

    Extension: Comparative analysis with other countries, policy proposal development, statistical modeling
