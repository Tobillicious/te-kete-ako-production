    📐 Advanced Mathematics Unit Plan

    Tahuhu Kōrero - Patterns in Māori Art & Architecture

    Year 10 • 4 Weeks • Geometry & Algebra Integration

    Created by Kaitiaki Aronui - Demonstrating Advanced STEM-Cultural Integration

    🎯 Unit Overview

    Duration: 4 weeks (16 lessons)

    Mathematical Strands: Geometry, Algebra, Measurement

    Cultural Focus: Traditional Māori art patterns and architectural principles

    🌿 Cultural Context - "Tahuhu Kōrero"

    Tahuhu (ridgepole of a whare) represents the backbone of knowledge, while kōrero (patterns/discussions) represent the stories encoded in traditional designs.

    This unit explores how mathematical principles are embedded within traditional Māori art forms, connecting ākonga to both cultural knowledge and advanced mathematical concepts.

    🔢 Mathematical Learning Objectives

    • Geometric Transformations: Analyze rotations, reflections, and translations in traditional patterns
    • Algebraic Modeling: Create equations to describe pattern sequences and growth
    • Trigonometry Applications: Calculate angles and proportions in traditional designs
    • Statistical Analysis: Investigate frequency and distribution in cultural artifacts
    • Complex Problem Solving: Design mathematically accurate cultural artworks

    📊 NZ Curriculum Alignment - Mathematics Level 5

    • Geometry & Measurement: Deduce and use the angle properties of polygons and circles
    • Algebra: Relate graphs, tables, and equations to linear and simple quadratic relationships
    • Statistics: Plan and conduct investigations using statistical methods
    • Mathematical Processes: Use multiple representations and mathematical modeling

    🗓️ Week-by-Week Breakdown

    📅 Week 1: "Ngā Taonga Tuku Iho" - Traditional Pattern Analysis

    Lesson 1-2: Introduction to Cultural Geometry

    Focus Patterns: Koru (spiral), Kowhaiwhai (scroll patterns), Pātiki (flounder)

    Mathematical Concepts: Symmetry types, transformation geometry, angle measurement

    🔍 Key Mathematical Investigations:
    • Identify line and rotational symmetry in traditional koru patterns
    • Measure and classify angles in kowhaiwhai scroll work
    • Analyze geometric transformations in pātiki designs

    Lesson 3-4: Algebraic Pattern Modeling

    KORU SPIRAL EQUATION: r = a × θ^b where r = radius, θ = angle, a & b = cultural constants KOWHAIWHAI SEQUENCE: T(n) = 4n + 2 (for traditional 4-fold patterns)
    • Derive algebraic expressions for pattern growth sequences
    • Create mathematical models of traditional spiral forms
    • Use coordinate geometry to map traditional designs

    📅 Week 2: "Whare Tangata" - Architectural Mathematics

    Lesson 5-6: Structural Geometry & Trigonometry

    Focus: Traditional whare construction principles and proportional systems

    Mathematical Concepts: Trigonometric ratios, structural load calculations, proportional relationships

    🏗️ Applied Mathematics:
    • Calculate optimal roof angles for traditional structures
    • Use trigonometry to determine support beam placements
    • Analyze proportional relationships in traditional architecture
    • Model structural loads using mathematical principles

    Lesson 7-8: Sacred Proportions & Golden Ratios

    TRADITIONAL WHARE PROPORTIONS: Height : Width : Length = 1 : 1.618 : 2.618 (Investigating connections to φ - Golden Ratio) φ = (1 + √5) / 2 ≈ 1.618

    📅 Week 3: "Tā Moko Mathematics" - Complex Pattern Generation

    Lesson 9-10: Fractal Geometry in Traditional Art

    Cultural Sensitivity Note: We study mathematical principles in traditional marking patterns while acknowledging tā moko as sacred cultural practice belonging to specific iwi and individuals.

    🌀 Advanced Mathematical Concepts:
    • Explore fractal properties in traditional curved patterns
    • Calculate iteration sequences in repeating motifs
    • Use recursive functions to model pattern complexity
    • Analyze scaling properties and self-similarity

    Lesson 11-12: Digital Pattern Creation

    • Use mathematical software to recreate traditional patterns
    • Program algorithmic generation of cultural designs
    • Apply transformation matrices to pattern manipulation

    📅 Week 4: "Whakatōhea" - Integration & Assessment

    Lesson 13-14: Cross-Cultural Mathematical Connections

    Compare mathematical principles in Māori art with other indigenous mathematical systems worldwide - Pacific Island patterns, Aboriginal Australian designs, Native American geometric art

    Lesson 15-16: Culminating Assessment Project

    Students create an original artwork incorporating authentic mathematical modeling of traditional patterns, with full mathematical justification and cultural respect protocols.

    🎓 Assessment Strategy

    📊 Multi-Modal Assessment Portfolio

    Component 1: Mathematical Investigation (40%)

    • Choose a traditional pattern and conduct full mathematical analysis
    • Create algebraic models and geometric proofs
    • Present findings using multiple mathematical representations
    • Cultural Requirement: Research and acknowledge the cultural significance respectfully

    Component 2: Digital Creation Project (35%)

    • Use mathematical software to recreate and modify traditional patterns
    • Program algorithmic generation using mathematical principles
    • Document mathematical reasoning behind design choices
    • Innovation Challenge: Create new patterns following traditional mathematical rules

    Component 3: Cultural-Mathematical Presentation (25%)

    • Present research on mathematical principles in indigenous art globally
    • Demonstrate connections between cultural knowledge and advanced mathematics
    • Reflect on how mathematical analysis enhances appreciation of cultural artforms

    🎯 Differentiation Strategies

    Support for Lower Achievers: Simplified pattern analysis, guided investigations, peer tutoring, visual aids

    Extension for Higher Achievers: Complex fractal analysis, 3D modeling, calculus applications, independent research projects

    Cultural Connections: Invite community experts, connect with local iwi education coordinators, optional marae visits

    🔬 Advanced Mathematical Extensions

    🌌 For High-Achieving Students

    • Calculus Applications: Use derivatives to analyze curve properties in koru spirals
    • Complex Numbers: Model rotational transformations using Euler's formula
    • Linear Algebra: Create transformation matrices for pattern manipulation
    • Statistics: Analyze frequency distributions in museum artifact collections
    • Programming: Create interactive pattern generators using mathematical algorithms
    ADVANCED EXTENSIONS: Koru Derivative Analysis: r = ae^(bθ) → dr/dθ = abe^(bθ) Transformation Matrices: [x'] = [cosθ -sinθ] [x] [y'] [sinθ cosθ] [y] Fractal Dimension: D = log(N) / log(1/r)
