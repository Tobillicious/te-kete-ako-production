    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Restoration Proposal Rubric

    📊 Ecological Restoration Proposal Assessment Rubric

    Type: Project-Based Assessment | Curriculum Level: 4-5 (Years 9-10)

    Assessment Overview

    Students design a comprehensive ecological restoration plan, demonstrating understanding of:

    • Ecosystem assessment and analysis
    • Evidence-based conservation planning
    • Kaitiakitanga principles in practice
    • Community engagement and partnerships
    • Project management and realistic implementation

    Assessment Rubric (75 points total)

    Criterion Not Yet
    0-7    		 Achieved
    8-10    		 Merit
    11-13    		 Excellence
    14-15
    1. Site Assessment (15 pts)
    Current state analysis & historical context    		 Limited or superficial analysis Adequate description of site conditions Thorough analysis with historical context Comprehensive assessment with multiple data sources
    2. Goals & Objectives (15 pts)
    SMART goals that are achievable    		 Vague or unrealistic goals Clear goals with basic measures Well-defined SMART goals Ambitious yet achievable goals with detailed metrics
    3. Restoration Methods (15 pts)
    Evidence-based approaches    		 Basic or inappropriate methods Appropriate methods clearly explained Well-researched methods with scientific justification Innovative approaches supported by best practice research
    4. Kaitiakitanga Integration (10 pts)
    Māori worldview & practices    		 Minimal or tokenistic inclusion Basic integration of guardianship principles Thoughtful incorporation of mātauranga Māori Deep integration showing consultation with mana whenua
    5. Timeline & Feasibility (10 pts)
    Realistic planning & phasing    		 Unrealistic or missing timeline Reasonable timeline with basic phases Well-planned timeline with contingencies Detailed, realistic project plan adaptable to challenges
    6. Budget & Resources (10 pts)
    Financial planning & sourcing    		 Minimal or unrealistic budget Basic budget with main costs identified Detailed budget with realistic funding sources Comprehensive financial plan with multiple funding strategies

    Grading Scale

    • Excellence: 68-75 points (90-100%)
    • Merit: 60-67 points (80-89%)
    • Achieved: 45-59 points (60-79%)
    • Not Yet Achieved: 0-44 points (below 60%)

    Required Components Checklist

    Proposal must include ALL of the following:

    Presentation Options

    Students may choose format:

    • 📄 Written Report: 2000-2500 words with appendices
    • 🎤 Oral Presentation: 12-15 minutes with slides
    • 📊 Poster/Display: Exhibition-style with visual elements
    • 🎬 Video Proposal: 8-10 minute documentary style
    • 💻 Interactive Website: Multi-page digital proposal

    All formats must include ALL required components above!

    Bonus Points Opportunities (+5 points each, max +10)

    • 🏆 Community Partnership: Document contact/consultation with real conservation organization or iwi
    • 🏆 Pilot Action: Implement small-scale pilot project (e.g., plant 10 natives, install 1 trap) with photographic evidence
    • 🏆 Expert Review: Get feedback from DOC ranger, conservation biologist, or kaitiaki and incorporate their suggestions
    • 🏆 Citizen Science: Collect and analyze actual field data from proposed site

    🎯 Teacher Implementation Guide

    Scaffolding for Different Learners:

    • Support: Provide template with sections pre-labeled, word banks for technical terms, simplified budget template
    • Core: Use standard proposal template as provided
    • Extension: Require additional sections (risk assessment, stakeholder analysis, 10-year monitoring plan)

    Assessment Tips:

    • Provide rubric at project start - students should self-assess before submission
    • Schedule checkpoint conferences at weeks 2 and 4
    • Use peer review with structured protocols
    • Consider inviting community members to hear presentations
    • Best proposals could be shared with local conservation groups!

    Time Allocation:

    • Week 1-2: Site selection, research, assessment
    • Week 3-4: Methods development, budget planning
    • Week 5: Drafting and peer review
    • Week 6: Revisions and final presentation prep
    • Week 7: Presentations and submission

    Real-World Connections:

    • Partner with local conservation groups for site suggestions
    • Invite DOC staff or iwi kaitiaki as guest speakers
    • Field trip to successful restoration project
    • Consider actually implementing best proposals as school project!
    • Submit excellence proposals to local councils or environmental awards

    📊 Assessment Tools | Ngā Taputapu Aromatawai

    ✅ Formative Assessment (During Learning)

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    Quick Check: Thumbs up/sideways/down for understanding

    🎯 Self-Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Not Yet Getting There Got It!
    I understand the key concepts ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can explain it to others ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can apply it to new situations ☐ ☐ ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
