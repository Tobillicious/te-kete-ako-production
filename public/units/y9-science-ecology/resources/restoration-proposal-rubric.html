Ecosystem Restoration Proposal Assessment Rubric

Evaluates scientific validity, feasibility, and cultural integrity of student restoration proposals.

Criteria Excellent (7-8) Proficient (5-6) Developing (3-4) Emerging (1-2)
Scientific Evidence & Data Proposal grounded in comprehensive field data; cites specific observations, measurements, species counts; identifies clear ecological relationships. Uses field data to justify actions; some quantitative evidence; shows understanding of ecosystem interactions. Limited data cited; mostly general observations; weak connection between evidence and recommendations. No clear data support; recommendations appear arbitrary; minimal scientific reasoning.
Ecological Understanding Demonstrates systems thinking; addresses root causes; considers cascading effects, feedback loops, keystone species; shows long-term perspective. Understands key ecological concepts; considers multiple species/factors; some systems awareness. Basic understanding; focuses on 1-2 species/issues; limited awareness of interconnections. Superficial understanding; treats ecosystem as simple; ignores complexity.
Feasibility & Planning Realistic timeline, budget, resources; identifies stakeholders; addresses potential obstacles; phased implementation plan. Generally feasible; considers resources and timeline; identifies key partners. Vague plan; unrealistic elements; limited consideration of practical constraints. Impractical or impossible; no clear plan; ignores real-world limits.
Kaitiakitanga Integration Deep cultural grounding; consults iwi/hap≈´ perspectives; applies tikanga throughout; balances science + mƒÅtauranga; shows relational ethics. Meaningful cultural connections; respectful language; considers MƒÅori values in decision-making. Superficial cultural references; kaitiakitanga mentioned but not applied; minimal cultural depth. No cultural consideration OR tokenistic; disrespectful or appropriative language.
Communication Quality Professional presentation; clear, persuasive writing; effective visuals; accessible to diverse audiences; polished. Well-organised; clear explanations; good visuals; minor errors; generally accessible. Somewhat unclear; basic visuals; several errors; hard to follow in places. Poorly organised; confusing; many errors; inadequate visuals.
Innovation & Creativity Novel, well-reasoned approach; shows originality while respecting tradition; considers multiple solutions. Some creative elements; thoughtful approach; shows personal investment. Mostly conventional; limited originality; basic effort. Generic or copied; minimal personal input.

Scoring Guide

  • 42-48 points: Excellent (A) - Publication-ready proposal
  • 30-41 points: Proficient (B) - Strong work with minor refinements needed
  • 18-29 points: Developing (C) - Good foundation, needs significant development
  • 6-17 points: Emerging (D/E) - Requires revision with teacher support
