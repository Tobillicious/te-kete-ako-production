    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    Possum Impact Lab Sheet

    🦡 Possum Impact Investigation Lab Sheet

    Purpose: Investigate the ecological impact of possums on New Zealand native ecosystems

    Background: The Possum Problem

    Australian brushtail possums were introduced to New Zealand in 1837 for the fur trade. Today, there are an estimated 30 million possums devastating our native forests and wildlife. They:

    • Eat up to 300g of vegetation per night (21,000 tonnes per year!)
    • Damage native trees, especially preferred species like rata and kohekohe
    • Compete with native birds for food and nesting sites
    • Prey on native bird eggs and chicks
    • Spread bovine tuberculosis to livestock and wildlife

    How does possum control relate to kaitiakitanga (guardianship)?

    🎯 Teacher Notes

    Safety & Ethics:

    • If visiting trap lines, ensure traps are clearly marked and students maintain safe distance
    • Do NOT handle dead possums without proper PPE (TB risk)
    • Discuss ethics of pest control sensitively - some students may have strong views
    • Acknowledge the conflict between animal welfare and conservation

    Site Selection:

    • Contact DOC or local conservation groups for suitable comparison sites
    • ZEALANDIA or similar fenced sanctuaries offer excellent "with control" sites
    • Regional parks often have both controlled and uncontrolled areas
    • Alternative: Use published data if field visits aren't possible

    Extensions:

    • Investigate possum population dynamics (birth rates, carrying capacity)
    • Research alternative control methods (fertility control, biocontrol)
    • Economic analysis: cost of control vs. agricultural/conservation benefits
    • Debate: Should NZ aim for complete possum eradication?

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
