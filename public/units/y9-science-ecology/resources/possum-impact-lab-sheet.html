Aim

To assess possum browsing damage on native plants and predict long-term forest health impacts.

Background

Possums (introduced from Australia) browse heavily on native trees (especially rƒÅtƒÅ, northern rƒÅtƒÅ, mistletoe). They prefer new shoots and flowers, preventing seed production and killing canopy trees. Approx. 30 million possums consume 21,000 tonnes of vegetation nightly in NZ.

Method

  1. Select study area: Native forest patch with known possum activity (look for bite marks, droppings, scratches).
  2. Establish transect: 50m line through forest.
  3. Sample trees: Every 5m, identify nearest tree and record:
    • Species (Te Reo + English)
    • Height estimate
    • Evidence of possum browse (bite marks on leaves/shoots)
    • Browse severity (0=none, 1=light, 2=moderate, 3=severe)
    • Tree health (healthy / stressed / dying)
  4. Count signs: Possum droppings, claw marks on trunks, chewed branches on ground.
  5. Photo evidence: Take 3-5 photos showing browse damage.
