Y9 Ecology Unit: Summative Assessment Rubric

Comprehensive rubric for portfolio assessment (field report + restoration proposal + presentation).

Component Weight Criteria for Proficiency (5-6 pts)
Field Report Quality 25% Systematic data collection; clear method; identifies 5+ species; constructs food web; scientific language; kaitiakitanga perspective integrated.
Restoration Proposal 30% Evidence-based recommendations; feasible plan with timeline/budget; stakeholder identification; balances ecological + cultural factors; shows systems thinking.
Presentation 20% Clear communication; engages audience; effective visuals; answers questions confidently; respects cultural protocols in delivery.
Cultural Integration 15% Applies kaitiakitanga authentically (not tokenistic); consults community perspectives; uses Te Reo appropriately; upholds tikanga; cites iwi knowledge respectfully.
Scientific Rigour 10% Accurate ecological concepts; appropriate methodology; valid conclusions; acknowledges limitations; uses evidence to support claims.

Overall Grading Scale

GradePointsDescriptor
A (Excellence)42-48Exceeds expectations in all areas; ready for community presentation
B (Proficient)30-41Meets all criteria; strong work with minor areas for growth
C (Developing)18-29Meets some criteria; shows understanding but needs development
D/E (Emerging)6-17Does not yet meet criteria; requires revision and resubmission

Moderation & Cultural Validation

Before finalizing grades, seek cultural validation from school's Māori cultural advisor or local kaumātua for proposals that involve iwi knowledge, sacred sites, or culturally significant species. Student work should be celebrated, not exploited—consider how to share excellent work respectfully.
