    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Final Assessment Rubric

    📊 Unit Summative Assessment Rubric - Y9 Ecology

    Purpose: Final assessment combining all unit learnings

    Assessment Components

    Students demonstrate mastery through CHOICE of presentation format:

    • 🎤 Oral presentation with visual aids
    • 📄 Written report (1500-2000 words)
    • 🎬 Video documentary (5-7 minutes)
    • 📊 Interactive display/poster
    • 💻 Website or digital resource

    Assessment Rubric (60 points total)

    Criterion (Weight) Not Achieved
    0-5    		 Achieved
    6-8    		 Merit
    9-11    		 Excellence
    12-15
    Ecological Understanding (15 pts)
    Demonstrates knowledge of ecosystems, biodiversity, food webs    		 Basic or inaccurate concepts Correct understanding of key concepts Thorough understanding with connections Sophisticated ecological thinking; systems perspective
    Conservation Knowledge (15 pts)
    Understanding of threats, conservation methods, solutions    		 Limited awareness of issues Clear understanding of main threats and conservation approaches Comprehensive knowledge of multiple conservation strategies Expert-level understanding with innovative solutions proposed
    Kaitiakitanga Integration (10 pts)
    Connection to Māori worldview and practices    		 Superficial or absent Basic connection to guardianship principles Thoughtful integration showing understanding Powerful synthesis of mātauranga Māori and Western science
    Research & Evidence (10 pts)
    Use of credible sources and data    		 Limited or unreliable sources Good range of credible sources Excellent research from multiple perspectives Outstanding research including primary sources, expert consultation
    Presentation Quality (10 pts)
    Clarity, organization, professionalism    		 Unclear or poorly organized Well-structured and clear Professional quality engaging audience Publication/broadcast quality inspiring action

    Grading Scale

    • Excellence: 54-60 points (90-100%)
    • Merit: 48-53 points (80-89%)
    • Achieved: 36-47 points (60-79%)
    • Not Yet Achieved: 0-35 points (below 60%)

    🎯 Teacher Note

    Provide students with this rubric at the START of the unit so they understand expectations. Use it for both formative feedback and final summative grading. Consider student self-assessment against criteria before final submission.

    📊 Assessment Tools | Ngā Taputapu Aromatawai

    ✅ Formative Assessment (During Learning)

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    Quick Check: Thumbs up/sideways/down for understanding

    🎯 Self-Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Not Yet Getting There Got It!
    I understand the key concepts ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can explain it to others ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can apply it to new situations ☐ ☐ ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
