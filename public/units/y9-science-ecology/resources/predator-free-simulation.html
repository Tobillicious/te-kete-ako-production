    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Predator Free Simulation

    🎮 Predator Free Simulation Activity

    Purpose: Interactive simulation exploring predator-prey dynamics in NZ ecosystems

    Game Overview: Predator Free 2050

    New Zealand has an ambitious goal: become completely free of introduced predators (rats, stoats, possums) by 2050. In this simulation, you'll manage a conservation area and make strategic decisions to protect native species.

    Your challenge: Increase native bird populations from 100 to 500 within 20 "years" (rounds) while managing limited resources.

    Setup: Choose Your Conservation Area

    Select site type: (Each has different challenges!)

    Island Sanctuary

    Easier to fence, fewer predators but expensive

    Forest Reserve

    Large area, medium difficulty, ongoing predator pressure

    Urban Park

    Community support, pest cats/rats, lower costs

    Mountain Sanctuary

    Remote, challenging access, stoat problems

    Game Mechanics

    Resources Available Each Round:

    • 💰 Budget: $50,000 per year
    • �� Volunteers: 20 person-days
    • ⏰ Time: Must make 3 decisions per round

    Conservation Actions (Choose wisely!):

    ActionCostVolunteersEffect
    Set Traps$5K5 daysReduce predators by 20%
    Predator Fence$30K2 daysBlock 80% of predator incursions (one-time)
    Aerial 1080$20K1 dayEliminate 70% predators instantly (lasts 3 years)
    Nest Boxes$3K8 days+15% bird breeding success
    Plant Natives$4K10 days+10% habitat quality (long-term)
    Supplementary Feeding$2K3 days+5% bird survival that year
    Volunteer Training$1K5 days+10 volunteers next round
    Community Engagement$3K4 days+$10K funding next round

    Gameplay Tracker: 20 Rounds

    Record your strategy and results each round:

    Round 1 (Starting State)

    Bird Population:100
    Predator Population:High
    Budget Available:$50,000
    Volunteers:20 days

    Actions Chosen:

    1. __________________________________________ (Cost: $________, Volunteers: _____ days)
    2. __________________________________________ (Cost: $________, Volunteers: _____ days)
    3. __________________________________________ (Cost: $________, Volunteers: _____ days)

    Results:

    • Bird population change: _____ → _____
    • Predator population: _____
    • Budget remaining: $_____

    Teacher Note: Copy this round template for rounds 2-20, or create a spreadsheet for tracking. Alternatively, use online simulation tools or board game format.

    Random Events (Roll dice or draw cards each round)

    Good Events (1-3 on d6):

    • 🌧️ Excellent Breeding Season: +20% bird population
    • 💰 Government Grant: +$20,000 bonus funding
    • 👥 Community Support Surge: +15 volunteer days
    • 📰 Positive Media Coverage: Next community engagement action costs half

    Bad Events (4-5 on d6):

    • 🌪️ Severe Storm: Damage to infrastructure, lose $10K
    • 🦡 Predator Invasion: Predator population surges +50%
    • 🦅 Avian Disease Outbreak: -15% bird population
    • ⚠️ Funding Cut: Budget reduced to $40K this round

    Neutral (6 on d6):

    • No special event this round

    Game End: Evaluation

    Final Results (After 20 Rounds):

    Final Bird Population:_________
    Goal Achievement:_____ / 500 (Target)
    Final Predator Level:_________
    Total $ Spent:$_________

    Performance Rating:

    • 🏆 Excellent (500+ birds): Predator Free Success!
    • ⭐ Good (350-499 birds): Strong Conservation Progress
    • ✅ Satisfactory (200-349 birds): Positive Impact
    • ⚠️ Needs Improvement (<200 birds): Strategy Rethink Needed

    Reflection Questions

    1. What was your overall strategy? Did it work?

    2. What were the most effective actions? Least effective?

    3. How did you balance short-term vs. long-term actions?

    4. If you played again, what would you do differently?

    5. How realistic is Predator Free 2050? What challenges does NZ face in real life?

    🎯 Teacher Implementation Guide

    Formats to Use:

    • Board Game: Physical tokens, dice, cards - great for kinesthetic learners
    • Spreadsheet: Excel/Google Sheets with formulas to auto-calculate results
    • Digital App: Use coding platforms to create interactive version
    • Team Competition: Groups compete for highest bird population

    Learning Extensions:

    • Research real Predator Free projects (ZEALANDIA, island sanctuaries)
    • Guest speaker from DOC or Predator Free 2050 organization
    • Field trip to see predator traps or fenced sanctuary
    • Debate: Is complete predator eradication achievable? Desirable?
    • Create promotional campaign for real local predator control project

    Curriculum Links:

    • Science: Ecology, predator-prey relationships, conservation biology
    • Math: Data tracking, graphing, percentage calculations, budgeting
    • Social Studies: Environmental management, resource allocation, community action
    • Te Reo Māori: Environmental vocabulary, kaitiakitanga concepts

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
