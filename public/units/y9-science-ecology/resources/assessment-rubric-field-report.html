Ecosystem Field Report Assessment Rubric

For Year 9 Science Ecology Unit field investigations. Assesses scientific rigour + kaitiakitanga integration.

Criteria Emerging (1-2) Developing (3-4) Proficient (5-6) Excellent (7-8)
Data Collection & Method Observations incomplete or unclear; method not described. Basic observations recorded; method described but lacks detail. Systematic data collection; clear method with justification; some quantitative data. Comprehensive, systematic data with replicable method; quantitative + qualitative; addresses limitations.
Ecological Analysis Identifies 1-2 species; limited understanding of interactions. Identifies 3-4 species; describes simple food chain; some awareness of factors. Identifies 5+ species with roles (producer/consumer); constructs food web; explains 2-3 interactions. Detailed analysis of ecosystem structure; complex food web; identifies keystone species, limiting factors, energy flow.
Kaitiakitanga Integration Minimal or superficial mention of kaitiakitanga; no connection to findings. Acknowledges kaitiakitanga; basic connection to observations. Applies kaitiakitanga to analysis; considers Māori values in recommendations; respectful language. Deep integration; uses mātauranga to interpret data; proposes actions grounded in both science and tikanga; consults community perspectives.
Communication & Presentation Unclear or disorganised; many errors; minimal visuals. Generally clear but some confusion; few errors; basic visuals (1-2 diagrams/photos). Well-organised; clear scientific language; effective visuals (3+ annotated diagrams/photos); minimal errors. Professional quality; precise terminology; compelling visuals with captions; polished; accessible to non-specialists.
Recommendations & Action No recommendations OR unrealistic suggestions. Vague recommendations; limited justification. Specific, evidence-based recommendations; considers feasibility; some stakeholder awareness. Actionable plan with timeline, resources, stakeholders; balances ecological + cultural + social factors; shows systems thinking.

Scoring Guide

  • 32-40 points: Excellent (A)
  • 24-31 points: Proficient (B)
  • 16-23 points: Developing (C)
  • 8-15 points: Emerging (D) - Revise & resubmit with teacher support
