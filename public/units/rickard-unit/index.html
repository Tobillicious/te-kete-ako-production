📖 Unit Overview

This unit explores the remarkable courage of Tuaiwa (Eva) Rickard (1925-1997), a Tainui Awhiro activist who led the 1978 Raglan Golf Course occupation to reclaim land stolen from her people during WWII. Through peaceful protest, civil disobedience, and decades of persistence, Rickard helped return Te Kōpua to Māori ownership - proving that principled resistance can achieve justice. Students will examine protest as a tool for change, the costs of activism, and the ethics of civil disobedience.

🏛️ School Values Connection:

Whaimana (Integrity)

Rickard maintained integrity to her people's land rights despite arrests, criticism, and personal cost

Whaiora (Wellbeing)

Recognized that land = wellbeing - returning whenua was essential for community healing

Whaiara (Rising Up)

Rose up against injustice through peaceful protest - inspiring modern activism movements
