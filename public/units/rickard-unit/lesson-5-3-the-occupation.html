The Occupation: 507 Days of Peaceful Protest

Unit: Rickard - The Price of Protest | Lesson 5.3

Year 10 | Social Studies | 75 minutes

🎯 WALT: Analyze the tactics, challenges, and community support during the 1978 golf course occupation

✊ May 1978: The Occupation Begins

Eva Rickard and supporters walked onto Raglan Golf Course and refused to leave. They set up camp, planted gardens, built structures - reclaiming their ancestral land through physical presence.

Protest Tactics:

  • ✊ Occupation: Physical presence on land - "We're not leaving until it's returned"
  • 📰 Media campaigns: National TV coverage, newspaper articles, public sympathy
  • 🤝 Community support: Food, supplies, legal help from supporters nationwide
  • 🎵 Cultural assertion: Waiata, karakia, flying Tainui flags - this is MĀORI land
  • ⚖️ Legal challenges: Court cases arguing confiscation was illegal

🚨 The Costs: Arrests and Persecution

  • Multiple arrests for trespassing (on their OWN ancestral land!)
  • Court fines, legal costs
  • Media portrayal as "troublemakers"
  • Personal sacrifice - time away from family, work, safety
  • But they persisted: 507 days of occupation

Activity: Protest Strategy Analysis

Create a table evaluating each protest tactic: What was the goal? Was it effective? What were the risks? Would you have done the same?
