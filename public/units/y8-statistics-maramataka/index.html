MÄramataka - Indigenous Data Science

MÄramataka is the traditional MÄori lunar calendar, dividing each month into approximately 30 named nights. Each night has distinct characteristics that guide activities such as planting, fishing, gathering kai (food), and understanding human health and wellbeing.

This unit positions mÄramataka as empirical pattern observation - essentially, traditional data science! By observing the moon's phases over generations, MÄori developed a sophisticated system for predicting optimal times for various activities.

Statistical Concepts in MÄramataka:

  • Data Collection: Systematic observation of moon phases and associated phenomena
  • Pattern Recognition: Identifying cycles and correlations in natural systems
  • Prediction: Using historical patterns to forecast future conditions
  • Variables: Moon phase, tides, weather, plant growth, fish behavior
  • Long-term Trends: Generational knowledge passed down through observation
