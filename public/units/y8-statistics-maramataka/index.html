Māramataka - Indigenous Data Science

Māramataka is the traditional Māori lunar calendar, dividing each month into approximately 30 named nights. Each night has distinct characteristics that guide activities such as planting, fishing, gathering kai (food), and understanding human health and wellbeing.

This unit positions māramataka as empirical pattern observation - essentially, traditional data science! By observing the moon's phases over generations, Māori developed a sophisticated system for predicting optimal times for various activities.

Statistical Concepts in Māramataka:

  • Data Collection: Systematic observation of moon phases and associated phenomena
  • Pattern Recognition: Identifying cycles and correlations in natural systems
  • Prediction: Using historical patterns to forecast future conditions
  • Variables: Moon phase, tides, weather, plant growth, fish behavior
  • Long-term Trends: Generational knowledge passed down through observation

💡 Big Ideas

  • Data collection and pattern recognition are fundamental to both modern statistics and mātauranga Māori
  • The lunar cycle influences natural phenomena in measurable ways
  • Statistical thinking involves observation, recording, analyzing, and predicting
  • Traditional knowledge systems are evidence-based and empirical

🎯 Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  • Describe the māramataka system and its purpose
  • Collect and record data systematically over time
  • Identify patterns and trends in moon phase data
  • Create graphs and charts to visualize statistical data
  • Make predictions based on observed patterns
  • Connect traditional Māori knowledge with statistical thinking
