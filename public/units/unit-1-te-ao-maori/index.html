Unit 1: Te Ao Māori Foundations

Explore learning through the worldview of mātauranga Māori—integrating indigenous knowledge systems across Science, Mathematics, English, and Digital Technologies.

14 Cross-Curricular Lessons Quality 90-95 100% Cultural Integration

🌿 Kaupapa | Big Ideas

This groundbreaking unit positions mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) not as an "add-on" but as a primary lens for learning across all subjects.

🧠 Pedagogical Approach
    🔬 Science Through Kaitiakitanga (5 Lessons)
    1

    Renewable Energy & Māori Innovation

    Focus: Geothermal, solar, wind energy through kaitiakitanga lens

    Explore how Māori communities lead NZ's renewable energy transition—Ngāti Awa geothermal, Tūaropaki Trust hydrogen projects. Connect traditional resource stewardship to modern sustainability.

    Science Environmental Q90
    2

    Climate Change Through Te Taiao Māori Lens

    Focus: Climate science meets traditional environmental knowledge

    Analyze climate data while understanding Māori perspectives on environmental change. Connect carbon cycles to whakapapa (genealogy), climate action to kaitiakitanga responsibilities.

    Science Climate Q90
    3

    Scientific Method Using Traditional Māori Practices

    Focus: Science methodology through mātauranga frameworks

    Discover how rongoā Māori (traditional medicine) research uses observation, experimentation, knowledge transmission—all core scientific methods. Dual knowledge systems validated.

    Science Methodology Q90
    4

    Physics of Traditional Māori Instruments

    Focus: Sound physics through pūtōrino, pūkāea, nguru

    Investigate sound waves, resonance, pitch using traditional instruments. Students measure frequency, analyze harmonics—discovering sophisticated acoustic engineering in ancestral taonga pūoro.

    Science Physics Arts Q92
    5

    Māori Migration: Science & History

    Focus: Polynesian navigation as applied science

    Study the Great Migration using astronomy, ocean science, environmental observation. Connect star navigation to modern physics, wave patterns to oceanography, waka design to engineering.

    Science Social Studies Q90

    ✍️ English & Literacy (4 Lessons)

    6

    Narrative Writing: Pūrākau Framework

    Focus: Traditional story structures in modern writing

    Master the 4-part pūrākau framework (Kupu whakataki→Huarahi→Taumata→Hokinga) to create compelling narratives. Apply ancestral storytelling wisdom to contemporary creative writing.

    English Creative Writing Q92
    7

    Poetry Analysis: Māori Literary Traditions

    Focus: Waiata, mōteatea, and oral poetry forms

    Analyze poetry through Māori literary lenses—exploring rhythm, metaphor, cultural symbolism. Understand how oral traditions shape poetic structure and meaning-making.

    English Poetry Q94
    8

    Argumentative Writing: Contemporary Māori Issues

    Focus: Persuasive writing on rangatiratanga, land rights, language revitalization

    Develop PEEL paragraphs and evidence-based arguments about real Māori issues. Research Treaty settlements, analyze policy impacts, write with conviction and cultural understanding.

    English Social Studies Q95
    9

    Debate Skills: Māori Oratory Traditions

    Focus: Whaikōrero frameworks for persuasive speaking

    Learn formal debate using whaikōrero (oratory) structures. Master tikanga for respectful disagreement, persuasive techniques from marae traditions, voice projection and presence.

    English Oral Language Q94

    💻 Digital Technologies & AI Ethics (3 Lessons)

    10

    AI Ethics Through Māori Data Sovereignty

    Focus: Te Mana Raraunga principles for AI development

    Investigate AI bias, facial recognition ethics, algorithmic justice through Māori data sovereignty frameworks. Who owns data? Who benefits? How can AI serve indigenous communities fairly?

    Digital Tech Ethics Q95
    11

    Game Development with Cultural Themes

    Focus: Create games that teach cultural values

    Design interactive games using Scratch/JavaScript that teach kaitiakitanga, whanaungatanga, or Māori history. Code with purpose—technology as a vehicle for cultural transmission.

    Digital Tech Coding Q90
    12

    Media Literacy: Analyzing Māori Representation

    Focus: Critical media analysis with cultural lens

    Deconstruct media portrayals of Māori people—news, film, advertising. Identify stereotypes, celebrate authentic representation, develop critical digital literacy for social justice.

    Digital Tech English Q92

    🎯 Career Pathways & Applications (2 Lessons)

    13

    Career Pathways in STEM for Māori Students

    Focus: STEM futures through cultural identity

    Explore STEM careers where Māori students can thrive while honoring cultural identity. Profile Māori scientists, engineers, researchers. Connect passions to pathways.

    Cross-Curricular STEM Careers Q95

    Cultural Assessment Principle: Value process and relationship-building, not just products.

    🏆 Pedagogical Excellence:
      🌿 Cultural Authenticity:
        Recommended Delivery:

        Option A: Integrated Cultural Unit (6-7 weeks)

        Teach all 14 lessons sequentially as a dedicated "Te Ao Māori" unit at start of year—establishing cultural foundation for all subsequent learning.

        Option B: Distributed Throughout Year

        Integrate lessons into subject units—science lessons during science term, English lessons during literacy term. Maintains cultural thread while fitting existing planning.

        Option C: Student Choice Pathways

        Students choose 5-7 lessons aligned with their interests/strengths, creating personalized cultural learning journey while meeting curriculum objectives.

        🌿 Cultural Protocol: Before starting unit, consult local iwi/Māori department for culturally-specific content (local place names, whakapapa connections, appropriate protocols). Invite kaumātua guest speakers where possible.
