👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

Tuakana-Teina Approach:

  1. Two stars: What did they do well?
  2. One wish: What could be improved?
  3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

---## **Teacher Implementation Guide**### **Lesson Breakdown****Lesson 1:**- Introduce contemporary Māori issues (videos, articles, discussion).- Group research on selected topics.**Lesson 2:**- Teach argument structure (thesis, evidence, counterarguments).- Begin drafting essays.**Lesson 3:**- Peer review and feedback.- Final revisions and submission.---## **Assessment Suggestions**- **Formative:** Peer feedback, draft check-ins.- **Summative:** Final essay graded using rubric.---## **Extension Activities**- **Community Engagement:** Interview local Māori leaders.- **Creative Response:** Write a persuasive speech or op-ed.---## **Adaptations**- **Visual Learners:** Use infographics or mind maps.- **Kinesthetic Learners:** Role-play debates.
