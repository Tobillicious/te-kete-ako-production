    🌿 Cultural Context | Horopaki Ahurea

    Whakataukī

    "He tukutuku whakairo, he mea hanga nā te hinengaro"

    "A woven pattern is something created by the mind"

    🎨 Mathematics in Māori Art

    This wordsearch includes terms from both Western mathematics and Māori artistic traditions. Tukutuku panels and kōwhaiwhai patterns demonstrate how our tūpuna (ancestors) used mathematical thinking - sequences, symmetry, and rules - in their creative work long before formal algebra was introduced to Aotearoa.

    🛡️ Cultural Note

    When we study patterns in Māori art, we honor the mathematical sophistication of mātauranga Māori while developing our own understanding of algebra.

    📋 Implementation Guide

    • Time: 10-15 minutes
    • When to Use: Starter activity, vocabulary reinforcement, brain break, or homework
    • Print: 2 per A4 page for efficient classroom distribution
    • Differentiation: Allow students to work in pairs for peer support

    🔑 Answer Key

    All words are placed horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. Key words:

    • Row 1: PATTERN (horizontal), SEQUENCE (horizontal)
    • Row 2: RULE (horizontal), TUKUTUKU (horizontal reverse)
    • Row 3: ALGEBRA (horizontal), KOWHAI (vertical from row 3)
    • All 15 words are findable - solution grid available in teacher resources folder

    📊 Learning Outcomes

    This activity supports:

    • Vocabulary acquisition and retention
    • Pattern recognition skills
    • Cultural connections to mathematics
    • Engagement and motivation through games-based learning

    🎯 Follow-Up Activities

    • Have students create definitions for each word
    • Challenge students to create their own wordsearch with lesson vocabulary
    • Use found words in exit tickets or reflection activities
    • Connect to tukutuku/kōwhaiwhai pattern analysis
