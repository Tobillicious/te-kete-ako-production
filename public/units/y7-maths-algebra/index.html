    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    📐 Y7 Mathematics: Algebra Through Patterns

    Foundational algebraic thinking using traditional Māori patterns and contemporary applications

    📅 5 Lessons 🎯 Year 7 🔢 Mathematics 🌿 High Cultural Integration

    🌿 Cultural Context

    "Ko te mātauranga te taonga tuku iho"

    Knowledge is a treasure passed down from our ancestors

    This unit explores algebraic thinking through the mathematical patterns found in tukutuku panels, kowhaiwhai designs, and whakapapa connections, honoring both ancient wisdom and contemporary mathematical understanding.

    🎯 Learning Objectives

    • Identify and describe patterns in traditional Māori designs
    • Use symbols and letters to represent unknown values
    • Write and solve simple linear equations
    • Connect algebraic thinking to whakapapa and natural patterns
    • Create mathematical rules for tukutuku patterns

    📋 Assessment

    • Pattern Detective investigations (formative)
    • Tukutuku Panel design project (summative)
    • Algebraic reasoning presentations
    • Problem-solving portfolio
    • Peer collaboration assessment

    📖 Lesson Sequence

    Lesson 1: Pattern Detectives

    Discovering mathematical patterns in traditional Māori designs and nature.

    Lesson 2: The Mystery of X

    Introduction to variables and algebraic symbols in cultural contexts.

    Lesson 3: Building with Algebra

    Constructing and understanding algebraic expressions and equations.

    Lesson 4: The Balancing Act

    Solving equations using balance and equality principles.

    Lesson 5: Tukutuku Design Challenge

    Creating mathematical art through algebraic pattern design.

    📊 Assessment Tools | Ngā Taputapu Aromatawai

    ✅ Formative Assessment (During Learning)

    Exit Ticket:

    • What is one thing you learned today?
    • What is one question you still have?
    • How does this connect to your own experience?

    Quick Check: Thumbs up/sideways/down for understanding

    🎯 Self-Assessment Rubric

    Criteria Not Yet Getting There Got It!
    I understand the key concepts ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can explain it to others ☐ ☐ ☐
    I can apply it to new situations ☐ ☐ ☐

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback

    📁 Portfolio Evidence

    Collect:

    • Completed activities/worksheets
    • Reflection journal entries
    • Photos of practical work
    • Peer feedback received

    Reflection Prompt: How does this work show your growth as a learner?
