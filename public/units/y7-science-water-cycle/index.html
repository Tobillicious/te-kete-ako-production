YEAR 7 SCIENCE UNIT • 5 LESSONS

💧 Water Cycle & Wai as Taonga

Explore the water cycle through science and mātauranga Māori. Understand wai (water) as a taonga (treasure) essential for all life, deserving our kaitiakitanga (guardianship).

🌊 Science (Living World) ⏱️ 5 × 75 minutes 👥 Year 7 🌿 Cultural: Wai as taonga

"Ko au te awa, ko te awa ko au"

I am the river, the river is me - We are inseparable from water

📚 Unit Overview

This 5-lesson unit integrates water cycle science with Māori perspectives on wai as taonga. Students learn the science of evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection while understanding water's cultural significance and the importance of water conservation.

🎯 Unit Goals

  • Explain water cycle processes scientifically
  • Understand wai as taonga in Māori worldview
  • Investigate local waterways
  • Apply kaitiakitanga to water conservation
  • Create action plan for protecting water

🌿 Cultural Integration

  • Wai as living taonga (not just H₂O)
  • Tangaroa (atua of the sea) connections
  • Awa (rivers) in pepeha and identity
  • Traditional water management practices
  • Modern water rights & Treaty settlements
