    🌿 Ngā Tikanga Ako | Māori Pedagogical Approaches

    This resource lends itself to authentic Māori pedagogical practices:

    • Ako: Reciprocal teaching and learning - students and teachers learn together
    • Tuakana-teina: Peer teaching with more experienced students supporting others
    • Wānanga: Deep collaborative discussion and knowledge sharing
    • Mahi tahi: Working together towards shared understanding

    💡 Teacher: Consider how these approaches can be authentically integrated into your lesson delivery.

    💡 Differentiation: Look for related resources at different NZ Curriculum phases to provide appropriate scaffolding for diverse ākonga. Consider creating learning pathways that connect resources at increasing complexity.

    Nested Question Hierarchies with Cultural Integration

    Every assessment with complete rubrics, answer keys, and cultural protocols

    📖 AsTTle-Style Reading Comprehension

    Question Hierarchy Structure:

    Level 1: Literal Comprehension (40%)
    • Direct information retrieval
    • Fact identification
    • Sequence understanding
    • Vocabulary in context
    Level 2: Inferential Comprehension (35%)
    • Reading between the lines
    • Cause and effect reasoning
    • Character motivation analysis
    • Theme identification
    Level 3: Critical Thinking (25%)
    • Evaluation and analysis
    • Personal response and connection
    • Cultural perspective consideration
    • Problem-solving application

    Cultural Integration Elements:

    • Reading passages with Māori cultural content
    • Questions about cultural protocols and tikanga
    • Vocabulary assessment including te reo Māori terms
    • Critical thinking about cultural perspectives

    Example Question Bank:

    Reading: "Traditional Māori Navigation Methods"

    Literal Question: "According to the text, how many directions did traditional navigators divide the horizon into?"

    Inferential Question: "What can you infer about the importance of mathematical accuracy in traditional navigation?"

    Critical Question: "How does traditional navigation knowledge compare to modern GPS technology? Consider both advantages and limitations."

    🔢 Mathematical Problem-Solving

    Problem Hierarchy Structure:

    Level 1: Basic Application (30%)
    • Direct calculation problems
    • Formula application
    • Basic problem-solving steps
    • Single-step solutions
    Level 2: Multi-Step Problems (45%)
    • Complex calculation sequences
    • Real-world application
    • Problem decomposition
    • Solution verification
    Level 3: Open-Ended Investigation (25%)
    • Mathematical modeling
    • Creative problem-solving
    • Cultural context integration
    • Extension and generalization

    Cultural Integration Elements:

    • Problems based on traditional navigation
    • Mathematical concepts in cultural context
    • Traditional measurement systems
    • Cultural problem-solving approaches

    Example Problem Bank:

    Basic: "Calculate 360° ÷ 32 directions"

    Multi-Step: "A traditional canoe travels at 15 km/h. How long will it take to travel 3,500 km from Rarotonga to Aotearoa? Plan the food and water needed for 20 people."

    Open-Ended: "Design a traditional navigation system using mathematical principles. Consider star positions, wave patterns, and environmental factors."

    🏛️ Cultural Competency Assessment

    Competency Hierarchy Structure:

    Level 1: Cultural Awareness (30%)
    • Basic cultural knowledge
    • Protocol recognition
    • Terminology understanding
    • Historical context awareness
    Level 2: Cultural Understanding (40%)
    • Cultural perspective analysis
    • Protocol application
    • Cultural connection making
    • Respectful engagement
    Level 3: Cultural Integration (30%)
    • Cultural knowledge application
    • Community engagement
    • Cultural leadership
    • Cultural bridge building

    Assessment Methods:

    • Cultural protocol demonstrations
    • Community consultation projects
    • Cultural knowledge presentations
    • Peer cultural teaching

    Cultural Competency Rubric:

    Exemplary: Demonstrates deep cultural understanding, respectful engagement, and community leadership

    Proficient: Shows good cultural awareness and appropriate respect for protocols

    Developing: Basic cultural knowledge with growing understanding

    Beginning: Limited cultural awareness requiring support

    👥 Peer Assessment Protocol

    Tuakana-Teina Approach:

    1. Two stars: What did they do well?
    2. One wish: What could be improved?
    3. Connection: How does this relate to our learning?

    Encourage manaakitanga (respect) in all feedback
