    🏛️ LEVEL 1: UNIT OVERVIEW & FRAMEWORK

    🎯 Cultural Foundation

    Whakataukī Selection & Context

    Māori: "Ko te pae tawhiti whāia kia tata, ko te pae tata whakamaua kia tīna"

    English: "Seek the distant horizons, hold fast to those close at hand"

    Cultural Significance: Emphasizes both ambitious goals (distant horizons) and practical skills (close at hand) - perfect for navigation mathematics that combines grand journeys with precise calculations.

    Pronunciation Guide: "Koh teh pie tah-fee fah-ee-ah kee-ah tah-tah, koh teh pie tah-tah fah-kah-mow-ah kee-ah tee-nah"

    Cultural Protocols & Tikanga

    • Begin each lesson with appropriate karakia
    • Ensure respectful use of te reo Māori throughout
    • Acknowledge traditional knowledge holders and sources
    • Provide cultural context for all mathematical concepts
    • Follow community consultation protocols
    • Respect cultural sensitivities in assessment

    📚 Curriculum Alignment

    NZ Curriculum Achievement Objectives

    • Level 4 Mathematics: Use measurement skills to solve problems
    • Level 4 Mathematics: Use geometric reasoning to solve problems
    • Level 4 Mathematics: Use number strategies to solve problems
    • Level 4 Social Studies: Understand how systems shape our lives

    Key Competencies Integration

    • Thinking: Mathematical and scientific reasoning applied to traditional knowledge
    • Using language, symbols and texts: Mathematical communication in bilingual context
    • Managing self: Independent learning through navigation challenges
    • Relating to others: Collaborative problem-solving in cultural context
    • Participating and contributing: Cultural understanding and community respect

    🎯 Learning Framework

    Knowledge (What students will know)

    • Traditional navigators divided horizon into 32 directions
    • Mathematical accuracy was essential for survival
    • Navigation combines astronomy, mathematics, and environmental observation
    • Māori mathematical knowledge is sophisticated and culturally grounded

    Skills (What students will be able to do)

    • Calculate angles between star directions
    • Use traditional measurement methods (fist, palm, finger)
    • Solve distance, speed, time problems
    • Apply mathematical concepts to real-world navigation

    Values (Attitudes and dispositions)

    • Appreciate sophistication of traditional knowledge
    • Respect cultural protocols and tikanga
    • Understand mathematics has cultural contexts
    • Recognize indigenous contributions to science

    📖 LEVEL 3: LESSON 1.1 - Introduction to Traditional Navigation (60 minutes)

    🎯 Lesson Learning Objectives

    Knowledge
    • Traditional navigators used sophisticated mathematical systems
    • Navigation required understanding of astronomy, mathematics, and environment
    • Mathematical accuracy was literally a matter of survival
    Skills
    • Participate respectfully in cultural opening protocols
    • Understand basic star compass system (32 directions)
    • Begin calculating angles between directions
    Values
    • Show respect for traditional knowledge and cultural protocols
    • Appreciate the sophistication of indigenous mathematical knowledge
    • Understand the importance of cultural context in learning

    ⏰ Minute-by-Minute Implementation

    0-5 minutes: Cultural Opening
    Exact Karakia Script

    Karakia: "He karakia mo te haerenga"

    Translation: "A prayer for the journey"

    Pronunciation: "Heh kah-rah-kee-ah moh teh hi-eh-reh-ngah"

    Teacher Actions: Lead students in karakia, ensure respectful participation, explain cultural significance

    Student Actions: Stand respectfully, participate in karakia, listen to cultural context

    Cultural Protocol: Face East (toward rising sun), maintain respectful posture, allow time for questions

    Whakataukī Introduction

    Māori: "Ko te pae tawhiti whāia kia tata"

    English: "Seek the distant horizons"

    Relevance: Connect to today's learning about ambitious navigation journeys

    Discussion Points: "What are our distant horizons? How do we work toward them?"

    5-15 minutes: Introduction Phase
    • Present learning objectives using 3-column format
    • Show traditional navigation overview video (3 minutes)
    • Introduce mathematical context of navigation
    • Engage students with questions about ocean travel
    Teacher Script

    "Today we begin an incredible journey into the mathematical knowledge of traditional Polynesian navigators. These navigators could sail thousands of kilometers across open ocean using only their knowledge of stars, waves, and mathematical calculations. Their mathematical accuracy was literally a matter of survival - getting calculations wrong could mean death at sea."

    15-35 minutes: Main Learning Activities
    Star Compass Demonstration
    • Display A1 star compass poster
    • Explain 32-direction system
    • Demonstrate angle calculations
    • Connect to traditional navigation methods
    Mathematical Calculations
    • Calculate 360° ÷ 32 = 11.25° per direction
    • Practice angle calculations with examples
    • Apply to star compass positions
    • Connect to real navigation scenarios
    35-50 minutes: Application Phase
    • Students complete "Introduction to Star Compass" worksheet
    • Peer collaboration on calculation problems
    • Teacher circulates providing individual support
    • Monitor progress and provide immediate feedback
    50-60 minutes: Reflection & Consolidation
    • Students write learning journal entry
    • Cultural understanding check with questions
    • Preview next lesson content
    • Assign homework: practice calculations
