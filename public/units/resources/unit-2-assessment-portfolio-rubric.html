    Unit 2: Decolonized History Assessment Portfolio

    Holistic Assessment Framework for Historical Understanding

    Assessment Philosophy - WhakatÅhea Principles

    This assessment honors the principle that every student brings unique perspectives and strengths to understanding history. Rather than testing memory, we assess critical thinking, cultural understanding, and the ability to see historical patterns that connect to today's world.

    Portfolio Component Options (Choose 3-4)

    Students select components that best showcase their understanding of decolonized Aotearoa history, ensuring authentic assessment that honors diverse learning styles.

    1. Historical Agency Portfolio

    Document and analyze examples of MÄori agency, leadership, and resistance throughout Aotearoa's history.

    What Students Create:

    • Profile collection of 5-8 MÄori leaders across different time periods
    • Analysis of strategies used to maintain mana and tino rangatiratanga
    • Visual timeline showing continuity of resistance and leadership
    • Personal reflection on leadership qualities that resonate today

    Success Indicators:

    • Demonstrates understanding of MÄori agency in historical events
    • Analyzes diverse forms of leadership and resistance
    • Connects historical strategies to contemporary examples
    • Shows respect for cultural protocols in presenting information

    2. Counter-Narrative Research Project

    Challenge colonial historical myths by researching and presenting alternative perspectives on significant events.

    What Students Create:

    • Research project examining colonial vs MÄori perspectives on a chosen historical event
    • Analysis of how different sources present the same events
    • Creation of alternative narrative highlighting MÄori experiences
    • Evaluation of why certain perspectives have been marginalized

    Success Indicators:

    • Critically examines bias in historical sources
    • Presents well-researched alternative perspective
    • Uses appropriate historical methodology and evidence
    • Demonstrates understanding of historiography and power

    3. Contemporary Connections Analysis

    Link historical patterns and issues to contemporary Aotearoa, showing ongoing relevance of decolonized history.

    What Students Create:

    • Case study connecting historical injustices to current issues
    • Analysis of how historical events shaped modern institutions
    • Investigation of ongoing impact on MÄori communities
    • Proposal for addressing contemporary manifestations of historical issues

    Success Indicators:

    • Makes clear connections between past and present
    • Understands ongoing impact of historical events
    • Analyzes systemic and structural continuities
    • Proposes realistic solutions based on historical understanding

    4. Primary Source Investigation

    Analyze historical documents, oral histories, and artifacts from multiple perspectives, focusing on MÄori voices and experiences.

    What Students Create:

    • Collection of primary sources representing different perspectives on chosen topic
    • Critical analysis of each source's context, purpose, and limitations
    • Comparison of how MÄori and colonial sources present same events
    • Reflection on what voices are missing from the historical record

    Success Indicators:

    • Demonstrates sophisticated source analysis skills
    • Recognizes bias and perspective in historical documents
    • Prioritizes MÄori voices and experiences in analysis
    • Understands limitations and gaps in historical record

    5. Activism Strategy Study

    Compare historical and contemporary MÄori activism strategies, analyzing their effectiveness and evolution over time.

    What Students Create:

    • Comparative analysis of activism strategies across different time periods
    • Case studies of successful and challenging activism campaigns
    • Examination of how tactics have evolved with changing contexts
    • Personal reflection on qualities of effective activism

    Success Indicators:

    • Analyzes diverse forms of resistance and activism
    • Understands relationship between context and strategy
    • Evaluates effectiveness of different approaches
    • Connects historical activism to contemporary movements

    6. Sovereignty Visioning Project

    Explore concepts of tino rangatiratanga and self-determination, imagining future possibilities based on historical foundations.

    What Students Create:

    • Research into historical expressions of MÄori sovereignty and self-determination
    • Analysis of how colonization disrupted traditional governance systems
    • Investigation of contemporary rangatiratanga movements and initiatives
    • Creative vision of future Aotearoa based on tino rangatiratanga principles

    Success Indicators:

    • Understands traditional MÄori governance and decision-making
    • Analyzes impact of colonization on MÄori self-determination
    • Explores contemporary sovereignty movements
    • Articulates future possibilities grounded in historical understanding
