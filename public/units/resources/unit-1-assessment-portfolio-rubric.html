Unit 1: Te Ao Māori - Assessment Portfolio & Rubric

Holistic assessment following Y8 Systems comprehensive evaluation model

🌿 Cultural Assessment Philosophy

Mana-enhancing assessment: This portfolio celebrates each student's unique cultural identity and learning journey. Assessment honors different ways of knowing and being, following Māori values of manaakitanga (caring) and whakatōhea (unity).

📁 Portfolio Components (Choose 4 of 6)

Component 1: Personal Whakapapa Presentation

Options: Visual constellation, digital story, oral presentation, creative artwork, written reflection, or multimedia project

Focus: Share your identity and connections in a way that feels authentic to you

Connected Resources: Whakapapa Template, Writers Toolkit PEEL Method, Cultural Assessment Framework

Component 2: Tikanga in Action Documentation

Options: Photo essay, video diary, written observations, interview project, or community service report

Focus: Document how you've observed or practiced tikanga Māori in real situations

Connected Resources: Tikanga Scenarios, Media Literacy Handout, Environmental Literacy Framework

Component 3: Mātauranga Māori Deep Dive

Options: Research project, infographic, teaching resource, podcast episode, or hands-on demonstration

Focus: Explore one area of Māori knowledge systems that interests you most

Connected Resources: Mātauranga Inquiry Cards, Māori Astronomy Handout, Traditional Navigation Mathematics

Component 4: Cultural Bridge-Building Project

Options: Cross-cultural comparison, family interview, community connection, or collaborative artwork

Focus: Show how different cultures (including your own) can learn from Te Ao Māori

Connected Resources: Treaty of Waitangi Podcast, Economic Justice Deep Dive, Cognitive Biases Handout

Component 5: Contemporary Te Ao Māori Analysis

Options: Current events analysis, social media study, film/music review, or youth perspective essay

Focus: Explore how Te Ao Māori perspectives apply to modern issues and youth experiences

Connected Resources: Media Literacy v2, Bastion Point Video Activity, Digital Citizenship Handout

Component 6: Future Whakapapa Visioning

Options: Goal-setting plan, community action proposal, creative writing, or leadership reflection

Focus: Articulate how you want to contribute to your whānau, community, and future generations

Connected Resources: Community Action Project Brief, Future Rangatiratanga Unit, Prompt Engineering 101

🌟 Culturally Responsive Feedback Framework

Mana Recognition

Acknowledge the unique mana (spiritual power) each student brings to their learning

Growth Mindset

Focus on ako (learning journey) rather than just achievement outcomes

Cultural Validation

Honor all cultural backgrounds while deepening Te Ao Māori understanding

Next Steps

Provide clear, actionable guidance for continued cultural learning
