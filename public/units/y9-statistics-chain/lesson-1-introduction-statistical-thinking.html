🤔 What is Statistics?

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to answer questions and make decisions. It helps us understand our world through numbers and patterns.

💡 Statistics Tells Us:

  • How many Māori speakers are in NZ? (Census data)
  • Is climate change affecting our coastlines? (Sea level measurements)
  • Which schools have the best student outcomes? (Education data)
  • How has Aotearoa's population changed over time? (Historical trends)

📊 Real Data: NZ Population Growth

🔍 What does this data tell us?

  • 1950: NZ had ~2 million people
  • 2024: NZ has ~5.3 million people
  • Growth: Population more than doubled in 74 years!
  • Questions: Why? Immigration? Birth rates? What does this mean for housing, schools, environment?

🌿 Whakapapa & Statistical Thinking

Māori have practiced data thinking for centuries through whakapapa - tracking relationships, connections, and patterns across generations:

📊 Counting & Recording

Whakapapa recorded births, deaths, marriages - demographic data tracking family growth

📈 Patterns Over Time

Maramataka (lunar calendar) tracked patterns in tides, weather, fishing - time-series data

🔗 Relationships

Understanding how things connect - correlation between environmental signs and outcomes

🎯 Activity: Ask a Statistical Question

Think about your community, school, or Aotearoa. What do you wonder about?

Example Questions:

  • What percentage of students at our school speak Te Reo Māori at home?
  • How many native bird species have we lost in NZ since 1800?
  • Is there a relationship between screen time and sleep quality?
  • How has my town's population changed over the last 50 years?

💡 Good statistical questions:

  • Can be answered with data (numbers, measurements)
  • Are specific and focused
  • Have real-world importance
  • Could help make decisions or solve problems
