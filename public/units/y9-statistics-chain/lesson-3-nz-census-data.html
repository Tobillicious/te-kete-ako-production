🇳🇿 NZ Census 2023: Ethnic Groups

🔍 Key Findings:

  • Māori: 17.8% identify as Māori (935,000 people)
  • Pacific Peoples: 8.1% (426,000 people)
  • Asian: 17.3% (910,000 people) - fastest growing
  • European/Pākehā: 70.2% (3.7 million) - note: People can select multiple ethnicities!

💡 Why percentages add to more than 100%:

Many New Zealanders identify with multiple ethnic groups! For example, someone might be Māori AND Pacific Peoples AND European. The Census allows multiple selections, so totals exceed 100%.

🗣️ Te Reo Māori Speakers (2023)

📈 Language Revitalization Success:

  • 186,000 people can hold a conversation in Te Reo (up from 150,000 in 2013!)
  • 9.5% of Māori speak it at home
  • Kura kaupapa and kohanga reo driving growth
  • Young people leading the revival

📝 Activity: Analyze Census Data

Using the data above, answer these questions:

  1. Approximately how many people in NZ identify as Māori?
  2. Why do the ethnicity percentages add up to more than 100%?
  3. Which ethnic group is growing the fastest? What might explain this?
  4. Has the number of Te Reo speakers increased or decreased since 2013?
  5. What does this data tell us about cultural diversity in Aotearoa?

🌿 Cultural Reflection:

Data about people isn't just numbers - it represents real whānau, communities, and stories. As kaitiaki of data, we must use it respectfully to serve communities, not just satisfy curiosity.
