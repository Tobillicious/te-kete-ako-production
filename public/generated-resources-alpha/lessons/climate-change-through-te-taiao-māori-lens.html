Climate Change Through Te Taiao Māori Lens

Year Levels: 10-12 | Subject: Science | Duration: 3-4 lessons

Learning Objectives

  • Understand climate change through both scientific and Māori worldviews
  • Explore the concept of kaitiakitanga (guardianship) in environmental management
  • Analyze how traditional Māori knowledge (mātauranga Māori) informs climate adaptation
  • Develop strategies for sustainable living based on indigenous wisdom
  • Connect NZC Science achievement objectives with Te Ao Māori perspectives

Cultural Context: Tikanga Māori Considerations

This unit approaches climate change through the interconnected Māori worldview where people (tangata) are part of nature (taiao), not separate from it. Key concepts include:

  • Whakapapa: The genealogical connection between all living things
  • Mauri: The life force present in all elements of the natural world
  • Kaitiakitanga: The responsibility to protect and care for the environment
  • Mana: The spiritual power and authority of natural elements

Teacher Guidance:

When teaching this content:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer) to acknowledge the mauri of the learning space
  • Consult with local iwi or kaumātua for region-specific knowledge
  • Emphasize the living nature of mātauranga Māori - it continues to evolve
  • Create space for student whakapapa connections to the environment

Activity: Research how changing seasons affect one traditional Māori resource (e.g., harakeke, kūmara, tī kōuka). Create a brief report on:

Self-Assessment Checklist

  • I can explain climate change using both scientific and Māori terms
  • I understand the concept of kaitiakitanga and its importance
  • I can identify at least three traditional environmental indicators
  • I've considered how climate change affects my local area
  • I've developed at least one personal action based on this learning

Extension Activities

1. Whakapapa Investigation

Research how your local environment has changed over generations. Interview family/community members about:

  • Weather patterns they remember from childhood
  • Changes in plant/animal life cycles
  • Traditional knowledge about seasonal changes

2. Marae Sustainability Audit

Design a plan to make a marae more climate-resilient using both modern technology and traditional knowledge. Consider:

  • Energy sources
  • Water collection and use
  • Food production
  • Building materials

Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge: Students should have basic understanding of climate change science (greenhouse effect, human impacts).

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide pre-filled sections of comparison table with some answers
  • Extension: Have students develop a full kaitiakitanga action plan for the school

Cultural Safety: Be aware that some traditional knowledge may be tapu (sacred). Only use publicly available information unless you have proper guidance.

Cross-Curricular Links: Social Studies (sustainability), English (report writing), Health (wellbeing and environment)
