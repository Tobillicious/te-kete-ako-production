    Cultural Context: Te Taiao Māori

    Tikanga Considerations

    When discussing Papatūānuku (Earth Mother) and environmental concepts in Māori worldview:

    • Begin with a karakia (prayer) to acknowledge the mauri (life force) of the environment
    • Respect local iwi knowledge and protocols - consult with mana whenua where possible
    • Recognize that some knowledge may be tapu (sacred) and not for public discussion
    • Emphasize the interconnectedness of all living things (whanaungatanga)

    Māori environmental knowledge (mātauranga taiao) offers unique perspectives on climate change through:

    • Whakapapa: Understanding relationships between all elements of nature
    • Kaitiakitanga: Guardianship and responsibility for the environment
    • Mauri: Recognizing the life force in all things
    • Tohu: Environmental indicators and traditional forecasting methods

    Teacher Implementation Notes

    Preparation

    • Consult with your school's Māori community liaison or local iwi representatives
    • Prepare karakia appropriate for environmental topics
    • Gather local examples of climate impacts and Māori responses

    Differentiation Strategies

    • Support: Provide pre-selected resources and structured templates
    • Extension: Challenge students to interview local kaumatua or environmental experts
    • Cultural Inclusion: Allow students to present learning in diverse formats (oral, visual, written)

    Assessment Notes

    Focus on the integration of knowledge systems rather than privileging one over the other. Acknowledge that some students may have personal connections to mātauranga Māori.
