Climate Change Through Te Taiao Māori Lens

Years 10-12 Science | NZ Curriculum Levels 5-7

Learning Objectives

  • Understand climate change through both scientific and Mātauranga Māori perspectives
  • Explore the concept of kaitiakitanga (guardianship) in environmental management
  • Analyze how traditional Māori environmental indicators relate to modern climate science
  • Develop strategies for climate action grounded in both Western science and indigenous knowledge
  • Understand the impacts of climate change on taonga (treasured) species and ecosystems

Cultural Context: Tikanga Māori Considerations

This unit approaches climate change through the interconnected worldview of Te Ao Māori, where:

  • The environment (te taiao) is understood as an ancestor (tupuna)
  • Humans are part of nature, not separate from it
  • Knowledge (mātauranga) comes from both observation and ancestral wisdom
  • All actions should consider impacts on future generations (uri whakatipu)

Cultural protocols to observe:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer/invocation) to acknowledge the mauri (life force) of the environment
  • When discussing specific places or species, acknowledge their whakapapa (genealogical connections)
  • Respect that some mātauranga may be tapu (sacred) to particular iwi or hapū

Traditional Environmental Indicators

Māori ancestors used natural signs to predict weather and seasonal changes:

  • Tohu o te rangi - signs in the sky
  • Tohu o te whenua - signs on the land
  • Tohu o te moana - signs in the sea
  • Tohu o ngā manu - signs from birds

Research Task: Investigate how climate change is affecting one traditional indicator in your local area. Interview a kaumātua (elder) or research local knowledge.

Extension Activities

1. Whakapapa of Climate Change

Research how your local iwi or hapū have experienced environmental changes over generations. Create a timeline showing:

  • Traditional knowledge about the environment
  • Historical changes observed
  • Current climate impacts
  • Future projections

2. Kōrero with Experts

Organize a panel discussion featuring:

  • A climate scientist
  • A Māori environmental expert
  • A local farmer or fisher
  • A youth climate activist

Compare their perspectives on climate change causes and solutions.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge

Students should have basic understanding of:

  • Climate change causes and effects (NZC L5 Science)
  • Basic Māori concepts like kaitiakitanga and whakapapa
  • Local environmental features

Differentiation Strategies

For less confident students:

  • Provide pre-filled portions of the comparison table
  • Offer sentence starters for the action plan
  • Use visual aids for traditional indicators

For more capable students:

  • Require primary source research with local iwi
  • Challenge to develop quantitative measures for traditional indicators
  • Have them present findings to a community group

Assessment Notes

  • Focus on depth of cultural understanding, not just scientific accuracy
  • Consider alternative assessments like oral presentations for some students
  • Be sensitive that some mātauranga may be tapu - check with local iwi if unsure
