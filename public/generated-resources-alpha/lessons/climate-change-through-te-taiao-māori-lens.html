Climate Change Through Te Taiao Māori Lens

Years 10-12 Science Resource

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand climate change concepts through both Western science and Mātauranga Māori perspectives
  • Explore the interconnectedness of natural systems (whakapapa) in environmental change
  • Analyze climate impacts on taonga species and ecosystems
  • Develop strategies for environmental stewardship (kaitiakitanga)
  • Compare and contrast indigenous and scientific knowledge systems

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

This lesson integrates Mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) with climate science. Key concepts include:

  • Whakapapa: The interconnected genealogy of all living things
  • Kaitiakitanga: Guardianship and sustainable management of resources
  • Mauri: The life force present in all things
  • Taonga: Treasured species and resources

Cultural protocols to observe:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer) to acknowledge the whenua (land)
  • Respect tapu (sacred) concepts when discussing environmental changes
  • Use correct Māori pronunciation (audio resources provided)
  • Consult local iwi for region-specific knowledge where possible

Extension Activities

1. Local Climate Impacts Research

Investigate how climate change is affecting your local area through both scientific data and kōrero tuku iho (oral histories). Interview elders about observed environmental changes.

2. Maramataka (Māori Lunar Calendar) Project

Compare traditional seasonal knowledge with modern climate data. Create a digital or physical display showing how maramataka indicators are changing.

3. Kaitiakitanga Action Plan

Develop a school or community climate action plan incorporating both scientific and Māori approaches to sustainability.

Students should have basic understanding of:
