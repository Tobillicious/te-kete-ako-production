Cultural Context: Tikanga Māori Considerations

This lesson approaches climate change through the framework of Te Taiao (the natural world) and kaitiakitanga (guardianship). Key concepts include:

  • Whakapapa - The interconnectedness of all living things
  • Mauri - The life force present in all things
  • Tapu and noa - The sacred and the everyday
  • Mana - The spiritual power and authority of the natural world

Tikanga for this lesson:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer) to acknowledge the mauri of the environment we're studying
  • Respect different iwi perspectives as environmental knowledge varies by region
  • When discussing local environments, acknowledge the mana whenua (local iwi with authority)

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge: Students should have basic understanding of climate change science (Years 9-10 level).

Duration: 3-4 lessons (adjust activities as needed)

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide pre-filled sections of comparison table with some answers
  • Extension: Challenge students to find primary sources of mātauranga Māori

Cultural Safety:

  • Consult with local iwi if possible before teaching
  • Be clear that mātauranga Māori is a valid knowledge system equal to Western science
  • Avoid presenting Māori knowledge as "beliefs" or "mythology"

Assessment: Can use the rubric for formal assessment or as formative feedback.
