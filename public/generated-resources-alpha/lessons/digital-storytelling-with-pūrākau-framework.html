Digital Storytelling with Pūrākau Framework

Years 8-10 | English | Traditional Māori Storytelling Methods

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the structure and purpose of pūrākau (Māori narratives)
  • Apply digital tools to create culturally authentic stories
  • Analyze how traditional storytelling techniques can enhance modern narratives
  • Demonstrate respect for tikanga Māori (cultural protocols) in digital storytelling
  • Collaborate effectively to produce a meaningful digital narrative

NZ Curriculum Links: English L4-5, Technology L4-5, Social Sciences L4-5

Cultural Context: Pūrākau in Māori Tradition

Pūrākau are traditional Māori narratives that carry knowledge, values, and history. They serve multiple purposes:

  • Preserving tribal history (whakapapa)
  • Teaching moral lessons
  • Explaining natural phenomena
  • Maintaining cultural identity

Tikanga Considerations

When creating digital pūrākau, we must respect these cultural protocols:

  1. Whakapapa: Ensure proper representation of people and places
  2. Mana: Respect the dignity of characters and their stories
  3. Tapu/Noa: Be aware of sacred elements that shouldn't be trivialized
  4. Kaitiakitanga: Take responsibility for the stories you share
  5. Whanaungatanga: Consider how your story connects people

Pūrākau Story Structure

Traditional pūrākau often follow this pattern:

Stage Māori Term Description
1. Opening Timatanga Establish setting and characters
2. Challenge Wero Introduce the main problem
3. Journey Haerenga Characters work through challenges
4. Resolution Whakatau Problem is resolved
5. Teaching Whakamārama Reveal the story's lesson

Activity 1: Digital Pūrākau Creation

Task: Create a digital story (video, slideshow, or interactive story) using the pūrākau framework.

Steps:

  1. Choose a theme (e.g., environmental protection, friendship, courage)
  2. Develop characters that represent Māori values
  3. Map your story using the 5-stage structure
  4. Create your digital product (2-3 minutes long)
  5. Include appropriate waiata (songs) or whakataukī (proverbs)

Digital Tools Options:

  • Video: Canva, Adobe Spark, iMovie
  • Slideshow: Google Slides, PowerPoint
  • Interactive: Twine, Scratch
  • Audio: Audacity, GarageBand

Extension Activities

  • Create a bilingual version (English/Te Reo Māori) of your story
  • Develop a companion website explaining the cultural elements
  • Interview a local kaumatua (elder) about traditional stories
  • Compare a pūrākau with a Western fairy tale structure

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge: Basic digital literacy and introductory understanding of Māori culture

Duration: 4-6 lessons (adjust as needed)

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Support: Provide storyboard templates or partially completed examples
  • Extension: Challenge students to incorporate more Te Reo Māori
  • Cultural Support: Invite Māori community members to share stories

Assessment Tips:

  • Use formative assessment during planning stages
  • Peer assessment can be valuable for cultural accuracy
  • Consider a reflection component about cultural learning
