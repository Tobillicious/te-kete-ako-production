Digital Storytelling with Pūrākau Framework

Year Levels: Years 8-10 | Subject: English

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand and apply traditional Māori storytelling structures to digital narratives
  • Create a digital story that demonstrates understanding of Pūrākau elements
  • Use digital tools to enhance storytelling while maintaining cultural integrity
  • Reflect on the relationship between traditional and contemporary storytelling methods

Cultural Context

Understanding Pūrākau

Pūrākau are traditional Māori narratives that carry knowledge, values, and worldviews. They follow specific structures and patterns that have been passed down through generations. When working with Pūrākau, we must approach them with respect (manaakitanga) and understanding of their cultural significance.

Tikanga Considerations

  • Whakapapa: Stories are connected to people and places - ensure proper attribution
  • Kaitiakitanga: Care for the stories and their appropriate use
  • Wairua: Recognize the spiritual dimension of traditional stories
  • Whanaungatanga: Consider how the story connects people and communities

Creating Your Digital Pūrākau

Activity 1: Story Planning

Using the Pūrākau structure, plan your digital story:

  1. Choose a theme or message for your story (e.g., kaitiakitanga, whanaungatanga)
  2. Develop characters that connect to your theme
  3. Map out the five stages of the Pūrākau structure
  4. Consider how digital elements (images, sound, video) will enhance each stage

Activity 2: Digital Creation

Choose one of these digital formats (or suggest your own):

  • Animated slideshow with narration
  • Digital comic using online tools
  • Short video with live action or stop motion
  • Interactive story using Twine or similar tools

Cultural Considerations:

  • Use appropriate imagery that respects Māori culture
  • Consider adding kupu Māori (Māori words) where relevant
  • Ensure any representations of Māori concepts are accurate

Self-Check Before Submission

Extension Activities

1. Comparative Analysis

Compare your digital Pūrākau with a traditional oral version. How does the medium change the storytelling experience?

2. Community Connection

Interview a kaumātua (elder) about storytelling in their youth and create a digital presentation comparing then and now.

3. Cultural Fusion

Create a story that blends Pūrākau structure with another cultural storytelling tradition you're familiar with.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Preparation

  • Familiarize yourself with local iwi stories and resources
  • Prepare examples of digital storytelling (both Māori and non-Māori)
  • Ensure access to appropriate digital tools (Chromebook-friendly options listed below)

Delivery Tips

  • Begin with oral storytelling to emphasize the traditional roots
  • Invite local storytellers if possible
  • Emphasize the process (whakapapa) as much as the product
  • Create a safe space for cultural exploration and questions

Differentiation

  • Support: Provide story starters or partial templates
  • Extension: Challenge students to incorporate more complex Māori concepts
  • Cultural: Be sensitive to varying levels of Māori cultural knowledge
