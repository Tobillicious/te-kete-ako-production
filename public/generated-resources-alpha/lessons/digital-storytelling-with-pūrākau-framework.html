Digital Storytelling with Pūrākau Framework

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the structure and purpose of pūrākau (Māori narratives).
  • Apply digital tools to create a modern retelling of a traditional Māori story.
  • Analyze how storytelling techniques influence meaning.
  • Reflect on cultural perspectives in storytelling.

Cultural Context & Safety Notes

Pūrākau are traditional Māori stories that carry history, values, and wisdom. They are often passed down orally and contain deep cultural significance.

Cultural Safety:

  • Respect the sacred nature of some stories—ensure students understand not all pūrākau are for public retelling.
  • Encourage students to research from reputable sources (e.g., Te Ara – The Encyclopedia of New Zealand).
  • Consult with local iwi or kaumātua if adapting specific tribal narratives.

Step-by-Step Lesson Plan

1. Introduction to Pūrākau (20 mins)

Watch: Te Papa’s resource on Māori oral traditions.

Discussion: What makes pūrākau different from other stories?

2. Story Analysis (30 mins)

Read: Māui and the Sun or Rata and the Waka (provided in class).

Group Activity: Identify key elements (characters, conflict, resolution, cultural values).

3. Digital Storytelling Task (60 mins)

Task: Retell a pūrākau using digital tools (e.g., Google Slides, Canva, Adobe Spark).

Requirements:

  • Must include oral narration (recorded voice or video).
  • Use symbolism (e.g., koru patterns, traditional motifs).
  • Keep the story culturally respectful.

Assessment Suggestions

Rubric (4-point scale):

  • Cultural Accuracy (4 pts): Story respects Māori traditions.
  • Creativity (3 pts): Engaging digital presentation.
  • Oral Delivery (3 pts): Clear and expressive narration.
  • Reflection (2 pts): Explains storytelling choices.

Extension Activities

  • Comparative Study: Compare a pūrākau with a Greek myth—how do cultural values differ?
  • Podcast Creation: Interview a kaumātua (with permission) about storytelling traditions.
  • Augmented Reality (AR) Story: Use apps like HP Reveal to add interactive layers.

Adaptations for Different Learners

  • Struggling Writers: Provide a storyboard template.
  • EAL Students: Allow bilingual storytelling (Māori/English).
  • Advanced Learners: Challenge them to incorporate whakataukī (proverbs).
