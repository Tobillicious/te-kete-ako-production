Traditional Navigation & Modern GPS Integration

Year Levels: Years 9-11 | Subject: Science

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand traditional Māori (Mātauranga Māori) navigation techniques
  • Compare and contrast traditional wayfinding with modern GPS technology
  • Analyze how indigenous knowledge systems contribute to modern science
  • Develop skills in celestial navigation and environmental observation
  • Create connections between cultural knowledge and technological innovation

Cultural Context: Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa (The Pacific Ocean)

The Māori people are descended from skilled navigators who traversed vast ocean distances using sophisticated wayfinding techniques. This knowledge system, called whakatere waka (canoe navigation), was passed down through generations.

Tikanga Considerations:

  • Respect for tupuna (ancestors) who developed this knowledge
  • Acknowledgment that this knowledge is taonga (treasure)
  • Understanding that different iwi (tribes) may have distinct navigation traditions
  • Proper pronunciation of Māori terms (provide audio links if possible)
  • Involvement of local kaumātua (elders) where appropriate

Integration of Knowledge Systems

Modern navigators are combining traditional knowledge with technology:

Case Study: Waka Tapu Voyage

In 2012, traditional waka (canoes) sailed from New Zealand to Rapanui (Easter Island) using both traditional navigation and modern technology for safety.

Discussion Questions:

  1. Why might traditional navigators choose to use some modern technology?
  2. How does GPS complement rather than replace traditional knowledge?
  3. What are the benefits of maintaining traditional navigation skills?

Extension Activities

1. Star Compass Construction

Create a working star compass using cardboard and markers. Label key navigation stars in both English and Māori.

2. Navigation Journal

Keep a week-long journal tracking natural navigation cues in your local environment (sun position, wind patterns, etc.).

3. Technology Debate

Prepare arguments for and against: "Traditional navigation methods are obsolete in the age of GPS."

Teacher Implementation Notes
