Traditional Navigation & Modern GPS Integration

Māori Wayfinding Knowledge Systems

Year Level: Years 9-11 | Subject: Science

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Explain key principles of traditional Māori navigation (whakatere waka)
  • Describe how modern GPS technology works
  • Compare and contrast traditional and modern navigation systems
  • Analyze how traditional knowledge can inform modern scientific understanding
  • Demonstrate understanding of celestial navigation techniques

NZ Curriculum Links: Nature of Science (Understanding about science), Physical World (Planet Earth and Beyond), Living World (Ecology)

Cultural Context: Tikanga Māori Considerations

This lesson explores whakatere waka (traditional navigation) which is part of mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge systems). When engaging with this knowledge:

  • Recognize that this knowledge belongs to tangata whenua (people of the land)
  • Understand that traditional navigation was a sacred (tapu) practice requiring specialized training
  • Appreciate that celestial bodies (ngā whetū) were considered ancestors by Māori navigators
  • Note that different iwi (tribes) may have distinct navigation traditions

Key Māori concepts: Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa (Pacific Ocean), whānau mārama (constellations), kaitiakitanga (guardianship of knowledge)

Modern GPS Technology

Global Positioning Systems (GPS) work through:

  • Network of satellites orbiting Earth
  • Triangulation of signals from multiple satellites
  • Precise timing measurements
  • Digital mapping integration

Comparative Analysis Activity

Create a Venn diagram comparing traditional Māori navigation and modern GPS:

Traditional Navigation

  • Relies on environmental observations
  • Passed down through oral traditions
  • Holistic understanding of nature

Modern GPS

  • Uses satellite technology
  • Digital data transmission
  • Precise mathematical calculations

Overlap: Both provide accurate positioning, require specialized knowledge, and have practical applications

Extension Activities

1. Celestial Navigation Challenge

Using a star chart (or digital planetarium app), plot a course between two Pacific locations using only traditional navigation methods.

2. GPS Error Analysis

Research common GPS errors and compare how traditional navigators would have overcome similar challenges.

3. Cultural Connections

Investigate how other Pacific cultures (e.g., Polynesian, Micronesian) developed similar navigation techniques.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Preparation:

  • Consult with local iwi or Māori knowledge holders if possible
  • Prepare star charts or digital planetarium resources
  • Test any GPS apps or tools beforehand

Lesson Sequence:

  1. Begin with karakia (optional) to set respectful context
  2. Introduce traditional navigation through storytelling
  3. Demonstrate GPS principles with practical examples
  4. Facilitate comparative analysis activity
  5. Conclude with reflection on knowledge integration

Differentiation:

  • For less confident students: Provide structured comparison templates
  • For advanced students: Challenge with real-world navigation problems
  • For kinesthetic learners: Include physical navigation activities
