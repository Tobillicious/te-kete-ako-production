Traditional Navigation & Modern GPS Integration

Māori Wayfinding Knowledge Systems

Year Levels: Years 9-11 | Subject: Science

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand traditional Māori navigation techniques (te kōkōrangi me te whakatere waka)
  • Compare and contrast celestial navigation with modern GPS systems
  • Analyze how indigenous knowledge systems contribute to modern science
  • Apply wayfinding concepts to solve practical navigation challenges
  • Develop respect for mātauranga Māori as a valid knowledge system

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

This lesson explores mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) about navigation, which was traditionally passed down through whakapapa (genealogical lines) and protected as tapu (sacred) knowledge.

Key concepts:

  • Te kōkōrangi: Māori astronomy and celestial navigation
  • Whakatere waka: The art of canoe navigation
  • Matariki: The Pleiades star cluster used for navigation and marking the new year
  • Te Ikaroa: The Milky Way, an important navigational marker

Tikanga (protocols) to observe:

  • Acknowledge the iwi (tribes) who are kaitiaki (guardians) of this knowledge
  • Treat this knowledge with the same respect as Western scientific knowledge
  • Recognize that some knowledge may be tapu (sacred/restricted)
  • Use te reo Māori terms correctly (pronunciation guide provided)

Key Navigation Methods:

  • Star paths: Following specific stars at specific times
  • Swell patterns: Reading ocean currents and waves
  • Bird migration: Following bird flight paths
  • Water temperature/color: Indicators of land proximity
  • Cloud formations: Certain clouds form over islands

Modern GPS Technology

Global Positioning Systems (GPS) work through:

  • Network of satellites orbiting Earth
  • Triangulation of signals from multiple satellites
  • Precise timing measurements
  • Digital mapping integration

Extension Activities

1. Star Compass Construction

Create a physical star compass showing key Māori navigational stars and their positions at different times of year.

2. Error Analysis

Research and compare potential errors in traditional navigation vs. GPS systems. Consider factors like weather, technology failure, and human error.

3. Modern Applications

Investigate how traditional navigation knowledge is being used today in areas like ocean conservation, climate research, or space exploration.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge:

  • Basic understanding of Earth's rotation and celestial movements
  • Familiarity with compass directions
  • Awareness of Māori culture (recommended pre-lesson on Māori worldviews)

Lesson Sequence:

  1. Engage: Show video of waka hourua (traditional canoe) voyage (10 min)
  2. Explore: Traditional navigation methods jigsaw activity (20 min)
  3. Explain: Teacher-led GPS explanation (15 min)
  4. Elaborate: Comparative analysis activity (20 min)
  5. Evaluate: Reflection questions or mini-quiz (15 min)

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide pre-filled Venn diagrams with some elements already placed
  • Extension: Have students research specific Polynesian navigation techniques from different regions
  • Māori learners: Invite whānau with navigation knowledge to share stories

Safety Considerations:

  • If doing outdoor star observations, ensure proper supervision
  • Be culturally sensitive when discussing tapu knowledge
