Career Pathways in STEM for Māori Students

Exploring opportunities that connect mātauranga Māori with science, technology, engineering and mathematics

He Whakamōhiotanga | Introduction

This resource explores STEM career pathways that value and incorporate Māori knowledge, perspectives and ways of being. We'll examine how mātauranga Māori intersects with modern STEM fields and highlight successful Māori professionals in these areas.

Quick Reflection

Before we begin, take 2 minutes to write down:

  • One STEM career you're interested in
  • One way you think Māori knowledge could connect to that career

We'll come back to these at the end of the lesson.

Ngā Whāinga Ako | Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  1. Identify at least three STEM career pathways that incorporate mātauranga Māori
  2. Explain how traditional Māori knowledge connects with modern STEM fields
  3. Describe support systems available for Māori pursuing STEM careers
  4. Develop a personal action plan for exploring STEM career options

Horopaki Ahurea | Cultural Context

Māori have always been scientists, technologists, engineers and mathematicians - from celestial navigation (tātai arorangi) to environmental management (kaitiakitanga). Today, there are growing opportunities to bring these knowledge systems together with contemporary STEM fields.

Case Studies: Māori in STEM

Dr. Ocean Mercier

Field: Physics and Science Communication

Iwi: Ngāti Porou

Dr. Mercier bridges Western science and mātauranga Māori in her work at Victoria University, exploring how these knowledge systems can work together to solve complex problems.

Ngā Ara Mahi | Career Pathways

STEM Fields with Māori Connections

Explore these STEM fields that actively incorporate mātauranga Māori:

Environmental Science + Kaitiakitanga

Roles: Environmental consultant, conservation biologist, resource management planner

Example: Developing land management plans that combine ecological science with traditional Māori environmental practices.

Data Science + Mātauranga

Roles: Data analyst, GIS specialist, research scientist

Example: Using data visualization to tell stories about Māori health, education or environmental outcomes.

Engineering + Rongoā Māori

Roles: Biomedical engineer, materials scientist, product designer

Example: Developing medical devices inspired by traditional Māori healing knowledge.

Support Systems for Māori in STEM

  • Te Pūnaha Matatini: Centre for research excellence focused on complex systems
  • Māori in STEM scholarships: Various universities and organizations offer targeted support
  • Tuakana-Teina programs: Peer mentoring within universities
  • Pūhoro STEM Academy: Program supporting Māori secondary students in STEM

Ngā Ngohe | Activities

Career Pathway Mapping

Task: Create a visual career pathway map for a STEM field that interests you, showing:

  1. Required education/training
  2. Key skills needed
  3. How mātauranga Māori could be incorporated
  4. Potential employers or work settings
  5. Support systems available to Māori

Aromatawai | Assessment

Students will be assessed on their career pathway map based on:

Criteria Excellent (4-5) Developing (2-3) Needs Work (0-1)
Career Information Accuracy All information is accurate and current Most information is accurate Contains significant inaccuracies
Māori Knowledge Integration Clear, meaningful connections made Some connections identified Little to no integration
Support Systems Identified Multiple relevant supports included At least one support identified No supports mentioned
Presentation Quality Professional, engaging and clear Generally clear presentation Difficult to follow

Ngā Mahi Whakawhānui | Extension Activities

For students who want to explore further:

  1. Interview a Māori professional in your chosen STEM field (in person or via email)
  2. Research a traditional Māori technology or practice and analyze its scientific principles
  3. Create a comparative analysis of Western and Māori approaches to a specific STEM concept
  4. Develop a proposal for a STEM project that would benefit your local Māori community

Ngā Whakarerekētanga | Adaptations

For students needing additional support:

  • Provide pre-selected career options to research
  • Offer sentence starters for written components
  • Allow oral presentation of career pathway instead of written
  • Provide more structured templates with guided questions

For distance learning:

  • Use digital collaboration tools for group work
  • Provide video resources of Māori STEM professionals
  • Offer virtual career exploration tools and websites

He Kupu Whakamutunga | Conclusion

Reflection

Return to your initial thoughts from the beginning of the lesson:

  1. How has your understanding of STEM careers changed?
  2. What new connections between Māori knowledge and STEM can you identify now?
  3. What's one action step you can take this week to explore these ideas further?
