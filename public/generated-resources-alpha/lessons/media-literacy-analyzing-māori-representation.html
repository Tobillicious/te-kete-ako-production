Media Literacy: Analyzing Māori Representation

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Critically analyze how Māori people and culture are represented in media.
  • Identify stereotypes, biases, and cultural misrepresentations.
  • Compare authentic Māori storytelling with mainstream media portrayals.
  • Reflect on the impact of media representation on identity and society.

NZ Curriculum Alignment: English (Levels 4-6) – Close Viewing, Critical Thinking, and Cultural Perspectives.

Cultural Context & Safety Notes

Cultural Context: This lesson encourages students to examine media portrayals of Māori through a critical lens, fostering awareness of cultural representation and its effects.

Safety Considerations:

  • Ensure discussions remain respectful—avoid reinforcing stereotypes.
  • Use authentic Māori voices (e.g., interviews with Māori filmmakers, articles by Māori journalists).
  • Be mindful of students who may have personal connections to the topics discussed.
  • Encourage students to speak from their own perspectives without making assumptions about others.

Lesson Activities

1. Warm-up Discussion (10 mins)

Prompt: "When you think of Māori people in movies, TV, or news, what images or stories come to mind?"

Record responses on the board (digital or physical).

2. Media Analysis Task (20 mins)

Watch: Selected clips from mainstream media vs. Māori-produced content (e.g., Once Were Warriors vs. Whānau Living).

(Note: Teachers should pre-select clips that highlight contrasting representations.)

Group Analysis: In small groups, students compare the two clips using guiding questions:

  • How are Māori characters portrayed?
  • Who is telling the story? (Māori or non-Māori creators?)
  • What messages are being conveyed about Māori culture?
  • Are there any stereotypes or biases present?

3. Class Discussion & Reflection (15 mins)

Groups share their findings. Discuss:

  • Why might different media portray Māori differently?
  • How does representation impact real-life perceptions?
  • What can we do to support authentic storytelling?

Assessment Suggestions

Formative: Participation in discussions, group analysis contributions.

Summative: Written reflection (300-500 words) or a short media critique.

Rubric for Media Critique

Criteria Advanced (4-5) Proficient (3) Developing (1-2)
Critical Analysis Clearly identifies stereotypes and biases; provides insightful comparisons. Identifies some stereotypes; makes basic comparisons. Limited analysis; may miss key points.
Cultural Awareness Demonstrates deep understanding of Māori perspectives. Shows some awareness of Māori perspectives. Lacks cultural sensitivity.
Reflection Thoughtful reflection on media impact. Basic reflection with some personal connection. Minimal reflection.

Extension Activities

  • Research Project: Compare historical vs. modern Māori representation in media.
  • Creative Task: Students create a short film or article portraying Māori culture authentically.
  • Debate: "Should only Māori filmmakers tell Māori stories?"

Adaptations for Diverse Learners

  • Visual Learners: Use graphic organizers for media analysis.
  • Struggling Writers: Allow verbal reflections or bullet-point responses.
  • Advanced Students: Assign additional research on indigenous media globally.
  • ESOL Students: Provide sentence starters for discussions.
