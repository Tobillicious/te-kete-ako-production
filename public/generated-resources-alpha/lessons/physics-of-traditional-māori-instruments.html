He Mihi / Introduction

This resource explores the physics of sound through the lens of traditional MƒÅori instruments (ngƒÅ taonga p≈´oro). Students will discover how these culturally significant objects demonstrate fundamental principles of sound waves, vibration, and resonance.

Cultural Context

NgƒÅ taonga p≈´oro (MƒÅori musical instruments) are more than just sound-making devices. They are considered treasures (taonga) that connect people to the natural world, ancestors, and spiritual realms. Each instrument has specific cultural protocols, stories, and appropriate contexts for use.

Traditional knowledge holders (tohunga) understood the physics of these instruments through observation and practice, even without modern scientific terminology. This resource respectfully bridges traditional MƒÅori knowledge with contemporary physics education.

Cultural Safety & Respectful Practice

  • Always handle instruments with care and respect
  • Some instruments have specific cultural protocols - research before use
  • When possible, invite local knowledge holders to share expertise
  • Acknowledge the iwi/tribal origins of specific instruments
  • Use te reo MƒÅori terms correctly and consistently

WhakamƒÅtau / Practical Investigation

Creating Your Own P≈´rerehua (Bullroarer)

Teacher Implementation Guide

Time: 45-60 minutes

Materials: Cardboard, string, scissors, markers for decoration

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Introduction (10 mins): Discuss the p≈´rerehua and its cultural significance. Show examples or videos.
  2. Design Phase (15 mins): Students design their p≈´rerehua on cardboard. Different shapes will produce different sounds.
  3. Construction (15 mins): Cut out designs and attach string. Ensure secure attachment points.
  4. Testing & Analysis (15 mins): Students test their instruments and observe how design affects sound.
  5. Discussion (5 mins): Connect observations to physics principles of vibration and frequency.

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Support: Provide pre-cut templates, work in small groups with teacher support
  • Extension: Challenge students to create p≈´rerehua that produce specific pitches or test different materials

Investigation Questions:

  • How does the size of your p≈´rerehua affect the sound it produces?
  • What happens to the sound when you spin it faster or slower?
  • How does the length of the string affect the rotation and sound?
  • Compare your p≈´rerehua with others - what design elements create different sounds?
