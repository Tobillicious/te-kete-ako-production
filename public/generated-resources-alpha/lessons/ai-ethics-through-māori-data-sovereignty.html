AI Ethics Through Māori Data Sovereignty

Digital Technology | Years 11-13

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Explain the concept of Māori Data Sovereignty (tino rangatiratanga over data)
  • Analyze AI systems through a Māori ethical framework
  • Apply principles of kaitiakitanga (guardianship) to technology design
  • Evaluate case studies of AI applications in Aotearoa
  • Develop ethical guidelines for AI development incorporating Māori perspectives

NZ Curriculum Alignment: Digital Technologies Progress Outcomes 6-8, Social Sciences, Te Marautanga o Aotearoa

Cultural Context: Tikanga Māori and Technology

This lesson is grounded in tikanga Māori (Māori customary practices) and the principle of manaakitanga (respect and care for people). Key considerations:

  • Whakapapa: Data represents people and their connections - treat it as tapu (sacred)
  • Kaitiakitanga: Responsibility to protect data for future generations
  • Mana: AI systems must maintain and enhance the mana (prestige, authority) of individuals and communities
  • Wairuatanga: Considering the spiritual dimension of technology and data

Note to kaiako (teachers): Be mindful that some students may have personal connections to data sovereignty issues. Create a safe space for discussion.

Understanding Māori Data Sovereignty

Māori Data Sovereignty asserts that Māori have inherent rights to control data about:

  • Māori people
  • Māori lands and resources
  • Māori culture and knowledge (mātauranga Māori)
  • Māori language (te reo Māori)

Reflection: Whose Data Is It?

Consider a social media platform that collects data about users in Aotearoa:

  • Who should control Māori users' data?
  • How might current data practices conflict with Māori values?
  • What would ethical data collection look like from a Māori perspective?

Case Study: Facial Recognition in Aotearoa

Analyze this real-world scenario:

Police Facial Recognition Trial (2020)

A police trial used facial recognition technology with drivers license photos. Key issues:

  • No specific consultation with Māori
  • Potential for bias against Māori facial features
  • Data stored overseas without Māori oversight

Discussion Questions:

  1. Which principles of Māori Data Sovereignty were potentially violated?
  2. How could tikanga have informed better design?
  3. What alternative approaches might align with Māori values?

Developing Ethical AI Guidelines

Create principles for ethical AI development in Aotearoa that honor Māori Data Sovereignty:

Co-Design Activity

Working in groups, develop 3-5 guidelines for ethical AI that incorporate both technical requirements and Māori values.

Example starter: "AI systems must be co-designed with Māori communities to ensure tino rangatiratanga over Māori data."

Extension Activities

1. Comparative Analysis

Research Indigenous data sovereignty movements in other countries (e.g., First Nations in Canada, Native Americans in US). Compare their approaches to Māori Data Sovereignty.

2. Prototype Design

Design a mock-up for a data collection interface that honors Māori Data Sovereignty principles. Consider:

  • Language options (te reo Māori first)
  • Clear explanations of data use
  • Options for Māori control

3. Community Consultation Plan

Develop a plan for how a tech company should consult with Māori communities before implementing AI systems that might affect them.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Before the Lesson:

  • Familiarize yourself with Te Mana Raraunga (Māori Data Sovereignty Network)
  • Connect with local Māori experts if possible (check with school's Māori liaison)
  • Prepare printed copies of case studies for students who prefer paper

During the Lesson:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer) if appropriate for your school context
  • Use think-pair-share strategies for sensitive discussions
  • Be prepared to address questions about government data use

Assessment Options:

  • Written reflection on case studies
  • Group presentation of ethical guidelines
  • Individual research project on related topic
