AI Ethics Through Māori Data Sovereignty

He Whakamārama | Overview

This lesson explores ethical considerations in artificial intelligence (AI) through the lens of Māori Data Sovereignty (Te Mana Raraunga). Students will examine how indigenous perspectives challenge dominant tech narratives and develop culturally responsive approaches to AI development.

Ngā Whāinga Ako | Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Explain key principles of Māori Data Sovereignty (Te Mana Raraunga)
  • Analyze AI ethics issues through a Māori worldview
  • Apply tikanga Māori to digital technology scenarios
  • Develop culturally responsive AI design principles
  • Evaluate existing AI systems using indigenous ethical frameworks

Horopaki Ahurea | Cultural Context

Te Mana Raraunga (Māori Data Sovereignty) asserts that Māori have inherent rights to control, access, and govern data about Māori people, resources, and knowledge. This includes:

  • Whakapapa (genealogical data)
  • Mātauranga Māori (traditional knowledge)
  • Taonga (cultural treasures in digital form)

Cultural Safety Considerations

  • Ensure discussions about Māori knowledge remain respectful
  • Use Māori concepts only with proper context and attribution
  • Invite Māori perspectives (students, community members, or experts)
  • Acknowledge that some knowledge may be tapu (sacred/restricted)

Ngā Mahi | Activities

1. Whakataukī Analysis (20 mins)

Examine these whakataukī (proverbs) about knowledge and guardianship:

"He kai kei aku ringa" (There is food at my hands - the value of self-determination)

"Toitū te kupu, toitū te mana, toitū te whenua" (The word is permanent, the mana is permanent, the land is permanent)

Discussion: How do these principles apply to data and AI?

2. Case Study: Facial Recognition (30 mins)

Analyze how facial recognition AI has been used with indigenous peoples:

  • Māori ancestral images in museum collections
  • Police surveillance in Aotearoa
  • Genealogical DNA databases

Task: In groups, create a Te Tiriti-based assessment framework for AI projects.

3. Design Challenge (40 mins)

Develop principles for an AI system that honors Te Mana Raraunga. Consider:

  • Who controls the data?
  • How is consent obtained?
  • How are benefits shared?
  • How is harm prevented?

Aromātai | Assessment

Students can demonstrate understanding through:

  • Group presentation of AI design principles
  • Written reflection on case studies
  • Participation in discussions
Criteria Achieved Merit Excellence
Understanding of Te Mana Raraunga Basic description Clear explanation with examples Insightful analysis with connections to AI
Application to AI Ethics Identifies simple connections Develops relevant principles Creates innovative, culturally-grounded solutions

Ngā Mahi Whakawhānui | Extension Activities

  • Research international indigenous data sovereignty movements
  • Interview local tech developers about Māori data practices
  • Create a prototype for a Māori-governed data repository
  • Compare Western and Māori ethical frameworks in AI

Ngā Whakarerekētanga | Adaptations

For diverse learners:

  • Visual learners: Create infographics of data sovereignty principles
  • Kinesthetic learners: Role-play data governance scenarios
  • ESOL students: Provide key terms in te reo Māori and English
  • Extension: Connect with Māori tech professionals via video

Ngā Rauemi | Resources

  • Te Mana Raraunga website: temanararaunga.maori.nz
  • AI Forum NZ's Māori AI report
  • Waitangi Tribunal WAI 262 report
