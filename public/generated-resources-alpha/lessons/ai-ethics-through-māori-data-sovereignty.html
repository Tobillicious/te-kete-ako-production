AI Ethics Through Māori Data Sovereignty

Digital Technology | Years 11-13

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand key principles of Māori Data Sovereignty (Tino Rangatiratanga over data)
  • Analyze AI systems through a Te Ao Māori ethical lens
  • Apply the Mātāpono (principles) of Māori data governance to technology design
  • Develop culturally responsive AI ethics guidelines
  • Create digital solutions that honor Te Tiriti obligations

Cultural Context: Tikanga in Technology

This lesson is grounded in Mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge systems) and the concept of Manaakitanga (care and respect) in digital spaces. Key considerations:

  • Whakapapa: Data as taonga (treasure) with ancestral connections
  • Kaitiakitanga: Guardianship responsibilities when handling data
  • Wairuatanga: Spiritual dimensions of digital information
  • Tikanga protocols for data collection, storage, and use

Cultural Safety Note: When discussing Māori concepts, ensure pronunciation is accurate and context is respectful. Invite Māori students to share perspectives if they choose, but don't require them to be cultural experts.

Understanding Māori Data Sovereignty

Māori Data Sovereignty asserts that Māori have inherent rights to control Māori data, including:

  • Data about Māori individuals, whānau, hapū, iwi, or communities
  • Data produced by Māori researchers or organizations
  • Data collected from Māori lands, waters, or resources

Case Study Analysis: Facial Recognition in Aotearoa

Read this scenario:

"A government agency proposes using AI-powered facial recognition in public spaces to improve security. The system would collect and analyze images of all people in these areas, including Māori cultural events held in public venues."
  1. How might this impact Māori mana (prestige/authority)?
  2. What tikanga concerns arise about collecting images at cultural events?
  3. How could the system design better honor Te Tiriti principles?
  4. What alternatives might better balance security and sovereignty?

Design Activity: Ethical AI Guidelines

Create a set of guidelines for ethical AI development informed by Māori Data Sovereignty principles.

Principle Matching Exercise

Match each Māori concept with its application in AI ethics:

Manaakitanga: Prioritizing user wellbeing in system design

Kaitiakitanga: Responsible data stewardship and environmental impact

Whanaungatanga: Community participation in technology development

Guideline Development Template

Use this structure to create 3-5 guidelines:

  1. Principle: (Māori concept)
  2. Application: (How it applies to AI systems)
  3. Implementation: (Practical steps for developers)

Extension Activities

Option 1: Comparative Analysis

Research another Indigenous data sovereignty movement (e.g., First Nations in Canada, Native Americans in US) and compare approaches with Māori Data Sovereignty.

Option 2: Technical Prototype

Design a wireframe for an AI interface that implements your ethical guidelines, showing how sovereignty principles would work in practice.

Option 3: Policy Brief

Write a 1-page recommendation for your school's technology policy incorporating Māori Data Sovereignty principles.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge:

Students should have basic understanding of:

  • AI and machine learning fundamentals
  • General data ethics concepts
  • Te Tiriti o Waitangi principles

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Scaffolding: Provide pre-filled examples for guideline development
  • Extension: Have advanced students develop implementation roadmaps
  • Cultural Support: Partner with local iwi or Māori technology professionals

Timing:

  • Introduction: 30 minutes
  • Case Study: 45 minutes
  • Guideline Development: 60 minutes
  • Presentation/Reflection: 30 minutes
