Cultural Context & Safety Notes

Cultural Context: Traditional MƒÅori narratives (p≈´rƒÅkau) often follow structures that differ from Western storytelling. Key elements include:

  • Whakapapa (genealogical connections)
  • Cyclical time rather than linear progression
  • Deep connection to whenua (land) and natural world
  • Teaching through metaphor rather than direct moral statements

Safety Notes:

  • Some traditional stories may be tapu (sacred) to specific iwi
  • Always acknowledge the source of any traditional stories used
  • Encourage students to create original stories rather than retell sacred narratives
  • Consult with local kaumƒÅtua (elders) if unsure about appropriate content
