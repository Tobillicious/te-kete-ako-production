Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop essential organizational skills for high school success
  • Understand key tikanga (cultural protocols) in secondary school settings
  • Apply time management strategies to balance academic and personal commitments
  • Build confidence in navigating new social and learning environments
  • Recognize the importance of whanaungatanga (relationships) in their learning journey

Te Ao Māori Context: Transitioning with Confidence

In te ao Māori, transitions are seen as important stages of growth (tipu). This resource incorporates key concepts to support your journey:

  • Manaakitanga: Showing care and respect for yourself, others, and your new environment
  • Whanaungatanga: Building positive relationships with teachers and peers
  • Kaitiakitanga: Taking responsibility for your learning and resources
  • Wairua: Maintaining your spiritual and emotional wellbeing during change

Tikanga tip: When entering new spaces (like different classrooms), pause briefly to acknowledge the space - this shows respect and helps you mentally prepare for learning.

Essential Skills for High School

1. Organization System

Create a system that works for you:

  • Use a physical planner or digital calendar
  • Color-code subjects for easy identification
  • Keep a "homework due dates" page visible
  • Pack your bag the night before

2. Time Management Techniques

Try these strategies:

  • Pomodoro Technique: 25 minutes focused work, 5 minute break
  • Te Wā Tautoko (Priority Time): Identify your most important 3 tasks each day
  • He Tikanga Mo Te Whakatā (Break Routine): Schedule regular short breaks to maintain focus

3. Building Whanaungatanga (Relationships)

Strong relationships support learning:

  • Learn your teachers' names and greet them
  • Find at least one classmate in each subject to connect with
  • Join a club or group that interests you
  • Practice active listening in conversations

Cultural connection: In te ao Māori, we say "He aha te mea nui o te ao? He tangata, he tangata, he tangata" - What is the most important thing in the world? It is people, it is people, it is people.

Extension Activities

1. Create a Personal Pepeha

Develop a pepeha (introduction) that shares who you are and your connections. This can help you introduce yourself to new people and feel grounded in your identity.

2. Design Your Ideal Study Space

Create a plan for a study space at home that supports your learning. Consider:

  • Lighting and comfort
  • Minimizing distractions
  • Having necessary supplies nearby
  • Personal touches that inspire you

Teacher Implementation Notes

Suggested Use: This resource works well as:

  • A transition unit at the start of Year 9
  • Weekly skill-building focus throughout Term 1
  • Individual student support resource
  • Whānau engagement tool for parent-teacher meetings

Differentiation:

  • For students needing more support: Focus on one skill area at a time with concrete examples
  • For extension: Have students create their own skill-building resources to share with peers

Cultural Considerations:

  • Invite local iwi representatives to discuss traditional Māori approaches to learning and transitions
  • Encourage students to share their own cultural perspectives on learning and community
