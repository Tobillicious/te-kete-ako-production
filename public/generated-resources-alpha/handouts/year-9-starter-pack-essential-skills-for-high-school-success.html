Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop essential skills for successful transition to high school
  • Understand key tikanga (protocols) for navigating school spaces
  • Apply time management and organization strategies
  • Build positive relationships with peers and kaiako (teachers)
  • Develop self-advocacy and problem-solving skills
  • Connect learning to personal identity and cultural values

Te Ao Māori: Cultural Context

This resource acknowledges the importance of cultural identity during transitions. In te ao Māori, transitions (whakawhiti) are seen as important stages of growth.

Tikanga for High School

  • Manaakitanga: Show care and respect for all people in your new school community
  • Whanaungatanga: Build positive relationships with peers and teachers
  • Kaitiakitanga: Take responsibility for your learning spaces and resources
  • Wairua: Maintain your wellbeing during this time of change

Essential Skills for High School

1. Organization System

Create a system to manage your:

  • Timetable and room locations
  • Homework and assignment deadlines
  • Equipment for different subjects

2. Navigating School Spaces

Complete these tasks in your first two weeks:

  • Locate your whare (form room), library, and key offices
  • Identify safe spaces and support people
  • Learn the school's pepeha (if available)
  • Find where to go for cultural support (e.g., Māori/Pasifika units)

3. Digital Citizenship

High school brings more digital responsibility. Complete this self-check:

Digital Readiness Checklist

I know my school email and password

I can access the school's learning management system

I understand our school's cyber safety policies

I know who to ask for tech support

I have a system for organizing digital files

Extension Activities

1. Create a Transition Guide

Design a guide for next year's Year 9 students including:

  • Map of important locations
  • Tips for managing different subjects
  • Cultural advice for new students
  • Your personal "wish I'd known" tips

2. Personal Pepeha

Develop your personal pepeha to introduce yourself. Include:

  • Your maunga (mountain) and awa (river)
  • Your whānau connections
  • Your aspirations for high school

Share with your whare group when comfortable.

Implementation Notes

Suggested Timeline

  • Week 1-2: Focus on orientation activities and cultural connections
  • Week 3-4: Implement organization systems and digital skills
  • Ongoing: Use rubric for term 1 progress checks

Differentiation Strategies

  • Provide templates for students needing more structure
  • Offer digital and paper-based options for activities
  • Create small group discussions for cultural reflections
  • Pair new students with Year 10 mentors

Cultural Considerations

  • Connect with local iwi for school-specific protocols
  • Invite senior Māori students to share transition experiences
  • Provide opportunities for students to share in safe spaces
