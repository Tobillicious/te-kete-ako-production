Year 9 Starter Pack: Essential Skills for High School Success

"Whāia te iti kahurangi ki te tūohu koe me he maunga teitei"

(Seek the treasure you value most dearly: if you bow your head, let it be to a lofty mountain)

Kia ora and Welcome to High School!

This starter pack will help you navigate your first year at high school with confidence. We've included practical tips, Māori perspectives on learning, and interactive activities to set you up for success.

Te Ao Māori Perspective on Transitions

In Māori tradition, transitions (huringa) are important times of growth. Just as the pūriri moth transforms, you are entering a new phase of learning. Remember:

  • You bring your whakapapa (genealogy) and unique strengths
  • Learning is a collective journey - don't be afraid to ask for help
  • Your kaiako (teachers) are here to guide you

Essential Skills for Success

1. Organisation

Whakataukī: "Ma pango, ma whero, ka oti te mahi" (With black and red, the work will be completed)

Try these strategies:

  • Use a planner or digital calendar for assignments
  • Keep subject materials in separate folders
  • Pack your bag the night before

2. Time Management

The Māori lunar calendar (maramataka) teaches us about cycles and timing. Apply this wisdom:

  • Break big tasks into smaller steps (divide your mahi)
  • Prioritize tasks using the 4-quadrant method (urgent/important)
  • Schedule regular breaks (pause to reflect)

3. Study Skills

Traditional Māori learning was multi-sensory - use these approaches:

  • Create visual mind maps (whakapapa of ideas)
  • Teach concepts to someone else (tuakana-teina approach)
  • Use mnemonic devices with Māori kupu (words)

Whanaungatanga (Building Relationships)

Success comes from strong connections. Try these relationship builders:

  • Introduce yourself to at least one new person each week
  • Join a school club or kapa haka group
  • Practice active listening (whakarongo)
  • Ask for help when needed - it shows strength

Cultural Safety Reminder

Our school is a place where:

  • All cultural backgrounds are respected
  • Māori knowledge is valued alongside other knowledge systems
  • We practice manaakitanga (care for others)

If you ever feel unsafe or disrespected, please talk to your dean or a trusted teacher.

Personal Reflection

Complete this whakaaro (reflection) at the end of each term:

  1. What am I most proud of this term?
  2. What was challenging, and how did I handle it?
  3. Who supported my learning journey?
  4. What will I focus on next term?
