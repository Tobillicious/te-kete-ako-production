Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop essential skills for successful transition to high school
  • Understand key school protocols and expectations
  • Apply time management and organization strategies
  • Build positive relationships with peers and kaiako (teachers)
  • Navigate school spaces with confidence
  • Incorporate tikanga Māori into daily school life

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Ko te reo te mauri o te mana Māori - Language is the life force of Māori prestige

Whakawhanaungatanga (Building Relationships)

In Te Ao Māori, relationships are foundational. As you start high school:

  • Take time to introduce yourself properly - share your pepeha if you have one
  • Respect the mana (prestige) of your teachers and classmates
  • Participate in school pōwhiri (welcome ceremonies)

Pepeha Template:

Ko ______ te maunga (mountain)
Ko ______ te awa (river)
Ko ______ te waka (canoe)
Ko ______ tōku iwi (tribe)
Ko ______ tōku hapū (sub-tribe)
Ko ______ tōku marae
Ko ______ ahau (I am ______)

Manaakitanga (Care and Respect)

High school spaces should be treated with care:

  • Remove shoes when entering wharenui (meeting houses)
  • Follow kawa (protocols) for your school's marae
  • Respect tapu (sacred) spaces and objects

Essential Skills for High School

1. Organization Systems

Create a system that works for you:

  • Use a physical planner or digital calendar
  • Color-code subjects
  • Set up subject folders (physical and digital)

2. Time Management

Balance schoolwork, extracurriculars, and personal time:

  • Use the Pomodoro technique (25 min work, 5 min break)
  • Prioritize tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix
  • Schedule regular breaks and self-care time
Urgent Not Urgent
Important Do now (e.g., due tomorrow) Schedule (e.g., long-term project)
Not Important Delegate (e.g., group work tasks) Eliminate (time-wasters)

3. Navigating School Spaces

Become familiar with your new environment:

  • Locate key areas: library, wharenui, nurse, deans' offices
  • Learn the quickest routes between classes
  • Identify safe spaces and support services

Activity: Draw a simple map of your school highlighting these key locations.
