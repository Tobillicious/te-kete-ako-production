Te Ao Māori Transition Support

In Māori worldview, transitions are significant events marked by poroporoaki (farewells) and mihi whakatau (welcomes). As you begin high school:

  • Whakawhanaungatanga - Building relationships is essential. Introduce yourself to teachers and classmates.
  • Manaakitanga - Show care and respect for others in your new school community.
  • Kaitiakitanga - Take responsibility for your learning environment and resources.
  • Wānanga - Approach learning as a collaborative journey with your teachers and peers.

Remember your pepeha (personal introduction) as you meet new people and establish your place in the school.

Essential Skills for High School

1. Time Management

High school requires balancing multiple subjects and activities. Try this:

Weekly Planner Challenge

Block out these essential elements in a typical week:

  • School hours (8:30am-3:00pm)
  • Homework/study time (aim for 1-2 hours daily)
  • Extracurricular activities
  • Family time
  • Personal relaxation

Tip: Use the Māori concept of taiao (environment) - create a study space that works for you.

2. Organization Systems

Develop a rauemi (resource) management system:

  • Subject folders/binders with color coding
  • Digital organization (Google Drive folders matching subjects)
  • Homework diary or digital calendar
  • Regular locker clean-outs (weekly māramatanga - clarity)

3. Relationship Building

Connection Challenge

This week, make an effort to:

  • Learn one teacher's name and something about them
  • Introduce yourself to someone new in your class
  • Find a Year 10 student who can share advice
  • Identify a staff member you can go to for help

Remember the Māori value of ako - we are all teachers and learners.

Extension Activities

1. School Whakataukī (Proverb) Research

Research your school's motto or find a whakataukī that resonates with you. Create a small poster explaining how it relates to high school success.

2. Digital Whakapapa of Learning

Create a digital timeline showing your learning journey from primary to high school, highlighting key skills and experiences that have prepared you for this transition.

3. Tuakana-Teina Mentor Program

Investigate if your school has a peer mentor program. If not, design a proposal for how Year 9 and Year 10 students could support each other.
