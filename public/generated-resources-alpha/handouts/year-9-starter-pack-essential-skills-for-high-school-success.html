Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, students will:

  • Develop essential skills for successful high school transition
  • Understand key school tikanga (protocols) and expectations
  • Apply time management and organization strategies
  • Build positive relationships with peers and teachers
  • Incorporate Te Ao Māori values into daily school life

Cultural Context: Te Whare Tapa Whā Model

The Te Whare Tapa Whā model reminds us that successful transition requires balance across four dimensions:

The Four Pillars of Wellbeing

  1. Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing): Maintain healthy routines with sleep, nutrition and exercise
  2. Taha Hinengaro (Mental/Emotional Wellbeing): Develop positive mindset and stress management
  3. Taha Whānau (Social Wellbeing): Build supportive relationships
  4. Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing): Connect with identity, values and purpose

Tikanga Considerations: Remember to mihi (greet) teachers properly, respect shared spaces, and participate in school karakia (prayers) and waiata (songs).

Tip: Include whānau time and cultural activities in your schedule.

2. Organization Check

I have a dedicated study space at home
My school bag is packed the night before
I use a diary or digital planner
I have systems for organizing notes
I know where to find school resources

3. Relationship Building

Practice introducing yourself using a mihi:

"Ko [your mountain] te maunga
Ko [your river] te awa
Ko [your iwi] te iwi
Ko [your name] tōku ingoa"

Great effort! Remember - it's okay if you're still learning your pepeha. What matters is showing respect by trying.

Extension Activities

1. Whakataukī (Proverb) Reflection

Choose one of these whakataukī and write how it relates to your transition:

  • "Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini" - My strength is not as an individual but as a collective
  • "Kia kaha, kia māia, kia manawanui" - Be strong, be brave, be steadfast

2. School Hīkoi (Tour)

With a buddy, create a map of important school locations including:

  • Māori department/wharenui
  • Health center
  • Library
  • Your locker
  • Favorite quiet space

Teacher Implementation Notes

Delivery Suggestions:

  • Use as a 2-3 lesson transition unit or spread across term 1
  • Pair with school-specific orientation information
  • Invite senior students to share their transition experiences
  • Connect with Māori department for cultural support

Differentiation:

  • Provide templates for students needing more structure
  • Offer digital and paper options for activities
  • Create small discussion groups for relationship activities
