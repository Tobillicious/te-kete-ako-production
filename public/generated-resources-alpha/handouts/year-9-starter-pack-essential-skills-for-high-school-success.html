Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop practical strategies for managing the transition to high school
  • Understand key tikanga (protocols) for navigating school spaces
  • Build organizational and time management skills
  • Apply Māori concepts of whanaungatanga (relationships) and manaakitanga (care) in school contexts
  • Create personal systems for academic success

Te Ao Māori: Cultural Context

Ngā Tikanga o te Kura Tuarua (High School Protocols)

In te ao Māori, transitions are marked by rituals and support systems. Consider these cultural concepts:

  • Whakawhanaungatanga - Building relationships with teachers and peers
  • Manaakitanga - Showing respect in shared spaces (classrooms, library, dining areas)
  • Kaitiakitanga - Caring for school resources and environment
  • Tuakana-teina - The importance of peer support systems

Remember: Your whakapapa (identity) and culture are strengths in your learning journey.

1. Navigating Your New Environment

  • Practice your timetable route before school starts

4. Building Whanaungatanga (Relationships)

Complete this whakawhanaungatanga (relationship building) challenge:

Introduce yourself to one new person each day this week
Learn one interesting fact about each of your teachers
Find a tuakana (senior student) who can offer guidance
Join one school club or group

Extension Activities

1. Create a Pepeha for School

Develop a school-specific pepeha (introduction) that includes:

  • Your whānau connections
  • Your previous school
  • Your hopes for high school
  • What you can contribute to the school community

2. Design a School Whakataukī (Proverb)

Create or find a whakataukī that represents your approach to high school. Example:

"Whāia te iti kahurangi ki te tūohu koe me he maunga teitei"
(Seek the treasure you value most dearly; if you bow your head, let it be to a lofty mountain)
