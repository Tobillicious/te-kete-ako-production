Workplace Readiness with Cultural Competency

Preparing for a Bicultural Aotearoa Workplace

Learning Objectives

  • Understand key aspects of workplace professionalism in Aotearoa
  • Develop cultural competency for working in bicultural environments
  • Apply Te Tiriti o Waitangi principles in workplace scenarios
  • Practice effective communication in diverse teams
  • Demonstrate awareness of tikanga Māori in professional settings

Cultural Context & Safety

This resource acknowledges:

  • The unique bicultural foundation of Aotearoa workplaces
  • The importance of Te Tiriti o Waitangi in professional environments
  • That cultural competency is an ongoing learning journey

Safety Note: Create a respectful space where students can ask questions about cultural concepts. Encourage curiosity while maintaining cultural integrity.

Key Concepts

1. Te Tiriti in the Workplace

How the principles of partnership, participation and protection apply in modern workplaces:

  • Partnership: Māori representation in decision-making
  • Participation: Equal opportunities for career advancement
  • Protection: Valuing te reo Māori and tikanga

2. Tikanga in Professional Settings

Common workplace scenarios where tikanga applies:

  • Mihi whakatau (welcome protocols)
  • Karakia (opening/closing meetings)
  • Whanaungatanga (relationship building)
  • Kaitiakitanga (environmental responsibility)

3. Cultural Intelligence Self-Assessment

Rate your confidence (1-5) in these areas:

  • Pronouncing Māori names correctly
  • Understanding basic workplace te reo
  • Recognising when to apply tikanga
  • Navigating cultural differences in teamwork

Implementation Guide

  1. Pre-lesson: Survey students' prior workplace experiences
  2. Session 1: Introduce bicultural workplace concepts
  3. Session 2: Practice workplace scenarios in pairs
  4. Session 3: Guest speaker from bicultural organisation
  5. Session 4: Reflection and goal-setting

Assessment Rubric

Criteria Emerging (1-2) Developing (3) Proficient (4) Advanced (5)
Cultural Awareness Basic recognition of cultural differences Can describe some cultural concepts Applies cultural knowledge appropriately Adapts behavior to cultural context
Communication Occasionally uses appropriate terms Generally communicates respectfully Adjusts communication style effectively Mediates cross-cultural misunderstandings
Professionalism Follows basic workplace norms Demonstrates reliable work habits Shows initiative in team settings Models exemplary professional conduct

Extension Activities

1. Bicultural Policy Analysis

Research and compare workplace cultural policies from 2-3 NZ organisations.

2. Te Reo in the Workplace

Create a glossary of 20 useful workplace te reo phrases with audio recordings.

3. Case Study Development

Write and present a case study about resolving a cultural misunderstanding at work.

Adaptations

For ESOL Students: Provide bilingual vocabulary lists and visual supports.

For Neurodiverse Learners: Offer structured templates for workplace scenarios.

For Advanced Students: Arrange workplace shadowing experiences.

Teacher Reflection

Consider: How will you model cultural competency in your teaching practice? What professional development might support you in this area?
