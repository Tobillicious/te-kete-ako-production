Workplace Readiness with Cultural Competency

Preparing for the bicultural workplace in Aotearoa New Zealand

Learning Objectives

  • Understand key principles of workplace professionalism in Aotearoa
  • Develop cultural competency for working in bicultural environments
  • Apply tikanga Māori (Māori customs) in workplace scenarios
  • Communicate effectively in diverse workplace settings
  • Reflect on personal cultural identity and its role in the workplace

Cultural Context & Safety Notes

Important considerations:

  • Māori perspectives should be treated with manaakitanga (respect)
  • Students should feel safe to share their cultural backgrounds
  • Use whanaungatanga (relationship building) to create an inclusive space
  • Consult local iwi if discussing specific tribal knowledge

Key Concepts

1. Bicultural Workplace Practices

In Aotearoa, workplaces often incorporate both Māori and Pākehā ways of working. This includes:

  • Formal pōwhiri (welcome ceremonies) for new staff
  • Use of te reo Māori in communications
  • Respect for mana whenua (local tribal authority)

2. Professional Communication

Effective workplace communication considers:

  • Active listening (whakarongo)
  • Clear, respectful language
  • Cultural differences in communication styles

Interactive Activity: Workplace Scenarios

Scenario 1: Your workplace is hosting a pōwhiri for new staff. What should you do?

Discuss: How would you prepare? What cultural considerations are important?

Scenario 2: A Māori colleague corrects your pronunciation of a whakataukī (proverb). How do you respond?

Role-play: Practice respectful responses in pairs.

Assessment Suggestions

Choose one:

  1. Create a workplace cultural competency guide (digital or print)
  2. Record a video demonstrating respectful workplace interactions
  3. Write a reflection on your own cultural identity in workplace contexts

Extension Activities

For advanced learners:

  • Interview a local business about their bicultural practices
  • Research and compare workplace cultural practices internationally
  • Develop a te reo Māori phrasebook for your future industry

Adaptations for Diverse Learners

Learning Need Adaptation
ESOL students Provide bilingual vocabulary lists
Visual learners Use infographics to explain cultural concepts
Neurodiverse students Offer structured templates for reflections

Teacher Implementation Guide

  1. Pre-lesson: Familiarize yourself with local iwi protocols
  2. Lesson 1: Introduce bicultural workplace concepts
  3. Lesson 2: Conduct scenario activities
  4. Lesson 3: Guide assessment work
  5. Follow-up: Invite local employers to discuss real-world applications

Whakataukī (Proverb) for reflection:
"He aha te mea nui o te ao? He tangata, he tangata, he tangata."
What is the most important thing in the world? It is people, it is people, it is people.
