---## **Cultural Context & Safety Notes**- **Māori Perspectives:** Emphasise mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) alongside Western information literacy frameworks.- **Cultural Bias:** Teach students to recognise when Indigenous voices are excluded or misrepresented.- **Safety Online:** Discuss how misinformation can harm communities (e.g., false claims about Te Tiriti).---## **Teacher Implementation Guide**1. **Introduction (10 min):** - Discuss why information literacy matters in Aotearoa. - Introduce the Pātai Framework.2. **Guided Practice (20 min):** - Model evaluating a website together (e.g., a news article vs. a government source).3. **Independent Activity (15 min):** - Students complete the "Source Detective" task in pairs.4. **Reflection (10 min):** - Share findings and discuss cultural representation.---## **Assessment Suggestions**| **Criteria** | **Achieved** | **Developing** | **Needs Support** ||----------------------------|-------------|---------------|------------------|| Identifies reliable sources | Uses multiple checks | Uses some checks | Struggles to evaluate || Recognises cultural bias | Clearly explains bias | Identifies some bias | Misses cultural context || Applies tikanga | Respects Māori knowledge | Attempts to consider | Overlooks cultural aspects |---## **Extension Activities**1. **Debate:** "Should social media platforms fact-check cultural information?"2. **Research Project:** Compare how different cultures document history online.3. **Create a Guide:** Design a poster teaching younger students about source evaluation.---## **Adaptations for Diverse Learners**- **Visual Learners:** Use colour-coded checklists.- **ESOL Students:** Provide bilingual (English/Māori) keyword lists.- **Kinesthetic Learners:** Role-play "fact-checking" scenarios.---This resource is ready for immediate classroom use, aligns with NZC, and supports culturally responsive teaching.
