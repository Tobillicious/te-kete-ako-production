Years 7-13 | Mathematics | Bilingual Resource

He Whakamārama | Learning Objectives

By using this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop bilingual mathematical literacy in both Te Reo Māori and English
  • Understand key mathematical concepts through a Te Ao Māori lens
  • Build confidence using Te Reo Māori in mathematical contexts
  • Make connections between mathematical concepts and Māori knowledge systems
  • Apply mathematical terminology appropriately in problem-solving contexts

Te Horopaki Ahurea | Cultural Context

Mathematics has always been present in Māori culture through:

  • Tātai (counting systems)
  • Whakapapa (genealogical calculations)
  • Māramatanga (patterns and spatial awareness)
  • Tikanga-ā-iwi (cultural practices involving measurement and calculation)

Teacher Note: Tikanga Considerations

When teaching mathematics with Te Reo Māori:

  • Pronounce Māori words correctly - practice with students
  • Highlight connections to Māori knowledge systems where appropriate
  • Encourage students to use terms in context, not just memorization
  • Be aware that some terms may have different meanings in different iwi dialects
  • Create a supportive environment for language learning - mistakes are part of the process

Te Kōnae Kupu | Glossary of Terms

Number & Algebra | Tau me te Tatau

English Te Reo Māori Example/Explanation
Number Tau Ko te 5 tētahi tau (5 is a number)
Addition Tāpiri 5 + 3 = 8 (Ko te 5 tāpirihia ki te 3 ka 8)
Subtraction Tangohanga 8 - 3 = 5 (Ko te 8 tangohia te 3 ka 5)
Multiplication Whakareanga 5 × 3 = 15 (Ko te 5 whakareatia ki te 3 ka 15)
Division Whakawehenga 15 ÷ 3 = 5 (Ko te 15 whakawehea ki te 3 ka 5)
Fraction Hautau ½ - te haurua (one half)
Decimal Tau-ā-ira 3.14 (toru ira tekau mā whā)
Equation Taurite 5 + x = 10 (Ko te 5 tāpirihia ki te x ka 10)

Geometry | Mātai ā-Ahua

English Te Reo Māori Example/Explanation
Shape Āhua He tapatoru tēnei āhua (This shape is a triangle)
Angle Koki He koki hāngai (right angle)
Triangle Tapatoru He tapatoru ōna taha e toru (It has three sides)
Circle Porowhita He porowhita te āhua o te marama (The moon is circular)
Measurement Inenga Te inenga o te tapa (length of the side)
Area Horahanga Te horahanga o te tapawhā (area of the rectangle)
Volume Nui Te nui o te pouaka (volume of the box)

Statistics | Tauanga

English Te Reo Māori Example/Explanation
Data Raraunga He raraunga mō ngā tāngata (data about people)
Graph Kauwhata He kauwhata rārangi (line graph)
Average Toharite Ko te toharite o ngā tau (average of the numbers)
Probability Tūponotanga Te tūponotanga o te kōwhiringa (probability of the outcome)
Survey Uiuianga He uiuianga mō ngā hiahia (survey about preferences)

Other Important Terms

English Te Reo Māori Example/Explanation
Problem Raruraru He raruraru pāngarau (a maths problem)
Solution Whakautu Te whakautu o te raruraru (solution to the problem)
Pattern Tauira He tauira o ngā tau (pattern of numbers)
Calculate Tātai Tātai i te otinga (calculate the answer)

Aromatawai | Assessment

Glossary Mastery Checklist

  • Can correctly pronounce at least 20 mathematical terms in Te Reo Māori
  • Can use at least 10 terms appropriately in mathematical sentences
  • Can explain the connection between at least 3 mathematical concepts and Māori knowledge systems
  • Can solve basic problems using Te Reo Māori mathematical terminology
  • Can create a simple bilingual word problem

Ngā Ngohe Anō | Extension Activities

1. Te Whare Tapa Whā Maths Challenge

Create mathematical problems based on the four dimensions of wellbeing:

  • Taha Tinana (Physical): Calculate nutritional values or exercise metrics
  • Taha Hinengaro (Mental): Create number patterns or logic puzzles
  • Taha Whānau (Family): Calculate whakapapa generations or family statistics
  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual): Explore mathematical patterns in nature

2. Te Reo Word Problems

Write and solve word problems entirely in Te Reo Māori. Example:

E 15 ngā āporo kei rō kete. Ka hokona e 7. E hia ngā āporo e toe ana?
