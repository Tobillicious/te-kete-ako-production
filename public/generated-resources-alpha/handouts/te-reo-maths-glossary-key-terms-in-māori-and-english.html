Learning Objectives | Ngā Whāinga Ako

By using this resource, students will:

  • Develop mathematical literacy in both English and Te Reo Māori
  • Understand key mathematical concepts through bilingual terminology
  • Make connections between mathematical ideas and Te Ao Māori perspectives
  • Build confidence in using Te Reo Māori in academic contexts
  • Recognise the value of indigenous knowledge systems in mathematics

Cultural Context | Te Horopaki Ahurea

Mathematics has always been present in Māori culture, from the complex geometric patterns in tukutuku panels to the sophisticated navigation systems used by Polynesian voyagers. This glossary acknowledges that mathematical thinking existed in Aotearoa long before European arrival.

Tikanga Considerations

  • Pronunciation matters - take time to learn correct pronunciation of Māori terms
  • Some concepts may not have direct translations - these are noted with explanations
  • Encourage students to explore Māori mathematical knowledge systems
  • Respect regional variations in terminology where they exist

Mathematics Glossary | Pātaka Kupu Pāngarau

Number | Tau
A mathematical object used to count, measure, and label.
Number Concepts
Fraction | Hautanga
Represents a part of a whole (e.g., ½ is "hautoru" for one half).
Number Concepts
Decimal | Tau ā-ira
A number that uses a decimal point to show tenths, hundredths, etc.
Number Concepts
Algebra | Tātaitanga
The branch of mathematics using symbols to represent numbers.
Algebra
Variable | Taurangi
A symbol (usually a letter) that represents an unknown value.
Algebra
Equation | Whārite
A mathematical statement showing two expressions are equal.
Algebra
Geometry | Mātauranga āhuahanga
The study of shapes, sizes, and properties of space.
Geometry
Triangle | Tapatoru
A three-sided polygon (note: "tapa" means side, "toru" means three).
Geometry
Circle | Porowhita
A perfectly round shape with all points equidistant from the center.
Geometry
Measurement | Inenga
The process of determining the size, length, or amount of something.
Measurement
Area | Horahanga
The size of a surface (traditionally important in land measurement).
Measurement
Volume | Nui ake
The amount of space occupied by a three-dimensional object.
Measurement
Statistics | Tatauranga
The collection, analysis, interpretation of numerical data.
Statistics
Average | Toharite
A single value representing the middle of a data set.
Statistics
Graph | Kauwhata
A visual representation of data or relationships.
Statistics
Pattern | Tauira
A repeated decorative design or sequence in mathematics.
General
Symmetry | Hangarite
When one shape becomes exactly like another when moved.
Geometry
Probability | Tūponotanga
The likelihood of an event occurring.
Statistics

Extension Activities | Ngā Ngohe Whakawhānui

1. Mathematical Whakataukī (Proverbs)

Research traditional Māori proverbs that contain mathematical concepts. For example:

"Ka pū te ruha, ka hao te rangatahi"

(As the old net withers, the new net goes fishing) - can be related to concepts of replacement and renewal cycles.

2. Create Bilingual Word Problems

Write mathematical word problems that incorporate both English and Te Reo Māori terms. For example:

"Kei te hokona e Hemi ētaha porowhita (circles) e rua. Ko te waenganui pātahi (radius) o te tuatahi he 5cm, ko te tuarua he 3cm. He aha te horahanga (area) katoa?"

3. Māori Measurement Systems

Research traditional Māori measurement systems for:

  • Land (whenua)
  • Time (wā)
  • Distance (tawhiti)

Compare these with modern measurement systems.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Suggested Approaches

  • Gradual Introduction: Introduce 3-5 new terms per week rather than all at once
  • Word Walls: Create a classroom display of terms that grows throughout the year
  • Peer Teaching: Have students teach terms to each other in pairs
  • Cross-curricular Links: Connect with Te Reo Māori and Social Sciences teachers

Differentiation Strategies

  • Support: Provide visual cues and cognates (words similar in both languages)
  • Extension: Challenge students to find Māori terms for more advanced concepts
  • Cultural Experts: Invite community members to discuss Māori mathematical knowledge

Assessment Ideas

  • Create bilingual flashcards for self-testing
  • Have students maintain a glossary journal with examples
  • Include bilingual terms in regular math assessments
