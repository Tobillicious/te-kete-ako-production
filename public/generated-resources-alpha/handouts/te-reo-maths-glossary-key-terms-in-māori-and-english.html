Key Mathematical Terms in Te Reo Māori and English

Years 7-13 | Mathematics

Learning Objectives

By using this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Develop bilingual mathematical vocabulary
  • Understand key mathematical concepts through both Māori and English lenses
  • Make connections between mathematical ideas and te ao Māori perspectives
  • Grow confidence in using te reo Māori in academic contexts
  • Recognize the value of indigenous knowledge systems in mathematics

Cultural Context

This resource acknowledges that mathematics has always existed in te ao Māori, with sophisticated systems of measurement, calculation, and pattern recognition embedded in traditional practices like whakairo (carving), raranga (weaving), and celestial navigation.

Tikanga Considerations

  • Whanaungatanga: Encourage students to work collaboratively when learning terms
  • Ako: Create reciprocal learning opportunities where students teach each other
  • Manaakitanga: Support all attempts at te reo pronunciation without correction unless invited
  • Wairua: Acknowledge the spiritual dimension of knowledge transmission

Bilingual Mathematics Glossary

Tatau
Number
The concept of quantity and counting. In te ao Māori, numbers often relate to natural patterns and whakapapa.
Whakarea
Multiplication
The mathematical operation of scaling one number by another. Traditionally used in calculations for food distribution.
Wehe
Division
Splitting into equal parts. Important in traditional resource allocation and land division.
Tāpiri
Addition
Combining quantities. Used in traditional trading systems and gift exchanges.
Tangohanga
Subtraction
Removing quantities from a whole. Applied in resource management and conservation.
Āhua
Shape
Geometric forms with cultural significance, like the spiral (koru) in Māori art.
Paerangi
Axis
Reference lines in coordinate systems. Relates to celestial navigation concepts.
Rārangi
Line
A straight path connecting two points. Fundamental in traditional carving designs.
Pāngarau
Mathematics
The study of patterns and relationships. Embedded in all aspects of Māori knowledge systems.
Taurangi
Variable
A symbol representing an unknown quantity. Similar to concepts in traditional proverbs.
Ture
Formula/Rule
A mathematical relationship. Comparable to tikanga (cultural protocols).
Inenga
Measurement
Determining size or quantity. Traditional methods used body parts and natural materials.

Extension Activities

1. Mathematical Whakataukī (Proverbs)

Research traditional Māori proverbs that contain mathematical concepts. Analyze how they express mathematical ideas through metaphor.

2. Create Bilingual Word Problems

Write mathematical word problems that use both te reo Māori and English, incorporating cultural contexts like marae protocols or natural resource management.

3. Mathematical Art Project

Create artwork that combines mathematical concepts (symmetry, fractals, geometry) with traditional Māori patterns and designs.

4. Measurement Investigation

Compare traditional Māori measurement systems (using body parts, natural materials) with metric system. Create conversion charts.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Before Teaching

  • Familiarize yourself with pronunciation of te reo terms (use Māori Dictionary audio)
  • Identify students with te reo knowledge who can support peers
  • Prepare physical examples where possible (woven patterns for geometry, etc.)

During Teaching

  • Introduce terms gradually, 3-5 per lesson
  • Use physical movement or visual cues to reinforce terms
  • Create word walls with terms and illustrations
  • Encourage students to create their own memory aids

Assessment Ideas

  • Matching games (term to definition in both languages)
  • Create bilingual flashcards
  • Record students explaining concepts in both languages
  • Peer teaching activities
