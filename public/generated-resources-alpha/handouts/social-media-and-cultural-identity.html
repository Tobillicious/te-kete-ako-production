---## **Teacher Implementation Guide**### **1. Learning Objectives (NZC Aligned)**- **Digital Technology (Progress Outcome 4-5):** - Understand the role of digital tools in shaping identity (DDDO – Designing and Developing Digital Outcomes).- **Social Sciences (Level 5):** - Investigate how cultural identity is expressed in digital spaces.### **2. Cultural Safety Considerations**- Ensure discussions on cultural representation are respectful and avoid stereotypes.- Encourage students to reflect on their own cultural backgrounds before analysing others.- Provide guidance on appropriate use of te reo Māori and Pasifika languages.### **3. Step-by-Step Lesson Plan**1. **Introduction (10 min):** - Discuss how social media influences identity. - Show examples of positive and problematic cultural representations.2. **Activity (20 min):** - Students analyse a social media post in pairs. - Facilitate a class discussion on findings.3. **Reflection (10 min):** - Students write a short response on how they can promote cultural respect online.### **4. Assessment Suggestions**- **Formative:** Participation in discussions and analysis activity.- **Summative:** Written reflection or a short digital presentation on cultural identity online.### **5. Extension Activities**- **Advanced Learners:** - Research a case study of cultural appropriation in social media and propose solutions. - Create a digital campaign promoting cultural awareness.### **6. Adaptations for Diverse Learners**- **Visual Learners:** Use infographics or video examples.- **ESOL Students:** Provide bilingual glossaries (English/te reo Māori).- **Struggling Writers:** Allow verbal responses or graphic organisers.---This resource is **ready for immediate classroom use**, aligns with NZC, and supports culturally responsive teaching.
