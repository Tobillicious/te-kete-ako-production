Social Media and Cultural Identity

Learning Objective: Explore how social media influences cultural identity and how individuals can express their culture online in a respectful and meaningful way.

Cultural Context

In Aotearoa New Zealand, social media plays a significant role in how people express their whakapapa (genealogy), tikanga (customs), and mana (prestige). However, it also presents challenges in maintaining cultural authenticity and safety.

Discussion Starter:

How do you see MƒÅori and Pasifika cultures represented on social media? Are these representations accurate and respectful?

Key Concepts

  • Cultural Identity: How individuals express their heritage, values, and traditions.
  • Digital Footprint: The lasting impact of online activity.
  • Cultural Appropriation vs. Appreciation: Understanding respectful engagement with other cultures.

Activity: Analysing Social Media Posts

Task: Choose a social media post that showcases cultural identity (e.g., a MƒÅori haka, Pasifika dance, or cultural celebration).

  1. Describe how the post represents culture.
  2. Identify whether it promotes cultural appreciation or risks appropriation.
  3. Reflect: How could the post be improved to better respect cultural values?

Creating Culturally Safe Content

Guidelines:

  • Always credit cultural knowledge sources.
  • Consult with kaumƒÅtua (elders) or cultural advisors when sharing sacred traditions.
  • Be mindful of tapu (sacredness) and noa (everyday) contexts.

Reflection:

How can you ensure your own social media presence respects and celebrates cultural diversity?
