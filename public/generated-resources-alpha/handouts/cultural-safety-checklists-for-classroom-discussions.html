Cultural Safety Checklists for Classroom Discussions

Year Levels: 7-13 | Subject: Social Sciences

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the concept of cultural safety (ahurea haumaru) in educational contexts
  • Apply tikanga Māori principles to create inclusive learning environments
  • Develop practical strategies for culturally safe classroom discussions
  • Reflect on personal and institutional practices that support Māori student success

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Cultural safety (ahurea haumaru) is grounded in Māori concepts of:

  • Manaakitanga - Showing respect, hospitality and care for others
  • Whanaungatanga - Building and maintaining relationships
  • Wairuatanga - Recognizing spiritual dimensions of learning
  • Kotahitanga - Unity and collective responsibility

These principles should guide all classroom interactions, particularly when discussing sensitive topics or Māori perspectives.

Extension Activities

For Students:

  • Research and present on a Māori model of wellbeing (e.g., Te Whare Tapa Whā)
  • Interview local Māori about their educational experiences and present findings
  • Create a visual representation of what cultural safety means to them

For Teachers:

  • Conduct a cultural safety audit of your classroom/school environment
  • Develop relationships with local iwi and Māori organizations
  • Participate in professional development on Te Tiriti o Waitangi in education

Teacher Implementation Notes

Timing: These checklists can be used before, during, and after any classroom discussion, particularly when addressing topics with cultural dimensions.

Differentiation:

  • For junior students, focus on 2-3 key checklist items at a time
  • With senior students, involve them in co-creating additional checklist items
  • Consider creating student-friendly versions of the checklists

Cultural Considerations:

  • Adapt protocols to your local iwi traditions
  • Consult with your school's Māori community for guidance
  • Remember that cultural safety is an ongoing process, not a checklist to 'complete'
