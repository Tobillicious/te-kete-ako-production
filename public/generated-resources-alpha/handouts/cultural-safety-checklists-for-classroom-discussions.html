Cultural Safety Checklists for Classroom Discussions

Creating Safe Learning Environments for Māori Students | Years 7-13 | Social Sciences

Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, students will:

  • Understand the concept of cultural safety (manaakitanga) in learning environments
  • Develop skills to create and maintain culturally safe spaces for discussion
  • Recognize and respect Māori cultural protocols (tikanga) in classroom settings
  • Apply strategies for inclusive participation that values Māori perspectives
  • Reflect on personal and collective responsibility in maintaining cultural safety

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Cultural safety in the classroom aligns with these key Māori concepts:

Manaakitanga: The process of showing respect, generosity and care for others.

Whanaungatanga: Building and maintaining relationships that create a sense of belonging.

Wairuatanga: Recognizing the spiritual dimension of learning environments.

Kotahitanga: Unity and collective responsibility for the wellbeing of all.

When preparing for discussions, consider:

  • The physical space (seating arrangements, visual representations of Māori culture)
  • Protocols for speaking (mihimihi, who speaks first)
  • Opportunities for different participation styles (written, verbal, group)
  • Methods for handling sensitive topics with care

Cultural Safety Checklists

Before Discussion

Have I established clear tikanga (protocols) for our discussion?
Is the physical environment welcoming to Māori students? (e.g., displays, seating)
Have I provided background information to ensure all students have necessary context?
Are there multiple ways to participate (verbal, written, digital)?
Have I considered any potentially sensitive aspects of the topic?

During Discussion

Am I allowing adequate wait time for responses?
Are Māori perspectives being valued equally to other viewpoints?
Am I monitoring participation to ensure all voices are heard?
Are students demonstrating respect for cultural differences?
Am I prepared to redirect inappropriate comments respectfully?

After Discussion

Have I provided opportunities for reflection on the discussion process?
Have I acknowledged contributions from Māori students appropriately?
Is there follow-up support available for any issues raised?
Have I gathered feedback on the cultural safety of the discussion?
Have I considered how to improve for next time?

Extension Activities

1. Cultural Safety Audit

In small groups, conduct an audit of your classroom/school environment using the checklists. Present findings and recommendations to the class.

2. Pepeha Sharing Circle

Create opportunities for students to share their pepeha (Māori introduction) in a culturally safe way, building connections and understanding.

3. Case Study Analysis

Analyze real or fictional classroom scenarios to identify cultural safety strengths and areas for improvement.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Preparation: Familiarize yourself with local iwi protocols and consult with Māori colleagues or community members when planning sensitive discussions.

Introducing the Concept: Begin with a karakia (prayer/invocation) to set a respectful tone. Explain why cultural safety matters using examples relevant to your students.

Ongoing Practice: Use the checklists regularly to build habits. Involve students in creating class protocols and reviewing them periodically.

Differentiation: Adapt checklists for different year levels - simplify language for junior students, add complexity for seniors.

Assessment: Use the rubric for self-assessment, peer assessment, and teacher evaluation of discussion practices.
