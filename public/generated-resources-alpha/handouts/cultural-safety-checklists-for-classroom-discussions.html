Cultural Safety Checklists for Classroom Discussions

Learning Objectives: Students will develop skills to create culturally safe learning environments, understand key Māori cultural concepts, and apply these principles in classroom discussions.

Cultural Context

Creating culturally safe spaces (wāhi haumaru) is essential for Māori student success. This resource draws on key concepts:

  • Manaakitanga - Showing respect, hospitality and care for others
  • Whanaungatanga - Building and maintaining relationships
  • Wairuatanga - Spiritual wellbeing and connection
  • Kotahitanga - Unity and working together

Tikanga Considerations: When discussing Māori topics, ensure you:

  • Pronounce Māori words correctly (practice with students)
  • Use appropriate protocols (tikanga) when discussing sensitive topics
  • Provide opportunities for whānau (family) involvement where appropriate
  • Recognize diverse Māori perspectives and experiences

Cultural Safety Checklists

Use these checklists to evaluate and improve cultural safety in your classroom discussions:

Before Discussion Checklist

I have considered the cultural backgrounds of all students
I have prepared correct pronunciation of Māori terms
I have identified potential sensitive areas in the topic
I have established discussion protocols with students
Alternative participation methods are available

During Discussion Checklist

I'm monitoring student comfort levels
All voices are being heard equally
Cultural perspectives are valued equally
Māori worldviews are given appropriate space
The discussion remains respectful

After Discussion Checklist

I've provided time for reflection
Students have debriefing options
I've noted areas for improvement
Follow-up support is available
Feedback has been gathered

Interactive: Cultural Safety Scenario

Read this scenario and discuss in small groups how you would respond:

"During a discussion about land rights, a Pākehā student says 'Māori get too many special privileges.' Several Māori students look uncomfortable. One rolls their eyes and stops participating."

Extension Activities

1. Cultural Safety Audit

Work in groups to assess another aspect of school life (e.g., sports, library, playground) using cultural safety principles. Present findings and recommendations.

2. Whakataukī (Proverb) Analysis

Research Māori proverbs about respect and community. Choose one that relates to cultural safety and create a poster explaining its relevance to classroom discussions.

3. Cross-Cultural Comparison

Investigate how another culture (e.g., Pasifika, Asian) approaches respectful discussion. Compare and contrast with Māori approaches.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Preparation

  • Familiarize yourself with correct Māori pronunciation using Māori Dictionary
  • Consult with Māori colleagues or community members about sensitive topics
  • Prepare visual aids that reflect Māori culture and perspectives

During Implementation

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer/invocation) if appropriate for your school context
  • Use think-pair-share strategies to allow processing time
  • Have a "pass" option for students who don't wish to speak
  • Watch for non-verbal cues of discomfort

Follow-up

  • Provide anonymous feedback options
  • Reflect on what worked well and areas for improvement
  • Share successes with colleagues to promote school-wide cultural safety
