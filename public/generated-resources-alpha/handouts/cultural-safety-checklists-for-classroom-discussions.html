Cultural Safety Checklists for Classroom Discussions

Subject: social-sciences | Year Level: Years 7-13

Cultural Context

Safe learning environments for Māori students

Cultural Safety in Practice

This resource provides practical tools for creating culturally safe learning environments for Māori students across all subjects.

Key Tikanga Considerations:

  • Whanaungatanga - Building positive relationships between all class members
  • Wairuatanga - Recognizing spiritual dimensions of learning
  • Kotahitanga - Working together in unity and solidarity
  • Ako - Reciprocal teaching and learning relationships

Interactive Cultural Safety Checklists

Before Discussion Checklist

  • ☐ I have considered how the topic might impact Māori students
  • ☐ I've prepared culturally appropriate resources
  • ☐ The physical space is welcoming to Māori students
  • ☐ I've established discussion protocols (tikanga kōrero)
  • ☐ Alternative participation options are available

During Discussion Checklist

  • ☐ I'm monitoring for microaggressions or cultural insensitivity
  • ☐ Māori perspectives are given equal weight
  • ☐ Students are using correct Māori pronunciation
  • ☐ I'm balancing airtime among all students
  • ☐ The discussion maintains mana (dignity) of all participants

After Discussion Checklist

  • ☐ I've provided opportunities for private feedback
  • ☐ Students can reflect on the cultural safety of the discussion
  • ☐ I've noted areas for improvement
  • ☐ Follow-up support is available if needed
  • ☐ I've acknowledged contributions appropriately

Extension Activities

  • Student-Led Protocols: Have students co-create discussion protocols that honor both Māori and other cultural perspectives
  • Cultural Safety Audit: Students can conduct an audit of school spaces using these checklists
  • Whānau Feedback: Invite whānau to share their perspectives on culturally safe learning environments
