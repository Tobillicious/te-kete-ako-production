Cultural Safety Checklists for Classroom Discussions

Creating Safe Learning Environments for Māori Students

Learning Objectives

By using this resource, students will:

  • Understand the concept of manaakitanga (care and respect) in classroom discussions
  • Develop skills to create culturally safe spaces for all learners
  • Recognize and apply tikanga Māori (Māori protocols) in group discussions
  • Reflect on their own cultural perspectives and biases
  • Practice active listening and respectful dialogue techniques

Cultural Context

Cultural safety in education means creating learning environments where Māori students feel valued, respected, and able to express their cultural identity without fear of discrimination or marginalization.

Key Māori Concepts

Manaakitanga: The process of showing respect, hospitality and care for others

Whanaungatanga: Building and maintaining relationships

Ako: Reciprocal learning where both teachers and students learn from each other

Wairuatanga: Spiritual dimension of wellbeing

Cultural Safety Checklist

Before the Discussion

Have I established clear tikanga (protocols) for respectful discussion?
Have I considered diverse cultural perspectives in planning this discussion?
Have I provided background information to ensure all students can participate meaningfully?
Have I created physical spaces that allow for kanohi ki te kanohi (face-to-face) interactions?

During the Discussion

Am I allowing appropriate wait time for responses (3-5 seconds)?
Am I using inclusive language that values all contributions?
Am I monitoring participation to ensure all voices are heard?
Am I respectfully correcting any cultural misunderstandings?

After the Discussion

Have I provided opportunities for quiet reflection (whakaaro)?
Have I acknowledged the mana (prestige) of all participants?
Have I gathered feedback on the cultural safety of the discussion?
Have I reflected on my own cultural biases and how they may have influenced the discussion?

Reflection Activity

Think about a recent classroom discussion. How many of these cultural safety elements were present?

Assessment Rubric

Criteria Developing (1-2) Achieving (3-4) Excelling (5)
Cultural Awareness Shows limited awareness of cultural perspectives Demonstrates understanding of multiple cultural perspectives Actively incorporates and values diverse cultural perspectives
Respectful Engagement Occasionally interrupts or dominates discussion Listens to others and contributes appropriately Facilitates balanced participation and shows deep respect for all views
Tikanga Application Follows basic discussion protocols Consistently applies appropriate tikanga in discussions Models and teaches others about culturally safe discussion practices
Reflective Practice Limited reflection on cultural aspects of discussion Reflects on cultural safety with some depth Engages in deep, critical reflection and seeks to improve practice

Extension Activities

1. Cultural Safety Audit

Work in small groups to assess your classroom environment using this checklist. Present your findings and recommendations to the class.

2. Whakataukī (Proverb) Analysis

Research Māori proverbs about communication (e.g., "Ko te reo te mauri o te mana Māori" - The language is the life force of Māori mana). Discuss how they relate to cultural safety in discussions.

3. Role Play Scenarios

Create and perform short scenarios showing culturally safe and unsafe discussion practices. Have the class identify the differences.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Differentiation: Adapt checklist items to suit your students' age and experience level. For younger students, focus on 3-5 key items.

Timing: Introduce the checklist gradually over several lessons. Begin with 1-2 items before building to the full checklist.

Assessment: Use the rubric for both formative and summative assessment. Peer assessment can be particularly valuable.

Whānau Engagement: Share the checklist with families to promote consistent expectations across school and home.

Digital Adaptation: For online discussions, adapt the checklist to include digital etiquette and cultural safety in virtual spaces.
