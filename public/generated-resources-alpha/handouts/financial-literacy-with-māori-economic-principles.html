Cultural Context: Māori Economic Systems

Traditional Māori economics was based on concepts of reciprocity, sustainability, and collective wellbeing rather than individual profit maximization. Key principles include:

Key Māori Economic Concepts

Utu (Reciprocity)

A system of balanced exchange where gifts or services create obligations for equivalent return.

Manaakitanga (Hospitality)

The practice of generous hospitality that enhances the mana (status) of both host and guest.

Kaitiakitanga (Guardianship)

The responsibility to care for resources to ensure their availability for future generations.

Whanaungatanga (Relationships)

Economic decisions consider impacts on extended family and community relationships.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge

Students should have basic understanding of:

  • Fundamental financial literacy concepts (budgeting, saving, investing)
  • Basic Māori cultural concepts (mana, tapu, noa)

Differentiation Strategies

  • Support: Provide vocabulary lists with definitions of Māori economic terms
  • Extension: Have students research specific iwi economic development projects

Cultural Considerations

  • Consult with local Māori advisors or kaumātua when discussing culturally sensitive topics
  • Be aware that some students may have whānau connections to Māori businesses or trusts
  • Ensure correct pronunciation of Māori terms (provide audio resources if needed)

Assessment Options

  • Written report comparing economic systems
  • Presentation on Māori business case study
  • Personal financial plan with Māori values integration
