## Teacher Support Materials### 1. Cultural Protocols- Begin the unit with proper acknowledgement of Māori as the source of this knowledge- Consider inviting a local kaumātua or tohunga rongoā to speak (with appropriate protocols)- Ensure all Māori terms are pronounced correctly (provide audio recordings if needed)### 2. Laboratory Safety- Any practical work with plant materials should follow standard laboratory safety procedures- Some plants may cause allergic reactions - conduct risk assessments- Never allow students to consume any plant materials### 3. Assessment Rubric (Simplified)| Criteria | Excellent (A) | Proficient (M) | Developing (E) ||---------------------|---------------|----------------|-----------------|| Scientific Accuracy | Thorough, detailed chemical analysis | Correct identification of key compounds | Basic description with some errors || Cultural Understanding | Deep respect and integration of Māori perspectives | Appropriate acknowledgement | Minimal cultural context || Investigation Design | Creative, rigorous experimental plan | Logical, testable hypothesis | Basic or unclear methodology || Critical Thinking | Sophisticated analysis of traditional vs scientific knowledge | Clear comparisons made | Simple statements without analysis |### 4. Digital Optimization Notes- The HTML is lightweight for Chromebooks- All interactive elements work without plugins- Text is scalable for different needs- Color contrast meets accessibility standards### 5. Additional Resources- Te Ara Encyclopedia: Rongoā Māori- Royal Society Te Apārangi reports on indigenous knowledge- NIWA plant chemistry databases- Local iwi educational resourcesThis resource provides a culturally grounded, scientifically rigorous exploration of traditional Māori medicine that meets Level 6-8 of the NZ Science Curriculum while honoring Te Ao Māori perspectives.
