Statistical Analysis of Treaty Settlement Data

Teacher Guidance

This resource explores statistical concepts through the lens of contemporary Treaty of Waitangi settlements. It combines mathematics with social studies to provide meaningful context for statistical analysis.

He Whakamārama | Introduction

The Treaty of Waitangi settlement process is a significant part of New Zealand's history and contemporary society. By analyzing settlement data statistically, we can better understand patterns, distributions, and relationships in the settlement process.

Mā te raraunga ka mārama, mā te mārama ka mōhio, mā te mōhio ka mātau.

Through data comes understanding, through understanding comes knowledge, through knowledge comes wisdom.

Ngā Whāinga Ako | Learning Objectives

  • Analyze and interpret statistical data relating to Treaty settlements (AS91264, AS91267)

  • Understand the cultural significance of Treaty settlements in Aotearoa
  • Communicate findings about settlement data using appropriate statistical language

Cultural Context and Safety

Cultural Considerations

When working with Treaty settlement data:

  • Recognize that settlements represent historical grievances and contemporary resolutions
  • Understand that financial amounts don't capture the full cultural significance
  • Be aware that some students may have personal connections to specific settlements
  • Approach the topic with respect for all perspectives

Recommended protocol: Begin with a karakia (optional) and acknowledge the iwi whose settlement data you'll be analyzing.

Data Exploration Activity

Use the dataset to complete these statistical investigations:

  1. Calculate measures of center (mean, median, mode) for financial redress amounts
  2. Create appropriate graphical displays of the distribution of settlement years
  3. Investigate whether there's a relationship between population size and settlement amount
  4. Compare the distribution of settlement amounts before and after 2005

Statistical Investigation Tasks

Task 1: Descriptive Statistics

For the financial redress amounts:

  • Calculate the mean, median, and standard deviation
  • Create a box-and-whisker plot
  • Describe the shape of the distribution
  • Identify any outliers and suggest possible reasons

Task 2: Time Series Analysis

Analyze how settlement amounts have changed over time:

  • Create a time series plot of settlement amounts by year
  • Calculate the average settlement amount by 5-year periods
  • Comment on any trends or patterns you observe
  • Research historical context that might explain these patterns

Task 3: Comparative Analysis

Compare settlements for different regions or iwi groupings:

  • Select two regions to compare (e.g., Northland vs. South Island)
  • Use appropriate statistical measures to compare their settlements
  • Create back-to-back stem-and-leaf plots or comparative box plots
  • Formulate hypotheses about why differences might exist

Assessment Suggestions

Assessment Options

Choose assessment methods appropriate for your students:

Extension Activities

For Advanced Students

  1. Investigate the relationship between settlement amount and:
    • Original land loss amounts
    • Contemporary iwi population size
    • Regional economic indicators
  2. Calculate per capita settlement amounts and analyze the distribution
  3. Research how inflation affects the real value of earlier settlements
  4. Compare NZ's settlement process with indigenous reconciliation processes in other countries
  5. Create a predictive model for future settlements based on historical patterns

Adaptations for Diverse Learners

Supporting All Students

For students needing more support:

  • Provide partially completed calculations or graphs
  • Offer simplified datasets with fewer variables
  • Use think-aloud modeling for statistical processes
  • Provide sentence starters for interpretations

For English Language Learners:

  • Provide bilingual statistics vocabulary lists
  • Use visual representations alongside text
  • Allow verbal explanations of statistical concepts
  • Pre-teach key terms like "redress" and "settlement"

He Kupu Whakamutunga | Conclusion

Through statistical analysis of Treaty settlement data, we gain valuable insights into this important aspect of Aotearoa New Zealand's history and contemporary society. Remember that while numbers tell part of the story, they don't capture the full cultural significance of these settlements.

Kia kaha, kia māia, kia manawanui.

Be strong, be brave, be steadfast.
