NCEA Level 1 Success Guide

This resource provides culturally responsive preparation for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy requirements.

Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Understand the key requirements for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards
  • Develop strategies for successful assessment across different contexts
  • Apply culturally responsive approaches to assessment preparation
  • Identify personal strengths and areas for development
  • Build confidence in meeting the essential skills requirements

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Preparing for assessments with a Māori worldview means:

  • Whanaungatanga: Building relationships with teachers and peers for support
  • Manaakitanga: Respecting the learning environment and resources
  • Wairuatanga: Maintaining spiritual and emotional wellbeing during preparation
  • Kaitiakitanga: Taking responsibility for your own learning journey

Remember: Your whakapapa (genealogy) and cultural identity are strengths you bring to any assessment.

Literacy in Real Contexts

Literacy skills are assessed across subjects. For example:

  • In Science: Writing lab reports or explaining concepts
  • In Social Studies: Analysing historical documents
  • In Te Reo Māori: Demonstrating understanding of whakataukī (proverbs)

Numeracy Essentials

Practice Activity: Everyday Numeracy

Solve this problem using appropriate methods:

"You're planning a hāngī for 50 people. Each person will eat about 0.4kg of meat. Potatoes cost $2.50/kg and you need 150g per person. Calculate the total cost if meat is $12/kg and you need 20% extra for manaaki (hospitality)."

Solution:

Meat: 50 × 0.4kg = 20kg × 1.2 = 24kg × $12 = $288

Potatoes: 50 × 0.15kg = 7.5kg × $2.50 = $18.75

Total cost = $288 + $18.75 = $306.75

Key Numeracy Skills

  • Number strategies (percentages, ratios, proportions)
  • Measurement (units, conversions, estimation)
  • Statistical literacy (graphs, tables, averages)
  • Algebraic thinking (patterns, formulas, equations)
  • Financial literacy (budgets, costs, discounts)

Assessment Success Tips

  • Practice with real-world contexts relevant to your interests
  • Work with whānau to connect learning to cultural experiences
  • Use Te Reo Māori terminology when appropriate
  • Seek support from teachers and peers (whanaungatanga)
  • Maintain balance between study and wellbeing (wairuatanga)
