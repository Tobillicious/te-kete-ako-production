Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

This resource acknowledges the importance of Te Ao Māori (Māori worldview) in assessment preparation:

Whanaungatanga (Relationships)

Learning is strengthened through relationships. Consider forming study groups (rōpū ako) to support each other.

Manaakitanga (Hospitality/Care)

Create a supportive environment for learning. This includes caring for your own hinengaro (mind) and tinana (body) during assessment preparation.

Ako (Reciprocal Learning)

Remember that everyone has strengths to share. You might be strong in literacy while a classmate excels in numeracy - teach each other.

Wairuatanga (Spirituality)

Connect your learning to what matters to you. How do literacy and numeracy skills help you serve your whānau, hapū, or community?

Extension Activities

Choose Your Challenge

Task: Compare a mainstream news article with a Māori Television or Te Karere report on the same event.

Questions to consider:

  • What perspectives are included in each?
  • How does the language differ?
  • Which cultural values are evident?

Task: Research traditional Māori measurement systems and compare to metric system.

Questions to consider:

  • How were lengths measured using body parts?
  • How was time measured naturally?
  • How could you apply both systems today?

Teacher Implementation Notes

Suggested Use:

  • Print as a booklet for students or display digitally on class site
  • Use sections progressively over a term or focus on specific areas as needed
  • Pair with subject-specific examples from your learning area

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Support: Pre-teach vocabulary, provide sentence starters, use concrete examples
  • Extension: Challenge students to create their own culturally-responsive examples
  • Māori Medium: Adapt activities to te reo Māori contexts

Assessment Integration:

  • Use the rubric to co-construct success criteria with students
  • Have students self-assess using the checklist before submitting work
  • Build reflection questions into tasks (e.g., "How did your cultural knowledge help with this?")
