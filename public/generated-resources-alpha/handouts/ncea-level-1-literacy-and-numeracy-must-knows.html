NCEA Level 1 Literacy & Numeracy Must-Knows

He whakamārama | Explanation

This resource prepares you for the NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy requirements while honoring Te Ao Māori perspectives. It combines essential skills with culturally responsive approaches to assessment.

Literacy Essentials

Reading for Meaning

  • Identify main ideas and supporting details
  • Understand vocabulary in context
  • Analyze different text types (reports, narratives, instructions)
  • Interpret visual information (graphs, diagrams, maps)

Te Reo Māori Connection

Practice reading comprehension with bilingual texts that include whakataukī (proverbs) and their English translations. Analyze how cultural concepts are expressed differently in each language.

Writing Effectively

  • Structure different text types (essays, reports, explanations)
  • Use appropriate vocabulary and tone
  • Organize ideas logically with clear paragraphs
  • Proofread for spelling, grammar, and punctuation

Try This: Whakapapa Writing

Write a short personal narrative that connects to your whakapapa (genealogy) or place. Include:

  1. 3 key events that shaped your identity
  2. Descriptions of important people/places
  3. Reflections on what these mean to you

Numeracy Fundamentals

Number Knowledge

  • Fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Ratios and proportions
  • Basic algebra concepts
  • Rounding and estimating

Māori Measurement Concepts

Explore traditional Māori measurement systems used in whare (house) construction and waka (canoe) building. Compare these to metric measurements.

Statistical Literacy

  • Read and interpret graphs/tables
  • Calculate mean, median, mode
  • Understand probability basics
  • Evaluate statistical claims

Try This: Data in Te Ao Māori

Analyze data about te reo Māori speakers in NZ:

  1. Create a bar graph showing regional differences
  2. Calculate percentage changes over time
  3. Write 2 conclusions about the data

He kupu āwhina | Helpful Advice

Remember that manaakitanga (care for others) includes supporting your classmates in their learning journey. Study groups can be powerful when based on whanaungatanga (relationships).

Study Plan Template

Create a weekly schedule that balances:

  • Subject review sessions
  • Practice assessments
  • Wellbeing activities
  • Whānau time

Extension: Research Project

Investigate how mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge systems) approach literacy and numeracy concepts differently. Present your findings as:

  • A comparative essay
  • An infographic
  • A short video presentation
