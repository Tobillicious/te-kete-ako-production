Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Understand the key requirements for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards
  • Develop strategies for culturally responsive assessment preparation
  • Identify personal strengths and areas for improvement in foundational skills
  • Apply ako (reciprocal learning) approaches to build confidence
  • Navigate assessment contexts with cultural awareness and confidence

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Preparing for assessments involves more than just academic skills - it's about mana (prestige), whakapapa (connections), and whanaungatanga (relationships).

Key Principles:

  • Manaakitanga: Support each other's learning journey with care and respect
  • Kaitiakitanga: Take responsibility for your own preparation
  • Wānanga: Engage in purposeful learning discussions
  • Ako: Recognize that we all learn from each other

Reflection: Whakaaro

Consider how your cultural background influences your approach to assessments. What strengths does your cultural perspective bring to your learning?

Extension Activities

For Further Development:

  • Create a study group using tuakana-teina (peer mentoring) approach
  • Research how numeracy is used in traditional Māori practices like whakairo (carving)
  • Analyze how different cultures approach problem-solving in mathematics
  • Interview family/community members about how they use literacy in their work

Teacher Implementation Notes

Suggested Use:

  • This resource can be used across multiple subjects to support NCEA preparation
  • Adapt the cultural context section to reflect your local iwi knowledge
  • Encourage students to complete the interactive elements digitally or print for written responses
  • Use the checklist as a formative assessment tool
  • Pair with practical demonstrations of literacy/numeracy in cultural contexts

Differentiation:

  • For extension: Have students create their own practice questions in cultural contexts
  • For support: Provide sentence starters for reflection questions
  • Allow verbal responses for students who struggle with written expression
