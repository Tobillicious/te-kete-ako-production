Ngā Whāinga Ako | Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Understand the key requirements for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards
  • Apply culturally responsive approaches to assessment preparation
  • Develop strategies for demonstrating skills across different contexts
  • Identify personal strengths and areas for development
  • Navigate assessment situations with confidence and cultural awareness

Te Horopaki Ahurea | Cultural Context

Success in assessment is about more than just academic skills - it's about bringing your whole self to the task. These tikanga (cultural practices) can support your preparation:

Whakataukī (Proverb) for Learning

"Whāia te iti kahurangi ki te tūohu koe me he maunga teitei"

"Seek the treasure you value most dearly: if you bow your head, let it be to a lofty mountain"

What does this mean for your NCEA journey?

Tikanga Considerations

  • Whanaungatanga: Build relationships with teachers and peers for support
  • Manaakitanga: Show respect for yourself and others in assessment situations
  • Ako: Learning is reciprocal - teach others to strengthen your own understanding
  • Wairua: Bring your whole self, including cultural identity, to assessments

Literacy Essentials

To meet NCEA Level 1 Literacy requirements, you need to demonstrate skills in:

Literacy Skills Self-Check

Rate your confidence with these skills (1 = not confident, 5 = very confident):

Understanding different text types (reports, articles, instructions)

Writing clear explanations

Presenting information visually (graphs, diagrams)

Participating in discussions

Key Vocabulary

These words often appear in assessment questions:

Analyse - Break down information

Compare - Find similarities

Contrast - Find differences

Evaluate - Judge the value

Justify - Give reasons

Summarise - Give main points

Numeracy Essentials

NCEA Level 1 Numeracy requires you to use mathematical skills in practical contexts:

Real-World Numeracy

How might you use these skills in everyday life?

Example:

Must-Know Numeracy Skills

  • Basic operations (+, -, ×, ÷) with whole numbers, decimals, fractions
  • Calculating percentages and ratios
  • Reading and creating tables, graphs, and charts
  • Measurement (length, area, volume, time, money)
  • Interpreting statistical information

Aromatawai | Assessment Preparation

Checklist for Success

  • I understand the assessment requirements
  • I know which standards I'm being assessed on
  • I've practiced similar tasks
  • I know where to get help if needed
  • I have strategies for managing assessment stress

Ngā Mahi Whakawhānui | Extension Activities

  • Create a pepeha (personal introduction) that includes your learning journey
  • Interview a family/community member about how they use literacy and numeracy in their work
  • Design a poster explaining an NCEA concept using both English and te reo Māori
  • Keep a learning journal reflecting on your progress using the Tuakana-Teina (mentoring) model

Ngā Tohutohu mā te Kaiako | Teacher Implementation Notes

Suggested Use: This resource can be used:

  • As a self-paced guide for students preparing for assessments
  • In whānau conferences to discuss NCEA requirements
  • As a basis for workshop sessions on specific skills
  • For cross-curricular literacy/numeracy development

Differentiation: Support students by:

  • Providing te reo Māori translations for key terms
  • Encouraging tuakana-teina (peer mentoring) relationships
  • Offering multiple ways to demonstrate understanding
