Learning Objectives

By engaging with this resource, ākonga (students) will:

  • Understand the key requirements for NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards
  • Develop strategies for culturally responsive assessment preparation
  • Apply literacy and numeracy skills across different learning contexts
  • Recognize how tikanga Māori (Māori customs) can support learning success
  • Create a personalized study plan that honors their cultural identity

Cultural Context: Te Ao Māori Perspectives

Success in NCEA assessments can be supported by understanding and applying Māori cultural concepts:

  • Whanaungatanga - Build study groups with peers to support each other
  • Manaakitanga - Show respect for yourself and others in your preparation
  • Ako - Recognize that learning is reciprocal; teach others to strengthen your own understanding
  • Tuakana-teina - Older/more experienced students can guide younger ones

Tikanga Considerations: When preparing for assessments, consider how you can bring your whole self to the process. This might include:

  • Starting with a karakia (prayer) to focus your mind
  • Creating study spaces that reflect your cultural identity
  • Using Māori metaphors for learning (e.g., viewing knowledge as a kete - basket to be filled)

Literacy Must-Knows

Key Skills Checklist

  • I can identify the main ideas and supporting details in texts
  • I can write clear, structured responses to questions
  • I can use appropriate vocabulary for different subjects
  • I can proofread my work for spelling, grammar, and clarity
  • I can interpret visual information (graphs, diagrams, etc.)

Numeracy Must-Knows

Key Skills Checklist

  • I can solve problems using basic operations (+, -, ×, ÷)
  • I can work with fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • I can interpret graphs, tables, and charts
  • I can measure and calculate using appropriate units
  • I can apply mathematical reasoning to real-world situations

Numeracy in Real Life

Numeracy isn't just for math class! Here's where you use these skills:

  • Science: Calculating concentrations, analyzing data
  • Social Studies: Interpreting population statistics
  • PE/Health: Calculating heart rates, nutrition percentages
  • Technology: Measuring materials, scaling designs
  • Everyday Life: Budgeting, measuring ingredients, comparing prices

Teacher Implementation Notes

Curriculum Links: This resource supports achievement of NCEA Level 1 Literacy and Numeracy standards across all learning areas of the New Zealand Curriculum.

Suggested Use:

  • Print as a workbook for students to complete over several weeks
  • Use sections as starter activities in different subject classes
  • Assign the extension activities as group projects
  • Incorporate the cultural elements into existing assessment preparation

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Provide Māori-English dictionaries for vocabulary support
  • Allow students to complete some tasks in te reo Māori
  • Offer multiple ways to demonstrate understanding (oral, visual, written)
  • Pair students using tuakana-teina (mentoring) approach
