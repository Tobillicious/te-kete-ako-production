## Comprehensive Teacher Notes### Cultural Safety Considerations1. **Protocols**: Begin with appropriate karakia if comfortable, or invite a kaumātua. Acknowledge the mana whenua of your area.2. **Language Use**: Ensure correct pronunciation of Māori terms. Consider having students practice with each other first.3. **Knowledge Sharing**: Emphasize that mātauranga Māori is a living knowledge system, not just historical information.4. **Sensitive Topics**: Some traditional food sources may now be scarce - handle discussions about this with care.### Implementation Tips1. **Prior Knowledge**: Assess students' familiarity with Māori perspectives early to address gaps.2. **Local Connections**: Where possible, connect with local Māori food initiatives or experts.3. **Hands-on Learning**: Incorporate practical activities like planting according to maramataka.4. **Digital Options**: For virtual field trips, explore: - Te Papa's online collections - Virtual tours of traditional gardens - Recorded interviews with Māori food experts### Assessment Alternatives1. **Formative Options**: - Concept maps showing connections between traditional and modern practices - Reflection journals on learning progress - Peer teaching of specific concepts2. **Summative Alternatives**: - Create instructional videos about traditional practices - Design infographics comparing food systems - Develop a school-wide food security campaign### Extension Project Ideas1. **Community Engagement**: - Partner with local marae on a food project - Organize a traditional food preparation day - Create educational resources for younger students2. **Research Pathways**: - Investigate scientific validation of traditional practices - Study traditional Māori plant breeding techniques - Explore traditional food preservation methods### Technology Integration1. **Digital Tools**: - Use Google Earth to examine traditional land use patterns - Create digital timelines of agricultural changes - Build collaborative documents for research2. **Accessibility Features**: - Text-to-speech for reading support - Video transcripts for hearing impaired - High contrast versions for visually impairedThis resource provides a culturally grounded, curriculum-aligned approach to exploring food security through mātauranga Māori while meeting diverse learning needs in New Zealand classrooms.
