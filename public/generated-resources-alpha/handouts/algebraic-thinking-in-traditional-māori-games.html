Algebraic Thinking in Traditional Māori Games

Cultural Context

Traditional Māori games (tākaro) were not just for entertainment - they developed skills, strategy, and mathematical thinking. This resource explores algebraic concepts through three traditional games:

  • Mu torere (strategy board game)
  • Poi rākau (stick games)
  • Whai (string patterns)

Cultural Safety Notes

  • Ensure proper pronunciation of Māori words (practice with students)
  • Explain the cultural significance of these games beyond mathematics
  • Invite local kaumātua or cultural advisors if available
  • Respect protocols - some games may have tapu (sacred) aspects
  • Encourage students to approach the games with respect and curiosity

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Identify and describe patterns in traditional Māori games (NZC L4-5 Mathematics: Patterns and Relationships)
  • Formulate algebraic expressions to represent game strategies (NZC L4-5 Mathematics: Equations and Expressions)
  • Analyze game outcomes using mathematical reasoning (NZC L4-5 Mathematics: Statistical Investigation)
  • Connect mathematical concepts to cultural practices (NZC L4-5 Social Sciences: Understand how cultural practices reflect and express people's customs, traditions, and values)

Activity 1: Mu Torere Strategy Analysis

Game Overview

Mu torere is a traditional Māori board game played on an 8-pointed star (pūtahi) with 8 playing pieces (4 per player).

Algebraic Challenge

After playing several rounds, identify:

  1. How many possible first moves exist?
  2. If each player has n pieces, how would you calculate possible moves?
  3. Create an equation to predict the minimum number of moves to win

Teacher Implementation

  1. Introduce the game with a demonstration (use drawn board or digital version)
  2. Have students play in pairs (15 minutes)
  3. Facilitate discussion about strategies and patterns
  4. Introduce algebraic representation of moves
  5. Challenge students to create their own equations

Activity 2: Poi Rākau Patterns

Game Overview

Poi rākau involves rhythmic passing of sticks in patterns. We'll analyze the mathematics behind these patterns.

Pattern Analysis

With a partner, try these passing sequences:

Sequence Algebraic Representation
Pass every 2 beats t = 2n
Pass on prime-numbered beats t = p (where p is prime)

Challenge: Create your own sequence and represent it algebraically

Teacher Implementation

  1. Demonstrate basic poi rākau rhythms (use rulers or rolled paper if sticks unavailable)
  2. Start with simple patterns, gradually increasing complexity
  3. Connect physical patterns to numerical representations
  4. Have students create and share their own algebraic patterns

Extension Activities

Advanced Mathematical Exploration

  • Calculate probability distributions for mu torere outcomes
  • Research and analyze the combinatorics of whai (string game) patterns
  • Create a digital simulation of poi rākau patterns using algebraic rules
  • Compare Māori game mathematics to other indigenous game systems

Cultural Research

  • Interview local experts about traditional games
  • Investigate regional variations of these games
  • Create a presentation on the cultural significance of mathematics in Māori society
