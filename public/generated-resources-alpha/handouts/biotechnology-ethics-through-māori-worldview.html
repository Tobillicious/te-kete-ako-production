Implementation Guide for Teachers

Before Teaching

  1. Familiarize yourself with basic MÄori concepts and their relevance to biotechnology ethics.
  2. Research local perspectives - consider reaching out to MÄori colleagues or community members for guidance.
  3. Prepare resources - gather articles, videos, and case studies that present diverse viewpoints.
  4. Establish classroom protocols for respectful discussion of sensitive cultural topics.

Lesson Sequence (Suggested 4-5 lessons)

  1. Introduction to Biotechnology Ethics (1 lesson)
    • Explore common biotechnologies and their ethical implications
    • Introduce basic Western ethical frameworks (utilitarianism, deontology, etc.)
  2. MÄori Worldview and Ethics (1-2 lessons)
    • Introduce key MÄori concepts (whakapapa, kaitiakitanga, mana, tapu/noa, mauri)
    • Compare and contrast with Western frameworks
  3. Case Study Analysis (1-2 lessons)
    • Apply both frameworks to specific biotechnology scenarios
    • Facilitate respectful debate and discussion
  4. Synthesis and Assessment (1 lesson)
    • Students develop their own ethical position on a biotech issue
    • Present findings incorporating both Western and MÄori perspectives
