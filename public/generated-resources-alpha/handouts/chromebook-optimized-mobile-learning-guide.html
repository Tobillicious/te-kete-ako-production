Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Digital Technology | Years 7-10

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
  • Configure and optimize Chromebooks for effective learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori principles to digital spaces
  • Evaluate digital tools for accessibility and cultural appropriateness
  • Create digital solutions that address equity challenges

Cultural Context: Te Ao Māori and Digital Equity

In te ao Māori, the concept of manaakitanga (care and hospitality) extends to digital spaces. When considering digital equity, we must ask:

  • How can we ensure all tamariki have equal access to digital tools?
  • How do we maintain whanaungatanga (relationships) in digital learning?
  • What tikanga (protocols) should guide our digital interactions?

Key Principle: He aha te mea nui o te ao? He tāngata, he tāngata, he tāngata (What is the most important thing in the world? It is people, it is people, it is people). Digital technology should serve people, not the other way around.

Chromebook Optimization Guide

Activity 1: Setting Up for Success

Configure your Chromebook for optimal learning:

  1. Adjust display settings (font size, contrast)
  2. Set up multiple user profiles if sharing devices
  3. Install essential extensions (Read&Write, Grammarly)
  4. Create bookmark folders for different subjects

Activity 2: Culturally Responsive Digital Practice

Create a digital pepeha (introduction) using Google Slides:

  1. Include your maunga (mountain), awa (river), and iwi
  2. Use appropriate images with cultural significance
  3. Share with classmates while observing tikanga

Extension Activities

Digital Equity Project

Research and propose a solution to a digital equity issue in your community. Consider:

  • Access to devices and internet
  • Cultural appropriateness of digital content
  • Support for diverse learning needs

Create a Digital Waharoa (Gateway)

Design a webpage that serves as a culturally welcoming entry point to digital learning resources.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge: Basic Chromebook familiarity. Some understanding of digital citizenship.

Duration: 4-6 lessons (adaptable)

Differentiation:

  • Provide alternative input methods (voice typing, touchscreen)
  • Offer templates for digital pepeha
  • Pair students for peer support

Cultural Considerations:

  • Consult with kaitiaki or cultural advisors
  • Ensure all students feel safe sharing cultural information
  • Respect diverse cultural backgrounds
