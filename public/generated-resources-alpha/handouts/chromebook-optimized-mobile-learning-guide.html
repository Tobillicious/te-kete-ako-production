Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Years 7-10 | Digital Technology | Digital Equity and Accessibility

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
  • Configure and optimize Chromebooks for effective learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori when using digital devices collaboratively
  • Identify accessibility features to support diverse learners
  • Create digital content that considers multiple access needs

Cultural Context: Tikanga in Digital Spaces

In te ao Māori, the concept of manaakitanga (care for others) extends to our digital interactions. When using Chromebooks:

  • Practice whanaungatanga by supporting peers with technology challenges
  • Respect mana by giving thoughtful feedback in shared documents
  • Consider kaitiakitanga by caring for devices as taonga (treasures)
  • Ensure wairua (spiritual wellbeing) through balanced screen time

Digital equity aligns with Māori values of kotahitanga (unity) by ensuring all students have equal access to learning tools.

Chromebook Optimization Guide

1. Essential Settings

  1. Open Settings (bottom-right clock)
  2. Configure WiFi and check signal strength
  3. Set display size for comfortable reading
  4. Enable night light to reduce eye strain
  5. Turn on high contrast mode if needed

2. Accessibility Features

Try these built-in tools:

  • ChromeVox: Screen reader (Ctrl+Alt+Z)
  • Magnifier: Zoom in/out (Ctrl+Alt+Brightness)
  • Dictation: Voice typing (Ctrl+Shift+S)
  • High contrast: Improves readability
  • Sticky keys: Helps with keyboard use

3. Digital Equity Challenge

Complete these tasks with limited resources:

  1. Create a Google Doc using only voice typing
  2. Research a topic using only text-to-speech
  3. Share your screen with a peer for feedback

Assessment Checklist

Extension Activities

1. Digital Equity Research

Investigate how different communities in Aotearoa access technology. Compare urban/rural, different age groups, or socioeconomic factors.

2. Accessibility Design Challenge

Redesign a school webpage to be more accessible. Consider color contrast, navigation, and alternative text.

3. Te Reo Digital Toolkit

Create a guide for using Chromebooks with Māori language settings and te reo Māori digital resources.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge

Students should have basic Chromebook familiarity. Pre-assess digital literacy levels.

Differentiation

  • Pair students for peer support
  • Provide printed step-by-step guides
  • Offer voice recording as an alternative to typing

Cultural Considerations

Begin with karakia (prayer) for new learning. Allow time for whakawhanaungatanga (relationship building) before collaborative tasks.

Timing

Allow 3-4 lessons for core content, plus optional time for extensions.
