Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Digital Technology | Years 7-10

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
  • Optimize Chromebook settings for effective learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori to digital spaces
  • Create accessible digital content
  • Troubleshoot common Chromebook issues

Cultural Context: Digital Equity in Aotearoa

He whakataukī (proverb): "Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini" - My strength is not as an individual, but as a collective.

In te ao Māori, the concept of manaakitanga (hospitality, kindness) extends to digital spaces. We must ensure all learners have equitable access to technology and digital resources.

Tikanga Considerations:

  • Whanaungatanga: Build relationships in digital spaces as you would in person
  • Kaitiakitanga: Be responsible guardians of digital tools and resources
  • Wairuatanga: Consider the spiritual wellbeing of yourself and others online

Chromebook Optimization Guide

Activity 1: Setting Up Your Chromebook

Follow these steps to optimize your Chromebook for learning:

  1. Click the time in the bottom right corner
  2. Select the gear icon for Settings
  3. Adjust display size for comfortable reading
  4. Enable high contrast mode if needed
  5. Set up multiple user accounts if sharing devices

Extension Activities

Digital Whakapapa Project

Create a digital family tree using Google Drawings or another Chromebook-compatible tool. Consider:

  • How to represent relationships digitally
  • Including both biological and chosen family
  • Respecting privacy considerations

Community Tech Audit

Research digital access in your local community:

  1. Identify places with free public WiFi
  2. Find community tech support services
  3. Create a resource map using Google My Maps

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge:

Students should have basic Chromebook navigation skills. Pre-assessment could include a quick quiz on:

  • Logging in/out
  • Opening apps
  • Basic keyboard shortcuts

Differentiation Strategies:

  • Support: Provide printed step-by-step guides with screenshots
  • Extension: Have students create tutorial videos for specific features
  • Cultural: Invite local iwi digital experts to share perspectives

Assessment Options:

  • Observation during setup activities
  • Student-created "how to" guides
  • Reflection journals on digital equity
