Digital Equity and Accessibility

Chromebook-Optimized Learning Guide for Years 7-10

Learning Objectives: Students will understand digital equity concepts, analyze accessibility barriers, apply tikanga Māori to technology use, and create inclusive digital solutions.

Te Ao Māori Perspectives

The concept of manaakitanga (care for others) reminds us that technology should serve all people equally. Kotahitanga (unity) encourages us to work toward digital inclusion for everyone in our communities.

Tikanga Considerations:

  • Technology should uphold whanaungatanga (relationships) by connecting rather than dividing people
  • Digital solutions should respect tino rangatiratanga (self-determination) by giving users control
  • Accessibility features honor mana tangata (human dignity)

Understanding Digital Equity

Digital equity means all individuals and communities have the information technology capacity needed for full participation in our society.

Quick Self-Assessment

How accessible is your current technology setup?

  1. Can you adjust text size on your device easily?
  2. Does your device have text-to-speech capability?
  3. Could someone with color blindness use your device comfortably?
  4. Could someone with limited hand mobility navigate your device?
  5. Is your internet connection reliable for video calls?

Activity: Accessibility Audit

Work in pairs to evaluate the accessibility of a Chromebook:

  1. Open Chrome browser settings
  2. Find and test three accessibility features
  3. For each feature, identify who it helps and how
  4. Brainstorm one additional feature that would improve accessibility

Feature Discovery

Try looking in Settings > Advanced > Accessibility, or right-click on a webpage to find "Inspect" tools.

Assessment Checklist

Students can demonstrate understanding by:

  • ✓ Explaining 3 digital equity barriers in Aotearoa
  • ✓ Identifying 4 Chromebook accessibility features
  • ✓ Proposing an inclusive design improvement with Māori values
  • ✓ Creating a basic accessible webpage (HTML headings, alt text, etc.)
  • ✓ Reflecting on personal digital privilege

Extension Activities

Digital Equity Challenge

Choose one:

  • Research internet access statistics for Māori communities
  • Interview a community member about their technology challenges
  • Design an accessibility feature for a Māori language app
  • Create a poster explaining digital rights in te reo Māori
