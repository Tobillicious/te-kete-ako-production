Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Years 7-10 | Digital Technology | Digital Equity and Accessibility

Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
  • Configure and optimize Chromebooks for effective mobile learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori principles to digital technology use
  • Evaluate digital tools for accessibility and cultural appropriateness
  • Create digital solutions that address equity challenges

Cultural Context: Tikanga Considerations

Manaakitanga: When designing digital solutions, consider how they show care and respect for all users, including those with different abilities or access to technology.

Kaitiakitanga: Responsible use of digital devices aligns with guardianship principles - we should care for our devices and use them sustainably.

Whanaungatanga: Digital tools should help build connections, not create barriers between people.

Wairuatanga: Consider the spiritual and emotional impact of technology - balance screen time with other activities.

Whakaaro: Reflection Activity

How might digital equity issues affect Māori communities differently? Discuss with a partner.

Optimizing Your Chromebook for Learning

Chromebook Setup Challenge

Complete these setup tasks to optimize your device:

  1. Enable offline mode for Google Docs
  2. Set up text-to-speech accessibility features
  3. Create a folder structure for your mahi
  4. Install these essential extensions: Read&Write, Kami
Show Setup Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance:

Offline Mode: Go to Settings > Offline > Enable offline sync

Text-to-Speech: Settings > Accessibility > Select-to-speak

Extensions: Visit Chrome Web Store to add tools

Extension Activities

Community Digital Equity Audit

Research and document digital access in your local community. Consider:

  • Internet availability and cost
  • Device access in homes
  • Digital literacy programs
  • Cultural appropriateness of available resources

Present your findings using Google Sites or Slides.

Create an Accessible Digital Resource

Design a digital resource (website, document, or presentation) that:

  • Follows accessibility guidelines
  • Incorporates te reo Māori
  • Works well on low-bandwidth connections
  • Has offline functionality

Teacher Implementation Notes

Prior Knowledge: Students should be familiar with basic Chromebook operation. Consider pairing less confident students with buddies.

Cultural Support: Engage with local Māori advisors to ensure cultural content is appropriate for your area.

Differentiation: Provide printed copies of key instructions for students who struggle with digital text.

Timing: Allow 3-4 lessons for core content, plus additional time for extension activities.

Assessment: Use both observational notes during activities and the final rubric assessment.
