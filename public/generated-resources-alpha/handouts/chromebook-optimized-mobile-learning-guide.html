Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the concept of digital equity and its importance in Aotearoa
  • Configure and optimize Chromebooks for effective learning
  • Apply tikanga Māori when using digital devices
  • Identify accessibility features to support diverse learners
  • Create digital content that considers multiple access needs

Cultural Context: Te Ao Māori and Digital Equity

In Māori worldview (Te Ao Māori), the concept of manaakitanga (hospitality, care for others) extends to digital spaces. When considering digital equity, we must ensure:

  • Equal access to technology for all tamariki (children)
  • Respect for tikanga (cultural protocols) in online spaces
  • Inclusion of Māori perspectives in digital content creation
  • Consideration of whānau (family) access to digital resources

Key consideration: How can we ensure our digital practices uphold Te Tiriti o Waitangi principles?

Chromebook Optimization Guide

1. Essential Settings

Configure your Chromebook for optimal learning:

  • Enable ChromeVox (screen reader) in Accessibility settings
  • Set up High Contrast Mode for better visibility
  • Adjust text size and page zoom as needed
  • Enable dictation for hands-free typing

2. Productivity Tips

Keyboard shortcuts for efficiency:

  • Ctrl + Shift + L - Lock your Chromebook
  • Ctrl + Shift + Q (x2) - Sign out
  • Alt + Search - Caps Lock
  • Ctrl + Alt + ? - See all keyboard shortcuts

Quick Knowledge Check

What does manaakitanga mean in digital contexts?

Manaakitanga in digital contexts means showing care and respect for others in online spaces, ensuring everyone has equal access and feels welcome.

Digital Equity in Action

Complete these tasks to demonstrate your understanding:

Task 1: Accessibility Audit

Review a school website or learning platform and identify:

  • 3 accessibility features present
  • 2 potential barriers to access
  • 1 improvement suggestion

Task 2: Culturally Responsive Design

Create a digital poster that:

  • Includes te reo Māori greetings
  • Uses culturally appropriate imagery
  • Considers color contrast for accessibility
  • Has clear, simple language
