Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Years 7-10 | Digital Technology

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand how to optimize learning experiences for Chromebook environments
  • Apply digital equity principles to create accessible learning resources
  • Incorporate tikanga Māori in digital learning contexts
  • Develop strategies for mobile-first learning design

Cultural Context: Digital Equity and Accessibility

In te ao Māori, the concept of manaakitanga (care for others) reminds us to ensure all learners have equitable access to digital learning opportunities. This aligns with the principle of whanaungatanga (relationships) in creating inclusive digital spaces.

Tikanga Considerations for Digital Learning:

  • Whakawhanaungatanga: Build relationships through collaborative digital tools
  • Kaitiakitanga: Be responsible digital citizens and caretakers of technology
  • Wānanga: Create spaces for digital discussion and knowledge sharing
  • Ako: Reciprocal learning - students and teachers learning together in digital spaces

Best Practices for Chromebook Learning

  • Lightweight resources: Optimize images and videos for faster loading
  • Cloud-first approach: Use Google Workspace tools that sync automatically
  • Keyboard shortcuts: Teach students time-saving combinations
  • Extension management: Recommend essential learning extensions
  • Digital wellbeing: Include regular breaks and offline activities

Extension Activities

1. Digital Equity Audit

Have students audit their school's digital resources using the assessment checklist above. They can present findings and recommendations to school leadership.

2. Create an Accessible Resource

Students design a Chromebook-optimized learning resource that incorporates:

  • Accessibility features
  • Māori cultural elements
  • Offline capabilities

3. Whakataukī (Proverb) Digital Posters

Create digital posters featuring Māori proverbs about learning and technology, ensuring they're optimized for Chromebook viewing and include alt text descriptions.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Before the Lesson:

  • Test all digital resources on a Chromebook
  • Prepare offline alternatives for key activities
  • Consult with Māori colleagues or community members for cultural input

During the Lesson:

  • Begin with a karakia (prayer) or whakataukī related to technology
  • Explicitly teach Chromebook skills as needed
  • Pair students for peer support with technology

Assessment Tips:

  • Use the checklist as both a self-assessment and teacher assessment tool
  • Consider video reflections where students explain their design choices
  • Assess both technical and cultural aspects of student work
