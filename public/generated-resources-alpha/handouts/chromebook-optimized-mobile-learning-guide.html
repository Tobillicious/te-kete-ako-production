Chromebook-Optimized Mobile Learning Guide

Digital Technology | Years 7-10

Learning Objectives: Students will understand digital equity concepts, develop Chromebook proficiency, apply tikanga in digital spaces, and create accessible digital content.

Cultural Context: Te Tiriti o Waitangi and Digital Equity

The principle of tino rangatiratanga (self-determination) in the digital context means all ākonga (students) should have equal access to technology and the skills to use it effectively. This resource embraces:

  • Manaakitanga - Supporting all learners with appropriate technology
  • Whanaungatanga - Building digital communities that connect people
  • Kaitiakitanga - Responsible use of digital resources

Tikanga Considerations: When using Chromebooks, remember to respect tikanga Māori by:

  • Asking permission before sharing digital content featuring people
  • Considering cultural protocols when accessing or sharing taonga (treasures) digitally
  • Ensuring digital spaces are inclusive and welcoming

Chromebook Essentials for Learning

Activity 1: Chromebook Navigation Challenge

Complete these tasks to build your Chromebook skills:

  1. Take a screenshot and save it to your Google Drive
  2. Bookmark three educational websites in Māori language
  3. Use the built-in screen reader to listen to a webpage
  4. Share a document with a classmate using Google Drive

Accessibility Features Explorer

Try these Chromebook accessibility tools:

  • High contrast mode
  • Screen magnifier
  • On-screen keyboard
  • Speech-to-text dictation

Reflection: Which feature would be most helpful for someone with visual impairments? Why?

Activity 2: Digital Equity Investigation

Research these questions in pairs:

  1. What percentage of New Zealand homes have reliable internet access?
  2. How does internet access differ between urban and rural areas?
  3. What initiatives exist to improve digital equity in Aotearoa?
  4. How could our school improve digital access for all students?

Present your findings as a simple Google Slides presentation (3 slides maximum).

Self-Checklist

  • I can use at least 5 Chromebook features confidently
  • I understand what digital equity means
  • I've considered accessibility in my digital work
  • I've applied tikanga Māori in my digital activities
  • I can help others with Chromebook basics

Extension Activities

Digital Leaders Challenge

Become a Chromebook expert and:

  1. Create a tutorial video for younger students
  2. Interview a community member about their digital access experiences
  3. Design a poster promoting digital inclusion
  4. Research Māori-led tech initiatives in Aotearoa

Accessibility Audit

Evaluate a school website or app for:

  • Text size and contrast
  • Alternative text for images
  • Keyboard navigation
  • Language options (including te reo Māori)

Present your findings and recommendations.

Teacher Implementation Notes

Timing: This unit can be completed in 4-6 lessons depending on depth.

Differentiation:

  • For students new to Chromebooks, provide printed step-by-step guides
  • Advanced students can explore Chrome OS developer mode (with supervision)
  • Pair students strategically for peer learning

Cultural Connections:

  • Invite a speaker from a Māori digital initiative (e.g., Te Hiku Media)
  • Explore how technology is used to preserve and share mātauranga Māori
  • Discuss digital versions of traditional Māori knowledge systems

Assessment Options:

  • Digital portfolio of completed activities
  • Peer teaching demonstrations
  • Reflection journal (digital or paper-based)
