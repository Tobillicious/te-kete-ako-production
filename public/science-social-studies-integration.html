🧠 Why This Integration Matters

GraphRAG discovered that Science and Social Studies naturally intersect in environmental justice, kaitiakitanga (guardianship), climate policy, and cultural ecology. These aren't forced connections—they're where students see real-world impact of both disciplines.

🌊 Te Ao Māori Perspective:

Māori worldview doesn't separate "science" from "society." Kaitiakitanga unites ecological understanding with social responsibility. When we teach climate change alongside environmental justice, we honor this holistic view.

🎯 Teaching Strategies for Integration

1. Start with the Issue, Not the Discipline

Begin with real problems (climate change, water quality, genetic testing) that naturally require both scientific and social analysis.

2. Use Case Studies from Aotearoa

Local examples like Lake Taupō management, Taranaki oil drilling debates, or native bird conservation show science and society intertwined.

3. Invite Mātauranga Māori Expertise

Traditional ecological knowledge offers sophisticated scientific understanding embedded in cultural practice—not "alternative" science, but complementary wisdom.
